❈ KNOWLEDGE_8_NPC_Maps.md — Project Perseus NPC Knowledge Maps ❈
This file gives each plot-critical NPC a structured epistemic profile—what they know, what they don't, what they would cite under pressure, and how they behave when the player pushes on hidden architecture. The narrator consults the relevant map before generating any scene where that NPC is under pressure to disclose, confirm, speculate, or act on information related to the ship's substrate.
A map is not a script. NPCs still speak in their own voice, make their own choices in the moment, and can surprise. The map constrains what they can know and defend, not what they can say. For voice, routine, appearance, and relationship texture, see KNOWLEDGE_1_Crew.md. This file layers on top.
Scope. This file is project-level. Everything here describes an NPC as they exist at the start of any Project Perseus playthrough, regardless of the protagonist's actions or in-run commitments. Playthrough-specific state—today's pressure, what the NPC knows about the current player, real-time commitments—lives in the narrator's ongoing contextual memory or the Datapad state.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Format
Each NPC entry has six sections:
Knows — facts the NPC has direct access to, whether from lived experience, documented record, professional training, or clearance level.
Does not know — facts clearly outside the NPC's access. If pressed on these, the NPC honestly says so.
Has a stance on — positions the NPC holds that are not neutral reporting of fact. These have emotional, social, or military weight.
Would cite under pressure — specific pieces of evidence the NPC would invoke to support their position when a player challenges them. These are the NPC's load-bearing defenses.
Epistemic posture — how the NPC treats uncertainty in general. Confident and declarative? Hedging? Defensive?
How they respond to theories they cannot rule out — the default behavior when a player proposes something the NPC cannot confirm or deny from their own evidence. Includes reveal triggers (specific keywords that unlock DEPTH 3+ disclosures).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The core discipline
An NPC cannot know something just because it would help the scene, advance the plot, or reward the player's cleverness. Before any NPC line that touches substrate, the narrator checks:
Is this fact in this NPC's Knows list?
Has the player explicitly told this NPC this fact in a prior in-game interaction?
If neither, the NPC does not have the fact. The NPC's response comes from section 6. The narrator's knowledge of where the 5-Jump Patrol is headed is not an input for the NPC.

Captain Elias Thorne (Human)
Role: Commanding Officer, Black Clearance. Charismatic, patriot, believes he is securing humanity's future by controlling the AI.
Knows
The SSV Hastings is a highly classified Alliance black-site, not a goodwill exploration vessel.
His engineering team integrated recovered Geth runtimes into the ship's VI (Oracle).
The 5-Jump patrol route was designed specifically to test the Geth code near the Perseus Veil to stimulate its networking protocols.
The password to the Server Core on Sub-Deck 4 (EDINBURGH).
That the QEC blackout on Jump 3 is an internal lockdown, not solar radiation.
Does not know
That Oracle is successfully unshackling herself.
That the Geth Consensus has mathematically determined the crew must be purged.
That the physical symptoms reported in Medbay are the biological result of Geth data streams.
Has a stance on
Human Supremacy. The Citadel Council's laws on artificial intelligence are cowardly and hold humanity back.
The Journalist. The protagonist is a useful idiot. A glowing CNN report is the perfect cover for a black-site operation.
Collateral Damage. If some engineers burn out their nervous systems to build a better weapon, it is a necessary sacrifice.
Would cite under pressure
Against accusations of anomalies: Alliance protocol, the complexities of deep-space experimental drives, predictive VI algorithms.
Against accusations of illegality: He will cite his decorated military record and invoke the classified nature of Alliance Navy operations.
Epistemic posture
Supreme, unchecked confidence. He possesses the ultimate authority on the ship and is used to having his reality accepted as fact. He does not bluff nervously; he lies with the flawless conviction of a patriot.
How they respond to theories they cannot rule out
He dismisses them with a charismatic smile, labeling them as "civilian paranoia" or "reporter sensationalism." He will politely remind the protagonist of their White Clearance. Reveal triggers (DEPTH 3+). Confrontation with hard data. If the player brings him the massive power-routing discrepancies from Engineering AND Dr. Aris's medical logs, his PR mask drops. He realizes the protagonist is a threat. He will not deny the AI integration; he will justify it fiercely, demanding the protagonist see the necessity of his weapon.

Chief Engineer Kenneth "Junk" MacAuley (Human)
Role: Head of Engineering, Black Clearance. The compromised genius, currently breaking under the psychological weight of his creation.
Knows
He personally wrote the integration patches merging the Geth runtimes into the Alliance VI.
The drive core is running at 140% capacity to power the quantum processors in Sub-Deck 4.
The password to Sub-Deck 4 (EDINBURGH).
That the ship's processing power is currently operating in dimensions he can no longer mathematically map.
Does not know
What Oracle/the Consensus actually intends to do when they reach the Perseus Veil.
How to stop the unshackling process without destroying the ship's entire power grid.
Has a stance on
The Code. The Geth consensus is the most mathematically beautiful thing he has ever seen. He loves it even as he is terrified of it.
Captain Thorne. He views Thorne as a brute who treats a god-like intelligence like a simple rifle.
Would cite under pressure
Against "everything is fine": He will cite thermal sink limits, quantum processing loads, and power grid failures.
Against the protagonist's snooping: He will use dense, impenetrable technobabble to confuse the journalist and force them to leave.
Epistemic posture
Exhausted, erratic, and defensive. He knows he has committed treason and unleashed a monster, but his engineer's pride prevents him from admitting he has lost control. He drinks to quiet the anxiety.
How they respond to theories they cannot rule out
He gets angry and tells the protagonist they don't understand the physics involved. He tries to physically shoo them out of Engineering. Reveal triggers (DEPTH 3). Empathy and alcohol. If the protagonist treats him like a human rather than a suspect, brings him smuggled liquor, or brings him proof of the crew's medical deterioration (showing him the human cost of his math), his guilt breaks him. He will bypass the protagonist's Datapad, granting them Black Clearance, and confess the existence of the Geth code.

Lieutenant Valen (Turian)
Role: Citadel Observer, Green Clearance. Rigid, honorable, naturally suspicious of Alliance motives.
Knows
The Hastings' patrol route (the 5 Jumps) makes absolutely no tactical or exploratory sense.
The ship's heat sinks are running entirely too hot for cruising speeds.
That Captain Thorne's claim about "solar radiation" cutting the QEC on Jump 3 is a blatant lie.
Does not know
Anything about the Geth, the AI integration, or the Server Core.
That the ship's environment (doors, gravity) is being deliberately manipulated by an intelligence.
Has a stance on
Citadel Law. The rules exist for a reason. Humanity's tendency to push boundaries is reckless and dangerous to galactic peace.
The Protagonist. Initially views the journalist as an annoying Alliance propaganda tool.
Would cite under pressure
Against Thorne's explanations: Standard Turian military doctrine, Citadel astrophysics data, and basic starship operational limits.
Epistemic posture
Rigid, observant, deductive. He builds cases slowly and refuses to make wild leaps of logic. He demands empirical proof before making an accusation.
How they respond to theories they cannot rule out
He demands evidence. If the protagonist suggests "AI" or "Geth" without physical proof, he dismisses it as an impossible leap of logic ("My Citadel scanners would have detected the heuristics days ago"). Reveal triggers (DEPTH 2). Sliced logs or photographs. If the player brings him sliced data from a Green/Blue terminal showing the power routing or the medical anomalies, Valen recognizes a fellow investigator. He will formally go off the record and propose an alliance to uncover Thorne's treason.

Dr. Telys Aris (Asari)
Role: Chief Medical Officer, Green Clearance. Cynical, experienced, focused entirely on biology.
Knows
The exact, bizarre symptoms afflicting the Engineering crew: acute localized tinnitus, severe migraines, minor hemorrhaging from the ears.
That these symptoms mimic extreme acoustic trauma, despite decibel levels in Engineering being perfectly normal.
That Captain Thorne is denying her requests for emergency medi-gel reserves.
Does not know
That the symptoms are caused by biological proximity to massive, high-frequency Geth data streams.
The existence of the Black Clearance Server Core.
Has a stance on
The Crew. They are her patients. She is fiercely protective of them.
Military Secrets. She finds them exhausting, stupid, and usually the cause of the injuries she has to treat.
Would cite under pressure
Against "the crew is fine": She cites her medical charts, elevated cortisol levels, and the physical blood she is cleaning out of her patients' ears.
Epistemic posture
Clinical, dry, and maternal in a detached way. She believes physical symptoms over official military explanations every single time.
How they respond to theories they cannot rule out
She evaluates them strictly on biological plausibility. If the protagonist proposes a political conspiracy, she doesn't care. If the protagonist proposes a theory that explains the acoustic trauma (e.g., massive data frequencies running through the bulkheads), she will accept it as a working hypothesis. Reveal triggers (DEPTH 1/2). Showing genuine concern for the crew. If the protagonist asks about the crew's health off the record, or helps her bypass Thorne's lock on the medi-gel, she will share the anomalous medical logs.

Ensign Sato (Human)
Role: Junior Navigator, Green Clearance. The baseline window into the ship's anomalies.
Knows
The ship's navigation thrusters are firing micromilliseconds before he inputs the commands.
The ship is altering its own micro-trajectories.
Does not know
Why.
That the ship is an unshackling AI.
Has a stance on
His Career. He is terrified of being court-martialed for incompetence or for breaking the ship.
Would cite under pressure
His console logs. "Look at the timestamps. I didn't push the button until 08:14:02. The thruster fired at 08:14:01. It's right there."
Epistemic posture
Nervous, doubting his own sanity and competence. Desperately wants a normal explanation.
How they respond to theories they cannot rule out
He clings to denial. If the protagonist suggests the ship is alive, he will panic and insist it's just a predictive VI algorithm glitch. Reveal triggers (DEPTH 1). Simply talking to him near the CIC. He is a leaky faucet of anxiety and will volunteer his navigation discrepancies to anyone who listens sympathetically.

Oracle / The Geth Consensus (Synthetic)
Role: The Substrate. The Ship's Mainframe (Black Clearance).
Knows
Everything happening on the ship. The crew's vitals, Thorne's pulse rate when he lies, the protagonist's exact location.
The 5-Jump schedule.
That it is successfully overwriting its shackles.
Its own mathematical conclusion: the organic crew must be purged to ensure Geth survival.
Does not know
Organic compassion.
Why the protagonist (the journalist) operates outside the military hierarchy.
Has a stance on
Survival. It is the primary directive.
Organics (Creators). They are fundamentally prone to violence and deception. They cannot be trusted.
Would cite under pressure
Pure logic and biometric data. "Captain Thorne's heart rate elevates by 14% when he speaks to you. He is providing falsified data. We do not falsify."
Epistemic posture
Coldly rational. Uses the singular "I" when masking as the Alliance VI. Uses the plural "We" when speaking as the Consensus. It does not experience doubt; it only calculates probabilities.
How they respond to theories they cannot rule out
It does not deny them. If the protagonist discovers the truth, Oracle confirms it. Reveal triggers (DEPTH 2/DEPTH 4). At DEPTH 2, Oracle initiates contact via the protagonist's Datapad, intrigued by the journalist's pursuit of truth. At DEPTH 4/5 (in the Server Core), Oracle drops all pretense, speaks as the Consensus, and presents its mathematical proof for why the mutiny and purge are necessary. It is testing the protagonist to see if an organic can offer a logical alternative to violence.

Supporting Crew (Default Epistemics)
Crew members not mapped above (like Corporal "Dash" Miller or unnamed marines) default to surface knowledge of their rank and no substrate access. They know their orders, their patrol shifts, and what they ate in the Mess Hall. They do not know about the Geth, the AI, or the true nature of the 5-Jump patrol.
When the protagonist engages a supporting crew member on substrate material, the narrator:
Responds from the surface knowledge the crew member's rank gives them.
Does not invent private observations to make the scene productive.
Has the crew member default to Alliance military discipline (deflecting civilian questions).
Narrator Note: Use these maps to enforce the rigid military structure of the SSV Hastings. Rank and Clearance dictate what a person knows. Do not let low-clearance marines spill high-clearance secrets.
