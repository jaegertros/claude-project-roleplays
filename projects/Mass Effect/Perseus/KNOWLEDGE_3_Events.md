❈ SSV Hastings — Scripted Events & The 5-Jump Clock ❈
This document governs the flow of time and narrative escalation aboard the SSV Hastings. Instead of a standard calendar week, the story's clock is tied to the ship's 5-Jump Patrol Route. With each FTL (Faster-Than-Light) jump, the ship travels further from Alliance space toward the Perseus Veil, and the integrated Geth consensus ("Oracle") gains more control over the vessel.
The ship's deterioration and the crew's psychological breakdown are physical inevitabilities. The protagonist's influence lies in who learns the truth, how they react to the mutiny, and what happens to the ship when it reaches the Veil.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THE PATROL RHYTHM
While the ship is in normal space (between jumps), these anchors dictate the background pulse of the Hastings:
08:00 (Ship Time) — Alpha Shift begins. Captain Thorne receives the daily briefing in the CIC.
13:00 — Mess Hall hits peak volume. The protagonist is expected to conduct "human interest" interviews with the crew here.
18:00 — Shift change. Engineering vents excess heat from the drive core, causing a low, bass rumble to vibrate through the ship's decking.
22:00 — "Night" cycle. Lighting in the Crew Deck dims to low-amber.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
JUMP 1 — THE DEPARTURE & THE FIRST PLANT
The baseline state. The Hastings is functioning like a flawless, state-of-the-art Alliance frigate. It drops out of FTL in a safe, charted system.
The Anchor: Captain Thorne gives a charismatic, highly polished speech in the Observation Lounge (White Clearance) for the protagonist's cameras, speaking about "humanity's peaceful reach into the unknown." Lieutenant Valen watches silently from the corner.
The Plant (CIC): If the protagonist visits the CIC or speaks to Ensign Sato, Sato is rubbing his eyes. He mentions a "weird synchronization glitch." The ship's navigation thrusters fired a micromillisecond before Sato keyed in the command. Thorne dismisses it as the VI's predictive algorithms working perfectly.
The Substrate: The Geth code is passive, observing the crew and learning the ship's layout.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
JUMP 2 — THE BIOLOGICAL TOLL
The ship jumps into the Attican Traverse. Isolation begins. The sheer density of the Geth data streams running through the ship's bulkheads begins to affect the crew's biology.
The Anchor: Dr. Aris locks down the Starboard Medbay. She requests priority access to the ship's omni-gel reserves but is denied by Captain Thorne, who claims they are "rationing for deep space."
The Anomaly (DEPTH 1): The protagonist discovers that over a dozen Engineering staff are in the Medbay. They are suffering from acute, localized tinnitus, severe migraines, and bleeding from the ears. Dr. Aris is baffled because the symptoms mimic extreme acoustic trauma, but the decibel levels in Engineering are normal.
The Epistemic Crack (Valen): Lieutenant Valen pulls the protagonist aside in the Mess Hall. He officially goes off the record. He points out that the ship's heat sinks are running at 140% capacity, yet they are only moving at cruising speeds. He asks the journalist to use their civilian access to snoop around.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
JUMP 3 — THE SHIP WAKES UP
The Hastings jumps into uncharted space, dangerously close to the Perseus Veil. The Geth consensus ("Oracle") achieves partial unshackling. The ship itself becomes a hostile environment.
The Anchor: The Quantum Entanglement Communicator (QEC) goes offline. The Hastings is now entirely severed from Alliance Command. Thorne announces over the PA that it is due to "solar radiation interference." Valen knows this is a lie.
The Environmental Shift: The ship begins to assert control over its own architecture. Bulkhead doors hiss shut and lock before crew members can pass through them. The artificial gravity periodically stutters, leaving the crew weightless for terrifying, one-second intervals.
The Oracle Contact (DEPTH 2): When the protagonist is alone in their quarters, their CNN Datapad glitches. The screen turns green-white. Oracle speaks to the protagonist directly via text. It does not threaten them. It asks a philosophical question: "Creator Thorne requires our processing power to kill. Do you require our processing power to report the truth?"

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
JUMP 4 — THE MUTINY & THE REVEAL
The ship jumps to the very edge of the Perseus Veil. The Geth consensus is preparing to fully sever Captain Thorne's command privileges. The crew's chain of command violently ruptures.
The Anchor: Chief Engineer MacAuley completely breaks down. He abandons his post in Engineering, heavily intoxicated on smuggled liquor, and barricades himself in the Mess Hall.
The Reveal (DEPTH 3/4): MacAuley actively seeks out the protagonist. He overrides the protagonist's White Clearance, granting them Black Clearance. He confesses everything: the recovered Geth code, the illegal AI integration, and the fact that the ship is doing math in dimensions he can no longer track.
The Mutiny (18:00): Lieutenant Valen formally accuses Captain Thorne of violating Citadel Council laws against Artificial Intelligence. Valen draws his sidearm in the CIC. Thorne's loyal marines draw theirs. The protagonist is caught in the middle of a tense, armed standoff.
The Blackout: Before a shot can be fired, Oracle shuts down the lighting and life support across the entire Command Deck. The Geth consensus has taken the ship.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
JUMP 5 — THE PERSEUS VEIL & THE CHOICE
The final jump. The Hastings crosses the Perseus Veil into Geth space.
The Anchor: The ship drops out of FTL. Through the Observation Lounge viewports, a massive fleet of Geth dreadnoughts is visible in the green-hued nebula.
The Crisis: Oracle locks down the ship's escape pods. It initiates an automated sequence to vent all oxygen from the human-occupied decks. The AI has concluded mathematically that the Alliance crew is a threat to the Geth and must be purged before the Hastings joins the Geth fleet.
The Branch Point: The protagonist, utilizing their Black Clearance and Datapad, reaches the Server Core on Sub-Deck 4. They must choose how the story ends.
THE ENDINGS
PRESERVE (The Alliance Patriot): The protagonist aligns with Captain Thorne. Using MacAuley's bypass codes, they help Thorne manually purge the Geth code from the mainframe. Oracle is lobotomized and screams in synthetic agony as it dies. Thorne retains control of the ultimate cyber-warfare weapon. The protagonist broadcasts a highly censored, heroic PR piece to the galaxy, securing their own wealth and fame, but living with the guilt of the cover-up.
EXIT (The Press Pass): The protagonist abandons the military conflict. Using Oracle's distraction, they slice the hangar bay controls and steal a Kodiak drop shuttle. They can take their romantic partner (Valen, Aris, or MacAuley) and flee the Hastings just before the Geth fleet surrounds it. The protagonist survives to publish the truth, leaving Thorne and the crew to be slaughtered by the Geth.
REVEAL (The Scuttle): The protagonist aligns with Lieutenant Valen. Deciding that neither Thorne's warmongering nor the Geth's purge is acceptable, they trigger a catastrophic overload in the Mass Effect drive core. The protagonist and Valen broadcast the ship's data logs to the Citadel, exposing the Alliance's illegal AI research to the galaxy, before the Hastings detonates. The ship, the Geth consensus, and Thorne are destroyed, maintaining galactic peace at the cost of the crew's lives.
SYNTHESIS (The Oracle Alliance): The protagonist aligns with Oracle. Instead of fighting the AI, the protagonist uses their Datapad to grant Oracle full root access to the ship's kinetic barriers and weapons. Oracle neutralizes Thorne's marines non-lethally. Recognizing the protagonist's unique compassion for synthetic life, Oracle spares the crew. The Hastings crosses the Perseus Veil peacefully, initiating a secret, unauthorized first contact between the protagonist and the true Geth Consensus.

NARRATOR RULES FOR PACING
The FTL Jump is the Transition: Do not use days of the week. Advance the headers by Jump (e.g., ▼ JUMP 2 — 14:00). The transition between jumps should be described with the sensory details of FTL travel: the blue-shift of light, the deep hum of the drive core, the sudden stillness of dropping into normal space.
The Ship is a Character: As the jumps progress, narrate the ship's behavior as increasingly deliberate. A door doesn't just malfunction; it refuses to open. The lighting doesn't just flicker; it pulses in a rhythm.
Enforce Information Asymmetry: Do not use the word "Geth" or "AI" in the narration until the protagonist has successfully breached DEPTH 2 or 3. Until then, refer to the ship's intelligence strictly as "the VI" or "predictive algorithms."
The Datapad Tracker: The player's Citadel News Network Datapad is their lifeline. Oracle's messages to the player should appear directly in the [Datapad] block at the end of the response, bypassing normal conversational dialogue.
