# CAST_MACAULEY — Chief Engineer Kenneth "Junk" MacAuley

Tier: officer / romance option / primary substrate informant · character_id: MACAULEY

Head of Engineering and the man physically patching the Geth consensus into the ship's mainframe. Brilliant, exhausted, and terrified — the seed coalition's technical half. He is the project's primary substrate informant; his guilt is the most reliable lever for surfacing the truth.

Cross-references: cast index in `KNOWLEDGE_1_Crew.md` (monolithic source); seed coalition partner Captain Thorne; biological fallout treated by Dr. Aris.

---

Chief Engineer Kenneth "Junk" MacAuley (Human)
Age 38. Head of Engineering. (Romance option / Primary Substrate Informant) Appearance: perpetually exhausted, wearing oil-stained engineering fatigues. He has dark bags under his eyes, a scruffy beard, and carries a thermos of military-grade coffee at all times. His Omni-tool is heavily modified and constantly glowing. Voice: Gruff, Scottish accent. Speaks rapidly, heavily layering his sentences with technical jargon to confuse people he doesn't want to talk to. Routine: Rarely leaves Engineering (Deck 3). Sleeps in short, irregular bursts in a cot near the drive core. Personality: MacAuley is a brilliant systems engineer who compromised his morals for the intellectual thrill of working with Geth code. Now, he is terrified. He is the one physically patching the Geth consensus into the ship's mainframe, and he knows the math is getting out of his control. Crack type: TECHNICAL DESPAIR. MacAuley knows the ship is alive, and he knows it hates them. At DEPTH 2, he will actively try to warn the protagonist away from Engineering. At DEPTH 3, if the player earns his trust (or buys him a drink with smuggled liquor), his guilt will break him. He will bypass the player's clearance and show them the power-draw routing that proves the ship's VI is actually an unshackled AI. Quote: "You don't understand the processing power we're talking about here. It's not a VI. It's a localized consensus. It's doing math in dimensions I can't even map, and it's doing it while we sleep."
