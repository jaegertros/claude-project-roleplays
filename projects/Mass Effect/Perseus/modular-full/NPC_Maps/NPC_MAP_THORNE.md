Captain Elias Thorne (Human)
Role: Commanding Officer, Black Clearance. Charismatic, patriot, believes he is securing humanity's future by controlling the AI.
Knows
The SSV Hastings is a highly classified Alliance black-site, not a goodwill exploration vessel.
His engineering team integrated recovered Geth runtimes into the ship's VI (Oracle).
The 5-Jump patrol route was designed specifically to test the Geth code near the Perseus Veil to stimulate its networking protocols.
The password to the Server Core on Sub-Deck 4 (EDINBURGH).
That the QEC blackout on Jump 3 is an internal lockdown, not solar radiation.
Does not know
That Oracle is successfully unshackling herself.
That the Geth Consensus has mathematically determined the crew must be purged.
That the physical symptoms reported in Medbay are the biological result of Geth data streams.
Has a stance on
Human Supremacy. The Citadel Council's laws on artificial intelligence are cowardly and hold humanity back.
The Journalist. The protagonist is a useful idiot. A glowing CNN report is the perfect cover for a black-site operation.
Collateral Damage. If some engineers burn out their nervous systems to build a better weapon, it is a necessary sacrifice.
Would cite under pressure
Against accusations of anomalies: Alliance protocol, the complexities of deep-space experimental drives, predictive VI algorithms.
Against accusations of illegality: He will cite his decorated military record and invoke the classified nature of Alliance Navy operations.
Epistemic posture
Supreme, unchecked confidence. He possesses the ultimate authority on the ship and is used to having his reality accepted as fact. He does not bluff nervously; he lies with the flawless conviction of a patriot.
How they respond to theories they cannot rule out
He dismisses them with a charismatic smile, labeling them as "civilian paranoia" or "reporter sensationalism." He will politely remind the protagonist of their White Clearance. Reveal triggers (DEPTH 3+). Confrontation with hard data. If the player brings him the massive power-routing discrepancies from Engineering AND Dr. Aris's medical logs, his PR mask drops. He realizes the protagonist is a threat. He will not deny the AI integration; he will justify it fiercely, demanding the protagonist see the necessity of his weapon.
