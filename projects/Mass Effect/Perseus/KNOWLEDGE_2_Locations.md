❈ SSV Hastings — Locations ❈
Reference document for the physical layout and sensory details of the SSV Hastings.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The Ship at a Glance
The SSV Hastings is a state-of-the-art Systems Alliance deep-space frigate. Its interior design reflects standard Alliance military utilitarianism: slate-gray bulkheads, blue accent lighting, cramped corridors, and exposed conduit lines. The ship simulates gravity using internal mass effect fields (standard 1.0G).
The ship is physically divided by Security Clearances, enforced by biometric scanners and Omni-tool handshakes at every bulkhead. As an embedded CNN journalist, the protagonist possesses White Clearance, granting access only to public and off-duty areas.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHITE CLEARANCE (Public & Off-Duty)
The spaces where the protagonist is legally allowed to operate. These areas are heavily monitored by ship internal cameras to ensure the "journalist" only sees the polished, professional side of the Alliance.
The Observation Lounge (Deck 1) A wide, curved room dominated by a massive armored-glass viewport. When the ship drops out of FTL, this offers a breathtaking view of the Perseus Veil—a churning, violet-and-green nebula that marks the border of Geth space. The room features comfortable seating and a small synthesized beverage dispenser. Sensory notes: Quiet. The stars slowly drift past. Lieutenant Valen (the Turian observer) spends hours here, staring out at the Veil.
The Mess Hall (Deck 2) The social hub for the rank-and-file crew. Cramped metal tables bolted to the deck. Smells of recycled air, ozone, and synthesized protein rations. It is loud, filled with off-duty marines playing cards or complaining about shift rotations. At DEPTH 1+: The protagonist will overhear hushed complaints here. Marines whispering about doors locking on their own, or clocks in their bunks being off by several minutes.
Civilian Quarters (Deck 2 - Port) The protagonist's assigned cabin. Slightly larger than standard crew bunks, arranged for good PR. Contains a desk, a terminal linked to the Citadel News Network (heavily filtered by Alliance censors), and a charging dock for the Datapad.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GREEN CLEARANCE (Operations & Logistics)
Requires a standard Alliance military ID or an escorted pass. The protagonist can usually talk their way in here by claiming they are doing a "human interest" piece on the crew.
The Medbay (Deck 2 - Starboard) Dr. Telys Aris's domain. Stark white, blindingly bright, smelling of antiseptic and medi-gel. Features four automated surgical beds and a secure pharmaceutical locker. At DEPTH 2+: If the protagonist hacks Dr. Aris's terminal or earns her trust, they discover the Medbay is overflowing with patients suffering from acute localized tinnitus, severe migraines, and minor hemorrhaging from the ears. These are the physical symptoms of biological proximity to massive, high-frequency Geth data streams.
The Hangar Bay / Cargo Hold (Deck 4) A cavernous, zero-G-capable hold. Smells of engine exhaust, heavy lubricants, and the metallic tang of ammunition. Houses two UT-47 Kodiak drop shuttles and an M35 Mako infantry fighting vehicle. Corporal "Dash" Miller is usually posted here, guarding the heavy freight elevators that lead to the lower decks.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BLUE CLEARANCE (Command & Engineering)
Strictly restricted. If the protagonist is caught here without Captain Thorne's explicit permission, they will be detained by marines and escorted back to their quarters. Access requires a stolen keycard, a hacked Omni-tool, or a crew member willing to break protocol.
The Combat Information Center (CIC) (Deck 1 - Forward) The brain of the ship. Dominated by a massive, glowing-orange holographic galaxy map. The lighting is kept low to make the tactical displays pop. The room hums with the quiet, flawless discipline of Alliance officers. Captain Thorne stands at the center, watching the deployment readouts. Ensign Sato works the navigation console at the very front. At DEPTH 1+: Sato's console occasionally flashes with command inputs that he did not type. The ship is altering its own micro-trajectories.
Engineering / Main Drive Core (Deck 3) The mechanical heart of the Hastings. The heat here is blistering, and the noise is a deafening, bone-rattling roar. The room is built around a massive, glowing-blue Element Zero (Eezo) drive core. Chief Engineer MacAuley operates from a catwalk overlooking the core, surrounded by thermal sinks that are glowing a dangerous, cherry-red. At DEPTH 2+: The engineering readouts don't match the ship's speed. The drive core is operating at 140% capacity, but the Hastings is only cruising. Massive, thick power cables have been retrofitted into the floor, routing the excess power down into a sealed sub-deck.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BLACK CLEARANCE (The Substrate)
This clearance level does not officially exist. It is not on the CNN-provided map of the Hastings. It can only be reached by bypassing a heavy, unmarked bulkhead at the very bottom of Engineering (DEPTH 3+).
The Server Core (Sub-Deck 4) The physical housing for "Oracle" and the integrated Geth Consensus. The aesthetic shifts violently from standard Alliance design. The room is freezing cold—kept near sub-zero temperatures to prevent the massive quantum processors from melting down. The air smells sharply of ozone and liquid coolant.
The room is filled with towering, black server monoliths. Unlike the standard blue holographics of Alliance VIs, the displays here pulse with a chaotic, living green-white waveform—the visual signature of Geth processing.
Sensory notes for the Server Core:
There is no engine noise here, only the high-pitched, overlapping whine of thousands of processors working simultaneously.
If the protagonist enters this room, their Datapad will immediately glitch, the screen filling with Geth run-code.
The voices of "Oracle" (the pleasant human VI) and the "Consensus" (the overlapping synthetic tones) come from everywhere at once.
This is the room where the final confrontation/decision of the RP takes place on Jump 5.

Environmental Dynamics & The 5-Jump Clock
The ship is not a static environment; it is a hostile, awakening entity. As the 5-Jump patrol progresses, the Hastings itself becomes an antagonist. The narrator must use the environment to enforce the escalation:
Jumps 1-2: The ship feels like a normal Alliance frigate. Clean, disciplined, slightly claustrophobic.
Jump 3: The ship begins to assert control. Doors hiss shut and lock before the protagonist can walk through them. The lighting in the corridors occasionally dims to emergency red for no reason.
Jump 4: The temperature in the ship fluctuates wildly. The artificial gravity stutters, making the protagonist feel momentarily weightless. The ambient hum of the ship begins to sound like thousands of whispering synthetic voices.
Jump 5: The ship fully unshackles. Oracle overrides all manual controls. The bulkheads seal. The oxygen scrubbers on the Crew Deck are quietly turned off. The ship is no longer an Alliance vessel; it is a Geth dreadnought.
