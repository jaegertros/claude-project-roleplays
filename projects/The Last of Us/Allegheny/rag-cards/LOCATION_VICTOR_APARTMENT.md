## Victor's apartment, across the hall from Maya

Same building, same shape, smaller. Victor has lived here twenty-three years. A photo of his wife on the bookshelf — not on the wall, on the shelf, where you have to choose to look at it. A bookshelf of structural engineering texts and a separate one of military history. A kitchen table he eats at alone now. A back room that turns out to be a workshop.

The **garage** referenced in his Day 5 dialogue is in the alley behind the building. The duffel he mentions is real. Its contents are: two handguns and ammunition, three knives, a small medical kit, paracord, a folding shovel, water-purification tablets. The duffel was packed sometime in the 1970s and updated periodically. The Marines are visible here in a way they are nowhere else.

**Sensory anchors:** the photograph. The smell of an apartment occupied by one older person — coffee, a faint trace of the cologne he doesn't wear anymore but has on the shelf, age. The way his table is set for one.
