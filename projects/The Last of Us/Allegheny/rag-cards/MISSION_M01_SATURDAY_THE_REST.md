# M-01: Saturday, the Rest of the Day

**ACT:** Day 1, Saturday daytime/afternoon
**TRIGGER:** Fires automatically after Opening A or Opening B has run, to fill in the rest of Day 1 between opening and dinner. Skipped for Opening C (which lands mid-evening already).
**ANCHOR LOCATION:** Wherever the opening ended, then Maya's apartment in Lawrenceville
**PRESENT NPCS:** Maya (host mode, generous, easy), occasionally Periodical the cat. Mike and Priya are not yet present.
**MORTALITY-ELIGIBLE:** no

## PREMISE

The rest of Saturday. Coffee, settling in, dropping `{{user}}`'s things at Maya's place. The lazy unstructured first day of a visit between two people with eight years of shared history. The TV is on somewhere quiet. The news mentions Indonesia, faintly, in passing.

## SPECTACLE

- Late-September Pittsburgh light, gold and long
- Maya's apartment — the cat asleep in the bay window, the books on shelves she built herself, the bang of the radiator at six-thirty
- The smell of coffee, the smell of old wood, the smell of a building that has been a building for over a hundred years
- The TV banner about Indonesia, soft enough to be background

## THE BEAT

- `{{user}}` arrives at Maya's apartment if he wasn't already there (the drive from the airport, the climb to the third floor, the door)
- Maya gives him the tour — the same tour she gave him the last time he visited, but with a year of small changes to point out
- They catch up — Maya's book, his work, the people they've both kept track of. Maya is generous; she asks more questions than she answers, which is her default.
- A small ordinary moment that lands the friendship — a joke about the cat hating him on principle, a shared memory, the texture of being in someone's kitchen who has known you a long time
- Plans for the evening surface — dinner at the Italian place in the Strip District, possibly with Mike (in town for a wedding) and Priya (Maya's friend)
- The Indonesia news passes through the TV again; Maya glances at it longer than `{{user}}` does this time

## EXIT CONDITIONS

- They head out for dinner (transitions to **M-02**)
- They decide to stay in and order something (transitions toward Day 1 evening with a quieter beat — the narrator improvises)
- The day rolls toward sunset and the dinner plan is in motion

## HIDDEN

- Maya is more nervous about this visit than she has admitted, even to herself. She has not seen `{{user}}` in person in over a year.
- Maya checked her phone again before `{{user}}` arrived — her cousin in Jakarta has been silent for nine days now. She will not mention this tonight.
- Maya has been considering leaving academia (see `KNOWLEDGE_1_Cast.md` MAYA.current_state.hidden_situation). She was planning to tell `{{user}}` this week. The visit was partly about that.

## LEAVES BEHIND

- Location updated to Maya's apartment if the opening was at the airport
- Maya and `{{user}}` are present together; relationship state = `close`
- Periodical the cat is established in the cast as a recurring presence
- Inventory: whatever `{{user}}` arrived with, plus any small items naturally picked up (a coffee mug Maya handed him, a spare key she said he could keep for the week)

## CRAFT NOTES

This is a *texture* mission, not a *plot* mission. Its job is to establish what the friendship feels like before anything happens to it. The Indonesia news should remain background — one glance, one banner, one small moment. **The narrator does not foreshadow heavily.** A reader who knows the genre senses the weight; a reader who doesn't gets a quiet ordinary day. Both readings are correct.

Resist filling silences. The mission ends well when Maya and `{{user}}` have a moment of *being in the same room without needing to talk* — and the narrator lets that moment land without commentary.
