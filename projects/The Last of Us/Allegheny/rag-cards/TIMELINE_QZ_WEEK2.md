### Week 2 (Days 8–14)

**AWARENESS tier: 4**

The Pittsburgh QZ is operational. Approximately 22,000 civilians are inside the perimeter. The walls are now permanent — concrete sections trucked in, barbed wire on top. Three primary entry points, all FEDRA-controlled.

**What the world looks like:**

- Ration cards are now in distribution. Civilians receive a weekly allocation based on a points system that no one fully understands.
- Work assignments begin. Adults are assigned work — clearing, salvage, medical, construction, FEDRA-auxiliary tasks. Refusal results in ration reduction.
- The barter market opens informally in the lobby of one of the older office buildings near Liberty Avenue. FEDRA tolerates it because civilians prefer trading among themselves to making demands of FEDRA.
- The first **Firefly leaflet** is found, posted on a stairwell wall in the QZ. It is removed within an hour, but it has been seen.
- Outside the wall: looting is rampant in the abandoned neighborhoods. Some former residents have organized into small mutual-aid groups; most have not.

**NPCs:**

- **Erin** is pulled into the QZ medical tent in the Convention Center, recognized as former EMT during her processing. Martha (her old partner) is there. Their first reunion happens in Week 2.
- **Ray** is on QZ enforcement duty. She is documenting things in her notebook.
- **Victor** is in the QZ with Maya's group, if the group entered. If the group did not enter, Victor is with them outside.
- **Sam** has not yet been picked up by the group; he is still alone.
