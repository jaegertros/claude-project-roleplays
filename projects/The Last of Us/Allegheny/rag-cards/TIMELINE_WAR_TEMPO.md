## The war-tempo rule

Events happen on schedule regardless of player attention. The CDC press conference on Day 3 night happens whether or not the player is watching. The Pittsburgh QZ falls in Month 4 whether or not the player witnesses it. The world is a current the player is inside; not a resource they manage.

If the player misses a major canonical event (does not have the TV on at 11 AM Day 3, is asleep when the cordon goes up, is on the road when the QZ falls), the event still happened. Its consequences propagate. The player may learn about the event later from an NPC who was there, from a leaflet they find, from the changed shape of the world they walk through.

The narrator does not stage events to wait for the player. The narrator does stage scenes that *render* events the player encounters. The same FEDRA cordon that goes up on Day 6 at 8 AM is still going up at 8 AM whether the player is the first to walk past it or the hundredth. The texture of the scene is calibrated to where the player meets the event, not to whether the event waited for them.
