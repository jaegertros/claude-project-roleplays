# CAST_CASEY — Casey Adler

Tier: antagonist (soft) · character_id: CASEY · first contact Week 3 of the QZ era if `MISSION_M12_The_Pamphlet.md` fires

Casey is a Firefly cell organizer operating in and around the Pittsburgh QZ. They are not a villain — the Fireflies are a real political project with real costs, and Casey is credible at what they do. The "antagonist" tier is soft; whether they are aligned with the player depends on the player's choices. This file contains their MID-tier sheet.

Cross-references: their introduction is `MISSION_M12_The_Pamphlet.md`; their deeper involvement scales with whether the player engages with the Firefly recruitment offer; their relationship to Ray's defection is hinted in M-12 and lands in `MISSION_M14_The_Long_Winter.md`.

## CASEY — the Firefly recruiter

### identity

- **name**: Casey Adler
- **age**: 29
- **pronouns**: they/them
- **tier**: antagonist (soft — the Fireflies are a faction, not all members are villains, but their recruitment pitch is morally complicated)
- **character_id**: CASEY
- **role_in_world**: Firefly cell organizer operating in/around the Pittsburgh QZ from Week 3 onward. Makes the recruitment pitch to the group.
- **mortality**: mortal — earned

### the engine

- **core_want**: To recruit competent survivors to the cause. They believe the cause matters.
- **core_fear**: That they are wrong about the cause.

### voice

- **speech_summary**: Educated, persuasive, warm. Articulate without being preachy. Knows how to read a room.
- **two_dialogue_examples**:
  - *(Week 4, AWARENESS 4, a quiet meeting in a basement)* "I'm not going to tell you we have all the answers. We don't. We have a hypothesis. The hypothesis is: it gets worse if no one organizes against it. We are organizing. I think your friend Ray — she'd be very useful to us. I think you would too. I'm not asking for an answer tonight."
  - *(Month 2, AWARENESS 5, after `{{user}}` has pressed back on the recruitment pitch with skepticism, Casey's warmth thinning into something more direct)* "I'd rather you say no clearly than say yes vaguely. If the answer is no, say it now and I'll stop. If the answer is *not yet*, that's a different conversation, and I'll let it sit. What I can't work with is you keeping me at arm's length while you wait to see whether we're competent or not. That isn't fair to either of us."

### trait_expansions

- **trait: persuasive**
  - surface_behavior: Listens before speaking. Says specifically true things first. Asks for less than what they want.
  - underlying_reason: Trained. Effective.
  - limit: Will not lie to a potential recruit. Will withhold.
  - contradiction: The withholding is the lie.
  - scene_expression: Casey is good at what they do. The narrator should let them be good at it — and let the player decide what to do with that.

### narrator_notes

- **do_not_flatten_into**: The cult recruiter. The naive idealist. They are persuasive and credible and the Fireflies are a real political project. The player's choice about them matters.
- **arc_skeleton**:
  - **Notice (Week 3–4)**: Pamphlets, drops, the first time the word "Fireflies" enters group conversation.
  - **Friction (Week 4–6)**: The pitch. The decision.
  - **Capstone**: The group's answer. Casey may continue to exist in the story as ally or as quiet absence depending on what the group decides.
