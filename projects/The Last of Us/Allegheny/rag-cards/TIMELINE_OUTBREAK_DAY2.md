### Day 2 — Sunday

**AWARENESS tier: 0, drifting toward 1 by evening**

**What is happening in the world:**

- The Jakarta numbers have grown overnight. Five hospitals are now reporting clusters. The Indonesian government has imposed travel restrictions on three provinces.
- US case count rises to twelve, with confirmed cases in **Los Angeles (5), San Francisco (2), Seattle (3), Chicago (1), and Atlanta (1).** All travelers or close contacts of travelers from Southeast Asia.
- The **CDC issues a private alert to major-city hospital networks** to watch for "unusual neurological/aggressive presentations with no known viral etiology." UPMC receives this alert mid-afternoon Sunday.
- **A CDC press conference is scheduled for Monday morning** to address the cluster.

**What is on the player's channels:**

- **News (TV, radio, online):** Indonesia is now the lead international story on most outlets. The word "fungal" surfaces more frequently. A WHO spokesperson is interviewed; she is careful and not reassuring. A scrolling banner mentions "US cases under investigation."
- **Phones:** Mostly normal. Some calls to Indonesia and Malaysia are lagging or not connecting; international networks are overloaded by people checking on family.
- **Social/conversation:** Some people are starting to pay attention. The bar television is on with sound off. Walter looks up at it more than usual. A regular at the Thunderbird mentions that her brother in Singapore is "freaking out." Maya's mother has called her once today and Maya called back. Both calls were brief and pleasant.

**The first real public crack:**

- Late Sunday afternoon, **a video circulates** on social media — taken in a Jakarta hospital corridor, showing a patient attacking medical staff before being restrained. The video is captioned in multiple languages. By Sunday evening it has been viewed by tens of millions of people. Most viewers think it is staged or misrepresented. Some don't.

**NPCs offscreen on Day 2:**

- **Arlen** worked the Saturday overnight shift and is sleeping through Sunday daytime. She has not yet seen the CDC alert (her hospital received it; the email is in her work inbox; she'll see it Monday morning when she clocks in).
- **Erin** is off work Sunday. She does laundry, waters her plants, calls her mother in Ambridge for forty minutes.
- **Maya's mother** (in Detroit) calls Maya around 3 PM to check in. Their conversation is normal. Maya does not mention her cousin.
- **Maya's cousin in Jakarta** is, by this point, almost certainly dead or turned. She had been ill since approximately ten days before the story opens.
