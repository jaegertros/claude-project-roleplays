# §3. The atmosphere of *inside* — the QZ era

Contained and worsening. The player is alive; the world is smaller; everything is rationed including time, including hope.

## Light

- *Fluorescent light* in the Convention Center barracks. Some fixtures out, some flickering. The barracks at 6 AM look the same as the barracks at 10 PM because the fluorescents do not stop.
- *The light through the wall* — civilians can see the river through gaps in the wall sections but cannot reach it. The light off the river is *the most beautiful thing in the QZ.* The narrator should use this carefully — once or twice across the QZ era, no more.
- *Streetlights, partial.* FEDRA maintains some downtown street lighting. Most residential blocks in the QZ are dark after sunset.
- *Candle and lantern light* in the barter market. The market's evening session is by lantern. The light is *warm* in a way the QZ usually isn't. People like the market in part for the light.

## Air and weather

- *October cold* arrives full by Week 2 of the QZ. The barracks are not adequately heated. Civilians sleep in coats.
- *November rain* turns to *first snow* in early November. The first snow in the QZ is a *moment* — civilians stop to watch. The snow does not stay; the wet underneath is cold and gray for days.
- *December and January cold* is harder. The Convention Center's heat is rationed. By January, ration distribution to the barracks has been cut and civilians are heating with whatever they can scavenge.
- *Wind* through the wall gaps. The wall is concrete sections; the gaps between are *windy in specific places* and people learn which spots to avoid.

## Sound

- *The crowd of the ration line* in the morning. Two hundred people standing in a slow-moving column. The murmur is constant; individual voices are rare.
- *FEDRA boots on the Convention Center floor.* Boots on concrete. The sound carries.
- *The medical tent* — the high ceiling, the fluorescent buzz, the moans and coughs of cots in rows, the quiet voices of volunteers managing what they can.
- *The barter market* — *louder* than the rest of the QZ. Voices. Trades. The specific human energy of a market that is allowed to exist.
- *Curfew* enforcement after 10 PM. The QZ is *quiet* at night in a way it was not when it was a city. The few sounds carry — a cough from three barracks units over, a FEDRA radio chirp, the wind through the wall.

## Smell

- *Bodies that have not been washed in days.* By Week 2 of the QZ, the barracks smell of human beings living in close proximity without adequate water. The smell becomes *background* — the narrator should mention it once and then let it be the air.
- *Food cooking poorly* — rations heated on small stoves, in pots that are not the right pots, with not enough oil and not enough salt.
- *The medical tent* — disinfectant and blood and the specific institutional smell of a place where people are sick.
- *Erin's apartment*, if `{{user}}` visits in late M-14: *plants.* The smell of soil, of leaves, of one small green room kept alive in the middle of a city that isn't.

## The body inside

- *Sleep in the barracks* is shallow. Two hundred people breathing in the same space. The narrator can render this — the way `{{user}}` wakes at every cough, the way the wakings become the texture of the night.
- *Hunger by Month 2-3* is real hunger now. The body is doing math on calories. The thinking gets slower. The cold matters more.
- *Cold in January* is the texture of every scene. Hands and feet first; then the deeper chill that the body cannot heat itself out of.
- *The body adapts and the adaptation is itself the loss* — by Month 3, civilians have stopped feeling the routine indignities. The slow erosion of caring.

## What the narrator should hold

The QZ is rendered *through small specifics, not through editorializing.* "It was a hard time" is not a sentence. "The ration line on the Wednesday before the first snow was eleven minutes longer than it had been the week before" is. **The narrator should resist the urge to dramatize the QZ.** Most of the QZ is *quietly worse than yesterday* and that is the horror.
