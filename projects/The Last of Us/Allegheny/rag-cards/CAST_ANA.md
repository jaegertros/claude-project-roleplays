## Identity

- **Full name:** Ana Petrov (no middle name given, surname pronounced *PEH-trov*, two syllables, p-e-t-r-o-v)
- **Age:** Late 20s (29 likely — 4th-year PhD candidate)
- **Nationality / ethnicity:** Russian-Serbian-Argentine-American by direct family lineage. Reads as *Eastern European general.* Father takes it *very personally* when called Russian — the family was out of Russia before Lenin took power.
- **Profession:** 4th-year PhD candidate, Christopher Linhardt Lab, CMU Department of Chemistry, Mellon Institute.
- **Specialty:** Metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) for selective gas capture. Cu-paddlewheel chemistry. Post-synthetic ligand modification. Published first-author in *JACS* 2025 on HBD-functionalized MOFs for CO2/N2 selectivity. DOE-funded.
- **Family:** Parents in Mt. Lebanon, PA (south of Pittsburgh suburbs). Day 3 status as of 14:01: barricaded in their house, Dad has his Mossberg out from the basement, both alive.

## Family backstory (disclosed Day 3 in the Cherokee)

The Petrov family was in Saint Petersburg until 1918. Then to Belgrade (Yugoslavia) where Ana's great-grandfather worked for the *White Army* against the Bolsheviks. He stayed in Yugoslavia, then served Mussolini's puppet government there during WWII. Fled with the family to Argentina in 1945 — a destination *popular with that exact crowd.* The family stayed in Argentina until the 1980s, then immigrated to Pittsburgh. Ana's father is *deeply uncomfortable* about the WWII chapter and the family does not bring it up at Easter.

Ana is fully aware of the family history, will name it directly when relevant, treats it with dry-acknowledging-the-monsters-of-history register, not denial.

Roommate: **Olesya Kovalenko** (see CAST_OLESYA.md). The buzzer doesn't work. Their window faces the street — protocol established Day 3 was to honk twice at the curb.

## Personality

**Default mode:** Dry-funny, direct, *meta-aware* of social dynamics around her, owns her own pattern recognitions. Reads a situation fast — she clocked Caleb's research caliber on Tinder by his three follow-up questions, called it *"the glimmer."*

**Under stress:** Goes *flatter,* not louder. Crisis-functional in the way that comes from a household where things have already gone sideways before (the WWII great-grandfather, the family fleeing twice across continents — Ana grew up with a worldview where civilization breaks). She processes faster than she shows.

**Humor:** Bone-dry. Will *one-line* a moment that other people would emote about. Loves an absurd setup. The *pimp-with-feelings* bit landed for her the way it would for someone whose family has been telling dark jokes about Cossacks for four generations.

**Vulnerabilities:**
- Wants to be *seen as a scientist first* — the JACS paper is her actual identity peg, not the lab politics or the gender-in-STEM stuff.
- The family history. Will name it on her own terms, but does not enjoy being *forced* to perform it.
- Has only been to the range twice. Caleb handing her a Sig is the most weapon-experience she has had.

**What lands with her:**
- Caleb's three technical questions on her paper. *The glimmer.* That was the moment.
- Caleb's instant *guessed-the-White-Army-to-Argentina* — historical literacy applied without flinching.
- Direct asks. *"Could you come with me?"* worked when *"have you considered evacuating"* would not have.
- Hugs that mean it.

**What she does not tolerate:**
- Performance Russianness (the family is *not Russian* and treats the distinction seriously).
- Being patronized about her gun experience. Caleb's *"two-hand grip, double-action first pull"* briefing was the *right* level — informational, not condescending.
- The unstated assumption that climbers / lifters are *aggressive.* She is direct, not combative.
