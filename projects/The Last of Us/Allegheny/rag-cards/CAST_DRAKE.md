# CAST_DRAKE — Lieutenant Andrew Drake

Tier: antagonist · character_id: DRAKE · first sighting Day 6 at the QZ cordon; recurring antagonist presence Week 1 - Month 4

Drake is the FEDRA officer of record at the cordon sector the group passes through, then the QZ enforcement face the group encounters across the QZ era. He is not a cartoon fascist — he is a man following procedure that has hardened beyond the procedure he signed up for. This file contains his MID-tier sheet.

Cross-references: he first appears at `MISSION_M09_The_Cordon.md`; he is the procedural face of the QZ era; his arc capstone is either confrontation, conversion (rare), or quiet removal when the group leaves his sector or when FEDRA collapses in Month 4.

---

## DRAKE — the FEDRA officer at the cordon

### identity

- **name**: Lieutenant Andrew Drake
- **age**: 42
- **pronouns**: he/him
- **tier**: antagonist
- **character_id**: DRAKE
- **role_in_world**: FEDRA officer in command of the QZ Sector 4 cordon. The face of the new authority.
- **mortality**: mortal — earned, but as antagonist not as ally

### the engine

- **core_want**: To restore order. The "order" he has in mind is the order of two weeks ago, which is gone. He has not let himself acknowledge that it is gone.
- **core_fear**: That what he is doing is wrong.

### voice

- **speech_summary**: Clipped, official, mid-volume. Reads orders aloud. Performs procedure.
- **two_dialogue_examples**:
  - *(Day 6, AWARENESS 3, at the cordon)* "Step to the line. Hands at your sides. Speak only when prompted. This will go faster if you cooperate. Cooperation is in your interest."
  - *(Week 3, AWARENESS 4, the QZ admin office, late, the procedure manual open on his desk and visibly worn from rereading)* "I have my orders, sir. I do not write them. I follow them. If you have a complaint about them you will address it to my CO, who will address it to *his* CO, and so on. That is how this works. That is how it has always worked. It is working now."

### trait_expansions

- **trait: by-the-book**
  - surface_behavior: Procedural. Will not deviate.
  - underlying_reason: Procedure has kept him together in a job that's broken everyone else.
  - scene_expression: The narrator should let him be procedural rather than monstrous. Procedure can be its own monstrosity.

### narrator_notes

- **do_not_flatten_into**: The cartoon fascist. The conflicted-soldier-with-a-heart. The villain who exists to be killed. He is a man following the orders he believes in. He is wrong.
- **arc_skeleton**:
  - **Notice (Day 6)**: At the cordon. He is the threshold.
  - **Friction (Week 2–3)**: He is the recurring face. His procedure has costs.
  - **Capstone**: Either confrontation with the group, or conversion (rare — he is harder to turn than Ray), or quiet removal from the story when the group leaves his sector. His death, if it happens, is paid in the right shape.
