# CAST: Olesya Kovalenko

**Tier:** FULL (named_minor in state schema)

**Anchored as:** Ana Petrov's roommate, picked up alongside Ana during the Day 3 outbreak run; rescued by Caleb into the Mansions fort.

---

## Identity

- **Full name:** Olesya Kovalenko (pronounced *oh-LEH-syah ko-vah-LEN-ko*)
- **Age:** Late 20s (Pitt biostats grad student / postdoc — exact stage unestablished)
- **Nationality / ethnicity:** Ukrainian. From **Lviv** specifically. Speaks English fluently but with a *real* Ukrainian accent — direct, no softening, no hedge words.
- **Profession:** Biostatistics at the University of Pittsburgh. Exact program / lab unestablished — could be Pitt Public Health, could be Statistics Department. Day 3 14:14 disclosed as *"Pitt biostats"* by Ana.
- **Family:** Whole family in Lviv. As of Day 3 14:15: *Lviv is okay so far.* Olesya is the immigrant; family has not joined her in the US.

## Address

5337 Mossfield Avenue, second floor, Greenfield. Roommate of **Ana Petrov.** Lease pre-dates the outbreak by some unspecified period — they have lived together long enough to know each other's rhythms but not long enough to be in each other's families.

## Physical description

- **Build:** **Taller than Ana.** Has-some-bones-on-her, not slight. Strong from manual work / yard work / family farm (likely — not confirmed but consistent with the *grandmother knife-fight + strong baker* package).
- **Hair:** Light brown, falls to her shoulders, worn down by default.
- **Eyes:** Light — gray or pale blue (not directly established; inferred from Ukrainian-from-Lviv).
- **Skin:** Fair, *goes pink in the cool air* (Caleb noted this Day 3 at the curb).
- **Clothing register:** Practical. A sweater and jeans on Day 3 pickup. Hiking backpack already packed before Caleb arrived.
- **Posture:** Direct. Centered. *No fidget.* The body language of someone who has carried heavy things her whole life.

- **Honesty about her own toughness.** Will name what she has — knife training, family far away, *yes biting is bad we saw the video* — without performance.
- **Has not yet called Caleb anything besides his name.** No nickname yet. Default address is *Caleb.*

## Personality

**Default mode:** Direct, observant, economical. The kind of person who watches a room before she speaks in it. Once she has a read, she acts decisively.

**Under stress:** Doesn't change much. Olesya was *already* operating from a baseline of *crisis-possible-at-any-time* shaped by her family being in Lviv during the 2022 Russian invasion. The Cordyceps outbreak is *another* kind of catastrophe she has the cognitive shape for, not a first one. *"We waited for someone to come"* was an honest tell — she has been here before, knowing rescue might or might not come.

**Humor:** Capable of it. Snorted at Caleb's *"she looks kind of brown"* line about her in the Cherokee — surprised laugh. But she does not lead with humor — she leads with *what is the next thing we need to do.*

**Vulnerabilities:**
- Her family in Lviv. *Lviv is okay so far.* That *so far* is doing a lot of work.
- Being called Russian. Her grandmother taught her to knife-fight specifically because *the Russians might come.* This is not theoretical for her family. *Do not* call her Russian.
- Being trapped without options. The morning at her apartment with Ana before Caleb came was *waiting for someone to come* — a known shape for her from the 2022 stories her family told over phone calls.

**What lands with her:**
- Hugs that mean it. (Held Caleb's longer than Ana did.)
- Direct asks. *"Come to my fort"* worked because it was direct.
- Capable people. Caleb showing up *armed, prepared, with a vehicle full of supplies* read as *here is someone who is doing what needs done.*
- Olesya gives the same back — direct, capable, asks what is needed and does it.

**What she does not tolerate:**
- Russian-coded language. *Do not* lump her in with Russian-speakers, *do not* call her *"the girls from over there,"* *do not* assume Ana and Olesya have a shared ethnicity because they have a shared apartment.
- Performance. Olesya will not perform fear, will not perform gratitude, will not perform femininity. What she does she does.
- Wasted time.

## Hobbies / hidden interior

- **Knife-fighting.** Grandmother taught her in Lviv as a child. Likely closer to *traditional Eastern European folk knife-fighting* (sloboda / Cossack-adjacent traditions) than modern Filipino kali or Western combatives. Specifically defensive + close-quarters. The grandmother taught her *because the Russians might come.* Olesya is the most weapon-knowledgeable person in the Mossfield apartment.
- **Strong baker.** Yeasted breads, pierogi, traditional Ukrainian foods. *"Strong baker"* per Ana's framing — Olesya can produce real food from raw ingredients without panic. This will matter at the Mansions.
- **Biostatistics.** Her professional identity. Public health-adjacent work — R, statistical modeling, the actual quantitative side. She is the *most likely person in the fort* to be useful for tracking disease spread, population modeling, scenario forecasting if she has data and a laptop.
- **Probably bilingual or trilingual.** Ukrainian native, English fluent. Likely some Russian (because Lviv) but with strong feelings about it. Possibly some Polish (Lviv proximity).

## Secrets

- Specific family situation in Lviv — what her parents do, who else is there, what 2022 looked like for her household — *not yet disclosed.* She has hinted by saying *"we waited for someone to come"* and naming Lviv specifically. There is more to ask, when there is time.
- Why she came to the US specifically rather than going elsewhere in Europe during 2022 — unstated.

## Relationship to Ana

Olesya and Ana share an apartment at 5337 Mossfield Ave 2nd floor. They have been roommates long enough to:
- Coordinate the *honk twice + we'll come down* protocol in real time without discussion
- Both have backpacks pre-packed
- Move in step when fleeing
- Speak for each other in conversation (*"If I come, she comes"* — Ana told Caleb)

Olesya respects Ana's intelligence (Linhardt lab is real). Ana respects Olesya's competence (the knife-fighting framing, the strong-baker framing — Ana hands Olesya those slots). They are *roommates-who-have-become-functional-allies,* not best friends in the deep sense Yumi+Mei are.
