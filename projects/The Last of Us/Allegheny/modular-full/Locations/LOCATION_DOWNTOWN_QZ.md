## Downtown Pittsburgh / the Golden Triangle (the QZ proper)

The wedge of land where the Allegheny and Monongahela meet to form the Ohio. The **Point** is the tip of the wedge — Point State Park, a fountain in the middle, the rivers around it. The Golden Triangle is the downtown proper: office towers, the **Cathedral of Learning** visible to the east (technically Oakland), the **PPG Place** glass cathedral, **PNC Park** across the Allegheny on the north shore, **Heinz Field** across the Ohio.

This is where the **Pittsburgh QZ** is established — per TLOU-adjacent canon and the realistic shape of an urban quarantine. FEDRA encloses the Triangle with walls along the river edges (the rivers themselves doing some of the work) and across the eastern boundary where it meets the rest of the city.

### PRE-OUTBREAK

Downtown is offices, government buildings, the symphony hall, the cultural district, the courthouse, the convention center, the stadiums on the north shore. Weekday lunch hour is busy; nights are quiet except around the cultural district and the stadiums on event nights. The Fort Pitt Bridge is the connector to the south side; the Sixteenth Street Bridge connects to the Strip District; the Veterans Bridge runs north out of downtown.

Saturday and Sunday in late September: leaf-peeping season is starting. The light off the rivers is particular. The fountain at the Point is running. There's a Steelers game on Sunday afternoon at Heinz Field.

**Sensory anchors:** rivers on three sides; the wind off the water; the specific bright steel of the PPG glass; the cathedral visible to the east.

### OUTBREAK WEEK

Day 3 afternoon: downtown is in chaos. People trying to leave the office towers and finding traffic stopped. Day 4: National Guard arrives. Day 5: FEDRA replaces the Guard. Day 6: the cordon goes up — the first walls. Plywood at first, concrete and rebar later. The bridges become checkpoints. The hospital surge has emptied UPMC and Allegheny General; the wounded and the infected are being processed here.

By Day 7, the downtown is FEDRA-controlled. The QZ is forming. The walls are not yet permanent but they are real. People are being scanned with bite-detector kits at the bridges.

**Sensory anchors:** military presence in a city that doesn't usually have it; the rivers, still beautiful, now strategic; the sound of helicopters; smoke from a fire in the cultural district nobody is fighting.

### QZ ERA (Week 1 – Month 4)

The Pittsburgh QZ. Civilian population approximately 20,000 by Month 1, declining over the era. FEDRA HQ is in the **Steel Tower** (the U.S. Steel building, the tallest in downtown). The **Convention Center** is the central distribution point — ration cards, work assignments, medical. The **stadiums** on the north shore are used for FEDRA staging. **PNC Park** becomes a refugee processing site. The **courthouse** is operational again for FEDRA tribunals.

Civilians live in commandeered office buildings, the high-rise apartment buildings near Mellon Square, and in the converted parts of the Convention Center. Rations are distributed weekly. The barter market is in the lobby of one of the older office buildings near Liberty Avenue.

The QZ is *survivable* in Week 2–4 if you have a ration card. By Month 3 it is becoming harder. FEDRA's procedure is rigid; their cruelty is bureaucratic rather than sadistic. By Month 4 it's clearly fraying.

**Sensory anchors:** the river is right there but unreachable for civilians — the walls are between you and it. The towers of downtown are still standing, lit at night where FEDRA has power. The smell of rationed food cooking in too-small kitchens; the smell of unwashed bodies in winter. The specific quiet of a city without cars.

### POST-QZ (Month 4 – Year 1+)

FEDRA pulls out — exact mechanism varies by run, but the canonical TLOU pattern is a civil insurrection followed by a FEDRA retreat. The Hunter faction takes the downtown. The walls remain, partially. The towers stand. The Convention Center becomes Hunter HQ. Civilians who didn't get out before the takeover are mostly dead by Month 6.

The downtown is **hostile territory** for the rest of the story. If `{{user}}` and group return — for a salvage, for a route through, for a specific objective — it is a high-stakes infiltration. The Steel Tower is a Hunter watchpoint. The bridges are blocked or guarded. The Allegheny still runs.

**Sensory anchors:** broken glass on the streets; the towers, dark now, in silhouette against a dirty sky; the fountain at the Point dry, then cracked; the rivers running unconcerned through the dead city.
