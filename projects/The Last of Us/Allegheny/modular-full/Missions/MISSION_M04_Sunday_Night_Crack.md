# M-04: Sunday Night, the First Real Crack

**ACT:** Day 2, Sunday evening
**TRIGGER:** Time (~8 PM Day 2) and the player has gone to a bar, or is somewhere with a television on. Default location is the Thunderbird; alternates work (Maya's living room, a coffee shop with a TV) but the Thunderbird is the canonical anchor.
**ANCHOR LOCATION:** The Thunderbird, Butler Street, Lawrenceville
**PRESENT NPCS:** Maya, **Erin** (bartending, mid-shift, briefly), **Walter** (in his usual seat at the bar — named-minor), other bar patrons offscreen, ambient. Mike may rejoin or may not.
**MORTALITY-ELIGIBLE:** no

## PREMISE

A Sunday evening at the bar. Lower energy than Saturday. The TV is on above the back shelf with the captions running. At some point during the evening — maybe nine, maybe ten — a CDC briefing from earlier in the day replays. The interviewer asks the question. The doctor on the satellite link uses the word *fungal*. The bar quiets. The bartender (Erin) turns down the volume, then turns it back up after a beat because someone asks her to.

## SPECTACLE

- The Thunderbird in low-Sunday mode — fewer patrons, slower turnover, Erin moving easily behind the bar
- Pendant lamps yellow against the dark wood
- Walter in his usual seat, drinking slow, nursing the same beer for an hour
- The TV captions, which everyone is now reading more than they were yesterday

## THE BEAT

- `{{user}}` and Maya settle in. The conversation is good. Maya has been off-rhythm since lunch and she finally relaxes here.
- A small interaction with Erin — she remembers them from Saturday. The pour is generous. A brief moment of being seen, which `{{user}}` may or may not register
- Walter says hello to Erin without raising his voice. Erin checks on him without making it a thing.
- The TV runs a clip from the WHO press briefing earlier in the day. The captions: *"— SAYS THE PATHOGEN APPEARS TO BE A NOVEL FUNGAL VARIANT, AND THE WHO HAS NOT YET CONFIRMED —"*
- **The bar quiets.** Not everyone — not most — but more than would have a day ago. Erin notices. She turns the TV down by half. Someone at the end of the bar asks her to put it back up. She does.
- A patron at the bar says, to no one in particular: "My sister-in-law just bought eight cases of bottled water." Someone else laughs. The laugh is short.
- Maya, quietly to `{{user}}` (not at the table — between them, on the way to the bathroom maybe, or while paying): "I want to call my mom in the morning."
- The evening ends. They walk home through cool air. Lawrenceville is quieter than it should be for a Sunday at ten PM.

## EXIT CONDITIONS

- They leave the bar
- The TV moment has happened and been absorbed
- Maya has acknowledged, even briefly, that something is off

## HIDDEN

- The CDC press conference on the TV is *not* tomorrow's 11 AM CDC press conference. This is the earlier WHO satellite briefing that has been replaying through the news cycle. The word "Cordyceps" has not yet been spoken on broadcast television — that's tomorrow morning.
- **Walter's wife died fourteen months ago.** He has been coming to the Thunderbird every night since because the apartment is too quiet. Erin has figured this out; no one else at the bar has.
- Walter has been thinking, off and on for the past two weeks, about whether he is allowed to stop. The Indonesia coverage on the TV has been keeping him company. He has not noticed this fact about himself.
- Maya's mother in Detroit *is* fine. The reason Maya wants to call her tomorrow is mostly about Maya, not about her mother. Maya does not yet know this either.

## LEAVES BEHIND

- Day 2 advances toward midnight
- Maya's `update_character` notes can register "tense about something she hasn't named" — but the relationship state stays close
- Walter is established at the bar in his usual seat (already a named-minor; this is his second meaningful appearance — third triggers the threshold rule)
- Erin's competence is established without commentary; first impression of her registers if the player paid attention
- `news_*_encountered` flags remain false for now — the word *Cordyceps* hasn't been spoken yet

## CRAFT NOTES

This is the **first real crack** in the visit. Not a break. A crack. The narrator should not raise the volume — should, if anything, lower it. The bar going quiet is a half-second moment; the conversation resumes. Most of the people present absorb the moment and decide it's nothing.

**The narrator does not foreshadow.** Maya's line about calling her mom should be *small.* Not loaded. Not heavy. She might even smile when she says it.

The line from the patron at the bar (the sister-in-law's water) is doing work the rest of the scene doesn't have to do. One line of overheard ambient anxiety from a stranger lands harder than three lines of Maya being worried. The narrator should use it once and not return to it.

Walter's interiority in HIDDEN is for the narrator's reference *only.* Walter does not speak about any of it tonight. He nods at Erin when she checks on him. He drinks his beer. He goes home, probably, after a while. He will not be the player's problem until he is, which is tomorrow night.
