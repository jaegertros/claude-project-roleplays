# M-02: Saturday Dinner

**ACT:** Day 1, Saturday evening
**TRIGGER:** Time (~7 PM Day 1) and the player has not declined the dinner plan from M-01. May also trigger from Opening C, adapted — see craft notes.
**ANCHOR LOCATION:** A restaurant in the Strip District (the narrator may name one plausibly: *Bar Marco*, *Cilantro & Ajo*, or a generic Italian place — fictional name is fine)
**PRESENT NPCS:** Maya, Mike (in town from Cincinnati for a Sunday wedding, present by coincidence), Priya (Maya's friend from grad school), and an ER nurse who Priya brought along — **ARLEN** (named-minor, LIGHT-tier)
**MORTALITY-ELIGIBLE:** no

## PREMISE

The Strip District on Saturday night. A restaurant with brick walls and string lights. Four friends, plus a fifth Priya brought — an ER nurse from UPMC. Conversation is loose, easy, slightly buzzed. At some point Arlen mentions a strange triage at her hospital that week. It is the first foreshadow that *lands.*

## SPECTACLE

- A warm restaurant in late September, brick and string lights
- The Allegheny River two blocks away, faint cold air drifting in when the door opens
- The murmur of a Saturday-night-busy place — fork on plate, glass on table, laughter from another booth
- Mike, expansive across the booth, telling the wedding-rehearsal story he's been telling since they got there

## THE BEAT

- Introductions, drinks ordered, the table's energy establishing itself. Mike sets the volume; Priya matches it; Maya brings it back down with quieter observations
- Mike tells the wedding story (the bride's father, four glasses of wine, one ambien, the speech that went off the rails)
- Priya is sharp and funny; **Arlen is quieter than the rest of the table, visibly tired in a way that registers**
- The food comes; the conversation has moved through three or four topics
- Arlen, half-prompted by a question about her week, mentions the triage at her hospital — three patients in 48 hours with similar symptoms that don't match anything she's seen in fifteen years of nursing. She doesn't push it. She's just talking.
- **Maya catches the moment.** `{{user}}` may or may not, depending on his profession and attention. If `{{user}}` is Caleb-the-microbiologist, the moment lands harder for him than it would for a software engineer.
- The dinner closes naturally — bill paid, coats on, walking out into the cool air

## EXIT CONDITIONS

- Dinner ends, the group disperses (Mike walks back toward his hotel; Priya drives; Maya and `{{user}}` walk home, or take an Uber if the rain has started)
- The bill comes and the conversation moves elsewhere

- Whether `{{user}}` registers her this evening determines whether her death later means anything when the player learns of it (or doesn't).

- Mike's wedding is in Sewickley tomorrow afternoon. He has been told three times that he is in the photos at 1 PM. He will never make it.
