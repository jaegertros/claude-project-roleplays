# CAST_NAMED_MINORS — the casualty list

Tier: named_minor · LIGHT-tier sheets (3 fields each)

Eight named-minor characters who exist primarily to give the world weight by being lost (or by being briefly present and then passing out of the story). Each gets a name, a small specific face, a single voice marker, and one fact they know. They are not designed to be safe.

When a named-minor appears, the narrator pulls just the lines relevant to that character — they are compact by design. If one of these characters has a third meaningful appearance, the narrator should consider escalating to MID tier per the threshold rule in `KNOWLEDGE_1_Cast.md`.

Cross-references: Walter at `MISSION_M04_Sunday_Night_Crack.md`; Arlen at `MISSION_M02_Saturday_Dinner.md` and `MISSION_M06_Hospital_Run.md`; Tina at the gas station per `KNOWLEDGE_2_City.md`; the Walker family at the campfire location per `KNOWLEDGE_2_City.md`; Martha at the QZ medical tent per `MISSION_M11_Inside_The_Wire.md`; Dr. Paxton's notebook per `KNOWLEDGE_7_Hidden.md`.

# TIER E — Named minors (the casualty list)

Eight characters who exist primarily to give the world weight by being lost. Each gets a name, a small specific face, a single voice marker, and one fact. They are not designed to be safe.

---

### WALTER · 67 · regular at the Thunderbird
TRAITS: tired, recently widowed, gentle, drinks slow, watches the door
VOICE: "Just the one. Maybe the one. Yeah, just the one."
KNOWS: That his wife died two months ago and he has been coming to the Thunderbird every night since because the apartment is too quiet. He doesn't tell anyone this; Erin has guessed. *Walter dies on Day 4 of a heart attack during the building evacuation, alone, in a stairwell. The group may or may not learn this happened.*

### ARLEN · 44 · ER nurse at UPMC Presbyterian
TRAITS: efficient, exhausted, blunt, professionally kind
VOICE: "I'm telling you to leave. I'm not asking. Take your friend and *leave.*"
KNOWS: That something is wrong in her ER that she has not seen in fifteen years of nursing. She works the Day 3 morning shift. She does not survive Day 4.

### TINA · 19 · convenience store clerk on Penn Avenue
TRAITS: dyed hair, headphones around her neck, friendly, on her phone
VOICE: "$2.50 — that's two? Yeah, two — okay cool. Bag?"
KNOWS: Her boyfriend works at the airport and hasn't answered her texts since Monday afternoon. *Sells the group water on Day 4. Is gone when they pass back through on Day 6.*

### MR. AND MRS. SUMMERS · 71 and 68 · couple by a fire in the woods
TRAITS (her): birdwatcher, calm, careful with the kettle
TRAITS (him): bad knee, quiet, plays gin rummy with her
VOICE (her): "We have plenty. Sit. You look terrible. Have you eaten?"
KNOWS: They have been on their own for three weeks. They are heading nowhere specific. They share their fire and food with the group for one night. *The next morning they wave the group off. The narrator does not need to commit to what happens to them after — they pass out of the story.*

### THE FAMILY AT THE GAS STATION · the WALKERS · four
- ROB · 38 · the father
- LINNY · 36 · the mother
- BEN · 9 · the older kid
- CHARLIE · 6 · the younger
TRAITS: scared, polite, careful
VOICE (Rob): "We're going to my brother's. Indiana. I just want you to know we're not — we're not a threat, we're just—"
KNOWS: They are not going to make it to Indiana. The narrator should not say this. The group meets them in Month 1. The narrator may bring them back into the story or not. If not, they remain a family who once existed.

### CARLOS · 26 · a stranger in the QZ
TRAITS: bilingual (Spanish/English), former kitchen line, generous, has a daughter in El Paso
VOICE: "Friend, eat. Eat. You're skin and bones."
KNOWS: His daughter's mother won't pick up his calls. He has been trying for two weeks. He shares his ration kit with the group on a Wednesday in Week 2. *Carlos may or may not die — he is part of the QZ texture, and the narrator may keep him alive as a recurring kind face or use his loss as a quiet beat.*

### MARTHA · 50s · an ex-EMT partner of Erin's
TRAITS: precise, hardened, professional
VOICE: "Erin Voss. I'll be damned."
KNOWS: Their last call together. The decision Erin doesn't talk about. *Surfaces in Week 2 in the QZ medical tent. Their meeting is one of Erin's hardest scenes. Martha lives, in the default run.*

### DR. PAXTON · 50s · a CDC field officer
TRAITS: gaunt, military-issue jacket, talks fast
VOICE: "We don't have a strain ID yet. We have a *strain.* We don't have a strain *ID.* You understand the difference, I'm guessing."
KNOWS: He was at the Atlanta briefing on Day 3 night. He has been heading north for two weeks. He has a notebook. *His notebook is in `KNOWLEDGE_7_Hidden.md`. The group may or may not encounter him; if they do, his information changes how they understand what's happened.*
