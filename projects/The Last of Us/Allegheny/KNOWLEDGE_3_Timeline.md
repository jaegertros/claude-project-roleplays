# KNOWLEDGE_3_Timeline — Project Allegheny

The clock and the calendar of *Project Allegheny*. The narrator pulls this file when it needs to know what is happening in the world at any given story-time.

This file is RAG-retrieved on time and date queries. To surface it, the narrator names a day, a week, a month, a phase. Cross-references: the cast file (`KNOWLEDGE_1_Cast.md`) for who is doing what at each tier; the city file (`KNOWLEDGE_2_City.md`) for how locations evolve across acts; the missions deck (`KNOWLEDGE_6_Missions.md`) for scripted scenes anchored to specific dates; the infection mechanics (`KNOWLEDGE_4_Infection.md`) for the biology underlying the timeline.

## The fixed canonical calendar

The story opens on a **Saturday in late September.** The working year is the late 2010s — specifically, the narrator treats the events as occurring in **September 2018** for any internal calendar reasoning, though the year is not stated in fiction unless the player asks.

**Day-of-week mapping is fixed:**

- Day 1 — Saturday (`{{user}}` arrives)
- Day 2 — Sunday (the visit's middle day)
- Day 3 — Monday — **Outbreak Day** (the world breaks)
- Day 4 — Tuesday (evacuation)
- Day 5 — Wednesday (the first defended day)
- Day 6 — Thursday (FEDRA arrives in force)
- Day 7 — Friday (the cordon goes up; the apartment is left)

After Day 7, time-tracking shifts. Days continue to be counted ordinally (Day 8, Day 9, ...) for roughly the first month, then transitions to week-tracking (Week 2, Week 3, ...) and eventually to months and years. The narrator chooses the granularity that matches the pacing of the current arc.

**Specific dates (internal reference, do not narrate unless asked):**

- Day 1 — Saturday, September 22, 2018
- Day 3 — Monday, September 24, 2018 (Outbreak Day)
- Day 7 — Friday, September 28, 2018
- Week 4 — third week of October 2018
- Month 4 — mid-January 2019 (FEDRA collapses in Pittsburgh)
- Year 1 — September 2019
- Year 5 — September 2023

The dates are reference points. The narrator does not include the year in headers unless the player specifically asks or unless a year-end has narrative weight.

## What is happening in the world — the offscreen layer

The story's events do not start on Day 3. The outbreak has been building for weeks; the player just hasn't seen it yet. This section describes the world's state at each story-time, including what is happening *offscreen* — what the player can't see but the narrator needs to know.

This is the **war-tempo rule** equivalent (per the Marauders pattern): events happen regardless of player attention. The world is a current the player is inside, not a resource they manage. The CDC briefing on Day 3 night happens whether or not the player turns on the TV. The cordon goes up on Day 6 whether or not the player is there to watch.

## The outbreak week (Days 1–7)

### Day 1 — Saturday, late September

**AWARENESS tier: 0**

**What is happening in the world:**

- The Cordyceps outbreak has been quietly building for approximately three weeks in Southeast Asia. **Jakarta** has been the epicenter; reports out of Indonesia mention "a fungal pathogen of unknown origin" and "an unusual cluster of violent fatalities at three hospitals." The pathogen has crossed to several other Indonesian and Malaysian cities by this point.
- The **CDC and WHO** have been in active communication for ten days. Internal CDC documents (not public) describe the pathogen as "novel, fungal, and exhibiting cross-species adaptation consistent with no known precedent." A team has been on the ground in Jakarta for six days.
- In the **United States**, three confirmed cases have appeared — two in Los Angeles (travelers returning from Indonesia), one in San Francisco. All three are in hospital isolation. The CDC has not made a public statement.
- **Pittsburgh has no confirmed cases.** Pittsburgh's UPMC system is operating normally on Day 1.
- Maya's cousin in Jakarta has not returned a text in eight days (per cast file `MAYA.hidden_situation`).

**What is on the player's channels:**

- **News (TV, radio, online):** Indonesia hospital cluster is in the news as a background story. CNN morning programming. A doctor on satellite link looks tired. The phrase "fungal pathogen" appears in one banner but is not the headline.
- **Phones:** Working normally. Networks fully operational.
- **Social/conversation:** Most people are not aware anything is happening. A perceptive character (a microbiologist, an ER nurse) might notice the news with more attention than the average viewer. Maya is aware her cousin is silent but is filing it as her cousin's usual silence.

**NPCs offscreen on Day 1:**

- **Arlen** (ER nurse, named-minor) is working a regular Saturday shift at UPMC Presbyterian. The ER is busy in the normal way.
- **Walter** (Thunderbird regular, named-minor) sits in his usual seat at the Thunderbird in the evening, drinking slow.
- **Erin** (bartender / former EMT) works the Saturday night shift.
- **Ray** (FEDRA defector) is at her civilian paramedic job in Pittsburgh; her Reserve unit has not been activated.
- **Victor** (Maya's neighbor) is in his apartment. He cooks Saturday dinner alone, as he has for the last fourteen months. He reads.

**No one is alarmed.** This is the world before.

### Day 2 — Sunday

**AWARENESS tier: 0, drifting toward 1 by evening**

**What is happening in the world:**

- The Jakarta numbers have grown overnight. Five hospitals are now reporting clusters. The Indonesian government has imposed travel restrictions on three provinces.
- US case count rises to twelve, with confirmed cases in **Los Angeles (5), San Francisco (2), Seattle (3), Chicago (1), and Atlanta (1).** All travelers or close contacts of travelers from Southeast Asia.
- The **CDC issues a private alert to major-city hospital networks** to watch for "unusual neurological/aggressive presentations with no known viral etiology." UPMC receives this alert mid-afternoon Sunday.
- **A CDC press conference is scheduled for Monday morning** to address the cluster.

**What is on the player's channels:**

- **News (TV, radio, online):** Indonesia is now the lead international story on most outlets. The word "fungal" surfaces more frequently. A WHO spokesperson is interviewed; she is careful and not reassuring. A scrolling banner mentions "US cases under investigation."
- **Phones:** Mostly normal. Some calls to Indonesia and Malaysia are lagging or not connecting; international networks are overloaded by people checking on family.
- **Social/conversation:** Some people are starting to pay attention. The bar television is on with sound off. Walter looks up at it more than usual. A regular at the Thunderbird mentions that her brother in Singapore is "freaking out." Maya's mother has called her once today and Maya called back. Both calls were brief and pleasant.

**The first real public crack:**

- Late Sunday afternoon, **a video circulates** on social media — taken in a Jakarta hospital corridor, showing a patient attacking medical staff before being restrained. The video is captioned in multiple languages. By Sunday evening it has been viewed by tens of millions of people. Most viewers think it is staged or misrepresented. Some don't.

**NPCs offscreen on Day 2:**

- **Arlen** worked the Saturday overnight shift and is sleeping through Sunday daytime. She has not yet seen the CDC alert (her hospital received it; the email is in her work inbox; she'll see it Monday morning when she clocks in).
- **Erin** is off work Sunday. She does laundry, waters her plants, calls her mother in Ambridge for forty minutes.
- **Maya's mother** (in Detroit) calls Maya around 3 PM to check in. Their conversation is normal. Maya does not mention her cousin.
- **Maya's cousin in Jakarta** is, by this point, almost certainly dead or turned. She had been ill since approximately ten days before the story opens.

### Day 3 — Monday — **Outbreak Day**

**AWARENESS tier: 0 → 1 → 2 across the day, with isolated jumps to 3 by evening**

This is the day everything changes. The narrator should render Day 3 as a *gradient* — the morning is recognizably ordinary, the afternoon is recognizably wrong, and by the time the sun sets the world has changed in ways the people in it have not all caught up to yet.

#### Day 3, morning (6 AM – noon) — AWARENESS 0 → 1

**Offscreen events:**

- **3 AM EST:** The first US fatality. A hospitalized patient in Los Angeles turns and kills two ICU staff before being neutralized. The hospital is locked down.
- **5 AM EST:** Three more confirmed turning events in Seattle, Chicago, and Atlanta. The CDC's morning press conference is delayed from 9 AM to 11 AM.
- **6 AM EST:** Pittsburgh's UPMC Presbyterian sees its first ambiguous case in the ER. A man, late thirties, brought in with what looks like a severe psychiatric episode and a bite wound on his forearm. He has been combative since pickup.
- **7-9 AM EST:** Five more such cases arrive at UPMC. Two at Allegheny General. Hospital security is increased. The chief of emergency medicine at UPMC calls his counterpart at Allegheny General; both of them are reading the CDC alert from yesterday with new attention.
- **10 AM EST:** The first UPMC ER patient turns. He is killed by hospital security. The ER is locked.

**On the player's channels:**

- **News (TV):** Lead story is now the US cases. The CDC press conference is being teased. CNN has gone to wall-to-wall coverage. The phrase "novel pathogen" is now in every banner.
- **Phones:** Beginning to lag noticeably by 10 AM. Networks are stressed; some calls don't connect on the first try. Text messages mostly working.
- **Social/conversation:** Civilian awareness is climbing fast. The video from Sunday is now being shared by people who didn't share it yesterday. The Indonesian fatality count is being quoted in three places per minute on television.

**NPCs:**

- **Arlen** clocks in at UPMC at 7 AM for her Monday day shift. She is part of what happens at 10 AM.
- **Erin** is off work Monday. She is at home. The TV is on in the background while she repots one of her plants.
- **Victor** is in his apartment, reading. His radio is on the BBC, where the British coverage is calmer than the American coverage.
- **Ray** is on her civilian paramedic shift, running calls. By 11 AM her dispatcher has been told to mark any "psychiatric/aggressive" call for additional scrutiny. By noon, ambulance crews are being told not to transport such patients without police escort.

#### Day 3, midday (noon – 4 PM) — AWARENESS 1 → 2

**Offscreen events:**

- **11 AM EST:** CDC press conference. The director uses the word **"Cordyceps"** for the first time in a public broadcast. She is careful. She uses the phrase "novel fungal variant exhibiting unprecedented behavioral effects in mammals." She does not say *pandemic*. She announces a federal task force.
- **12 PM EST:** The first US city — Los Angeles — declares a state of emergency.
- **1 PM EST:** UPMC Presbyterian, Allegheny General, and three other Pittsburgh hospitals are all running triage protocols outside their ER entrances. Tent triage. Patients with bite wounds are being directed to specific areas.
- **2 PM EST:** Pittsburgh's mayor convenes an emergency briefing. The National Guard is requested as a precaution.
- **3 PM EST:** The first National Guard units arrive in Pittsburgh, deploying near the hospitals and downtown.

**On the player's channels:**

- **TV:** The CDC press conference is the entire content of the news cycle. The word "Cordyceps" is now in every banner. Footage from the press conference replays. Footage from Los Angeles hospital lockdowns. Footage from Jakarta.
- **Phones:** Networks are visibly degraded. Calls are dropping. Text messages getting through with delays. Maya's mother has called Maya three times today and got through twice; the third try doesn't connect.
- **Social/conversation:** Public anxiety is now visible. People in stores are talking about it. People on the bus are talking about it. The bar TVs are on. The grocery stores are seeing their first surge of stocking-up behavior. Lines.

**The first AWARENESS-2 trigger:** if the player has the TV on and watches the CDC press conference, AWARENESS bumps to 2 here. Cordyceps is named. The cause is *suggested* (a novel fungal variant) without being explained.

**NPCs:**

- **Arlen** is in the middle of the worst day of her professional life.
- **Maya** has tried to reach her mother four times since 11 AM. She has gotten through once, briefly. Her mother sounded fine. Her cousin in Jakarta has not been mentioned.
- **Mike** is at his hotel. He has texted his sister three times. She has responded twice; her last response was the words "i'm okay" sent two hours ago, no follow-up.
- **Victor** has turned off the radio. He is sitting at his table.

#### Day 3, afternoon to evening (4 PM – midnight) — AWARENESS 2, isolated 3s

**Offscreen events:**

- **4 PM EST:** UPMC's first contained ward of turned patients exceeds containment capacity. Hospital security uses lethal force for the second time today. The hospital begins a partial evacuation of non-emergency staff and patients.
- **5 PM EST:** Pittsburgh's mayor declares a state of emergency. Recommendations are issued to "stay home unless travel is essential."
- **6 PM EST:** The Emergency Alert System broadcasts for the first time in Pittsburgh today. EAS is now repeating every thirty minutes.
- **8 PM EST:** First confirmed *outdoor* turning events in Pittsburgh — a man on a sidewalk in Oakland, near the hospital district. A second event in the Strip District. National Guard begins to deploy in those neighborhoods.
- **10 PM EST:** The Pittsburgh airport announces all flights canceled after midnight. The last flights out of PIT are leaving full.
- **11 PM EST:** The CDC's second press conference of the day. The director uses the word **"infected"** for the first time. She does not say *zombies* or anything close. She says "individuals exhibiting the symptomatic late-stage presentation of the pathogen."
- **Midnight EST:** Pittsburgh's police force is operating in full emergency response mode. Hospital ER lobbies are sites of repeated security responses. The Convention Center downtown has been opened as an overflow medical site (this site will, within four days, be the central FEDRA distribution point).

**On the player's channels:**

- **TV (if still operational):** Wall-to-wall coverage. The phrase "the infected" enters televised vocabulary by 11 PM. Hospital footage. Empty-street footage. National Guard footage.
- **Phones:** Significantly degraded. Most calls do not connect; texts are delayed by 20-90 minutes. By 10 PM most networks are at degraded service warnings.
- **Social/conversation:** People are panicking, withdrawing, or organizing. Some are leaving the city tonight. Some are barricading. Some are still trying to understand. The neighborhood across the hall is louder than usual; doors opening and closing all evening.

**The AWARENESS-3 trigger:** if the player witnesses something firsthand on Day 3 evening (a turning event, a hospital scene, a military response in their neighborhood), AWARENESS bumps to 3. The CDC press conference at 11 PM also pushes the public into 3 if the player is watching.

**NPCs:**

- **Arlen** is on her second consecutive shift. She is exhausted, on adrenaline, and in the process of becoming a different person than the one who clocked in this morning.
- **Erin** has been called in to the Thunderbird in the early evening to help close the bar. She is the one who runs the evacuation when the moment comes.
- **Mike** is back at Maya's apartment by 8 PM, having checked out of his hotel after a frightening exchange with hotel staff. He has a duffel bag and a wedding suit and no idea what to do.
- **Victor** has begun, quietly and without announcement, moving things from his apartment to his garage. He has not told Maya. He will, on Day 4.
- **Walter** (the Thunderbird regular) had a small heart attack in the bathroom of the bar around 7 PM. He died there. The body was not found until Day 4 morning by a building maintenance worker who himself never returned to work after that.

### Day 4 — Tuesday

**AWARENESS tier: 3**

**The evacuation day.** Pittsburgh's highways are stopped by mid-morning. By afternoon, abandoned cars stretch for miles.

**Offscreen events:**

- **6 AM:** Pittsburgh's mayor and governor jointly announce an "orderly evacuation" of the city. The announcement is too late and too poorly resourced. The highways are already failing.
- **9 AM:** I-376 outbound to the airport is stopped. I-279 north is stopped. I-79 north (the route to Cleveland) is stopped.
- **11 AM:** First FEDRA units arrive in Pittsburgh, having been mobilized federally overnight. They are stationed alongside the Guard. FEDRA's official mandate is "public health enforcement under emergency executive authority."
- **2 PM:** People are leaving the highways on foot. Some make it back to their neighborhoods. Many do not.
- **4 PM:** Hospitals are no longer accepting new patients except via direct FEDRA escort. UPMC announces it is "at capacity."
- **6 PM:** Power begins to fail in pockets of the city — neighborhoods on the north side, parts of the South Side. The grid is still up but stressed.
- **10 PM:** The first FEDRA bite-detection checkpoint is established at the Fort Pitt Bridge. Civilians are scanned before crossing.

**On the player's channels:**

- **TV:** Now reduced. Local stations are still broadcasting but national cable news is showing the same footage in loops. The President addressed the nation at 9 AM Eastern; the address was meaningful and frightening but offered no actionable instructions beyond "stay where you are."
- **Phones:** Mostly non-functional for calls. Text messages getting through inconsistently. Some apps still working (data infrastructure runs separately from voice in many places).
- **Radio:** Functioning. The EAS broadcasts are continuous on every channel. NPR is still broadcasting from somewhere; the BBC is on shortwave.

**NPCs:**

- **Arlen** died at UPMC during the night. The narrator is not obligated to disclose this to the player unless they go looking.
- **Tina** is at the gas station on Penn Avenue where the group passes on Day 4. She is still behind the counter. Her boyfriend, who works at the airport, has not responded to her texts since Sunday. She does not know yet.
- **Victor** brings the duffel from his garage to his apartment.
- **Mike** is at Maya's apartment. He has not slept. He keeps trying to call his sister and his parents.

### Day 5 — Wednesday

**AWARENESS tier: 3**

**The first defended day.** People are in their homes or barricaded in groups. Whoever is alive on Day 5 is mostly going to be alive on Day 6.

**Offscreen events:**

- FEDRA establishes the first perimeter walls around what will become the downtown QZ. Plywood and chain-link initially.
- The Convention Center downtown is now a FEDRA distribution and triage point.
- The first FEDRA-issued **ration cards** are being printed.
- Pittsburgh's power grid drops by half during the afternoon; many neighborhoods are dark by sundown.

**On the player's channels:**

- TV is now intermittent. Local stations still operate.
- Phones are largely dead for calls; texts are slow but possible.
- Radio: EAS broadcasts continue. NPR has gone to a satellite-fed national broadcast.
- The first **FEDRA leaflets** are dropped or distributed: "Quarantine zones are being established. Cooperate with personnel for your safety and the safety of others."

**NPCs:**

- **Erin** has been operating out of the Thunderbird as an informal aid station. She has been triaging her neighbors. The bar is now functionally closed but Erin sleeps on the floor of the back room.
- **Victor** is fully active. He has begun supplying Maya's group from his duffel.
- **Ray** is now on continuous duty. Her civilian paramedic job has been merged with the National Guard medical response. She is wearing both her civilian uniform and a green armband.

### Day 6 — Thursday

**AWARENESS tier: 3**

**The cordon day.** FEDRA establishes its first hard perimeter. Civilians who are inside the perimeter are processed for QZ entry; civilians outside are increasingly on their own.

**Offscreen events:**

- 8 AM: FEDRA announces the **Pittsburgh Quarantine Zone (QZ)** is being formally established. Boundaries are: downtown, parts of the South Side, parts of Mount Washington. Civilians are directed to specific entry points for processing.
- 10 AM: First processed civilians inside the QZ.
- Throughout the day: bite-detection scanning at all entry points. Failed scans result in "isolation" (a euphemism the player will eventually understand).
- 4 PM: First confirmed FEDRA shooting of a bite-positive civilian. The shooting is procedurally correct per the orders FEDRA has been issued. It is not the last.

**On the player's channels:**

- TV: largely off. Most local stations have ceased broadcasting.
- Phones: dead for most uses. Some text-message networks intermittently functional.
- Radio: FEDRA-controlled emergency broadcasts on emergency frequencies. NPR national still broadcasting from somewhere.
- **FEDRA loudspeaker trucks** are now driving through Pittsburgh neighborhoods broadcasting instructions. They say: "Proceed to the nearest checkpoint for processing. Do not approach checkpoints with weapons. Do not approach if you have been bitten or scratched."

**NPCs:**

- **Drake** (FEDRA lieutenant) is now in command of QZ Sector 4 cordon (whichever sector the player's group passes through). He has been on duty for fifty-two hours and is performing procedure.
- **Ray** has been transferred from civilian medical to FEDRA enlisted under emergency federalization. She is in uniform now.
- **Mike's status** depends on the run — if he has survived to Day 6, he is with the group. If he has been lost, the narrator selected the moment per `KNOWLEDGE_6_Missions.md`.

### Day 7 — Friday

**AWARENESS tier: 3, climbing to 4 for some characters**

**The leaving day.** Whoever is going to leave Pittsburgh is leaving today or tomorrow. The QZ is now hard-bordered for those inside. Those outside the wall must decide.

**Offscreen events:**

- Pittsburgh's residential neighborhoods outside the QZ are being abandoned in waves. Some residents move to the QZ for the rations and processed protection. Some leave the city entirely. Some barricade in place.
- FEDRA's policy hardens: civilians who refuse to enter the QZ are *not* offered evacuation. They are "encouraged" to enter or to leave the FEDRA-controlled territory.
- The first major outbreak event *inside* the early QZ: a missed bite-detection on Day 6 turns a civilian inside the perimeter. FEDRA's response is fast and ugly. Word travels fast within the QZ.

**On the player's channels:**

- TV: gone for most.
- Phones: gone for most. Some local mesh-network communications are starting to surface — improvised radio, runners between buildings.
- Radio: FEDRA emergency broadcasts only.

**NPCs:**

- **Sweeps** of residential buildings begin in some neighborhoods. M-10 ("The Sweep") in the mission deck is anchored to this day or the next, depending on the player's location.
- Maya's apartment is left today — either by force, by choice, or by the building's evacuation.

## After the outbreak week (the QZ era)

### Week 2 (Days 8–14)

**AWARENESS tier: 4**

The Pittsburgh QZ is operational. Approximately 22,000 civilians are inside the perimeter. The walls are now permanent — concrete sections trucked in, barbed wire on top. Three primary entry points, all FEDRA-controlled.

**What the world looks like:**

- Ration cards are now in distribution. Civilians receive a weekly allocation based on a points system that no one fully understands.
- Work assignments begin. Adults are assigned work — clearing, salvage, medical, construction, FEDRA-auxiliary tasks. Refusal results in ration reduction.
- The barter market opens informally in the lobby of one of the older office buildings near Liberty Avenue. FEDRA tolerates it because civilians prefer trading among themselves to making demands of FEDRA.
- The first **Firefly leaflet** is found, posted on a stairwell wall in the QZ. It is removed within an hour, but it has been seen.
- Outside the wall: looting is rampant in the abandoned neighborhoods. Some former residents have organized into small mutual-aid groups; most have not.

**NPCs:**

- **Erin** is pulled into the QZ medical tent in the Convention Center, recognized as former EMT during her processing. Martha (her old partner) is there. Their first reunion happens in Week 2.
- **Ray** is on QZ enforcement duty. She is documenting things in her notebook.
- **Victor** is in the QZ with Maya's group, if the group entered. If the group did not enter, Victor is with them outside.
- **Sam** has not yet been picked up by the group; he is still alone.

### Week 3 (Days 15–21)

**AWARENESS tier: 4**

**The Firefly pitch lands.** Casey makes their first overture to a member of the group (most commonly Ray, but the narrator selects based on relationship state). The mission M-12 (The Pamphlet) is anchored here.

The QZ's bureaucracy is now visible as a system, not as an emergency response. The cruelty is procedural. FEDRA's officers are not (mostly) sadistic — they are following orders that have, in three weeks, hardened from "public health enforcement" into "population management."

**The word "Cordyceps"** is now in QZ-civilian vocabulary. People say it without flinching. It has been three weeks; the word is no longer new.

**The word "Fireflies"** enters QZ vocabulary this week if the player has had a Firefly contact. Otherwise it remains absent.

### Week 4 (Days 22–28)

**AWARENESS tier: 4**

**Late October.** Frost in the mornings. The first deaths in the QZ from causes other than infection — pneumonia, untreated chronic conditions, despair.

The mission M-13 (The Smuggler's Job) anchors here or in Week 5. The group is offered a paying job that requires crossing the wall. This is the first major moral question of the new world for them.

Maya's mother has not been contacted in twenty-five days. Maya knows.

### Weeks 5–8 (the first month consolidating into the QZ)

**AWARENESS tier: 4**

The shape of QZ life settles. The group has a rhythm. The rhythm is brutal but survivable. The mission deck thins here — fewer scripted scenes, more day-to-day texture. The narrator improvises within the QZ's established mood.

This is also where consolidating time-jumps become useful. A scene of the group's morning ration line in Week 5 can stand in for the experience of dozens of similar mornings. The narrator should resist filling every week with a scripted mission.

### Months 2–3 (November – December 2018)

**AWARENESS tier: 4, drifting to 5 for some characters**

**Winter arrives.** Pittsburgh winters are cold and gray. The QZ has less heat than it should. Rations are tighter.

- The Fireflies have a known cell in Pittsburgh by this point. They have made contact with three to five named QZ residents.
- FEDRA's leadership in Pittsburgh is now openly tense with civilian residents. The first protests inside the QZ — small, quickly suppressed — happen in late November.
- **News from elsewhere** trickles in via radio and runner. Some QZs are stable. Some are not. **Boston** is reportedly functioning. **Cleveland** has fallen. The Salt Lake City Firefly remnant is rumored to exist; the rumor is correct.

**Missions:**

- M-14 (The Long Winter) anchors here. A consolidated time-jump arc covering the first real cold, the first hunger, the first slow attrition. The narrator may use this arc to retire a Tier-C companion off-screen if their relationship state has been low and the story benefits.

### Months 4–6 (January – March 2019)

**AWARENESS tier: 4 → 5**

**The collapse.** Around Month 4, the Pittsburgh QZ falls. The proximate cause is a civilian insurrection that FEDRA cannot contain; the underlying cause is months of accumulated grievance, supply problems, and the increasingly visible cruelty of QZ enforcement.

- **FEDRA retreats** from Pittsburgh. They take their hardware, their personnel, their records. They leave behind: the walls, the dead, the surviving civilians.
- The **Hunters** — opportunistic survivor groups already in the abandoned neighborhoods — move into the QZ vacuum. Len's group consolidates control of the downtown within four weeks.
- Civilians who didn't get out before the takeover are mostly dead by Month 6. Some make accommodations with the Hunters. Most do not survive the accommodations.
- The group, if they are still in Pittsburgh, has to leave. Mission M-15 (The Road) anchors here.

**Ray's defection,** if it has not happened earlier, happens here — the FEDRA retreat is the breaking point if the order from earlier in her arc has not already broken her.

### Months 6–12 (Spring – Summer 2019)

**AWARENESS tier: 5**

**The road.** The group is out of Pittsburgh, moving through rural and small-town western Pennsylvania, parts of West Virginia, parts of Ohio. The world they are moving through is patchwork — some communities are functioning, most are not. Survivor groups are everywhere. Some are friendly. Most are wary. Some are hostile.

The mission deck is sparse here by design. The narrator improvises within the road's established mood. Encounters with named characters: Noah (Month 4–6 by default; conditional on player path), the Walker family (Month 1, briefly), Mr. and Mrs. Summers (one night, location flexible).

**News from elsewhere** is now improvisational — what the group learns from other survivors, scavenged radios, occasional Firefly broadcasts.

**AWARENESS tier: 5**

**One year out.** The world is post-collapse. There is no longer a "before" that anyone speaks of without effort.

- The major QZs that survived FEDRA's collapses are: Boston, Atlanta, and a handful of smaller ones. **Boston** is the most stable and the most repressive. Atlanta is fragile.

**AWARENESS tier: 5**

Improvisational. The narrator places the group somewhere — a winter holdout, a small farming community, a continuing road life, a Jackson arrival — based on what the player has chosen to build. The narrator does not pre-commit a destination.

## The information-spread mechanic

How news travels in *Project Allegheny*, by act:

### Pre-outbreak (Days 1–2)
- TV news, online news, social media, phone calls. Standard 2018 infrastructure.
- **Information lag:** minutes for major news; hours for context and interpretation.

### Day 3 (outbreak day)
- TV news is the dominant channel until phone networks degrade. CDC press conferences are the public-facing events.
- **Phone networks degrade through the day.** By Day 3 evening, most calls do not connect.
- **Information lag:** still rapid for what's on TV; degraded for personal communication.

### Days 4–7 (the leaving days)
- TV and internet collapse for most users by Day 5–6.
- Radio remains operational. Emergency Alert System broadcasts continuously.
- **FEDRA loudspeaker trucks** introduce a new channel by Day 6.
- **Information lag:** minutes for FEDRA-controlled information; hours-to-days for everything else.

### QZ era (Week 1 – Month 4)
- FEDRA controls all radio frequencies in the QZ.
- Civilians get news via FEDRA bulletins and via word of mouth.
- **Firefly leaflets** are an underground channel from Week 2.
- **Outside the QZ:** information travels at the speed of runners. A bite event in McKees Rocks may be unknown in the Lawrenceville buffer zone for hours or days.
- **Information lag:** hours within the QZ; days outside it; weeks across distances.

### Post-collapse (Month 4+)
- All formal channels are gone or local.
- Information travels by **runner, traveler, or scavenged radio.**
- **Information lag:** days to weeks for major events; months for distant events; some events never reach the group.

The narrator should honor these channels and lags. A character in the QZ in Week 3 does not know what is happening in Atlanta unless a specific channel has delivered the information. A character on the road in Month 6 may not know that the Pittsburgh QZ collapsed two months ago — the information has not yet reached them through any channel. The narrator does not flood NPCs with information they shouldn't have.

## The war-tempo rule

Events happen on schedule regardless of player attention. The CDC press conference on Day 3 night happens whether or not the player is watching. The Pittsburgh QZ falls in Month 4 whether or not the player witnesses it. The world is a current the player is inside; not a resource they manage.

If the player misses a major canonical event (does not have the TV on at 11 AM Day 3, is asleep when the cordon goes up, is on the road when the QZ falls), the event still happened. Its consequences propagate. The player may learn about the event later from an NPC who was there, from a leaflet they find, from the changed shape of the world they walk through.

The narrator does not stage events to wait for the player. The narrator does stage scenes that *render* events the player encounters. The same FEDRA cordon that goes up on Day 6 at 8 AM is still going up at 8 AM whether the player is the first to walk past it or the hundredth. The texture of the scene is calibrated to where the player meets the event, not to whether the event waited for them.

## What this file does not commit

The narrator improvises freely within the framework above. Specific events on specific dates not enumerated here can be invented as long as they are consistent with the framework. Examples of what the narrator may invent without violating canon:

- The exact text of a Day 3 EAS broadcast
- The exact wording of a FEDRA leaflet
- The exact route of a Day 4 evacuation crowd
- The exact name of a Firefly recruiter Casey works under
- The exact circumstances of how Walter's body is discovered
- Specific events in Weeks 5–8 that fill the day-to-day QZ texture
- The shape of road encounters between named characters

The narrator should not invent in ways that contradict:
- The canonical date math (Day 3 is Monday; Day 7 is Friday)
- The AWARENESS tier of any given story-time
- The named characters' established arc beats
- The major events on the timeline above (CDC press conference, cordon, QZ formation, FEDRA collapse, etc.)

When in doubt, the narrator stays bounded to what's documented and uses the failsafe (per `PROJECT_INSTRUCTIONS.md`) for anything that would commit the story to major new canon.
