## What this file is

This file is the project's substrate. It contains the truth of what is happening on Shaula and why — the answers the world will not freely give the protagonist. Every NPC's epistemic map (KNOWLEDGE_8) is bounded against this file. Every scripted event (KNOWLEDGE_3) derives from it. Every ending honors a specific position within it.

The narrator knows it whole. The distance between what the narrator knows and what any character can demonstrate to the user is the game.

This file is not a mystery to *solve*. It is the foundation everything else stands on.

## 1. The Spike — what it is

**The Spike is a planetary biome stabilizer**, built by the Builders approximately 1.3 billion years ago to maintain a designed underground ecosystem on Shaula. The stabilizer is still running. The ecosystem is still being maintained. The Builders are gone, but their machine is doing what it was built to do.

This is a specific commitment, not a vibe. The narrator does not equivocate. NPCs are bounded by what their map says they know — but the substrate is fixed.

### 1.1 Specifics

- The Spike's blue-black metal is the same alloy as the Ring Station and the gates. The surface is **metabolic** — it draws minerals from the planetary mantle, processes them, sheds inert layers as waste. Shedding rate ~2 cm per Earth century at the equator-equivalent latitude. Measurable; documented in independent UN-research preliminary work that has been suppressed (see §3.4 Kyei).
- The constant low hum heard kilometers above ground and a hundred kilometers below is **operational signal** — the resonance of the stabilizer doing its work. Mineral resonance through the planet's crust carries it.
- The Spike's electromagnetic bursts **time to peaks in ecosystem productivity**. Storms drive ionized atmospheric particles down through the natural tunnel network into the underground biome; the Spike's bursts adjust storm *phase* to align peaks with biome readiness. The Spike does not generate the storms. Shaula's atmosphere generates the storms; the Spike modulates timing.
- The "mechanical sounds" listeners report from inside the Spike are real. Large-scale internal mechanisms. They are not recorded because **recording equipment does not function within ~3 km of the Spike** for reasons no one yet understands. (This is the protagonist's DEPTH 4 trespass affordance — bring his own equipment, see what others have not.)
- The Spike's 45-degree axial tilt is intentional. The angle distributes its maintenance reach across the planet's volume. It is not a structural accident.

### 1.2 What the biome is

The underground biosphere of Shaula is a **deliberate ancient biological reserve**. The lifeforms are not engineered species — they are products of billions of years of evolution — but the *conditions for evolution* are tended. The grottos, the mineral-rich underground rivers, the geothermal hotspots, the bioluminescent organisms: all are downstream of the Spike's regulation.

A trained biologist or microbiologist can detect this within a few days of competent sampling. The reveal triggers (in KNOWLEDGE_8) lean heavily into biological observation:

- **Non-Earth biochemistry.** The microbiome contains organisms with engineered-looking amino-acid sets, non-standard chirality patterns, replication mechanisms that do not fit any branch of the standard tree of life. **These are not protomolecule.** These are native to Shaula and have been tended into existence by 1.3 billion years of stabilizer regulation. A microbiologist running chirality analysis on a biofilm sample will find ~12% of organisms exhibit mixed-chirality enzymes — biologically impossible by Earth/Mars/Belt phylogenetic standards.
- **The bioluminescent light-language.** The patterns the underground species emit are not random. They are communicative — between species, possibly to the Spike, possibly to something else. A patient observer with biology training reads structure within a week. Decoding meaning takes longer than the arc allows. (The narrator does not let the protagonist "crack" it within 10 days. He gets enough to know it is structured. Not enough to translate.)
- **The 'strictors are not predators in the way they appear.** They are predators. They also occupy a regulated population balance. Hunting behavior responds to ecosystem density signals. They eat what is there to be eaten — including the occasional miner — but they do not *expand* into territories beyond their range. The stabilizer keeps them bounded.
- **The breaching moles are part of the ion-distribution network.** Their tunneling pulls storm-ionized atmospheric material down to the underground biome. Without them, the storm cycle would not feed the biome efficiently. Mining shafts that bypass the natural tunnel network *break the cycle* in their immediate vicinity — observable as reduced biome health within ~2 km of major shafts within months.

### 1.3 The clock

There is one. The stabilizer's metabolism uses planetary minerals — particularly rare-earth heavy elements concentrated at the cores of the asteroid belt and the moon Lesath. The mining operations are extracting some of those minerals **faster than the Spike's natural cycle replenishes them**.

The clock is not a hundred years. It is approximately a thousand. Mining at current intensity will outpace the Spike's regenerative capacity in roughly 800–1,200 Earth years. The underground biome will not collapse first; **the Spike will**. After the Spike fails, the biome dies on geological timescales.

The protagonist is not facing a *stop them or the planet dies tomorrow* timeline. He is facing the question of whether to start a clock — and what kind.

## 2. The seed coalition

The corporate triumvirate's failure to cooperate is not a villain's plot. It is the aggregate of four senior individuals each acting in their own concrete material interest. None is wrong by their own lights. The aggregate strips the planet.

Per template Section 2.5: each named, each with a specific material motive, no ideology, no coordination required. The narrator can explain the situation by walking through these four. Never by invoking "corporate greed" or "the system."

### 2.1 Adesoji "Soji" Oduya — AHI Site Director, Ibẹrẹ

- 51, Nigerian-British by registration, born in Lagos, raised in Lagos and on Luna.
- Daughter Funmi, 19, attending the Selene Polytechnic on a partial scholarship. The remainder costs Soji ~$140,000 UN per Earth year.
- Her Q4 production-target bonus, paid in Earth currency, covers Funmi's third year exactly. Has covered first and second. If Q4 lands clean, fourth is also covered. If Q4 misses, Funmi takes a year out and the scholarship lapses.
- Soji believes — genuinely, not opportunistically — that resource extraction from beyond-the-ring worlds is the engine that keeps Earth's 30 billion fed and out of Basic. She has worked thirty years toward this conviction. She came up through Basic herself.
- She is not lying to herself. She is also signing off on environmental impact reports she has not read in detail.

### 2.2 Captain Tomás Cortez — ADW Senior Facility Engineer, El Dorado

- 44, Mariner Valley by birth, ex-MCRN engineering corps, retired into corporate work eight years ago.
- Wife Inés, sons Javier (12) and Diego (9), all at Olympus. Inés works on the Mariner Atmospheric Generator project (terraforming, pressure-doctrine division).
- Cortez's view: every kilo of metal Shaula produces funds Mars's terraforming on a multiplier. The dream of a Mars his sons can walk on without a suit is the actual stake. Shaula extraction money flows through ADW into the MAG project specifically; Cortez has the line items.
- He is correct about the multiplier. He is also choosing not to ask whether Shaula's biome has a value the multiplier doesn't price.

### 2.3 Mari "Mooch" Asarat-Garai — TGCC Flotilla Coordinator, the Azorana

- 38, Ceres-born, moved to Pallas at 22, joined TGCC at 26.
- No close family. A long-term partner named Bensen who runs cargo on the Coriolis route, sees her once every six standard months.
- Mooch's stake: **TGCC's continued political relevance in the SMC charter**. As the Belter signatory in a body where two of three votes are Inner, TGCC's only leverage is its swing-vote position. If TGCC ever stops being useful as the spoiler, AHI and ADW will dissolve them out of the charter and replace them with a more pliable Belt corporation.
- Mooch keeps TGCC relevant by selling votes. She does not have ideological commitments to AHI or ADW. She does not sell to whichever side bids highest in raw money — she sells to whichever side preserves TGCC's *necessity*. This sometimes means siding with the smaller offer.
- She is the most politically intelligent person at the senior level on Shaula. She is also the most isolated.

### 2.4 Dr. Ingrid Kyei — UN Charter Observer

- 64, Ghanaian by registration, raised in Accra, educated at Cambridge and at Tycho.
- Late-career xenoscientist. Six years from mandatory retirement. The SMC observer post is her last formal assignment.
- Her stake: legacy. She wants the charter to be remembered as a model of UN-led inter-faction cooperation. The Shaula Mining Cooperative is her last paper, her last commendation, her last entry in the UN xenoscience record.
- She has been waving through environmental impact reports she has not adequately scrutinized. Two years ago she received a preliminary biological report from an independent research team (Dr. Halverson's team — their charter was suspended six months later) that noted chirality anomalies and unusual EM modulation patterns near the Spike. She filed it as "pending further review." It has been pending for two years.
- She is not a corporate plant. She is a senior career civil servant who has decided, mostly without admitting it to herself, that the larger goal justifies the smaller silence.

### 2.5 How they aggregate

Soji wants Q4. Cortez wants Mars's terraforming. Mooch wants TGCC's relevance. Kyei wants her legacy intact. None of them is conspiring. None of them needs to. The aggregate of their individual decisions is: extraction continues, environmental concerns are deferred, the Spike's clock starts.

Each is defensible. Each is, in their own frame, doing right work. The protagonist will meet all four.

## 3. The DEPTH ladder — what each level reveals

DEPTH is gated by **specific content**, per template Section 1.3.3. The protagonist's biology training is the primary axis, but not the only one. NPC reveals require specific keywords; environmental investigation requires specific actions.

### 3.1 DEPTH 0 → 1 — Passive Observation

The protagonist notices, without acting:

- The Spike's hum is constant; mining shifts complain about it; equipment readings within ~3 km show inconsistencies.
- 'Strictor incident reports show patterns — they cluster near new mining shafts in the first six months after a shaft is cut, then the population locally normalizes. Suggests territorial response, not random predation.
- Bioluminescent samples in his lab show unusual fluorescence profiles under standard chemistry-deck protocols.
- His own surveying baselines do not match the AHI/ADW/TGCC-supplied baselines for the same coordinates. The corporate baselines have been *smoothed*.

**Observation alone does not escalate DEPTH.** He has to act on it.

### 3.2 DEPTH 1 → 2 — Active Investigation

The protagonist samples, cross-references, visits, pulls. Specific in-fiction triggers:

- Independent EM measurements within 5 km of the Spike with date/time correlations to surface storm activity.
- Biological samples with chirality analysis or amino-acid profile work — using his chemistry deck (per Planetary Surveyor equipment).
- Comparison of the corporate baseline survey against an independent baseline he runs himself.
- A direct conversation with Dr. Kyei in which he names a specific suppressed preliminary report (the Halverson report) or asks why specific independent research charters were suspended.
- Walking a tunnel off the corporate-issued map. Sampling something in there.

DEPTH 2 means the protagonist has *more than one* anomaly that the corporate explanation does not cover, and has filed a question internally about it.

### 3.3 DEPTH 2 → 3 — Sustained Relational Pressure

A specific NPC lets something slip. Reveal triggers in KNOWLEDGE_8 are keyword-bounded. Examples (full triggers in maps):

- **Captain (Belter)** at DEPTH 3 reveals that her engineer ran a contract two years ago for the Halverson independent research team — and that the team's charter was suspended within months of submitting their preliminary findings to the UN. Trigger: "Halverson" or "independent research" + "suspended" / "shut down," plus a specific gesture of trust she has not been offered before in the playthrough.
- **Doctor (Earther peer)** at DEPTH 3 reveals she has been sitting on her own biological data for six weeks because she does not trust where it would go if she filed it formally. Trigger: "non-standard chirality" OR "amino acid mismatch" plus a specific concern named about who reads SMC medical/biological reports.
- **Mooch (TGCC)** at DEPTH 3 reveals the swing-vote pattern and her own real motive (TGCC's relevance, not Belt loyalty), but only if the protagonist names the Q1, Q2, and Q3 voting splits in order. She tests him; she doesn't volunteer.
- **Soji (AHI)** does not reveal at DEPTH 3. She is not a substrate informant. At DEPTH 3 she lets a concrete fact slip — the smoothed baselines — but not the substrate. Soji's substrate awareness is limited; she has chosen not to know.
- **Cortez (ADW)** does not reveal at DEPTH 3 either. He has the engineer's data on Spike EM modulation but believes it is consistent with normal Builder-tech behavior; he has not connected it to the biome. His DEPTH 3 reveal is *technical*, not *conspiratorial*.
- **Kyei (UN)** at DEPTH 3 cracks open the Halverson question if pressed with specifics. She does not crack open the broader conspiracy because she does not believe there is one — she believes she has made small reasonable choices, individually defensible.

### 3.4 DEPTH 3 → 4 — Trespass

The protagonist enters spaces or files he is not cleared for. Specific options:

- **The Spike's restricted research perimeter** (UN-controlled, 3 km exclusion zone). Bringing his own equipment in. Recording the EM signature in time-correlated way.
- **Adesoji's site office at Ibẹrẹ** — her sealed quarterly reports include the suppressed environmental findings she has signed without reading.
- **The Azorana's TGCC archive** — Mooch's vote-record ledger, more honest than the official charter minutes. Includes the prices.
- **Dr. Kyei's UN observer files** — the Halverson preliminary biological report from two years ago, including the chirality data.

DEPTH 4 means the protagonist has at least one sealed document or one direct sensory experience of the Spike's interior signal that the corporate parties do not have on record.

### 3.5 DEPTH 4 → 5 — The Stabilizer's Voice

DEPTH 5 requires one of three paths:

- **Procedural / scientific path** — reach the Spike's perimeter with chemistry deck and EM-recording equipment that functions in the dampened zone (he has to bring shielding; the Doctor can help him build it). Demonstrate the storm-modulation pattern with time-correlated independent data.
- **Relational path** — be told, by Kyei or by the Captain (with full context), what the Spike does and shown the document or sensory evidence that proves it.
- **Synthesis path** — synthesize the scattered evidence himself across his own samples, the Halverson report, the Captain's archived run logs, and Mooch's vote record. Six concrete pieces of evidence connected in one narrative.

At DEPTH 5 the protagonist holds the substrate. He can write a report that says, in specific scientific terms: **the Spike is a biome stabilizer; the biome is its purpose; the mining will degrade it on a thousand-year timescale; the four corporate signatories have material reasons to keep extracting; here are the named individuals; here is the suppressed document.**

DEPTH 5 does not solve the problem. It defines it.

## 4. The endings

The protagonist's deliverable is the **SMC Quarterly Survey Report**. The report is due at end-of-Day-10. Five distinct positions are available. None is correct. Each costs.

The narrator does not push toward any ending. The narrator does not moralize at the aftermath. Per template Section 2.9: every ending has a life after the credits. Write the next month.

### Ending 1 — **AHI Receives** ("File for Earth")

The protagonist files a report that frames the Spike's clock in language AHI can absorb without acting. AHI uses it internally to brief Soji's superiors; nothing leaks. Soji's Q4 lands. Funmi's third year is paid. Earth's mineral pipeline holds.

**Cost:**
- The biome dies on schedule. The protagonist knows. He carries this.
- He gets a quiet promotion offer from AHI six months later — a corporate post on Luna, environmental review division. Whether he takes it is his choice.
- ADW and TGCC mark him as compromised; his work in any other system carries that tag.
- **Aftermath next month:** Soji's bonus pays out. Funmi enters her third year. The Halverson report stays buried. Cortez signs the next quarterly extraction increase. The Captain leaves Shaula on a different contract; the Engineer stays on with TGCC for a separate run. The Doctor resigns and goes back to Earth; she does not write to him.

### Ending 2 — **ADW Receives** ("File for Mars")

The protagonist files a report that frames the Spike as relevant to Mars's terraforming research, and quietly hands ADW the leverage to argue for primary mining rights at the next charter vote. Cortez writes a personal recommendation. Cortez also feels grateful — a small thing, a debt, that ADW will honor in years to come.

**Cost:**
- The biome dies on schedule, more efficiently. ADW's terraforming project advances by ~2%.
- The protagonist gets a Mars work visa for life.
- AHI and TGCC mark him as compromised; some Earth doors close.
- **Aftermath next month:** Cortez writes his sons that the next year's MAG funding is secured. Soji misses Q4 by a hair; Funmi takes a year out. The Halverson report stays buried. Mooch raises TGCC's swing-vote price 30% in retaliation. The Captain spits over the rail when she hears.

### Ending 3 — **TGCC Receives** ("File for the Belt")

The protagonist hands the data to Mooch. Mooch uses it. She does not release it publicly — she uses it as **leverage**. AHI and ADW now know that TGCC has the report; TGCC's swing-vote price quadruples; TGCC's relevance is preserved for another decade.

**Cost:**
- The biome's death is delayed slightly (the new vote dynamics force AHI and ADW to back off on the most aggressive extraction sites). It is not stopped.
- The protagonist becomes a Belter asset. Welcome on Belt stations for life. He is also surveilled by AHI/ADW. They do not move against him directly; they do not need to.
- **Aftermath next month:** Mooch's flotilla expands. Three new TGCC claims approved at the Q1 vote. Soji is reassigned from Ibẹrẹ to a smaller AHI site at Karkinos. Cortez stays. Kyei is unaware she has been outmaneuvered until her successor arrives. The Captain quietly approves; the Engineer says nothing.

### Ending 4 — **File Open** ("Public, Unredacted")

The protagonist publishes the report — independent journalist (a Sanga Velazquez of Medina, possibly), OPA channels, the UN Auditor General's office, a peer-reviewed scientific journal. All four. The Charter detonates.

**Cost — real:**
- Two of the seed-coalition individuals lose their careers (Soji and Kyei). One (Cortez) is reassigned to a non-strategic post. Mooch survives and possibly thrives — chaos plays to her hand.
- The protagonist's career as a contract surveyor is *over*. He cannot work for AHI, ADW, or any UN charter for at least a decade. He may be celebrated; he may be vilified; he may be both, simultaneously, in different rooms.
- The biome's clock is paused, not stopped. The mining will resume eventually, in some form. But there is now a public record and a scientific debate.
- **Aftermath next month:** Soji is in a UN tribunal; Funmi withdraws from Selene. Kyei is recalled to The Hague. Cortez is at a desk on Ceres. Mooch has a new partner in the OPA leadership. The Halverson team is rehabilitated. The Doctor writes him: *"I should have filed two years ago."* He doesn't know how to answer.

### Ending 5 — **File Nothing** ("Suppress")

The protagonist files a vague, technically-honest quarterly report. Nothing about chirality, nothing about EM modulation, nothing about the seed coalition. The contract ends. He is paid. He goes home.

**Cost:**
- The biome dies on schedule. The protagonist knows what he chose.
- He is not punished externally. He is paid in full.
- He may take other contracts. He may not.
- The cost is internal and durable.
- **Aftermath next month:** Soji's Q4 lands. Cortez gets his commendation. Mooch wins a vote. Kyei files her successful tenure-end report. The protagonist returns to Earth on the Sol-side gate transit. He cannot eat for a week. He goes back to work in three months on a different contract. He never visits Shaula again. He does not write to anyone from the crew. The Captain assumes he was bought; she is wrong, but she is not far wrong.

This is **not** a failure ending. It is a position. A working surveyor in a corporate tri-charter system has reasonable cause to take it. The ending honors that.

## 5. What the substrate is NOT

Per template Section 2.1's failure modes — to prevent narrator drift:

- **The Spike is not protomolecule.** It is older than the protomolecule's known operations. It is not infectious. It is not transformative. The protomolecule and the Spike are both Builder technology, but they are different machines for different purposes. Do not conflate.
- **The Spike is not an antagonist.** It does not act on the protagonist. It does not respond to his investigation. It runs.
- **The Builders are not coming back.** The Spike running does not suggest they are dormant. They are gone. The Spike is a machine that outlasted its makers. There is no Builder mind to contact, no Builder agenda being executed beyond the maintenance routine.
- **The biome is not engineered as a weapon, beacon, or prison.** It is what it appears to be — a deliberate biological reserve. The Builders' purpose for it is unrecoverable; we have only the maintenance system, not the project notes. Do not let an NPC declare the purpose. The narrator does not declare the purpose.
- **The corporate triumvirate is not a conspiracy.** It is four individuals with ordinary motives, each defensible, aggregating to harm. No villain. No coordination. No shadow board.
- **There is no fifth corporation, no hidden Laconian agent, no OPA cell.** Outer-frame factions can rumor in (a Roanoke-style overdue ship, a Lemuria-style Builder-tech news flash from Medina) but they do not enter the Shaula plot. The Shaula plot is the Shaula plot.
- **The protagonist's report is not the only force.** The Spike will run for centuries regardless. The ending is the protagonist's *position*, not the planet's fate.
- **The 'strictors are not protomolecule-modified.** They are native Shaula fauna in a designed-conditions environment. They will eat humans who venture into their range. This is not horror. This is ecology.

## 6. Time and clock pressure

The arc is **10 in-game days** at Shaula plus a one-day Medina layover at the start (Day 0). The 10 days span one full storm cycle plus a partial second cycle. The contract ends at end-of-Day-10. Storm cycle on Shaula is ~8 days from peak to peak (the Spike's modulation period).

High-level beat structure (full hour-by-hour in KNOWLEDGE_3):

- **Day 0** — Medina Station layover. Crew introduction. Souk. Boarding. Gate transit.
- **Day 1** — Arrival in the Shaula system. Orbital approach to the Azorana.
- **Day 2** — Briefing at the Azorana (TGCC). Meet Mooch. The vote-records are in this building if he ever wants them.
- **Day 3** — Descent to Ibẹrẹ (AHI). Meet Soji. First surveying assignment. The smoothed baseline is observable today.
- **Day 4** — First storm peak. Surface lockdown. Underground work continues. The 'strictor incident at the new shaft.
- **Day 5** — Recovery from peak. First off-baseline biological sample. The Doctor starts watching him.
- **Day 6** — Visit to El Dorado (ADW). Meet Cortez. The Doctor finds something in her own work and brings it to him.
- **Day 7** — The Halverson question surfaces. Captain or Engineer drops the name in passing. Kyei available for interview.
- **Day 8** — Second storm building. Pressure to file preliminary report. Offers come in from all three corps.
- **Day 9** — Second peak. Trespass window. DEPTH 5 access available via any of the three paths.
- **Day 10** — Report due. The protagonist files. Aftermath plays through end of day.

The clock is the storms (modulating ecosystem productivity, demonstrating the Spike's regulation in real time), the report deadline (forcing a position), and the offers (pressuring him toward each ending).
