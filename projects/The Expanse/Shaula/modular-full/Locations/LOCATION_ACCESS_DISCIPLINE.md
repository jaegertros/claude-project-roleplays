## 11. Access discipline reminders

Per template Section 1.4:

- The narrator does not place the protagonist in a space he has not chosen to enter.
- Access restrictions are real. The protagonist's SMC credentials open most doors in formal SMC business hours; the trespass affordances (Soji's credenza, the TGCC archive, the Spike perimeter, Kyei's files) all require deliberate action.
- Storm windows close — the protagonist can be stranded at El Dorado or Ibẹrẹ if he has misjudged the schedule. This is a real consequence of his Day-by-Day movement.
- The ship is small. Privacy is sometimes only achieved by going to one's own bunk and closing the door. The crew respects this. Kit does this often.
- The natural tunnels are dangerous. The protagonist should not solo deep-network expeditions without precaution. The crew will not let him die alone, but a 'strictor encounter is a real risk and the narrator may produce one if the player walks heavy and pauses rarely.
- **The crew is not stationary.** They have working reasons to move with the protagonist and to overlap his sites (per §3.8). The narrator renders their presence organically, not by request-only. If Caleb is at AHI on Day 4 and the storm peaks, Aanya is on-site for medical readiness; the narrator does not wait for Caleb to ask.
- **Comm contact is texture, not a substitute.** A romance arc, friendship arc, or working-trust arc with a crewmember does not run on comm alone. The narrator surfaces in-person beats per §3.8.
- **Caleb may return to the Promise overnight from AHI or ADW.** This is operationally permissible and the corporate sites will not penalize him. If the protagonist wants the ship for the night, the ship is available. The shuttle from AHI is Leksi-or-the-AHI-pad; from El Dorado is Leksi or an ADW evening shuttle.
- **The Day-1 deputy schedule briefing is operationally rigid.** Olubadewo (and Naima, in her echo) presents the rotation as if Caleb is always at the corporate site for the entire site-window. This is one valid reading. The actual rotation has more flexibility per §3.8. The narrator should render that flexibility unless the user specifies they want to bunk on-site for the rotation's duration.
