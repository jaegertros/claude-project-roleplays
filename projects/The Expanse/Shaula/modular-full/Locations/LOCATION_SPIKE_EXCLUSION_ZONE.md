## 8. The Spike's Exclusion Zone

A 3 km radius around the Spike's surface contact point at the equator-equivalent latitude. UN-controlled. Two checkpoints, one on the Ibẹrẹ approach road, one on the El Dorado approach road. Inside the perimeter: a small UN observation post (Kyei's domain when she is on-site, which is rare — she prefers her Ibẹrẹ administrative office), the Spike itself.

### 8.1 The UN Observation Post

`location: spike_un_post`

A small reinforced building 2 km from the Spike, on the road approach. Three permanent staff, rotating. A research bay (rarely used since Halverson). A holding area for confiscated unauthorized equipment.

**Sensory anchor:** The Spike-hum is loud here — perceptibly louder than at Ibẹrẹ underground. The auroras on the Spike's surface are visible from the post on clear nights.

**Who's here:** Three UN staff. Kyei visits once a week, takes a tea, signs the books, leaves.

**Access:** UN staff and approved visitors. Permission to enter the perimeter requires UN charter sign-off.

### 8.2 The Perimeter

`location: spike_perimeter`

The 3 km marker. Beyond it is the Spike's immediate environs. The hum is louder still. Recording equipment begins to fail at ~2.5 km from the Spike's base — the dampened zone the protagonist will need shielded equipment to penetrate.

**Sensory anchor:** The hum is now physical — it can be felt in the chest, in the molars. The auroras at this distance are full sky-canopy. The Spike itself fills the horizon: a 50 km cylinder rising into the sky and descending into the ground at 45 degrees, blue-black, the surface patterns moving.

**Access:** **DEPTH 4 trespass affordance.** Authorized only with UN sign-off, which the protagonist's credentials do not include by default. Entering with shielded recording equipment is the procedural / scientific path to DEPTH 5. Kit can advise on the trespass; Aanya can help build the shielding.

### 8.3 The Spike Itself

`location: spike_proximate` (only reachable in DEPTH 4–5 path)

Within ~500 m of the Spike's base. The hum is overwhelming; conversation is difficult. The auroras are continuous and wrap the sky. The Spike's surface, up close, shows the patterns moving in deliberate ways — the "light language" Aanya theorized about. A trained observer with biology training and time can begin to read structure.

**Sensory anchor:** The hum *in the bones*. The visual sublime. The recording equipment that does not work.

**Function:** The substrate's physical reality. DEPTH 5 is reachable here.
