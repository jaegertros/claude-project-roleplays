## 2. Medina Station (Day 0 only)

The protagonist's only on-screen time on Medina is the Day 0 layover before the gate transit to Shaula. He arrives via Sol gate transfer from a Tycho-side liner; the *Tessek's Promise* is in dock at Medina's commercial-tier berths. He boards the ship that evening.

### 2.1 The Berth — Medina Commercial Tier 4

`location: medina_berth_t4`

Standard ship-berth corridor, recycled air, the soft hum of the station's spin engines under everything. The Promise is in slip 4-Echo-3, a working-class slot among similar ships — independent freighters, charter haulers, a handful of corporate shuttles waiting for senior personnel. The corridor smells of warm metal, food from a dozen kitchens, and the faint sweetness of the station's water-recycler signature that everyone notices for an hour and then forgets.

**Sensory anchor:** The light is yellow-warmer than Earth fluorescents, the spin-gravity is ~0.6 g, footsteps echo just slightly off-rhythm because the floor is curved and the wall isn't. The protagonist's first beyond-Sol step. He notices.

**Who's here:** Dock workers (mostly Belter-coded), a few SMC representatives waiting for their shuttle, the Captain at the airlock with her hex bolt rolling. She has been here for about an hour, watching for him.

**Access:** Public. Dock badges required for the slip itself.

### 2.2 The Souk

`location: medina_souk`

The Souk is the social heart of Medina — a sprawling, vibrantly reconfigured market originally designed by the Mormons as a community center. By 2353 it is dozens of small businesses: bars, coffee shops, restaurants, tea houses, gambling parlors, hookah lounges, weapons traders (discreet), bioscience supply shops (less discreet), bookbinders, music kiosks, drone repair, a Belter Methodist mission hall, two competing Mormon historical centers, a Sufi reading room, *The Q* (the only barbecue restaurant beyond the Sol gate, run by an old Belter named Harry with Earth-grandfather recipes).

**Sensory anchors:** Smell-stack — barbecue from The Q, kebabs from three different vendors, hookah smoke, the iron-water smell of recycled-from-mining-runoff station water, fried dough, beer. Sound-stack — three languages overlapping (Lang Belta, English, Spanish), live music from at least one of the venues at any hour, the constant low rumble of dock work two decks down.

**Who's here:** Everyone in transit through Medina. Crews on layover. A handful of journalists (Sanga Velazquez has been seen here, if the protagonist is paying attention). UN observers. OPA-adjacent contacts who do not advertise. The protagonist could spend the whole Day 0 in the Souk and not see the same face twice.

**Access:** Public. Some of the back rooms are not.

**Function in arc:** Day 0's only on-screen Medina scene. The cold open uses it. After Day 0, Medina becomes off-screen — a place rumor comes from, where the *Soji-Bee* runs to.
