## 6. El Dorado — ADW cliff-face complex

ADW's ground operation, built into a massive cliff face on the coast of the eastern continent (~6,000 km from Ibẹrẹ). The complex is an inverted high-rise — six "floors" cut horizontally into the cliff face, with the upper levels closest to the surface and the lower levels accessible only via internal lifts. ~900 ADW staff resident.

El Dorado is more aesthetically Mariner-Valley than Ibẹrẹ is corporate-Lagos. The protagonist arrives Day 6.

### 6.1 The Cliff-Face Approach

`location: el_dorado_approach`

A landing platform built out from the cliff at the upper level, with a glass-walled receiving area inside. The cliff face is dramatic — a 400 m drop to the ocean below, the ocean a deep blue-green from the underground biome's microbiome leak, sea spray reaching the platform on rough days.

**Sensory anchors:** The view (the only place on Shaula where the sky is always visible — the eastern continent has fewer storms than the Iji range), the salt-spray smell, the wind.

**Who's here:** Landing crew. Beni Vargas is rarely here; he runs the pavilion two levels down.

**Access:** ADW staff and credentialed visitors. SMC credentials work.

### 6.2 The Pavilion

`location: el_dorado_pavilion`

ADW's social heart, two levels below the surface entry. A genuinely beautiful space — Mariner Valley-style with Mars-imported pine paneling (the wood smell is *expensive*), a long bar of polished stone, three card-game tables, a small stage for live music two evenings a week. Terraforming songs play at the volume people want — louder after the second drink.

**Sensory anchors:** Real wood smell (the only place on Shaula with this), real coffee (also Mars-imported), the hum of conversation in Mariner Valley drawl with occasional standard English.

**Who's here:** Beni Vargas runs it. Marisol Halász-Tejeda drinks here most evenings (Conde tables). Cortez stops in once or twice a week; ADW senior staff who want to be seen drink here on Tuesdays. Sister Gabriela Mendez holds informal evening prayer in a quiet corner of the pavilion at 1900 every weekday — five to fifteen attendees.

**Access:** ADW staff and registered guests. The protagonist's credentials work; Cortez personally vouches for him on Day 6.

### 6.3 Cortez's Office

`location: el_dorado_cortez_office`

ADW Senior Engineering Office on Level 3. Less ornate than Soji's; engineering-functional. Three terraforming maps of Mars on the walls, color-coded for atmospheric pressure progression over decades. Hand-terminal photos of his sons and wife in a small frame on the desk. The MCRN signet ring he wears is mirrored by a small framed photo of his old MCRN unit, taken at his retirement.

**Sensory anchor:** The maps. The protagonist will look at them and Cortez will catch him looking and say something true.

**Access:** By appointment for SMC charter business. **DEPTH 3–4 affordance:** Cortez's EM modulation data on the Spike is in his work files — accessible if he chooses to share, which at DEPTH 3+ with the right technical engagement he might.

### 6.4 The Geological Survey Wing

`location: el_dorado_survey`

Tariq Halász-Cortez's department. Three scientists, four technicians, a well-equipped lab and field-prep room. Coffee station perpetually running.

**Sensory anchor:** Geology smells — rock dust, the faint petrichor of recent water samples, coffee.

**Who's here:** Tariq runs it. The protagonist may visit professionally; Tariq is friendly, and his data is comparable-but-narrower than the protagonist's SMC scope.

**Access:** ADW staff and inter-corporate visitors. Standard professional courtesy.

### 6.5 Sister Gabriela's Reading Room

`location: el_dorado_reading_room`

A small alcove off the pavilion, partitioned with hanging fabric (Mars-woven, geometric patterns), housing a low table with cushions, a brass tea service, and a stack of books — Sufi poetry in Persian and English, a Bible, a Bhagavad Gita, three Belter-Methodist hymnals from Medina. Sister Gabriela serves tea here in the afternoons; anyone may sit.

**Sensory anchor:** Cardamom tea (the same blend Soji drinks — both Earther blends from a particular Lagos exporter), the soft fabric and the muted light.

**Who's here:** Sister Gabriela, daily. Marisol does not visit. Cortez does, occasionally, in moments of quiet.

**Access:** Public to ADW staff and credentialed visitors. The protagonist can find peace here if he needs it. No one will report on his presence.
