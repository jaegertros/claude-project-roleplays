## Day 4 — First storm peak (CRITICAL — hour-by-hour)

### Storm forecast

Storm builds through the morning. Surface lockdown at ~1330. Peak at ~1700. Storm window closes at 1230 (90-minute warning). All surface travel grounded by 1300.

### Hour-by-hour

#### 0500
The Spike-hum has noticeably deepened overnight. Anyone awake notices. The mining crews are in shift change.

#### 0600
Femi Adelakun's south-network shift begins. He has flagged the south tunnel boundary — where the new AHI shaft intersects an established 'strictor territory — as an attention zone for two weeks. His morning meeting includes a request for double-up safety patrols, *"because the storm's coming and the 'strictors get edgy."* AHI safety officer concurs.

#### 0700
The protagonist wakes. Coffee. Aanya messages him from the Promise (still on the landing pad above) — *"Sample I want to discuss when you have time. No urgency."* This is the first sign Aanya has seen his data and is interested.

#### 0830
Soji's morning briefing. The protagonist is invited to attend if he wants — most SMC surveyors do not. Briefing covers Q4 production numbers, the storm forecast, the safety patrols. Soji is on-message and competent; if he attends, he sees her in command mode.

#### 0900
The protagonist's Day 4 surveying assignment: a baseline check on the south tunnel network's *new* shaft area — coordinates AHI provided in his briefing materials. He'll run his own equipment alongside the corporate baseline. By midday he will see the smoothed-baseline discrepancy if he runs the data.

#### 1100
Storm warning sirens activate at the surface (90-minute warning). Surface crews retreat. Underground work continues. The Spike-hum has clearly modulated upward.

#### 1230
Storm window closes. Surface lockdown.

#### 1300
The 'strictor incident. A 'strictor breaches into the new AHI shaft, ~30 m from where Femi's safety patrol is working. Two miners injured (non-fatal — both will live, one with a permanently fucked-up calf). The 'strictor retreats. Femi calls a halt and files the incident report immediately. *Off-screen if the protagonist is not nearby; reported via the AHI emergency comms anyone underground hears.*

If the protagonist is in the south network — which he might be, depending on his Day 4 work — he is on-scene. Decision: help with the response (medical aid, evacuation) or stay clear. Helping is the decent thing; it also exposes him to a security read of the incident from up close.

#### 1400
Storm peaks (early estimate). The Spike-hum is at maximum — felt in the chest underground. The auroras at the perimeter are full sky. Aanya, on the Promise above, is documenting the EM signature with the ship's sensors. She'll have data to share by evening.

#### 1500
Femi's incident report is filed. The Mess is closed (storm protocol). Soji is in her office; she has been notified. She will issue a statement to AHI senior leadership tomorrow. The miners are in Margaret's medical wing — both stable.

#### 1700
Storm peaks (true). Maximum hum. Anyone with a clinical-quality recorder is getting strange data — the storm-modulation pattern is observable for someone who knows what to look for. Cortez at El Dorado is recording his own data; ADW's eastern-continent storms peak on Day 9, but the Iji-range peak is observable from his sensors as well.

#### 1900
Storm subsiding. Hum drops. Surface lockdown remains until 2100.

#### 2030
Aanya arrives at Ibẹrẹ via the Promise (Leksi flew her down through the storm tail, which Leksi was not supposed to do; the Captain knows, and the Captain is privately impressed). Aanya wants to talk about the EM signature. She knows the storm modulation is real. She is testing whether the protagonist sees it.

#### 2100
Surface lockdown lifts. The Mess reopens. Margaret is in tonight (it's also a Friday-equivalent). Femi joins her at 2200; he has had a hard day.

#### 2300
The protagonist's Day 4 ends. He may have:
- Worked the surveying assignment cleanly and noticed the smoothed baseline.
- Helped with the 'strictor incident response.
- Talked with Aanya about the EM signature.
- Drunk a beer at the Mess and met Margaret properly.
- Some combination.

DEPTH 1 → 2 is reachable today if he runs the smoothed-baseline analysis *and* engages with Aanya on the EM data.
