## Day 3 — Descent to Ibẹrẹ

### Spine

The Promise descends from the Azorana to the Iji range landing pad at Ibẹrẹ. Leksi flies. The descent takes 90 minutes; the protagonist's first 1.6 g hit on disembark. Soji meets him in person at the elevator on Admin Level 1. First in-person Soji conversation. First surveying assignment.

### Beats

- **0600** — Crew preps the Promise for descent. The protagonist is on board.
- **0800** — Descent begins. Leksi narrates atmospheric entry like a tour guide if asked. Storm windows clear; landing on schedule.
- **0930** — Landing pad arrival. The 1.6 g. The cold air. The storm shutters glinting. AHI staff handles the receiving.
- **1000** — Elevator descent to Admin Level 1. Soji is in the elevator's vestibule, waiting. *"My dear,"* she says, without preamble. *"Welcome to Ibẹrẹ. You and I are going to get along very well."*
- **1030** — Soji's office. Tea (cardamom, Lagos blend). She walks the protagonist through AHI's stake in the SMC charter, what she expects from him, what she hopes for. She asks where he's from and listens to the answer. She recognizes him as a Basic-survivor within the first ten minutes; the recognition is real, not theatrical.
- **1200** — Lunch in the AHI cafeteria. Solo unless he sought company. He may meet Femi Adelakun at lunch by chance; Femi is friendly.
- **1400** — First surveying assignment: a calibration run on AHI's north tunnel network's established baseline. Routine. The protagonist's equipment, AHI's coordinates. He runs it competently. The AHI baseline data is the *clean* (older) data, not the *smoothed* (recent quarter) data; today's results match. Tomorrow's, in different coordinates, will not.
- **1800** — End of work day. The Mess is open. Margaret Hwang-Adelakun is there tonight (it's a Tuesday-equivalent — Margaret drinks Tuesday and Friday). Femi joins her at 1900. The protagonist may go.
- **2100** — Sleeps in his AHI guest quarters. Bunk smaller than the ship's.

### What's off-screen

Soji's Q4 numbers track in the morning briefing she ran before the protagonist arrived. She is on schedule, barely. The deputy who handles the senior environmental impact filings has been by twice this week; she has signed two cover sheets and read neither in detail. Margaret has noticed Soji is more tense than usual; she is keeping an eye on her.

### Decision point

Whether the protagonist accepts Soji's warmth at face value or holds reserve. Her warmth is real *and* deliberate; both can be true. The narrator does not signal which way to read it.
