## 2. The seed coalition

The corporate triumvirate's failure to cooperate is not a villain's plot. It is the aggregate of four senior individuals each acting in their own concrete material interest. None is wrong by their own lights. The aggregate strips the planet.

Per template Section 2.5: each named, each with a specific material motive, no ideology, no coordination required. The narrator can explain the situation by walking through these four. Never by invoking "corporate greed" or "the system."

### 2.1 Adesoji "Soji" Oduya — AHI Site Director, Ibẹrẹ

- 51, Nigerian-British by registration, born in Lagos, raised in Lagos and on Luna.
- Daughter Funmi, 19, attending the Selene Polytechnic on a partial scholarship. The remainder costs Soji ~$140,000 UN per Earth year.
- Her Q4 production-target bonus, paid in Earth currency, covers Funmi's third year exactly. Has covered first and second. If Q4 lands clean, fourth is also covered. If Q4 misses, Funmi takes a year out and the scholarship lapses.
- Soji believes — genuinely, not opportunistically — that resource extraction from beyond-the-ring worlds is the engine that keeps Earth's 30 billion fed and out of Basic. She has worked thirty years toward this conviction. She came up through Basic herself.
- She is not lying to herself. She is also signing off on environmental impact reports she has not read in detail.

### 2.2 Captain Tomás Cortez — ADW Senior Facility Engineer, El Dorado

- 44, Mariner Valley by birth, ex-MCRN engineering corps, retired into corporate work eight years ago.
- Wife Inés, sons Javier (12) and Diego (9), all at Olympus. Inés works on the Mariner Atmospheric Generator project (terraforming, pressure-doctrine division).
- Cortez's view: every kilo of metal Shaula produces funds Mars's terraforming on a multiplier. The dream of a Mars his sons can walk on without a suit is the actual stake. Shaula extraction money flows through ADW into the MAG project specifically; Cortez has the line items.
- He is correct about the multiplier. He is also choosing not to ask whether Shaula's biome has a value the multiplier doesn't price.

### 2.3 Mari "Mooch" Asarat-Garai — TGCC Flotilla Coordinator, the Azorana

- 38, Ceres-born, moved to Pallas at 22, joined TGCC at 26.
- No close family. A long-term partner named Bensen who runs cargo on the Coriolis route, sees her once every six standard months.
- Mooch's stake: **TGCC's continued political relevance in the SMC charter**. As the Belter signatory in a body where two of three votes are Inner, TGCC's only leverage is its swing-vote position. If TGCC ever stops being useful as the spoiler, AHI and ADW will dissolve them out of the charter and replace them with a more pliable Belt corporation.
- Mooch keeps TGCC relevant by selling votes. She does not have ideological commitments to AHI or ADW. She does not sell to whichever side bids highest in raw money — she sells to whichever side preserves TGCC's *necessity*. This sometimes means siding with the smaller offer.
- She is the most politically intelligent person at the senior level on Shaula. She is also the most isolated.

### 2.4 Dr. Ingrid Kyei — UN Charter Observer

- 64, Ghanaian by registration, raised in Accra, educated at Cambridge and at Tycho.
- Late-career xenoscientist. Six years from mandatory retirement. The SMC observer post is her last formal assignment.
- Her stake: legacy. She wants the charter to be remembered as a model of UN-led inter-faction cooperation. The Shaula Mining Cooperative is her last paper, her last commendation, her last entry in the UN xenoscience record.
- She has been waving through environmental impact reports she has not adequately scrutinized. Two years ago she received a preliminary biological report from an independent research team (Dr. Halverson's team — their charter was suspended six months later) that noted chirality anomalies and unusual EM modulation patterns near the Spike. She filed it as "pending further review." It has been pending for two years.
- She is not a corporate plant. She is a senior career civil servant who has decided, mostly without admitting it to herself, that the larger goal justifies the smaller silence.

### 2.5 How they aggregate

Soji wants Q4. Cortez wants Mars's terraforming. Mooch wants TGCC's relevance. Kyei wants her legacy intact. None of them is conspiring. None of them needs to. The aggregate of their individual decisions is: extraction continues, environmental concerns are deferred, the Spike's clock starts.

Each is defensible. Each is, in their own frame, doing right work. The protagonist will meet all four.
