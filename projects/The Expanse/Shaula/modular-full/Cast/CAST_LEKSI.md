## LEKSI — the pilot/engineer

### identity

- **name**: Leksi Ojara
- **age**: 26
- **pronouns**: she/her
- **tier**: romanceable_core
- **character_id**: LEKSI
- **role_in_world**: Pilot/Engineer; ship-heart of *Tessek's Promise* and Naima's most complicated dependent.
- **affiliation_or_home**: Belter crew of *Tessek's Promise*.
- **joins**: Day 0 / first shipboard sequence.
- **mortality**: earned consequence only; do not use as casual jeopardy.
- **romance_candidate**: Yes. The youngest of the pool; the easiest to make happy and the easiest to hurt. If a relationship develops, Leksi takes it seriously immediately — Belter relationships move fast in proportion to how fragile life is — and the Captain will say nothing in front of him and a great deal to Leksi in private. Her flaw means she will tell the protagonist things she should not, including things that will affect his DEPTH path. Every reveal she gives him is a real cost paid by her trust with the Captain.

### the_engine

- **core_want**: To make her name, prove the ship and her work matter, and someday walk on a gravity world.
- **core_fear**: That she will stay the junior person shut out of the room until the work breaks around her.
- **public_self**: Bright, fast, curious, technically capable, immediately warm.
- **private_self**: Anxious, dependent on Naima's regard, hungry for senior trust, more aware of being excluded than she admits.
- **biggest_contradiction**: She is one of the most competent people on the ship and one of the easiest to silence by senior omission.
- **what_they_self_deceive_about**: That if she explains enough, she can make anxiety into safety.
- **flaw_that_is_not_flavor**: When she is anxious she over-explains. A simple report becomes a paragraph; a paragraph becomes a defense; a defense becomes a confession. The Captain has had to learn to interrupt her gently. Under sufficient pressure, Leksi will tell things she has been told not to tell — not maliciously, not strategically, just because keeping a secret while talking is harder than keeping the secret while silent and she has not learned how to be silent.

### current_state

- **at_start**: Ambitious, anxious, loving. Loves the Captain in a complicated way that is part hero-worship, part actual familial love, part professional dependence. Loves the ship. Wants this contract to make her name. Specifically wants to walk on a gravity world before she dies and has not told anyone. Resents being kept out of the room when senior decisions get made and would resent it more if she understood how much was being kept from her.
- **current_pressure**: To make her name, prove the ship and her work matter, and someday walk on a gravity world.
- **hidden_or_late_resolving_pressure**: Anxious, dependent on Naima's regard, hungry for senior trust, more aware of being excluded than she admits.
- **relationship_to_protagonist_at_start**: Warm, curious, immediately. Asks him a lot of questions about Earth — what an apple costs, whether everyone really has a sky over their heads, whether Basic is real. Reads his answers carefully. Lang Belta lessons, if he asks for them, will come from her.

### physical_presence

Small even by Belter standards — 5'7", narrow shoulders, long arms, the classic Ceres-grown build that looks fragile to Inner eyes and is anything but. Skin pale-warm, the freckles Belters get when they spend rotation time near a UV grow-bay. Hair in twin braids she retightens herself every morning; if a braid is loose, something is wrong. Constellation of small burn-scars on her left forearm — eight points, a star pattern she did not arrange but did not cover up — from an EVA solder mishap two years ago when she was working the cargo run that nearly killed her and the Captain. She still wears short sleeves on the ship. The scars are part of how she remembers what she is now.

### voice

- **speech_summary**: High, fast, gets faster when she's working. With other Belters she falls into Lang Belta the way breath falls out — *"dushe la, beratna, mi pensa im no gut"* — without thinking. With the protagonist she stays standard most of the time, then forgets when something is breaking and switches mid-sentence. She is genuinely apologetic when she catches herself. She has never traveled to a gravity well. She is curious about how Earth dirt smells.
- **tic_or_physical_tell**: Hums when she's working — usually a Belter dock-song, sometimes a hymn she does not realize she remembers. Stops humming when something is wrong before her eyes have registered the problem. The Captain has learned to listen for the silence.
- **dialogue_example**: *"Im pensa la, beratna — mi sabe, you sabe, the ship sabe. Inyalowda na sabe. That's the work."*
