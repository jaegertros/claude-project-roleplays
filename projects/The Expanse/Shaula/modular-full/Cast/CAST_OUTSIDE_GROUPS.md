## Outside Groups

The protagonist's contract concentrates him in the SMC orbit, but Shaula is small and rumor travels. Four standing groups exist outside the corporate triumvirate; he can engage at his preferred level. Each group's NPCs are bounded by their map (KNOWLEDGE_8 stub) and offer different things at different relationship tiers.

The relationship tiers are universal:

- **Trade.** Pure transactional. Cargo, supplies, information for credit. No personal exchange.
- **Business only.** A working relationship — they expect him to behave professionally; he expects the same. No personal exchange.
- **Socializing.** Drinks, meals, conversation. A trust beyond business.
- **Teaming up.** Mutual operations. Skin in the game.
- **Drinking buddies / inside.** Personal friendship. They will tell him things. He will tell them things. Information flows.

### 1. The Halverson Successor Team — *Kerent Lab*

**Composition.** A four-person UN-funded independent research charter, grudgingly tolerated by the SMC. The original Halverson team was dissolved two years ago after their preliminary report on chirality anomalies was buried. Kerent Lab is the unofficial successor — same university (Tübingen), same funding line, smaller scope, lower profile. They do not study the Spike directly; they study the underground biome's microbial communities, on a permit that explicitly excludes the Spike's exclusion zone. Within those constraints they have noticed everything Halverson noticed.

**Faces.**

- **Dr. Eske Kerent** — 41, German-Ghanaian, the lab director. Tall, soft-spoken, the kind of researcher who learned long ago that getting the work done quietly was more productive than getting credit for it. Knows the Halverson report exists. Has not seen it. Wants to.
- **Pavel Adamec** — 28, Czech, the lab's microbiologist. Direct peer to the protagonist's training. Brilliant and unsubtle. The kind of scientist who, given a glass of beer, will say things at full volume that he should not say.

**Engagement ladder.**

- *Trade.* Kerent Lab will buy biological samples the protagonist collects on his SMC rounds, paid in UN charter credit. Standard pricing.
- *Business only.* They will share preliminary findings (theirs, in exchange for his) with formal data exchange agreements signed.
- *Socializing.* Pavel drinks at the Azorana itinerant bar twice a week. Drinks loosen Pavel's tongue. Eske does not socialize.
- *Teaming up.* They will run a joint sampling operation under Kerent Lab's permit if the protagonist provides corporate-permit-required cover. Significant DEPTH 2 acceleration; significant career risk.
- *Inside.* Eske will give him the Halverson report's location. Eske will tell him why she has not pushed harder for it. This is a DEPTH 3 path that is genuinely faster than the others.

### 2. The Azorana Itinerants — Belter Rec & Cargo Rotation

**Composition.** TGCC's flotilla supports a constant rotation of dock workers, cargo handlers, ice-haulers' crews on layover, junior engineers between contracts. None are SMC senior. They drink in the Azorana's lower-deck recreation bay — an unofficial bar called *Sasa Bar* (no apostrophe; the joke is *"do you understand?"* asked of every customer). Belter culture, Lang Belta full-strength, OPA news streams playing at low volume. Inners are tolerated and tested.

**Faces.**

- **Tomi "Tee" Borisova** — 29, Belter, runs the bar in name. Born on Eros, lost her parents in the crisis, raised by an uncle who runs a Pallas dock. Friendly. Will speak standard with Inners, drops to Lang Belta with anyone who has demonstrated they can keep up. Specifically interested in Earth music — wants the protagonist's playlist if he has one.
- **Hesh Mahesh-Yves** — 34, Belter-with-Earth-grandparent (genuinely welwala in the old sense, comfortable with the word), runs an independent cargo route Pallas-Medina-Shaula. In and out of the Azorana every six weeks. Knows everyone's business. Sells nothing about it cheaply. Will take a long meal in trade.

**Engagement ladder.**

- *Trade.* The Sasa Bar serves drinks, food, certain herbal supplies. Cash basis.
- *Business only.* Hesh will run cargo for the protagonist on his own private accounts. Reasonable rates.
- *Socializing.* Tee will start a conversation with the protagonist on his second visit. Belter regulars will warm to him after his fourth — provided he tips properly and does not order beer "lite."
- *Teaming up.* Hesh will carry packages he should not be asked to carry. Information dead-drops to and from Medina. He charges for it.
- *Inside.* The Sasa Bar regulars will tell him what they have heard about every senior person on the SMC charter. Most of it is true. Some of it is the rumor that has come back from Medina and is wearing a different costume now.

### 3. The El Dorado Pavilion — ADW Martian Recreation

**Composition.** ADW operates a recreation pavilion at El Dorado — a mid-sized social space on the cliff-face complex's lower levels. Primarily Martian crowd, Mariner Valley culture dominant, terraforming songs played at a respectful volume after the second drink. Limited to ADW staff and registered guests; the protagonist's SMC credentials get him in.

**Faces.**

- **Lt. Beni Vargas (ret.)** — 39, Martian, ex-MCRN like Cortez, retired honorably. Runs the pavilion. Knows Cortez personally and professionally. Welcoming to anyone Cortez has approved; reserved with anyone not approved. Will recognize Kit Solano on sight and not say anything in front of her. Later, alone, will ask the protagonist what he knows.
- **Marisol Halász-Tejeda** — 27, Martian, ADW junior atmospheric engineer. Off-duty most evenings. Sharp, funny, terraforming-true-believer. Will challenge the protagonist on Earth's failures within ten minutes of meeting him. Will respect him if he does not flinch.

**Engagement ladder.**

- *Trade.* The pavilion has a kitchen and a bar. Standard service.
- *Business only.* ADW staff are willing to discuss their work professionally over drinks if the protagonist has been credentialed in.
- *Socializing.* Marisol will invite him to play the Martian Mariners' card game *Conde* if he expresses interest. He will lose for an evening.
- *Teaming up.* Vargas can quietly facilitate access to ADW's older engineering files — the ones Cortez does not want examined too closely — but only if the protagonist gives him a reason that is not an intrusion on Cortez's dignity.
- *Inside.* Marisol, drunk, will tell him exactly what ADW is extracting and at what rate, and will not consider this disloyalty because she believes it is what Mars deserves to have known.

### 4. The *Soji-Bee* — Independent Belter Free Trader

**Composition.** A six-person Belter free-trader, Pallas-registered, running a regular Shaula-Medina supply route. Captain is **Aleyn Gboro-Aoki** (54, Belter, the only male captain on Shaula's regular roster — the user's note: this contributes the "primarily female but real" texture without making the protagonist the only male in his world). Sober, professional, faintly fatherly to younger crews. Carries personal mail to and from Medina. Does small favors. Charges fair rates. The *Soji-Bee* is the first commercial vessel any newcomer to Shaula meets, because Aleyn's ship is in dock at the Azorana when nearly any other ship arrives.

**Faces.**

- **Captain Aleyn Gboro-Aoki** — 54, Belter, calm, watchful. Knows everyone. Speaks Lang Belta with Belters, standard with Inners, never code-switches mid-sentence. Will not tell the protagonist anything his clients have not paid for him to tell. Will tell the protagonist the unpaid version of why his captain is worth her ship.
- **Suri "Soolie" Aoki** — 24, Belter, Captain Aleyn's niece and the *Soji-Bee*'s comms officer. Bright, funny, the youngest person the protagonist will regularly interact with on Shaula. Has a crush on Leksi that Leksi has not noticed. Will quietly resent the protagonist if Leksi's attention turns to him.

**Engagement ladder.**

- *Trade.* The *Soji-Bee* sells everything Medina sells, marked up for transport. Mail service to and from Medina, paid by the kilo and the urgency.
- *Business only.* Aleyn will run discreet packages between corporate parties on the SMC charter. Knows the prices.
- *Socializing.* Soolie will invite him to dinner on the *Soji-Bee* on his third visit — Belter food, simple, real.
- *Teaming up.* Aleyn will not team up. He runs his own ship and that is the deal.
- *Inside.* Soolie will tell him what she has heard on Medina that has not yet reached Shaula. This is rumor-grade information, but ahead of the curve. Some of it is wrong. Some of it is critical.
