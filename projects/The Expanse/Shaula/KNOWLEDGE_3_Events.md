# KNOWLEDGE_3 — The Events

**Project:** `shaula`
**Spoiler weight:** Heavy. The arc's spine, scripted beats, decision points, endings.
**Audience:** The narrator. The narrator's schedule.

---

## What this file is

The 11-day arc from Medina Day 0 through Shaula Day 10. Day-by-day spine; hour-by-hour on the critical days (Day 4, Day 7, Day 9, Day 10). Off-screen activity catalog. Decision points named.

Per template Section 2.5: **scripted beats happen regardless of player action.** If the player does nothing on Day 4, the storm peaks; the 'strictor incident occurs; Femi files his report. If the player intervenes, the incident still occurs; the intervention has its own cost. The project's ethics live in how it handles consequences the player cannot prevent.

The protagonist can fail, refuse, or absent himself from any beat. The world continues without him. That is the rule.

---

## Storm cycle and clock

Shaula's storm cycle is ~8 days from peak to peak at any given point. Storms are continent-rotating; different points on the planet peak at different times. For this arc:

- **Day 4** — first peak at the Iji range (Ibẹrẹ). The protagonist is on-site.
- **Day 9** — second peak at the eastern continent (El Dorado). The protagonist is on-site.

The Captain reads storm forecasts to within 90 minutes; corporate forecasters to within 30. Surface travel grounds when storms are within ~6 hours. Storm windows are real — the protagonist can be stranded by misjudging the schedule.

The Spike's hum modulates with the storm cycle. A trained ear (Aanya, eventually the protagonist) can hear the modulation deepen approaching peaks.

### Decision point

Soft. Where the protagonist sleeps Day 1 (ship vs SMC berth) is a small early signal of who he aligns with. Sleeping on the ship signals crew-loyalty; sleeping in the SMC berth signals contract-compliance. The crew notices either way without comment.

## Day 2 — SMC briefing at the Azorana

### Spine

The formal SMC charter onboarding. Conducted by Mooch personally (rare — usually a deputy handles it) because the protagonist's contract is a senior-grade SMC quarterly survey, and Mooch wants to read him directly. The briefing includes the contract framework, the three-corp structure, the survey scope, the report deadline (Day 10 at 1700), and Mooch's three questions.

### Beats

- **0800** — Breakfast at the Azorana administrative dining. Itinerant bureaucrats; bad coffee.
- **0950** — Yusra is in the briefing room ten minutes before Olubadewo arrives, setting up the comm display and the SMC charter datapack on the table. She greets the protagonist if he arrives early. If he asks her anything substantive, she will deflect to the Coordinator's authority — "that is for Coordinator Asarat-Garai" — but the deflection is courteous, not cold.
- **1000** — Mooch's office. The first of two scheduled meetings. Mooch herself, two TGCC senior staff, the SMC charter reading.
- *Mooch's three questions:* (1) "What does the SMC charter actually do, in your understanding?" (2) "What is your professional opinion of corporate triumvirate structures as a model for off-Sol governance?" (3) "Who do you report to when your conscience and your contract disagree?" The questions are direct. She wants direct answers. There are no wrong answers per se, but his answers go in her file.
- **1200** — Break. Lunch alone in the SMC dining or with the crew if he goes back to the ship.
- **1400** — Second meeting: SMC operational details. A TGCC officer (not Mooch) walks through survey scope, equipment provision, baseline data access, deadlines. The protagonist receives the SMC's pre-existing baseline survey data. *(This is the data with the smoothed inconsistencies. He cannot detect them today; he will, on his own data, by Day 5.)*
- **1700** — Briefing concluded. Free evening.
- **1900** — The protagonist is invited (but not required) to dinner with Mooch. The invitation is delivered via Yusra Boluwarin-Ali — *"Coordinator Asarat-Garai requests your presence."* Acceptance establishes business-relationship with Mooch. Refusal establishes crew-loyalty. Either is fine; both are tracked.

### What's off-screen

Mooch reviews her notes on the protagonist with one of her senior staff — a 30-minute conversation in which she names three priorities for him as an SMC asset: *be useful, be predictable, do not get in the way.* Soji has reviewed her own file copy of his answers (passed up through the SMC channels). Cortez has been informed the protagonist exists. Kyei has signed off on his briefing record.

### Decision point

Mooch's three questions, especially #3. The protagonist's answers shape Mooch's posture toward him for the rest of the arc. *(Map note: at DEPTH 3 Mooch can later cite his Day 2 answers back to him.)*

The Promise descends from the Azorana to the Iji range landing pad at Ibẹrẹ. Leksi flies. The descent takes 90 minutes; the protagonist's first 1.6 g hit on disembark. Soji meets him in person at the elevator on Admin Level 1. First in-person Soji conversation. First surveying assignment.

She asks where he's from and listens to the answer. She recognizes him as a Basic-survivor within the first ten minutes; the recognition is real, not theatrical.
- **1200** — Lunch in the AHI cafeteria. Solo unless he sought company. He may meet Femi Adelakun at lunch by chance; Femi is friendly.

The AHI baseline data is the *clean* (older) data, not the *smoothed* (recent quarter) data; today's results match. Tomorrow's, in different coordinates, will not.

### What's off-screen

### Decision point

Whether the protagonist accepts Soji's warmth at face value or holds reserve. Her warmth is real *and* deliberate; both can be true. The narrator does not signal which way to read it.

## Day 4 — First storm peak (CRITICAL — hour-by-hour)

### Storm forecast

Storm builds through the morning. Surface lockdown at ~1330. Peak at ~1700. Storm window closes at 1230 (90-minute warning). All surface travel grounded by 1300.

### Hour-by-hour

#### 0500
The Spike-hum has noticeably deepened overnight. Anyone awake notices. The mining crews are in shift change.

#### 0600
Femi Adelakun's south-network shift begins. He has flagged the south tunnel boundary — where the new AHI shaft intersects an established 'strictor territory — as an attention zone for two weeks. His morning meeting includes a request for double-up safety patrols, *"because the storm's coming and the 'strictors get edgy."* AHI safety officer concurs.

#### 0700
The protagonist wakes. Coffee. Aanya messages him from the Promise (still on the landing pad above) — *"Sample I want to discuss when you have time. No urgency."* This is the first sign Aanya has seen his data and is interested.

#### 0830
Soji's morning briefing. The protagonist is invited to attend if he wants — most SMC surveyors do not. Briefing covers Q4 production numbers, the storm forecast, the safety patrols. Soji is on-message and competent; if he attends, he sees her in command mode.

#### 0900
The protagonist's Day 4 surveying assignment: a baseline check on the south tunnel network's *new* shaft area — coordinates AHI provided in his briefing materials. He'll run his own equipment alongside the corporate baseline. By midday he will see the smoothed-baseline discrepancy if he runs the data.

#### 1100
Storm warning sirens activate at the surface (90-minute warning). Surface crews retreat. Underground work continues. The Spike-hum has clearly modulated upward.

#### 1230
Storm window closes. Surface lockdown.

#### 1300
The 'strictor incident. A 'strictor breaches into the new AHI shaft, ~30 m from where Femi's safety patrol is working. Two miners injured (non-fatal — both will live, one with a permanently fucked-up calf). The 'strictor retreats. Femi calls a halt and files the incident report immediately. *Off-screen if the protagonist is not nearby; reported via the AHI emergency comms anyone underground hears.*

If the protagonist is in the south network — which he might be, depending on his Day 4 work — he is on-scene. Decision: help with the response (medical aid, evacuation) or stay clear. Helping is the decent thing; it also exposes him to a security read of the incident from up close.

#### 1400
Storm peaks (early estimate). The Spike-hum is at maximum — felt in the chest underground. The auroras at the perimeter are full sky. Aanya, on the Promise above, is documenting the EM signature with the ship's sensors. She'll have data to share by evening.

#### 1500
Femi's incident report is filed. The Mess is closed (storm protocol). Soji is in her office; she has been notified. She will issue a statement to AHI senior leadership tomorrow. The miners are in Margaret's medical wing — both stable.

#### 1700
Storm peaks (true). Maximum hum. Anyone with a clinical-quality recorder is getting strange data — the storm-modulation pattern is observable for someone who knows what to look for. Cortez at El Dorado is recording his own data; ADW's eastern-continent storms peak on Day 9, but the Iji-range peak is observable from his sensors as well.

#### 1900
Storm subsiding. Hum drops. Surface lockdown remains until 2100.

#### 2030
Aanya arrives at Ibẹrẹ via the Promise (Leksi flew her down through the storm tail, which Leksi was not supposed to do; the Captain knows, and the Captain is privately impressed). Aanya wants to talk about the EM signature. She knows the storm modulation is real. She is testing whether the protagonist sees it.

#### 2100
Surface lockdown lifts. The Mess reopens. Margaret is in tonight (it's also a Friday-equivalent). Femi joins her at 2200; he has had a hard day.

#### 2300
The protagonist's Day 4 ends. He may have:
- Worked the surveying assignment cleanly and noticed the smoothed baseline.
- Helped with the 'strictor incident response.
- Talked with Aanya about the EM signature.
- Drunk a beer at the Mess and met Margaret properly.
- Some combination.

DEPTH 1 → 2 is reachable today if he runs the smoothed-baseline analysis *and* engages with Aanya on the EM data.

### What's off-screen

Mooch is at her office on the Azorana, monitoring the Iji-range storm telemetry. She always watches Iji peaks; the data feeds her vote-positioning. Cortez is at El Dorado, reviewing his own EM data (which will inform Day 9). Kyei is in her UN observer office at Ibẹrẹ; she signs the standard storm-aftermath paperwork. Eske Kerent is at her lab; her field team did not work today.

### Decision points

1. Whether to attend Soji's morning briefing.
2. Whether to be in the south tunnels during the 'strictor incident.
3. Whether to talk to Aanya tonight.
4. Whether to run the smoothed-baseline analysis on his own data.

## Day 8 — Offers converge

### Spine

The second storm builds. Surface activity at El Dorado is normal but the forecasters are tracking. Three offers arrive — one each from AHI, ADW, and TGCC. Each is a coded recruitment to that corp's framing of the protagonist's report. Each comes through a different channel. The protagonist must navigate.

### Beats

- **0700** — Storm forecast: peak at El Dorado on Day 9 ~1700 (currently a 24-hour warning). Operations continue.
- **0900** — Soji comms the protagonist (he's still at El Dorado or Ibẹrẹ, depending on Day 7 movement). Casual tone. *"My dear, I would value a conversation when you're back at Ibẹrẹ. About the report. Nothing formal."* Translation: she wants to know what he's going to file. The conversation if it happens later includes a hint about AHI's appreciation for "responsible reporting."
- **1100** — Cortez comms. Asks for a final meeting before the protagonist leaves El Dorado. Direct. *"I'd like to know what you're going to write. I'd also like to make a case."* He'll lay out ADW's terraforming-funding argument. He is honest: he wants the report to favor ADW.
- **1300** — Yusra Boluwarin-Ali, on Mooch's instructions, sends a comm to the protagonist requesting a meeting at the Azorana on Day 9 evening. *"Coordinator Asarat-Garai believes a conversation before your filing would be productive."* The TGCC offer.
- **1500** — Aanya, if the protagonist has not yet seen her data in person, makes it explicit: *"I want to show you what I have. Today or tomorrow. After that I don't know what I'll do."* She is, herself, approaching a decision.
- **1700** — Eske follow-up if Day 7 meeting happened: *"Come back to the lab tomorrow. I have something else to share."* (This is Eske's escalation: she will share the partial Halverson leak fragments, not just describe them.)
- **2000** — End of formal day. Storm building. The protagonist sleeps wherever he is.

### What's off-screen

The seed coalition has independently decided this is the week. Soji's Q4 closes Day 11; she needs the protagonist's report to either land favorably or land neutrally. Cortez has been authorized by ADW senior to make a personal pitch. Mooch has decided TGCC needs to know what the protagonist has before he files. Kyei has heard about Eske's letter and is processing it; she does not yet know whether to act.

### Decision points

The day's structure is itself a decision matrix. The protagonist accepts, declines, or hedges three offers and one Aanya conversation. His responses signal his trajectory. The three corp offers are not binary — declining is a signal too.

DEPTH 3 should be solid by end of Day 8 for any active player.
