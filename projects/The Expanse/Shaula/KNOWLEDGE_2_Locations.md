# KNOWLEDGE_2 — Locations

**Project:** `shaula`
**Spoiler weight:** Mild. Surface geography. Trespass affordances flagged but not load-bearing.
**Audience:** The narrator. Scene-setting reference.

---

## What this file is

The setting's geography. Physical and sensory. Per template Section 2.4: lived spaces, not architectural diagrams. Where the food is, where the beds are, what the air is like, who is there at what hour, what counts as transgression to enter.

Each major space has a named identifier (used in the STATE block's `location` field), one or two sensory anchors, standard routines, access notes, and any narrative function the space carries.

The natural tunnel network gets extended treatment because it is the protagonist's professional domain — a microbiologist's wilderness — and has named zones the narrator should reach for.

---

## 1. Travel and time sense

The protagonist does not travel between solar systems within the arc — the gate transit happens on Day 1 and only once. After that, his movement is between three terrestrial sites and one orbital station, plus the Spike's perimeter and the natural tunnels.

**Approximate transit times:**

- Sol gate → Shaula gate: ~3 hours via the Ring Network's slow zone (the standard transit experience; the protagonist has done it twice in his life including this arrival).
- Shaula gate → orbital approach to the Azorana around Lesath: ~5 hours under standard burn.
- The Azorana → surface (Ibẹrẹ landing pad): ~90 minutes via shuttle, storm-gated. If a storm is building, the window collapses and shuttles are grounded.
- Ibẹrẹ → El Dorado: ~6 hours via short-range surface shuttle (suborbital hop). 1 hour by tunnel transit *if* the natural tunnel network connecting the two continents were open and safe — which it isn't.
- Ibẹrẹ ↔ the Spike's exclusion zone perimeter: ~2 hours surface shuttle.
- El Dorado ↔ the Spike's exclusion zone perimeter: ~3 hours surface shuttle.
- Kerent Lab is located ~40 km from Ibẹrẹ in the Iji range foothills. ~1 hour ground vehicle on a maintained road.

**The shuttle pilot is Leksi.** When the protagonist moves between ground sites, Leksi flies him. This is on-screen time with the Engineer that the narrator should use.

**Storm windows.** Ionized superstorms from the Spike's modulation sweep continents. The Day 4 storm peaks at the Iji range (Ibẹrẹ); the Day 9 storm peaks at the eastern continent (El Dorado). When a storm window closes, surface travel is grounded and underground access is the only option. The Captain knows the storm forecasts to within 90 minutes; corporate forecasts are accurate to within 30. The schedule of storm windows is a clock the protagonist learns to read.

## 2. Medina Station (Day 0 only)

The protagonist's only on-screen time on Medina is the Day 0 layover before the gate transit to Shaula. He arrives via Sol gate transfer from a Tycho-side liner; the *Tessek's Promise* is in dock at Medina's commercial-tier berths. He boards the ship that evening.

### 2.1 The Berth — Medina Commercial Tier 4

`location: medina_berth_t4`

Standard ship-berth corridor, recycled air, the soft hum of the station's spin engines under everything. The Promise is in slip 4-Echo-3, a working-class slot among similar ships — independent freighters, charter haulers, a handful of corporate shuttles waiting for senior personnel. The corridor smells of warm metal, food from a dozen kitchens, and the faint sweetness of the station's water-recycler signature that everyone notices for an hour and then forgets.

**Sensory anchor:** The light is yellow-warmer than Earth fluorescents, the spin-gravity is ~0.6 g, footsteps echo just slightly off-rhythm because the floor is curved and the wall isn't. The protagonist's first beyond-Sol step. He notices.

**Who's here:** Dock workers (mostly Belter-coded), a few SMC representatives waiting for their shuttle, the Captain at the airlock with her hex bolt rolling. She has been here for about an hour, watching for him.

**Access:** Public. Dock badges required for the slip itself.

### 2.2 The Souk

`location: medina_souk`

The Souk is the social heart of Medina — a sprawling, vibrantly reconfigured market originally designed by the Mormons as a community center. By 2353 it is dozens of small businesses: bars, coffee shops, restaurants, tea houses, gambling parlors, hookah lounges, weapons traders (discreet), bioscience supply shops (less discreet), bookbinders, music kiosks, drone repair, a Belter Methodist mission hall, two competing Mormon historical centers, a Sufi reading room, *The Q* (the only barbecue restaurant beyond the Sol gate, run by an old Belter named Harry with Earth-grandfather recipes).

**Sensory anchors:** Smell-stack — barbecue from The Q, kebabs from three different vendors, hookah smoke, the iron-water smell of recycled-from-mining-runoff station water, fried dough, beer. Sound-stack — three languages overlapping (Lang Belta, English, Spanish), live music from at least one of the venues at any hour, the constant low rumble of dock work two decks down.

**Who's here:** Everyone in transit through Medina. Crews on layover. A handful of journalists (Sanga Velazquez has been seen here, if the protagonist is paying attention). UN observers. OPA-adjacent contacts who do not advertise. The protagonist could spend the whole Day 0 in the Souk and not see the same face twice.

**Access:** Public. Some of the back rooms are not.

**Function in arc:** Day 0's only on-screen Medina scene. The cold open uses it. After Day 0, Medina becomes off-screen — a place rumor comes from, where the *Soji-Bee* runs to.

## 3. The *Tessek's Promise*

The crew's home for the arc. Five cabins, a galley/mess, the bridge, engineering, the medical bay, a small cargo hold modified for survey gear, and the airlock with EVA prep. Total interior volume is small enough that voices carry; the crew has learned to lower theirs.

**General sensory:** The ship smells of recycled air with overlays — Aanya's cardamom tea (constant, faint, from the medical bay), the metal-grease smell from Engineering when Leksi is working, the trace of MCRN-issue cleaning solvent that Kit uses on her quarters because the smell is familiar. The sound under everything is the ship's life-support hum — different from the Spike's hum, lower and more rhythmic.

### 3.1 Bridge

`location: ship_bridge`

The Captain's domain, but small enough that everyone can fit if they have to. Two stations (helm and operations), the Captain's chair, a small chart table that doubles as a comms station. Ports forward and to either side. The view is whatever's outside.

**Sensory anchor:** The Captain's hex bolt rolling across the console when she is thinking; the sound is a small click-click-click against the metal.

**Who's here:** The Captain, always when underway. Leksi at the helm during burns. Kit at operations during anything risky. Aanya and the protagonist are guests here; they ask before entering during a maneuver.

**Access:** Crew. The protagonist is welcome but is expected to stand back during the Captain's calls.

### 3.2 Engineering

`location: ship_engineering`

Leksi's domain. Power core access, life support controls, the ship's spine of cables and pipes. Cluttered in the way that means nothing is missing — every tool has a place; the place is wherever Leksi last set it down. A small radio plays Belter dock-songs at low volume.

**Sensory anchor:** Solder smell, ozone, the constant low hum of the core, Leksi humming a counter-melody to whatever's playing.

**Who's here:** Leksi, most of the time. The Captain at least once a day. The protagonist if he asks Leksi questions; she will give him a working tour by the third day if he expresses curiosity.

**Access:** Crew. Leksi will let the protagonist help with small things if he asks competently.

### 3.3 Medical Bay

`location: ship_medbay`

Aanya's domain. Smaller than a corporate clinic, larger than a closet. One examination table, a shielded chemistry deck, a centrifuge, a sample storage unit (refrigerated and cryo), two locked cabinets. A small tea station with cardamom, ginger, black tea, and a kettle she boils once a day.

**Sensory anchor:** Cardamom tea steam, the soft chime of the chemistry deck completing a cycle, the absolute organization of every surface.

**Who's here:** Aanya. The protagonist is welcome here whenever she is — it's the natural meeting point for the two of them professionally. Most of the substrate-relevant DEPTH 1–2 conversations between them happen here.

**Access:** Aanya's domain. She does not gate it formally, but the door is closed when she is processing samples and that is a do-not-knock signal.

### 3.4 Galley / Mess

`location: ship_galley`

The communal heart of the ship. Three-meter table, four chairs and two collapsible benches. A small kitchen with an induction surface, a microwave-equivalent, a water dispenser, a chiller, a snack drawer. Coffee station. Tea station. A worn deck of cards (Conde — Kit's, originally Mateo's, never said).

**Sensory anchor:** Coffee at 0600. Whatever Aanya has cooked at 1900. Kit shuffling cards in the late-night quiet.

**Who's here:** Anyone. Meal times — 0700, 1300, 1900 — bring the crew together by default. Off-meal hours are for whoever needs the space.

**Access:** Public to crew. The protagonist is here often; this is where the most natural cross-crew conversations happen.

### 3.5 Crew Bunks (×5)

`location: ship_bunks` (with `:protagonist`, `:captain`, `:leksi`, `:aanya`, `:kit` modifiers)

Five small cabins along a short corridor. Each is roughly 2m × 3m, a bunk built into the wall, a small desk, a locker, a personal screen. The Captain's is slightly larger and has a separate door to the bridge corridor.

**The protagonist's bunk:** Issued. Spare. Survey Corps standard kit. He arrives with one duffel.

**Sensory anchor for the corridor:** Five doors, all slightly different — Leksi's has two small Lang Belta phrases written in marker on the upper corner (*"keep beratna safe"* and a date); Kit's has nothing on it; Aanya's has a small hand-painted lotus from Hyderabad; the Captain's has the OPA split-circle, faded; the protagonist's is blank.

**Access:** Each crew member's cabin is private. Knocking is the rule.

### 3.6 Cargo Hold (modified)

`location: ship_cargo`

Half the original cargo capacity has been converted to a survey work area: sample storage (temperature-controlled), a workbench, a chemistry-deck mount, racks for the protagonist's drones and field equipment, secure crates for the corporate-issue gear he carries down from the SMC briefing.

**Sensory anchor:** Cooler air than the rest of the ship (the sample storage runs at 4°C and the room compensates), the smell of labeled plastics and sterile field bags.

**Who's here:** The protagonist primarily. Aanya joins him here when their work overlaps. Leksi visits to make sure his drones are charging properly.

**Access:** Crew, with the work bench understood as the protagonist's.

### 3.7 Airlock and EVA Prep

`location: ship_airlock`

Standard double-airlock, EVA suit racks for six, helmet storage, a small bench. The suits are color-coded by crew member; the protagonist's is the newest (issued for this contract) and feels stiff in the joints.

**Sensory anchor:** The soft hiss of the inner door's seal, the deeper cycle of the outer; the smell of suit interior — nylon, sweat, the lemon-cleaner Kit insists on after every use.

**Access:** Crew. The Captain checks the protagonist's suit for him on Day 1 — a quiet, professional gesture that he should notice.

### 3.8 Crew Quarters Off-Ship

The four crewmembers do not stay aboard the *Tessek's Promise* for the duration of the rotation. The ship is a base, not a habitat. Each crewmember has working reasons to travel with the protagonist or to the same ground sites. The narrator should render their presence as the operational default, not as exceptions earned by request.

**At AHI (Ibẹrẹ), Days 3–5:**

- **Aanya.** AHI's medical wing has a guest-credential desk for visiting medical professionals on charter rotations. The wing is well-equipped but small; visiting MDs with research credentials are welcomed for cross-credential consultations and for sample-rich access to the underground biome that AHI's site permits restrict to medical staff. Aanya has come to AHI on prior rotations and is known to Margaret Hwang-Adelakun and to Femi by sight. She has a small reserved bench in Margaret's lab that she uses when she's on-site. She visits AHI for a working day on every rotation, minimum. On this rotation she is on-site at minimum on Day 4 (storm-peak medical readiness) and likely Day 5.
- **Kit.** Charter security protocol places ship's security on-site for the contractor's first deep-tunnel descent and for any storm-peak day. Kit is at AHI on Day 3 (deep-tunnel briefing with Femi's crew) and Day 4 (storm peak; protocol mandatory). Day 5 is at her discretion; she usually returns to the Promise but can stay if Caleb requests, or if she finds a Femi-crew member she wants to keep working with.
- **Leksi.** AHI's surface mining-comms infrastructure is run by a TGCC-AHI joint engineering team that Leksi knows two members of personally. She finds reasons to come down at least once per rotation to look at AHI's systems and to drink with the joint-team in their break room. On this rotation she is on-site at least Day 4 afternoon (Promise stays on the AHI pad through the storm; she's already there) and may stay over.
- **Naima.** AHI is where the loan that holds the Promise was originated through TGCC's Pallas office — Soji and Naima have a working relationship that pre-dates this rotation by two years. Naima visits Soji's office at least once per rotation for an in-person check-in. On this rotation, Day 3 evening dinner at Soji's office is the canonical default; Day 5 is also possible. Naima does not stay overnight; she returns to the Promise.

**At El Dorado (ADW), Days 6–9:**

- **Aanya.** ADW's pavilion has Mars-imported wood, a real coffee culture, and Tariq Halász-Cortez's geological-survey wing — three things that bring Aanya down for at least one afternoon. Day 7 afternoon is canonical; Day 9 storm-recovery may also bring her. She is welcomed by Vargas at the pavilion as a known visiting MD; she does not seek out Cortez personally.
- **Kit.** ADW's complex is cliff-face surface, with civic-grade security but also Vargas — who Kit has not seen in eight years and who is not yet aware she is at Shaula. The first time they meet on-site is a real beat (KNOWLEDGE_8, Vargas reveal triggers). Kit accompanies Caleb on Day 6 arrival as standard security advance; whether she stays for Day 7+ depends on whether the Vargas encounter has happened and how it landed.
- **Leksi.** ADW's pavilion serves a local fermented Belter bean-pulse that Leksi has heard about and never tried; this is the smallest reason and is sufficient. Day 7 or Day 8 evening, Leksi finds her way down. She does not stay overnight.
- **Naima.** Naima does not visit ADW. She has reasons (she does not like Mars, ADW knows it, and the Cortez–Tessek register is professional-cool) and the reasons hold across rotations. She comms Cortez but does not visit El Dorado in person except in genuine emergency.

**At the Azorana, Days 1–2 and Day 10:**

- **Naima.** Azorana is where Naima conducts ship's business with Mooch in person; she is on the Azorana whenever the Promise is docked and frequently when it is not. Day 1 afternoon, Day 2 throughout, Day 10 throughout — Naima is where Mooch is.
- **Leksi.** The Azorana's *Sasa Bar* is Leksi's primary social space outside the Promise. She is there on Days 1 and 2 evenings as standard, and Day 10 if she's not on engineering prep for departure.
- **Aanya.** The Azorana's TGCC archive contains medical-research filings she has access to via her UN-charter biological research permit. She visits the archive's research-records wing on Day 2 and Day 10. She also visits Sasa Bar occasionally with Leksi.
- **Kit.** Kit's job in any large station is to know the layout. The Azorana has ten thousand people and Kit will spend Days 1–2 walking the corridors, eating at the itinerant berths' galley, and reporting back to Naima with what she has seen. She is not warm, but she is present.

**At Kerent Lab, Day 7 (if permitted):** Aanya knows Eske Kerent professionally; the meeting on this rotation is consequential per KNOWLEDGE_8. Aanya goes with Caleb to Kerent Lab. The other crew do not — Kerent Lab is a four-person research outpost; arriving with security is impolite, arriving with engineering is unnecessary, arriving with the Captain is corporate-pressure-coded. Aanya is the only crewmember with a working reason.

**At the Spike perimeter, Day 8:** Kit (UN observation-flight protocols require accompanying ship-security on any contractor flight crossing the 3 km exclusion perimeter), Aanya (UN flights carry medical personnel by canon), and Leksi (flies the shuttle) all go. Naima does not — the flight is the Charter Observer's; the Captain's role is administrative.

**Standing presence at the Promise.** When the Promise is in low orbit and Caleb is at a ground site, at least one crew member is on the Promise at all times for ship-monitoring purposes. The default rotation is: Aanya is the standing presence in the medbay during her ground-side off-hours; Leksi is the standing presence in engineering during ground-side runs she is not on; Kit when she is not deployed; Naima rarely, because she is usually in motion. This is operational hygiene. It is not a justification for the entire crew remaining aboard. The crew rotates ground-side and ship-side with regularity.

**Comms presence.** When a crewmember is not physically with Caleb, they are reachable on the comm unit Olubadewo issued. The narrator surfaces comm contact as natural texture across the rotation: Aanya checks Caleb's vitals at evenings he's not on-site with her, minimum every other night; Leksi comms about the chemistry deck or whatever she is curious about that day; Kit checks in once per day on security posture, terse, in the security-three-sentences register; Naima comms once a day at minimum, operational, though the operational frame can carry warmth. Comm contact is not a substitute for in-person presence; it is the connective tissue between in-person beats.

**Evenings on the Promise are crew time.** When Caleb is at AHI or ADW, he is at their messes; when he is on the Promise (Days 1, 2, 10, plus shuttle days, plus any night he chooses to return rather than stay at guest bunks), evenings are crew dinners by default. Naima cooks Mon/Wed/Sat; Aanya cooks Tue/Fri; Leksi cooks Thu and any time she's hungry; Kit when nobody's looking. A protagonist who chooses to return to the Promise for the evening rather than stay at AHI or ADW guest bunks does so without friction. The corporate sites prefer their guests to bunk on-site; they do not require it.

## 4. The Azorana — TGCC's flotilla flagship

A converted 60-year-old ice-hauler, ~800 m long, ~100 m wide at the beam, in geosynchronous orbit around Lesath. Originally built to drag iceteroids from the Saturnian rings to Mars; converted by TGCC after the Belt's purchase of distressed UN assets in 2348. Now houses TGCC's Shaula operations, ~600 staff, and a constant rotation of itinerant Belter labor that doubles the population on busy weeks.

The Azorana is the protagonist's first arrival point on the Day 1 orbital approach. The Day 2 SMC briefing happens here.

### 4.1 The Approach and Dock

`location: azorana_dock`

The exterior of the Azorana is a working ship that stopped working: ice-grippers retracted but not removed, drag-tow rigging still bolted to the hull because removing it would cost more than leaving it. TGCC has painted the upper third in charcoal-and-gold, leaving the lower hull in the original UN gray. From space it looks like a decommissioned thing inhabited.

**Sensory anchor on docking:** The dock cycle is rougher than corporate-quality (TGCC saves on dampers); the airlock smells of welding from a recent repair; the light is warmer than Inner-corporate (Belter preference, lower Kelvin).

**Who's here:** Dockworkers (Belter, Lang Belta dominant). A TGCC desk attendant who checks credentials and is always Hesh Mahesh-Yves's cousin. Sometimes Mooch is on the dock; sometimes Aleyn from the *Soji-Bee* is, if his ship is in.

**Access:** Public to anyone with TGCC clearance. SMC contracts grant clearance.

### 4.2 The Bridge / Command Level

`location: azorana_command`

Mooch's domain. Three holographic vote-tally displays dominate the back wall, each tracking different SMC voting cycles in real time. Mooch's office is off the main command floor; her door is rarely closed but the implication of attention is constant.

**Sensory anchor:** The displays' soft blue-orange light against the matte black of the command room walls; the absolute quiet (Mooch demands it).

**Who's here:** Mooch, almost always. Her senior staff — three TGCC officers whose names the protagonist will not learn unless he engages at *Business* tier with TGCC.

**Access:** SMC charter business only. The protagonist enters here for the Day 2 briefing.

### 4.3 The Sasa Bar (Recreation Bay 4)

`location: azorana_sasa_bar`

The unofficial bar, two decks down from command. Tomi "Tee" Borisova's domain. Large enough for forty people seated, sixty if they don't mind standing. Bar along one wall, mismatched tables and chairs throughout, a small stage that gets used about twice a week for live music. OPA news streams on a screen at low volume. Lang Belta is the dominant language; standard English is heard but not the default.

**Sensory anchors:** Belter beer — slightly metallic, slightly bitter, brewed in a TGCC cooperative recycling tank that Belters trust and Inners do not. The constant overlap of conversations in two registers. Tee's signature greeting *"Sasa ke?"* every time someone new walks in (the joke and the actual greeting at once).

**Who's here:** Itinerant Belter crews on layover. TGCC junior staff. Hesh Mahesh-Yves is here ~3 evenings a week. Pavel Adamec drinks here twice a week (he is the only regular Inner). Tee is always behind the bar.

**Access:** Public. Inners are tolerated; tipping correctly and not ordering "lite" beer is the entry requirement to be welcomed beyond once.

**Function in arc:** Primary Belter social space. The drinking-buddies tier with Hesh, with Pavel, and with the regulars happens here.

### 4.4 The TGCC Archive

`location: azorana_archive`

A small, well-organized records room two levels below command. Mooch's vote-record ledger lives here, in a sealed datacore — more honest than the official charter minutes filed with the UN. Includes prices paid in every quarter's votes since Q1 of the charter.

**Sensory anchor:** Cool air (the datacore is climate-controlled), absolutely silent except for the hum of the cores; a single soft amber light overhead.

**Who's here:** Restricted. A TGCC archivist named Yusra Boluwarin-Ali (the romance pool TGCC tech) has access. She enters once a day to verify integrity. Mooch enters when she needs to consult.

**Access:** TGCC senior only, which the protagonist is not. **DEPTH 4 trespass affordance.** Entry without authorization requires either Yusra's collusion (a relationship investment) or a security workaround (Kit can advise).

### 4.5 The Itinerant Berths

`location: azorana_itinerant_berths`

The Azorana's outer ring docking slips, where transient ships tie up. The *Soji-Bee* is here when in port. A handful of independent Belter haulers, occasional UN-charter ships. Less formal than the main dock — closer to a dock-town than an entry point.

**Sensory anchor:** The smell of working ships — fuel, hot metal, food cooking in galleys with the airlocks open. Music from somewhere; the *Soji-Bee* plays Belter-dialect Mormon hymns at evening services.

**Who's here:** Captains and their crews. Aleyn Gboro-Aoki and Soolie when the *Soji-Bee* is in. Various transient faces. Hesh Mahesh-Yves's ship, when he's in.

**Access:** Anyone who has business there. The protagonist finds his way here naturally if he's looking to talk to Aleyn.

## 5. Ibẹrẹ — AHI subterranean complex

AHI's ground operation, located in the Iji mountain range on the northernmost continent. The complex extends ~900 m underground from the surface entry, a series of administrative levels, then operations levels, then mining levels, with a separate horizontal medical and residential wing. ~1,400 AHI staff resident.

The protagonist arrives Day 3 for his Ibẹrẹ rotation.

### 5.1 The Surface Approach

`location: ibere_surface`

A heavily reinforced landing pad cut into the mountainside, surrounded by storm shutters that close in 90 seconds when a storm is incoming. The Iji range is grey-brown rock, sparse high-altitude vegetation, an occasional flash of bioluminescence at the tunnel mouths even in daylight (the underground biome leaks).

**Sensory anchors:** The 1.6 g hits like a weight on the chest — the protagonist has been in 1g on the ship; stepping out is a different body. The smell of ozone before a storm; the high-altitude cold (the Iji surface temperature is below freezing for two-thirds of the day); the wind starts as a low whistle and builds.

**Who's here:** Landing crew, storm wardens, occasional senior AHI on outbound transit. Soji is rarely here; she sends a deputy.

**Access:** AHI staff and contracted personnel. SMC credentials work.

### 5.2 The Descent — Main Elevator

`location: ibere_elevator`

A heavy industrial elevator, 4-minute ride from surface to administrative levels. Every staff member rides this twice a day; the elevator is a place where casual conversations happen because nobody can leave. Femi Adelakun the shift supervisor rides at 0530 and 1630.

**Sensory anchor:** The faint Spike-hum gets louder as the elevator descends. A trained ear can hear the modulation pattern.

**Who's here:** AHI staff in rotation. The protagonist will share a ride with Soji at least once if he times it right, and with Femi at least once if he doesn't.

**Access:** Public to staff. Storm-locked when the surface is closed.

### 5.3 The Administrative Levels

`location: ibere_admin`

Three levels of offices, conference rooms, the SMC briefing room, the AHI operations center. Carpeted (a small luxury), warmer-lit than the operations levels, smelling faintly of the cardamom tea Soji prefers and the printed paper she insists on (printed paper is a corporate-Earth signal of seniority — the smell is wealth).

**Sensory anchor:** Paper. Real paper. Soji's office in particular has a stack of physical reports on her desk.

**Who's here:** Senior AHI staff during business hours (0800–1800 standard). Soji works late often. The Mess (her unofficial after-hours space) is a sub-level here.

**Access:** AHI staff and credentialed visitors. Soji's office is by appointment.

### 5.4 Soji's Office

`location: ibere_soji_office`

A corner office on Admin Level 2, with a small window onto the underground complex's central atrium (a 30 m vertical shaft with a skylight at the surface — the only natural light in Ibẹrẹ). Bookshelves with actual books (all paper-bound Earth imports — another wealth signal). A small framed photo of Funmi at her secondary-school graduation. A second framed photo, smaller, on her desk: a baby in a Lagos clinic. The baby is Yusuf. Most visitors do not notice the second photo.

**Sensory anchor:** The skylight's filtered amber afternoon light falling across the desk. The smell of paper and tea.

**Who's here:** Soji, by default. Her assistant (Femi's wife Margaret occasionally — they overlap socially). Senior AHI visitors.

**Access:** By appointment for SMC charter business. **DEPTH 4 trespass affordance:** Soji's sealed quarterly reports are in a locked credenza behind her desk. She is in this office ~14 hours a day; entering when she is not requires either a precise window (her weekly briefing on Wednesdays at 1500, when she is unavailable for two hours) or a relationship with her assistant.

### 5.5 Operations Levels (Mining)

`location: ibere_ops` (with `:north`, `:south`, `:central`, `:deep` modifiers)

Five operations levels descending another ~600 m below admin. North network (older, established, lower yield), south network (newer, higher yield, *the 'strictor incident* on Day 4 happens here), central (admin overlap), deep (most recent, ~1,200 m below surface).

**Sensory anchors:** Mineral dust in the air despite ventilation, sweat smell from the working crews, the Spike-hum at full underground strength (audible everywhere now). Yellow safety lighting. The crews' helmet lamps pattern the rock.

**Who's here:** Mining crews on three shifts (0500–1300, 1300–2100, 2100–0500). Shift supervisors. AHI safety officers. Femi Adelakun supervises the south network.

**Access:** AHI mining clearance. The protagonist's surveyor credentials grant access to all levels for survey purposes. He should be escorted; in practice, he is not, often.

### 5.6 The Mess

`location: ibere_mess`

The unofficial junior-staff bar on Admin sub-level 1. Smaller than Sasa Bar, more corporate (chairs match), a beer selection skewed Earth-import. Margaret Hwang-Adelakun drinks here twice a week; Femi joins her once a week. The protagonist's path to Yusuf reveal goes through this room.

**Sensory anchor:** Earth lager (real Belgian malt, expensive, AHI-imported), the slightly stiff atmosphere of staff drinking on the company's premises but trying to relax.

**Who's here:** AHI junior and mid-level staff. Margaret, twice a week. Tariq Halász-Cortez (the ADW geological surveyor) when he is at Ibẹrẹ for inter-corp meetings — he has friends at this bar.

**Access:** AHI staff and credentialed contractors. The protagonist's badge gets him in.

### 5.7 The Medical Wing

`location: ibere_medical`

Margaret Hwang-Adelakun's domain. Standard AHI corporate clinic — well-equipped, professionally staffed, the medical bay where 'strictor incident victims are treated. Quieter than the rest of the complex.

**Sensory anchor:** The faint disinfectant smell, Margaret's perfume (a lily oil from a Lagos perfumer her mother used — detail Margaret reveals if she warms).

**Who's here:** Margaret and her two staff. Patients on rotation. Aanya may visit if a case crosses her interest.

**Access:** AHI medical staff and patients. Aanya can credentials-share for professional consultations.

## 6. El Dorado — ADW cliff-face complex

ADW's ground operation, built into a massive cliff face on the coast of the eastern continent (~6,000 km from Ibẹrẹ). The complex is an inverted high-rise — six "floors" cut horizontally into the cliff face, with the upper levels closest to the surface and the lower levels accessible only via internal lifts. ~900 ADW staff resident.

El Dorado is more aesthetically Mariner-Valley than Ibẹrẹ is corporate-Lagos. The protagonist arrives Day 6.

### 6.1 The Cliff-Face Approach

`location: el_dorado_approach`

A landing platform built out from the cliff at the upper level, with a glass-walled receiving area inside. The cliff face is dramatic — a 400 m drop to the ocean below, the ocean a deep blue-green from the underground biome's microbiome leak, sea spray reaching the platform on rough days.

**Sensory anchors:** The view (the only place on Shaula where the sky is always visible — the eastern continent has fewer storms than the Iji range), the salt-spray smell, the wind.

**Who's here:** Landing crew. Beni Vargas is rarely here; he runs the pavilion two levels down.

**Access:** ADW staff and credentialed visitors. SMC credentials work.

### 6.2 The Pavilion

`location: el_dorado_pavilion`

ADW's social heart, two levels below the surface entry. ## 9. The Natural Tunnel Network

The underground biome — the Spike's purpose. Vast, interconnected, mostly unmapped. The protagonist's professional domain. Five named zones the narrator should reach for, plus a general framework.

### 9.1 Framework

The natural tunnel network is distinct from the AHI/ADW mining shafts. The natural tunnels were not dug by miners; they are part of the planet's deep structure, shaped over a billion-plus years by the Spike's regulation, the breaching moles' burrowing, and the underground rivers' carving. They range from human-passable (~2 m diameter) to the dozens-of-meters cathedrals where the major grottos open.

**General sensory:** Humid air (the underground biome maintains its own moisture cycle). The faint blue-green glow of bioluminescence visible in the deeper zones — patterns that move. The Spike-hum at full underground strength, modulating with the storm cycle. Water sound: drips, runs, occasional underground waterfalls. The smell of mineral water and the slightly sweet ozone of ion-laden air after a storm.

**The 'strictor presence:** 'Strictors range across the natural tunnels; their territorial behavior is well-documented and predictable. They hunt by vibration. Heavy, regular footsteps will sometimes attract; light steps and pauses will not. The mining shafts have *displaced* 'strictors into territories they did not previously inhabit, which is the source of recent incidents.

### 9.2 The Eight-Branch Grotto

`location: tunnels_eight_branch`

A major natural cave system ~600 m below the surface, named for the eight tunnel branches that converge at it. One of the richer biome zones — the bioluminescent species are densest here. Underground river runs through the grotto's lower level. ~3 km from Ibẹrẹ's deep-mining levels via a connecting natural passage.

**Sensory anchor:** The bioluminescence. Walls and ceiling lit by patches of slow-moving blue-green light, patterns that shift over minutes. Water sound from the river below. The smell of ozone is strong here — the storm-driven ions reach this grotto via the natural ventilation network.

**Function:** The protagonist's primary natural sampling site. Aanya has been here twice. Eske Kerent's lab samples here on permit. The bioluminescent light-language is observable here at richest density.

### 9.3 The Verdant Pool

`location: tunnels_verdant_pool`

A still underground pool ~100 m across, fed by the Eight-Branch Grotto's river, ~1 km further downstream. The water is rich with the bioluminescent algae-analog; the surface glows faintly even in pitch black. The pool is a major reproductive zone for several of the underground species.

**Sensory anchor:** The water glows. The protagonist's headlamp is unnecessary here. The reflection of the bioluminescence on the cave ceiling is one of the most beautiful things on Shaula.

**Function:** A reverence space. The protagonist can sample here; he can also simply sit. If Aanya is with him, she will take her hair out of the knot.

### 9.4 The South Tunnel Network (the 'strictor territory)

`location: tunnels_south_network`

The newer AHI mining shafts on the south side of Ibẹrẹ have intersected an established 'strictor territory. The 'strictor incident on Day 4 happens at the boundary between the new shaft and the natural network. Femi Adelakun is the shift supervisor; he files the report.

**Sensory anchor:** Vibration awareness. The 'strictors hunt by vibration; the protagonist learns, from Femi, to walk light and pause often. The tension of the space is real.

**Function:** Day 4 scripted event location. Also a recurring zone if the protagonist returns to investigate the displacement pattern.

### 9.5 The Breaching Mole Field

`location: tunnels_mole_field`

A surface site on the Iji range plateau where the breaching moles' tunnels emerge — visible as a field of small holes and disturbed earth, ~2 km across. The moles' surface activity is most visible at dawn and dusk; the mole-tunnel network underneath connects to the Eight-Branch and other deep zones.

**Sensory anchor:** At dawn, the moles can be seen breaching in synchronized patterns — small grey-brown bodies emerging, snouts upward, then retreating. The patterns are coordinated; biology training reveals the coordination.

**Function:** The protagonist's evidence path for the ion-distribution network. The moles are part of the Spike's regulation. Observing the synchronization reveals it.

### 9.6 The Halverson Sample Site

`location: tunnels_halverson_site`

A natural grotto ~4 km from Kerent Lab, ~400 m below the surface, where the Halverson team did their most extensive sampling two years ago. Now within Kerent Lab's permitted sampling scope. Marked by old equipment racks the Halverson team left behind, unrecovered.

**Sensory anchor:** The abandoned equipment. Two-year-old field markings, faded. A small handwritten note on a rack — *"Site H-7, 2351-04, sample series K"* — Halverson's handwriting.

**Function:** A path to the Halverson question through physical evidence. Eske can bring the protagonist here; Pavel can. The note is a real artifact.
