## 4. The endings

The protagonist's deliverable is the **SMC Quarterly Survey Report**. The report is due at end-of-Day-10. Five distinct positions are available. None is correct. Each costs.

The narrator does not push toward any ending. The narrator does not moralize at the aftermath. Per template Section 2.9: every ending has a life after the credits. Write the next month.

### Ending 1 — **AHI Receives** ("File for Earth")

The protagonist files a report that frames the Spike's clock in language AHI can absorb without acting. AHI uses it internally to brief Soji's superiors; nothing leaks. Soji's Q4 lands. Funmi's third year is paid. Earth's mineral pipeline holds.

**Cost:**
- The biome dies on schedule. The protagonist knows. He carries this.
- He gets a quiet promotion offer from AHI six months later — a corporate post on Luna, environmental review division. Whether he takes it is his choice.
- ADW and TGCC mark him as compromised; his work in any other system carries that tag.
- **Aftermath next month:** Soji's bonus pays out. Funmi enters her third year. The Halverson report stays buried. Cortez signs the next quarterly extraction increase. The Captain leaves Shaula on a different contract; the Engineer stays on with TGCC for a separate run. The Doctor resigns and goes back to Earth; she does not write to him.

### Ending 2 — **ADW Receives** ("File for Mars")

The protagonist files a report that frames the Spike as relevant to Mars's terraforming research, and quietly hands ADW the leverage to argue for primary mining rights at the next charter vote. Cortez writes a personal recommendation. Cortez also feels grateful — a small thing, a debt, that ADW will honor in years to come.

**Cost:**
- The biome dies on schedule, more efficiently. ADW's terraforming project advances by ~2%.
- The protagonist gets a Mars work visa for life.
- AHI and TGCC mark him as compromised; some Earth doors close.
- **Aftermath next month:** Cortez writes his sons that the next year's MAG funding is secured. Soji misses Q4 by a hair; Funmi takes a year out. The Halverson report stays buried. Mooch raises TGCC's swing-vote price 30% in retaliation. The Captain spits over the rail when she hears.

### Ending 3 — **TGCC Receives** ("File for the Belt")

The protagonist hands the data to Mooch. Mooch uses it. She does not release it publicly — she uses it as **leverage**. AHI and ADW now know that TGCC has the report; TGCC's swing-vote price quadruples; TGCC's relevance is preserved for another decade.

**Cost:**
- The biome's death is delayed slightly (the new vote dynamics force AHI and ADW to back off on the most aggressive extraction sites). It is not stopped.
- The protagonist becomes a Belter asset. Welcome on Belt stations for life. He is also surveilled by AHI/ADW. They do not move against him directly; they do not need to.
- **Aftermath next month:** Mooch's flotilla expands. Three new TGCC claims approved at the Q1 vote. Soji is reassigned from Ibẹrẹ to a smaller AHI site at Karkinos. Cortez stays. Kyei is unaware she has been outmaneuvered until her successor arrives. The Captain quietly approves; the Engineer says nothing.

### Ending 4 — **File Open** ("Public, Unredacted")

The protagonist publishes the report — independent journalist (a Sanga Velazquez of Medina, possibly), OPA channels, the UN Auditor General's office, a peer-reviewed scientific journal. All four. The Charter detonates.

**Cost — real:**
- Two of the seed-coalition individuals lose their careers (Soji and Kyei). One (Cortez) is reassigned to a non-strategic post. Mooch survives and possibly thrives — chaos plays to her hand.
- The protagonist's career as a contract surveyor is *over*. He cannot work for AHI, ADW, or any UN charter for at least a decade. He may be celebrated; he may be vilified; he may be both, simultaneously, in different rooms.
- The biome's clock is paused, not stopped. The mining will resume eventually, in some form. But there is now a public record and a scientific debate.
- **Aftermath next month:** Soji is in a UN tribunal; Funmi withdraws from Selene. Kyei is recalled to The Hague. Cortez is at a desk on Ceres. Mooch has a new partner in the OPA leadership. The Halverson team is rehabilitated. The Doctor writes him: *"I should have filed two years ago."* He doesn't know how to answer.

### Ending 5 — **File Nothing** ("Suppress")

The protagonist files a vague, technically-honest quarterly report. Nothing about chirality, nothing about EM modulation, nothing about the seed coalition. The contract ends. He is paid. He goes home.

**Cost:**
- The biome dies on schedule. The protagonist knows what he chose.
- He is not punished externally. He is paid in full.
- He may take other contracts. He may not.
- The cost is internal and durable.
- **Aftermath next month:** Soji's Q4 lands. Cortez gets his commendation. Mooch wins a vote. Kyei files her successful tenure-end report. The protagonist returns to Earth on the Sol-side gate transit. He cannot eat for a week. He goes back to work in three months on a different contract. He never visits Shaula again. He does not write to anyone from the crew. The Captain assumes he was bought; she is wrong, but she is not far wrong.

This is **not** a failure ending. It is a position. A working surveyor in a corporate tri-charter system has reasonable cause to take it. The ending honors that.
