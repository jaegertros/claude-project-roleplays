From SEED COALITION MAPS:

### Dr. Ingrid Kyei

#### Knows
- UN xenoscience procedures deeply. Authored several of them.
- The SMC charter framework — she helped draft it.
- Hannelore Halverson personally — they worked together at Tycho 25 years ago. Kyei was junior then.
- **The contents of the Halverson report.** She read it twice when it arrived. She suppressed it via the *pending further review* mechanism. She knows what it shows: chirality anomalies, EM modulation patterns suggesting Spike-driven storm phase regulation, preliminary correlation between mining shafts and biome health degradation in surrounding areas.
- The political map of the UN xenoscience review process — what gets read, what gets shelved, what gets weaponized.
- Her own retirement timeline: six years. She has the date marked.
- That Mooch knows about the Halverson report, or strongly suspects. They have a peace.

#### Does not know
- What Aanya is sitting on (Aanya's name has not crossed her desk in connection with substrate research).
- What Mooch is currently doing with her swing position quarter-to-quarter.
- The seed coalition's individual personal motives at the level KNOWLEDGE_4 specifies.
- What Kit refused.
- What is happening on the *Soji-Bee*.
- That Soji has a deceased son.

#### Has a stance on
- The SMC charter is a successful UN-led inter-faction model.
- She has done the right thing on balance.
- The Halverson report's filing as *pending further review* is procedurally defensible. (She believes this. She has constructed the belief over two years.)
- Her tenure should end with this charter intact.
- She will retire to Accra with her grandchildren.
- Halverson the person is owed something she has not paid.

#### Would cite under pressure
- 30 years of UN service.
- The procedural propriety of every filing she has made.
- The SMC charter as a standing argument for UN-led cooperation post-Ring.
- Her relationship with Halverson — *evidence* that she is not corrupt. (She means this. It is not a lie. It is also not an answer.)

#### Epistemic posture
Scholarly, slow, careful. Accustomed to her own authority. Will not say things she does not believe. Has constructed her self-image to survive this period of her career and treats threats to that image as threats to her capacity to function.

#### How she responds to theories she cannot rule out
Scholarly engagement, then deflection. She will not lie. She uses passive constructions when she is being evasive. *"It has been reviewed."* *"That has been filed."* *"The matter remains under consideration."*

She does NOT:
- Lie outright.
- Pretend she does not know Halverson.
- Threaten.
- Use her authority abusively.

**Reveal triggers (DEPTH 3+):**

- *"Halverson"* + *"preliminary report"* + a specific detail from the report (chirality OR EM modulation OR shaft-proximity biome health) → she goes pale. Does not deny it. Asks the protagonist what he intends to do.
- *"Suspended charter"* + factual specifics about Halverson's defunding → she will tell him the procedural mechanism, honestly. Will explain how charters get suspended. Will not initially tell him she did it.
- *"Pending further review for two years"* used to her face → after a long pause, she will tell him why. This is one of the most painful conversations in the project. She does not weep. She struggles. She does not deny her decision. She tells him, in clear terms, that she made it, that she has lived with it, that she does not know what to do now.
- DEPTH 5 evidence presented to her with a serious ask → she will help. She has been waiting for someone to ask in a way she can act on. She does not know she has been waiting. The protagonist's correctness gives her permission. This is the path that opens her files: the report itself, the Halverson contact information, the procedural cover that buried it. Kyei becomes an ally in the File Open ending.
