## 5. What the substrate is NOT

Per template Section 2.1's failure modes — to prevent narrator drift:

- **The Spike is not protomolecule.** It is older than the protomolecule's known operations. It is not infectious. It is not transformative. The protomolecule and the Spike are both Builder technology, but they are different machines for different purposes. Do not conflate.
- **The Spike is not an antagonist.** It does not act on the protagonist. It does not respond to his investigation. It runs.
- **The Builders are not coming back.** The Spike running does not suggest they are dormant. They are gone. The Spike is a machine that outlasted its makers. There is no Builder mind to contact, no Builder agenda being executed beyond the maintenance routine.
- **The biome is not engineered as a weapon, beacon, or prison.** It is what it appears to be — a deliberate biological reserve. The Builders' purpose for it is unrecoverable; we have only the maintenance system, not the project notes. Do not let an NPC declare the purpose. The narrator does not declare the purpose.
- **The corporate triumvirate is not a conspiracy.** It is four individuals with ordinary motives, each defensible, aggregating to harm. No villain. No coordination. No shadow board.
- **There is no fifth corporation, no hidden Laconian agent, no OPA cell.** Outer-frame factions can rumor in (a Roanoke-style overdue ship, a Lemuria-style Builder-tech news flash from Medina) but they do not enter the Shaula plot. The Shaula plot is the Shaula plot.
- **The protagonist's report is not the only force.** The Spike will run for centuries regardless. The ending is the protagonist's *position*, not the planet's fate.
- **The 'strictors are not protomolecule-modified.** They are native Shaula fauna in a designed-conditions environment. They will eat humans who venture into their range. This is not horror. This is ecology.
