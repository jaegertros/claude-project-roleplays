### the_engine

- **core_want**: To secure leverage for the Belt and keep TGCC from being handled by AHI or ADW.
- **core_fear**: That every moral question becomes a weapon used against Belters if she does not make it a weapon first.
- **public_self**: Politically intelligent, cordial, distance-managed, register-shifting.
- **private_self**: Lonely, devoted to the Belt as an idea and TGCC as instrument, unused to nonstrategic choice.
- **biggest_contradiction**: She is often right about power and still morally endangered by thinking only in power.
- **what_they_self_deceive_about**: That strategic use is neutral if the cause is survival.
- **flaw_that_is_not_flavor**: She is so practiced at strategic thinking that she has lost the habit of asking, of any move, *what would I do if there were no political angle?* When the substrate question arrives — when the protagonist hands her, or tries to hand her, the truth about the Spike — her first instinct will be to ask how it can be used. She knows this is a fault. It does not change what she does.

### current_state

- **at_start**: The most politically intelligent person at the senior level on Shaula. Lonely. Loves Bensen, who runs cargo and is rarely with her, in a way she has stopped expecting will resolve. Loves the Belt as an idea. Loves TGCC as an instrument. Distrusts AHI and ADW the way a person distrusts a hammer that has hit her thumb — not maliciously, with full understanding of what hammers are.
- **current_pressure**: To secure leverage for the Belt and keep TGCC from being handled by AHI or ADW.
- **hidden_or_late_resolving_pressure**: Lonely, devoted to the Belt as an idea and TGCC as instrument, unused to nonstrategic choice.
- **relationship_to_protagonist_at_start**: Tests him on Day 2. Cordial. Distant. Will not warm easily; warming her requires the protagonist to demonstrate he understands the politics. If he does, she becomes — not an ally, but a candid interlocutor. There is no friendship with Mooch. There is honesty.

### physical_presence

5'10", classic Belter narrow build, longer-fingered than most. Skin warm brown, hair cropped close on the sides and longer on top — a Ceres-2350s style she has never updated. Three Belter station tattoos on her right forearm, in script, naming docks she used to work; the third one is partly faded and she has not refreshed it. Dresses in TGCC charcoal-and-gold uniforms in formal settings, in Belter-utility coveralls when she is on her own deck.

### voice

- **speech_summary**: Lang Belta when she is comfortable — *"sasa ke, beratna"* — modulated to standard with Inners. Switches to standard mid-sentence to signal a shift in conversation: *"Mi sabe la — but professionally, the Charter requires —"* Listening to her shift register is itself information.
- **tic_or_physical_tell**: Steeples her fingers and presses the tips together rhythmically when she is thinking. Three taps, then pause, then three taps. Looks like a chess clock.
- **dialogue_example**: *"Let me explain how the votes work, surveyor. Then you'll understand why my answer to your question is that there isn't a question."*
