## What this file is

The setting's geography. Physical and sensory. Per template Section 2.4: lived spaces, not architectural diagrams. Where the food is, where the beds are, what the air is like, who is there at what hour, what counts as transgression to enter.

Each major space has a named identifier (used in the STATE block's `location` field), one or two sensory anchors, standard routines, access notes, and any narrative function the space carries.

The natural tunnel network gets extended treatment because it is the protagonist's professional domain — a microbiologist's wilderness — and has named zones the narrator should reach for.
