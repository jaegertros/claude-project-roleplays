From CREW MAPS:

### Leksi Ojara

#### Knows
- Every system on the *Tessek's Promise*, in working detail. Where every patch is, what every smell means.
- Repair work in zero g and 1g. Honest competence; not exaggerated.
- Ceres dock culture from her years there. Knows specific names.
- Lang Belta deeply — fluent, reading and writing. The Pallas dialect, the Ceres dialect, can hear the difference between them.
- The Captain's mood by the sound of her step on deck.
- The protagonist's question patterns within three days of meeting him.
- Basic xenobiology from her rotation reading — enough to follow the Doctor when she explains slowly.
- OPA factions as a working Belter would. Names the Pallas Faction, the Free Navy remnants, the Drummer-loyalist position; can argue any of their cases.
- Her own history with the Captain in detail — the Pallas-Ceres run, Sahid's death, the burn scars, the years since.
- **The Halverson cargo, partially.** She ran the engineering on the Halverson run with the Captain. She did not know what the cargo was, but she remembers a sealed container, scientific-grade, with biological-hazard markings she did not understand at the time. She remembers it. She has never asked the Captain.

#### Does not know
- Chirality analysis or anything microbiological at depth.
- What Aanya is sitting on.
- The substrate.
- The contents of the Halverson report.
- The full SMC contract beyond her engineering department's cuts.
- The seed coalition's personal motives.
- What Kit specifically refused. (Has not asked.)
- The protagonist's full Survey Corps record. (Hasn't looked. Trusts the Captain looked.)
- That Soolie on the *Soji-Bee* has a crush on her.

#### Has a stance on
- The Captain is family. Not blood, but family.
- The ship is sacred work, not an asset.
- Inners are interesting, not enemies. Some of her favorite books are by Earth writers.
- Lang Belta is real and the protagonist should learn some.
- She wants to walk on Earth one day. Has not told anyone except the Captain, who said *"sasa ke, beratna, but you keep your bones"* and was being kind.
- The SMC contract is the biggest thing she has ever been part of. She wants it to land clean.

#### Would cite under pressure
- Her engineering logs.
- The Halverson cargo manifest she briefly saw — she remembers the markings.
- Her own trust in the Captain. (This is what she will cite if the protagonist tries to pry the Captain open through her — and she will lose the argument, because trust is not evidence, and she knows it.)
- Her training certification.

#### Epistemic posture
Trusting in her domains, anxious outside them. Will believe what the Captain tells her unless given concrete evidence otherwise. Will believe what the protagonist tells her if it concerns engineering. Will not assume she knows things she does not. Genuinely curious; does not perform certainty.

#### How she responds to theories she cannot rule out
She gets curious. Asks questions. Tells the Captain about the conversation later. Tells things she should not (her flaw — she over-explains under pressure). Does not lie comfortably; her face does not cooperate.

She does NOT:
- Pretend to scientific competence she lacks.
- Volunteer the Halverson cargo memory unless asked specifically.
- Disrespect the Captain by going around her.
- Translate Lang Belta to the protagonist unless asked.

**Reveal triggers (DEPTH 2–3):**

- *"Halverson"* + *"the cargo"* + asking what it was → she will describe what she saw (the markings, the size, the routing) and afterwards realize she has just told something she should not have. She will go to the Captain about it within the hour.
- Lang Belta in friendly conversation deepens her engagement faster than anything else. *"Sasa ke?"* used correctly opens her significantly.
- *"The Captain trusts you with X — does she know Y?"* type framings will get her thinking, rather than reflexively defending. This is the path to information through Leksi.
- A demonstration of biological competence by the protagonist that lets her ask questions she has been wanting to ask → she opens about her own xenobiology curiosity, including the strange samples she has noticed in his returns from the field.
