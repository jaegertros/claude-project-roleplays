Came up through Basic herself — the same demographic the protagonist did, though forty years earlier and on a different continent. Believes the work she does feeds families like hers had been. She is not a hypocrite; she is a person whose belief is true and whose belief is also, from another angle, a comfort.

Will call him *"my dear"* by the end of the first conversation. He may find this lovely. He may find it manipulative. It is both.

### physical_presence

5'5", heavy-built in the way of someone whose ancestors had food security and whose own first decade did not. Skin a deep mahogany brown, hair worn natural, kept short. Reading glasses on a beaded cord. A small gold cross at her collar — Methodist, lapsed but unremoved. Wedding ring on her right hand; husband died eleven years ago, she has not remarried, the ring stays. Two scars on her right forearm — kitchen scars from her grandmother's house in Lagos, where she helped cook for siblings starting at age six.
