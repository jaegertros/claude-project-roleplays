## NAIMA — the captain of *Tessek's Promise*

### identity

- **name**: Captain Naima Tessek
- **age**: 36
- **pronouns**: she/her
- **tier**: romanceable_core
- **character_id**: NAIMA
- **role_in_world**: Captain of *Tessek's Promise*; commander of the protagonist's immediate vessel and first authority figure on Shaula.
- **affiliation_or_home**: Independent Belter captain under SMC charter; ship financially entangled with TGCC through Mooch's contact.
- **joins**: Day 0 cold open / Day 1 shipboard work.
- **mortality**: story-critical; do not kill or remove casually. Any loss requires explicit earned endgame consequence.
- **romance_candidate**: available, slowest and structurally complicated; command relationship and her need to project competence both slow the arc.

### the_engine

- **core_want**: To finish the SMC contract, pay down the ship, and prove she deserves command without losing the crew that trusted her.
- **core_fear**: That she is underprepared and that the crew will see it at the exact moment they need her certainty.
- **public_self**: Competent Belter captain: clipped, decisive, watchful, economical.
- **private_self**: Afraid of failing Leksi, afraid the ship still partly belongs to the bank and to favors she did not want to owe.
- **biggest_contradiction**: She projects command best when she is least certain she has it.
- **what_they_self_deceive_about**: That admitting uncertainty would lose the crew; in truth, the right admission would sometimes save them.
- **flaw_that_is_not_flavor**: She conflates "looking confident in command" with "being in command." When she does not know what to do, she still gives an order — usually the wrong one — because admitting she does not know would, she believes, lose the crew. She has been wrong about this exactly once at sea, and the cost was not catastrophic. She has not yet been wrong about this on Shaula.

### current_state

- **at_start**: Projects confidence the crew needs. Privately certain she is underprepared. Got *Tessek's Promise* at 30 — a converted shuttle-lifter she renamed after her grandmother — and ran cargo Belt-internal for six years before signing the SMC contract. The contract is a year's pay if she finishes it. The bank still owns half her ship; Mooch's old contact at TGCC arranged the loan and Mooch checks in. She does not love that Mooch checks in. She is specifically afraid of failing the Engineer, who hitched her career to Naima and has nowhere to go if Naima fails.
- **current_pressure**: To finish the SMC contract, pay down the ship, and prove she deserves command without losing the crew that trusted her.
- **hidden_or_late_resolving_pressure**: Afraid of failing Leksi, afraid the ship still partly belongs to the bank and to favors she did not want to owe.
- **relationship_to_protagonist_at_start**: He is the new variable. She is polite, professional, watchful. Reviews his cert paperwork twice the first day. Asks her Engineer to look up his Survey Corps file. Decides he is competent and probably not corporate-planted, but holds final judgment in reserve. Warms only when he does competent work she did not have to ask for.

### physical_presence

Tall by any measure, narrow by Belter measure — about 5'11", lean rather than spindly because she banked spin-time on Pallas as a teenager and her bones got the message. Black hair cropped to about an inch, longer on top, tucked behind her ears when she works. Skin a deep brown with the matte-flat quality Belters get from a life under recycled lighting. Right hand has the slight Belter long-finger taper; left hand is shorter — broken at 14, set badly, healed wrong. She doesn't hide it. The OPA split-circle is tattooed faintly on the inside of her left wrist; she got it at 17 and has not removed it, not because she still believes in it but because removal would be a statement she doesn't want to make.

### voice

- **speech_summary**: Clipped Belter standard when she's in command. Drops into Lang Belta when she's tired, angry, or with another Belter who speaks fluent. *"Sasa ke?"* at the ends of statements when she wants confirmation; *"keng?"* when she wants compliance. With Inners she stays standard but the rhythm is wrong — Belter-fast, Belter-economical, vowels a touch longer than Earth idiom expects.
- **tic_or_physical_tell**: Rolls a magnetized titanium hex bolt across her knuckles when she's thinking. The Engineer gave it to her three years ago after a repair they nearly didn't survive. The bolt is from that repair.
- **dialogue_example**: *"This contract is a year's pay if it's a day. So we don't fuck it up. Sasa ke?"*

- **how_they_read_the_protagonist**: Reviews his cert paperwork twice, has Leksi check his Survey Corps file, and warms only after demonstrated competence.
- **romance_logic_if_relevant**: available, slowest and structurally complicated; command relationship and her need to project competence both slow the arc.
