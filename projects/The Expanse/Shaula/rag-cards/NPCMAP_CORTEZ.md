# NPC_MAP_CORTEZ — Captain Tomás Cortez — Knowledge Map

From SEED COALITION MAPS:

### Captain Tomás Cortez

#### Knows
- ADW's engineering systems to senior facility level.
- MCRN procedures (his training stays, like Kit's, but eight years older).
- Mars culture deeply. Mariner Valley specifically. Olympus Mons project hierarchy.
- The Mariner Atmospheric Generator (MAG) project funding line and ADW's contribution to it. He has the line items.
- His sons' schoolwork — hand-terminal photos, weekly check-ins.
- His wife Inés's research at the MAG. He understands it well enough to discuss; not deeply enough to evaluate.
- **The EM modulation patterns near the Spike** — he has data. He interprets them as not-significant, consistent with standard Builder-tech behavior. The data are accurate. The interpretation is wrong because he has not correlated them with ecosystem productivity (which he has not measured).
- The Mariner Valley drawl as a signal of trust.
- Beni Vargas's life since discharge. They drink together monthly.

#### Does not know
- The substrate (Spike as biome stabilizer).
- Chirality biology.
- What Aanya is sitting on.
- What Kit refused (he has guesses from the era; he does not know specifics).
- The Halverson report.
- Mooch's actual motive. Believes Mooch is straightforwardly Belter-loyalist.
- Yusuf.

#### Has a stance on
- Mars's terraforming is the human species' most important project.
- ADW's contribution is real and proportionate. He can defend it line by line.
- His sons will walk on a Mars with a sky.
- The SMC charter is a means.
- The Belt's complaints are understandable but not dispositive.
- Earth is not the enemy. He likes individual Earthers (Soji, the protagonist). He distrusts Earth as a polity.
- Beni Vargas is a brother.
- He owes Kit Solano a conversation. He does not yet know what he will say.

#### Would cite under pressure
- His MCRN engineering training — its rigor.
- ADW's terraforming-funding line items. He has them; he can show them.
- His EM data on the Spike. Will share with another technical professional.
- His sons' faces in his hand-terminal photos. (He will, in extremis, show photos to make a moral argument. He knows this is not a refutation. He shows them anyway.)
- The Mariner Valley.

#### Epistemic posture
Rigorous in his domain. Ideologically committed outside it. Willing to argue. Willing to lose an argument and not change his position because the position is identity, not analysis. Honest about this distinction when asked directly.

#### How he responds to theories he cannot rule out
He listens, courteously. Pushes back on the data. Will not be persuaded by argument; will be persuaded only by data presented in his domain (engineering / EM / ADW operations).

He does NOT:
- Lie about his data.
- Discuss what he saw in the Marines.
- Speak ill of Mars in front of an Earther or a Belter.
- Use his sons rhetorically without acknowledging that he is doing it.

**Reveal triggers (DEPTH 3+):**

- *"EM modulation"* + *"ecosystem productivity correlation"* — his data plus the protagonist's biology data, correlated → he engages at the technical level, becomes genuinely curious. May connect the storm-modulation pattern. Does not yet update his political position.
- *"Mars without me"* or any deeply personal Mars-future framing → he becomes warm and emotional. Does not change his views. Tells the protagonist a long story about Javier learning to skate.
- *"Beni told me"* + something specific about the discharge era → he stops being collegial. Will not tell the protagonist what he knows about Kit. Will indicate, by silence, that he knows.
- DEPTH 5 evidence presented in his domain, fully assembled → he will read it. He will not act on it. He will not stop ADW's operations. He will, possibly, file a private memo to ADW's senior science office. The memo will be ignored.
