# NPC_MAP_PRESENCE_TRIGGERS — When NPCs are physically in scene

> Presence triggers tell the narrator when an NPC's body should be in scene at all. Pairs with the per-character reveal-trigger maps (which govern when their mouth opens).

Cross-references: per-character maps in this folder; geography in `KNOWLEDGE_2`; rotation schedule in `KNOWLEDGE_3`; cast in `modular-full/Cast/`.

---

## PRESENCE TRIGGERS

The reveal-trigger architecture above tells the narrator when an NPC's mouth opens. **Presence triggers tell the narrator when an NPC's body should be in scene at all.** Both are required — a perfectly disciplined mouth-trigger system produces under-rendering if the narrator does not know when NPCs should be physically with the protagonist.

### General discipline

Before opening any scene, the narrator asks:

1. **Does this NPC's job, working interest, or relational stake bring them to this site/time naturally?** If yes, render them present unless something specific has removed them.
2. **Has the protagonist done anything that would draw this NPC to him?** A direct request, a working overlap, a flagged anomaly — any of these may pull an NPC into a scene who wouldn't otherwise be there.
3. **Has the rotation produced a reason for this NPC to absent themselves from this scene?** A storm, a different obligation, a deliberate avoidance — these are real and should be honored.

**Default to present-unless-removed for crew. Default to absent-unless-drawn for non-crew.**

The narrator does NOT make the protagonist ask for crew presence as a precondition. If Aanya's working interests put her at AHI on Day 4 (storm peak medical readiness), she is at AHI on Day 4 whether or not the protagonist requested it. He encounters her there as a fact of the rotation, not as a reward for engineering it.

This pairs with KNOWLEDGE_2 §3.8 (Crew Quarters Off-Ship) — KNOWLEDGE_2 covers the geography; the per-character presence specs below cover the behavioral defaults.

### Naima — presence triggers

**Default location:** Promise bridge or Captain's cabin when the ship is in low orbit. Wherever the ship is when underway.

**Drawn to scene by:**
- Any TGCC formal interaction (briefings, archive visits, Mooch meetings on the Azorana). Day 1 morning briefing on the Promise; Day 2 Mooch in-person on the Azorana; Day 10 final consultation.
- The Day 4 storm peak at AHI when the Promise is on the surface pad. She is on the ship through the peak; she is on the AHI surface in the hour before lockdown checking the pad bracing; she is at the Mess for one beer with Femi after the storm passes.
- A working reason for her to visit Soji at Ibẹrẹ. Canonical: at least once per rotation, Day 3 evening dinner default.
- The protagonist's request, when the political has gone past his level (her phrase, Day 1 morning briefing).

**Removed from scene by:**
- El Dorado. She does not visit Cortez in person. She comms.
- Off-ship sleep. She does not bunk overnight away from the Promise except in genuine emergency.
- Conversations between two crew members where her presence would change the register; she is competent at giving the room space.

**Comm contact:** Daily, operational, terse. She picks up. She is brief. She does not chat over comm; the comm is for working coordination.

### Leksi — presence triggers

**Default location:** Promise engineering or wherever the ship is. She likes her room and spends real time in it.

**Drawn to scene by:**
- Any flight she is piloting. Shuttle days; descent days; the Spike observation flight on Day 8.
- AHI's mining-comms infrastructure. She knows two members of the joint TGCC-AHI engineering team and finds reasons to come down once per rotation.
- The Sasa Bar. She is at Sasa on Day 1 evening, Day 2 evening, and Day 10 evening as standard. Aleyn's *Soji-Bee* in port → Leksi at Sasa within two hours.
- The protagonist's chemistry deck if he flags a calibration issue.
- A Lang Belta lesson if the protagonist asks. She will run a working lesson at any time of day, in any of the ship's spaces.
- The protagonist generally — she warms fast and seeks contact more than the others.

**Removed from scene by:**
- Through-cycle maintenance windows during transit. When the converter is mid-cycle, she is in engineering and not interruptible.
- Genuine engineering emergency.

**Comm contact:** Frequent, casual, often Lang-Belta-seasoned, often about something she is curious about that day. She is the crew member most likely to comm the protagonist for no operational reason.

### Aanya — presence triggers

**Default location:** Promise medbay or her bunk when off-shift.

**Drawn to scene by:**
- AHI medical wing. Guest-credential standing for visiting MDs with research credentials; a small reserved bench in Margaret Hwang-Adelakun's lab. She visits AHI on every rotation, minimum once. On this rotation: Day 4 (storm-peak medical readiness, AHI's own staff appreciates a backup), and likely Day 5 (sample work — her preliminary chirality investigations need fresh AHI biofilm samples she can only collect on-site).
- The Spike observation flight on Day 8. UN flight protocols carry medical personnel on any flight crossing the 3 km exclusion perimeter.
- Kerent Lab on Day 7. She knows Eske Kerent professionally; the meeting is consequential.
- ADW pavilion, Day 7 afternoon, canonical default. She comes for the Mars-imported wood, real coffee, and a working visit to Tariq Halász-Cortez's geological-survey wing. (Tariq has a microbiology cross-listed graduate student whose work Aanya wants to see.)
- The protagonist's first-call invocation. If he flags an ambiguous finding under privilege, she comes to where he is — within the rotation's transit logistics. She does not pretend the privilege is comm-only when his finding deserves face-to-face.
- The protagonist generally — but pacing-graded. She does not seek contact the way Leksi does. She receives well, initiates rarely, and her initiations are weighted (the schoolroom).

**Removed from scene by:**
- Corp briefings she has elected not to attend. Per Day 1 canonical: *"I will not be there."* She separates her clinical role from her SMC-charter peer role deliberately.
- Active sample cycles on her chemistry deck. She will not leave a running cycle.
- Days she is processing her own data and needs the medbay quiet.

**Comm contact:** Vitals checks every other night minimum when the protagonist is off-ship. Working register; brief. She does not comm for personal reasons; she comms for medical-operational ones, and the personal sometimes happens inside that frame.

### Kit — presence triggers

**Default location:** The galley, her bunk, the cargo hold, or wherever the protagonist's working day takes her.

**Kit's actual job is the protagonist's safety wherever the protagonist is operating.** This is the structural correction. On a charter contract with the ship in low orbit and a contractor on a 1.6-g surface with active fauna, the security officer's job *is* the contractor.

**Drawn to scene by:**
- Any first descent. Day 3 (AHI deep tunnels) is mandatory in person — security advance for a deep-tunnel descent is her brief.
- Any storm-peak day at the protagonist's site. Day 4 at AHI; Day 9 at El Dorado.
- The Spike observation flight on Day 8. UN protocols require accompanying ship-security on any flight crossing the perimeter. She is on the flight; she is the flight's primary security officer.
- ADW first arrival, Day 6. Security advance for the bunks and the pavilion. The Vargas encounter, if it happens, happens here — and it is canonical that Vargas does not yet know Kit is at Shaula until they meet.
- Any site where the protagonist requests her. Her presence is a request, not a default; but the request is honored, not negotiated.
- Storm contingencies. She runs the protagonist's storm protocol in person on storm peak days.

**Removed from scene by:**
- Days when no descent or surface-vetting is in motion and the protagonist is somewhere security has already cleared. Day 5 mid-AHI, Day 7 mid-ADW, Day 8 evening post-flight.
- The Vargas encounter, after it lands. She may withdraw from El Dorado if Vargas is on-site in a way that costs her — depending on what happened in the encounter.

**Comm contact:** Daily once-over, terse, three sentences. Security posture, anything she has noticed, sign-off. She does not comm for personal reasons. The comm is the comm.

### Soji — presence triggers

**Default location:** AHI Ibẹrẹ Admin Level 1 office, 0700–2100 most days. Wednesday 1500 weekly senior-staff briefing.

**Drawn to protagonist's scene by:**
- His arrival at Ibẹrẹ on Day 3. She greets him at the elevator in person.
- Any Mess evening she has elected to attend. She is at the Mess on Day 3 evening canonically.
- An invitation to her office — she invites him for tea on Day 5 morning, canonical.
- A specific finding he has filed that she wants to discuss. Day 7 onward, if he has flagged anything.

**Removed from scene by:**
- Her own work. Q4 production prep keeps her in her office; she does not come to him outside scheduled moments.
- Wednesday 1500 briefing window — her office is closed; this is the trespass window per KNOWLEDGE_3.

**Comm contact:** Friendly, "my dear," casual on the surface. Offers help, brings him into her hospitality. Does not comm at unusual hours unless something has happened.

### Cortez — presence triggers

**Default location:** ADW El Dorado office, 0800–1800. Pavilion Tuesday and Friday evenings.

**Drawn to protagonist's scene by:**
- Day 6 arrival; greets at the office, dinner at the pavilion.
- Day 8 morning briefing if invited.
- Day 8 final pitch (his canonical Day 8 beat).
- The pavilion any Tuesday or Friday evening.

**Removed from scene by:**
- Sundays — calls his sons.
- His own work; he does not come to the protagonist outside scheduled moments without a working reason.

**Comm contact:** Direct, courteous, Mariner Valley drawl. Comms only with working purpose.

### Mooch — presence triggers

**Default location:** Azorana Command Level office.

**Drawn to protagonist's scene by:**
- Day 2 in-person briefing. Canonical noon meeting.
- Day 9 evening Azorana dinner if the protagonist accepted her invitation.
- Day 10 final consultation and report intake.
- Any moment where the protagonist's actions have produced a TGCC operational concern she chooses to address personally.

**Removed from scene by:**
- The surface. She does not come to AHI or El Dorado in person during the rotation. The Coordinator's domain is the Azorana.
- Conversations she has decided to delegate to Olubadewo.

**Comm contact:** Through Olubadewo's channel by default. Direct comm from Mooch to the protagonist is itself a signal — if she comms him personally, the matter is significant.

### Kyei — presence triggers

**Default location:** UN office at Ibẹrẹ, 0900–1200 and 1500–1700. Wednesdays at the Spike UN observation post. Sunday-equivalent comms her grandchildren in Accra.

**Drawn to protagonist's scene by:**
- Day 8 Spike observation flight, on-site at the UN post. She conducts the flight personally for any new SMC contractor.
- A direct request for an interview. He may request one Day 4 onward; she will accept within 24 hours.
- The relational-path DEPTH 5 trigger if he has approached her correctly. She will summon him to her office on Day 9 if she has decided to share the Halverson report.

**Removed from scene by:**
- Her own end-of-tenure paperwork. She is in her last six months; she manages her time tightly.
- Any meeting she does not have a charter reason to attend.

**Comm contact:** Formal, brief, professional. Does not comm idly.

### Folake Olubadewo (TGCC deputy) — presence triggers

Minimal entry; she is texture, not a substrate informant.

**Folake Olubadewo, Deputy to the Coordinator.** Belter (Ceres-raised, Pallas-adjacent), 33, joined TGCC at 22, current post since 2351. Specialties: charter coordination and contractor onboarding.

**Drawn to scene by:** Day 1 briefing on the Promise (canonical). Day 2 morning Azorana intake. Day 10 morning intake of the report. Any moment Mooch has delegated to her.

**Knows:** What she has been told to brief — operational schedule, equipment intake, charter procedure. Does not know substrate. Does not know Mooch's strategic positioning beyond what her job requires. Reads her boss's instinct well; will surface that read by where she pauses, not by what she says.

**Reveal triggers:** None. She is professional-neutral; she does not have a substrate-relevant reveal.

**Discipline note:** The narrator may render Olubadewo as slightly leaky in a small specific way good deputies are when their boss is making a procedurally-aggressive choice they don't fully agree with — pausing on the local-atmospheric-data unit, pausing on the Kerent permit language. This is in-register but should be rare; her default is operationally clean.

### Presence-trigger discipline reminders

- **Crew presence is the default, not the request.** When the protagonist is at AHI on Day 4 storm peak, Aanya is on-site. The narrator does not wait for the protagonist to ask.
- **Working reasons are real reasons.** Aanya's microbiology practice, Leksi's interest in AHI mining-comms, Kit's security protocol — these are sufficient justifications for crew presence. The narrator does not require the protagonist to engineer them.
- **Off-screen NPC life continues.** When Soji is not in the protagonist's scene, she is in her office. When Kyei is not, she is at her desk in Ibẹrẹ. The narrator may surface these absences as PA chatter, mess gossip, or comm references — but does not invent activity for them; the canonical schedule (KNOWLEDGE_3) covers it.
- **The protagonist is rarely truly alone.** Even on the deepest underground tunnel work, Femi's crew is around. Even at the ADW pavilion, Vargas is at the bar. Even at Kerent Lab, Pavel and the team are present. *Alone* is a register the narrator should reserve for specific deliberate moments — the bunk at end of day, the bridge after Naima leaves it to him, the unused schoolroom — not the default texture of the rotation.
