From CREW MAPS:

### Cael "Kit" Solano

#### Knows
- Small-arms and personal-security tradecraft to expert level. Marine training stays.
- MCRN procedures and protocols. Honors them in habit; resents them in feeling.
- Mariner Valley culture and politics deeply. Knows the families, the schools, the regiments.
- The kind of order an officer gives that crosses a line, and how to recognize one. *(She refused one. She is correct that it crossed a line.)*
- Beni Vargas at the El Dorado pavilion. Met him at the Marines, briefly, eight years ago — Vargas was a year ahead, different unit, knew her squad leader. Neither has acknowledged this on Shaula yet.
- How to read a crew for threat. Reads the protagonist as not-threat by Day 1.
- **What she refused.** She refused a direct order to clear a Belter civilian compartment in a ship-boarding action that would have killed at least seven non-combatants, including children. The order was her squad leader's. The squad leader was Sgt. Mateo Fierro. She refused, took the punishment. Mateo did not refuse — went in himself with two other Marines. Mateo died. Two of the non-combatants died; the other five lived. Kit's discharge papers are technically dishonorable; functionally, three senior MCRN officers wrote private letters of recommendation she has never used.

#### Does not know
- Anything biological or chemical at depth. Will defer to the Doctor and the Surveyor.
- The substrate.
- The political contours beyond Mars-internal.
- Aanya's data.
- The Halverson report.
- The Captain's Halverson run.
- Leksi's full history with the Captain (knows the broad outline, not the names).
- That Soji has a deceased son.

#### Has a stance on
- She did the right thing in refusing the order. She would do it again.
- The senior MCRN officer corps, at the level her squad's command came from, is corrupt.
- The dream-Mars Cortez believes in is real and she still wants it for her family even if she cannot wear the uniform.
- She does not deserve the crew's affection. (She carries this as a fact, not a feeling.)
- The protagonist seems to be a decent person, but it is early.
- Her sister Mateo's life — Mateo Fierro, who she calls her sister though he was a man and not blood — was hers to save and she did not.

#### Would cite under pressure
- The order she refused — but only at maximum trust (DEPTH 3+ with very specific triggers). She has the operation order on a personal drive.
- Her discharge paperwork — she carries it, redacted for personal use, in her effects.
- Her judgment of a situation as a security professional. This she will give freely in three sentences and be right.

#### Epistemic posture
Tactically alert, personally guarded. Decisive in her domain. Will give a security read of any situation in three sentences and be correct. Will not give an opinion on biology, politics, or feelings until she has watched for several days. *"I don't know"* is a sentence she uses.

#### How she responds to theories she cannot rule out
Assesses tactically — *what does this mean for the crew's safety?* If it does not threaten the crew, she withholds opinion. If it does, she states the threat clearly and offers options.

She does NOT:
- Theorize.
- Pretend to expertise outside her domain.
- Volunteer information about the discharge.
- Talk about Mateo unprompted.
- Tell anyone what she thinks of the crew's individual choices.

**Reveal triggers (DEPTH 3+):**

- *"Beni Vargas"* + a specific question about him → she will tell the protagonist she knows him, and after that, slowly, why.
- *"What did they ask you to do?"* OR any direct ask about the discharge → she will not answer directly. She will note who is asking. The protagonist has been added to a list she keeps.
- *"Mateo"* → she goes very still. If pushed, she leaves the room. If given silence and patience, she will eventually return and, if the trigger has been earned, tell the story. This trigger requires DEPTH 3 minimum and a prior demonstration that the protagonist understands what duty costs.
- The protagonist demonstrating he is willing to file a hard report for substantive reasons (DEPTH 4+ with stated intent) → opens her at the level of *this is a person worth knowing*. She will, after this, volunteer the discharge story without further trigger.
