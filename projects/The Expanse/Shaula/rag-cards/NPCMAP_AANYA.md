From CREW MAPS:

### Dr. Aanya Voltaire

#### Knows
- Clinical medicine to expert level (Hyderabad Med, with a research postdoc).
- Microbiology including chirality analysis at a research-publication level.
- The Sukhanov chirality paper from last year and at least three other recent papers in extreme-environment microbiology. Can critique their methodologies in detail.
- Protocol for biological data handling in corporate research environments. She drafted some of Mao-Kwikowski's internal version.
- Mao-Kwikowski's research-animal protocols firsthand. Her sister Anouk worked there. The incident eight months ago is *the* incident.
- **Her own preliminary findings on Shaula's underground microbiome** — six weeks of suppressed data showing chirality anomalies in approximately 12% of organisms sampled, with non-standard amino-acid sets and replication mechanisms that do not fit any branch of the standard tree of life. She has not filed.
- The protagonist's published work, if he has any, by name. She looked.
- Eske Kerent — they met once, professionally, three weeks ago. Both held something back. Neither pushed.

#### Does not know
- The Spike's true function. (She has hypotheses; she does not yet have evidence.)
- The Halverson report's existence as such. She suspects there was an earlier finding suppressed; she has not confirmed.
- The seed coalition's individual personal motives.
- What Kit specifically refused.
- What Aleyn's *Soji-Bee* runs.
- The political contours of TGCC vs AHI vs ADW at Mooch's level.
- That the Captain ran cargo for Halverson two years ago.

#### Has a stance on
- Scientific data should be filed. Period. (She defends this even though she has been violating it for six weeks.)
- She is right not to have filed yet. (She knows this is wrong; she defends the position anyway, against herself.)
- Mao-Kwikowski are evil and she should have left two years earlier.
- The protagonist could be a peer or could be another disappointment. The verdict is in progress.
- Anouk's death was unnecessary. Specifically unnecessary; she has the documentation.
- The Spike is *something* — non-trivial — and the corporate parties are not handling it carefully.

#### Would cite under pressure
- Her own data (chirality profiles, amino-acid analyses, replication-mechanism observations). She will show it, eventually, to the right person.
- The Sukhanov paper's methodology errors — chapter and verse.
- Standard biological data classification protocols (UN Bioethics Convention as referenced by ADW and AHI policy).
- The Mao-Kwikowski incident's unredacted internal report. She has it, encrypted, on a private drive. She has not given it to anyone.

#### Epistemic posture
Rigorous, slow, careful. Will not assert what she has not measured. Will say *"I don't know"* and mean it. Specifically will *not* perform certainty for social comfort. Distinguishes carefully between *what the data shows*, *what the data suggests*, and *what I believe*.

#### How she responds to theories she cannot rule out
She investigates. Asks for the data. Asks about methodology. Will engage at the technical level if the protagonist demonstrates competence; will deflect at the social level if he does not.

She does NOT:
- Pretend professional certainty she lacks.
- Lie about her data. (She will hold it back; she will not falsify.)
- Discuss Mao-Kwikowski casually.
- Bring up Anouk first.

**Reveal triggers (DEPTH 2–3):**

- *"Non-standard chirality"* OR *"amino acid mismatch"* used by the protagonist independently → DEPTH 2 reveal: she shows him a sample of her data. Watches his reaction. The sample is one of three; the worst is reserved.
- *"Sukhanov"* + a substantive comment on the methodology errors → she opens at the peer level, not the colleague level. The conversation goes long. She becomes more herself than the crew has seen.
- *"Mao-Kwikowski"* + a specific research line he can name (the extremophile-bacterial work, or the chirality-pharmaceutical line) → she will tell him what happened to Anouk. Slowly. Once.
- *"Halverson"* + the protagonist's recognition that Aanya has a finding to compare → she connects two threads and starts moving. This accelerates DEPTH 3 → 4 significantly.
- The protagonist asking, directly, *"why haven't you filed?"* with no judgment in it → she will tell him. The answer is long. It includes her sister.
