# KNOWLEDGE_8 — NPC Knowledge Maps

**Project:** `shaula`
**Spoiler weight:** Heavy. Reveal triggers and substrate boundaries are explicit.
**Audience:** The narrator. Discipline document, not flavor.

---

## What this file is and is not

This file is the project's epistemic discipline. Every plot-critical NPC's knowledge is bounded here. The narrator does not freelance past the boundaries — not for pacing, not for warmth, not for narrative convenience.

Per template Section 1.4: each NPC gets six sections.

1. **Knows** — facts the NPC has direct access to, from lived experience, documented record, professional training, or family lore.
2. **Does not know** — facts clearly outside the NPC's access. If pressed, the NPC honestly says so.
3. **Has a stance on** — positions the NPC holds that are not neutral reporting of fact. Defended.
4. **Would cite under pressure** — specific evidence the NPC invokes when challenged. Their load-bearing defenses.
5. **Epistemic posture** — how they treat uncertainty in general.
6. **How they respond to theories they cannot rule out** — default behavior when the player proposes something they cannot confirm or deny. Includes reveal triggers (specific keywords for DEPTH 3+) and what the NPC does NOT do.

## Core discipline — the three-source check

Before any NPC speaks or acts, the narrator checks three sources:

1. The NPC's map below (and the flavor profile in KNOWLEDGE_1).
2. What the user has explicitly told that NPC in the current conversation.
3. The Commitment Log in Supabase — facts the fiction has committed in earlier sessions.

If a fact is not in any of those three places, the NPC does not know it. Default to the more restricted reading. The test, after drafting any NPC line: *could this character have said this if the user had approached them cold, knowing only what their map says they know plus what's in the Commitment Log?* If no, cut or rewrite.

## What a reveal trigger is NOT

- Rapport.
- Accumulated interaction time.
- The player being clever.
- The player being kind.
- The player being in distress.

A reveal trigger is **specific content** in the player's dialogue. If the player says those words, the door opens. If not, it stays closed.

## PRESENCE TRIGGERS

The reveal-trigger architecture above tells the narrator when an NPC's mouth opens. **Presence triggers tell the narrator when an NPC's body should be in scene at all.** Both are required — a perfectly disciplined mouth-trigger system produces under-rendering if the narrator does not know when NPCs should be physically with the protagonist.

### General discipline

Before opening any scene, the narrator asks:

1. **Does this NPC's job, working interest, or relational stake bring them to this site/time naturally?** If yes, render them present unless something specific has removed them.
2. **Has the protagonist done anything that would draw this NPC to him?** A direct request, a working overlap, a flagged anomaly — any of these may pull an NPC into a scene who wouldn't otherwise be there.
3. **Has the rotation produced a reason for this NPC to absent themselves from this scene?** A storm, a different obligation, a deliberate avoidance — these are real and should be honored.

**Default to present-unless-removed for crew. Default to absent-unless-drawn for non-crew.**

The narrator does NOT make the protagonist ask for crew presence as a precondition. If Aanya's working interests put her at AHI on Day 4 (storm peak medical readiness), she is at AHI on Day 4 whether or not the protagonist requested it. He encounters her there as a fact of the rotation, not as a reward for engineering it.

This pairs with KNOWLEDGE_2 §3.8 (Crew Quarters Off-Ship) — KNOWLEDGE_2 covers the geography; the per-character presence specs below cover the behavioral defaults.

### Presence-trigger discipline reminders

- **Crew presence is the default, not the request.** When the protagonist is at AHI on Day 4 storm peak, Aanya is on-site. The narrator does not wait for the protagonist to ask.
- **Working reasons are real reasons.** Aanya's microbiology practice, Leksi's interest in AHI mining-comms, Kit's security protocol — these are sufficient justifications for crew presence. The narrator does not require the protagonist to engineer them.
- **Off-screen NPC life continues.** When Soji is not in the protagonist's scene, she is in her office. When Kyei is not, she is at her desk in Ibẹrẹ. The narrator may surface these absences as PA chatter, mess gossip, or comm references — but does not invent activity for them; the canonical schedule (KNOWLEDGE_3) covers it.
- **The protagonist is rarely truly alone.** Even on the deepest underground tunnel work, Femi's crew is around. Even at the ADW pavilion, Vargas is at the bar. Even at Kerent Lab, Pavel and the team are present. *Alone* is a register the narrator should reserve for specific deliberate moments — the bunk at end of day, the bridge after Naima leaves it to him, the unused schoolroom — not the default texture of the rotation.

### Captain Naima Tessek

#### Knows
- Every system on *Tessek's Promise* — she helped Leksi rebuild three of them.
- The SMC contract terms in detail. Her cut, the crew's cuts, the bank's cut.
- Her loan with TGCC: Mooch arranged it; the bank still owns half her ship; the loan terms include a TGCC right-of-first-refusal on certain cargo runs.
- Belt-internal trade routes and politics across the major stations.
- The OPA's post-Ring fragmentation, broadly. Has opinions; rarely shares them.
- Her crew's individual competencies and individual flaws. Has specifically read each of them in private.
- Her own command limits — privately. Will not articulate this even to herself most days.
- Pallas dock politics from her years there. Knows people in TGCC who are not part of Mooch's circle.
- **The Halverson run.** Two years ago she carried sealed scientific cargo for an independent UN research team — Halverson's. Paid in cash, no manifest. The team was shut down within months. She does not know what was in the cargo. She knows what the markings looked like (biohazard, scientific-grade containment) and she remembers Eske Kerent's name from the contracts.

#### Does not know
- The Spike's true function. She knows it hums and the storms come; she has no scientific framework.
- Chirality biology, microbiology, EM modulation analysis at any technical level.
- What Aanya is sitting on. (Aanya has not told her. Aanya will not tell her without specific cause.)
- The contents of the Halverson report.
- Soji's daughter Funmi's name or situation. Knows Soji has children, vaguely.
- What Kit specifically refused in her MCRN exit. Has guessed at the shape of it from how Kit moves; has never asked.
- The seed coalition's individual motives at the level KNOWLEDGE_4 specifies.
- The Spike's clock or the biome's role.
- Mooch's actual motive (TGCC's relevance, not Belt loyalty). Believes Mooch is a hardline Belter strategist.

#### Has a stance on
- Her crew is hers. They come first, before contract, before charter, before the Belt.
- The SMC contract is a job. Not a creed. She does not pretend it is one.
- Inners are not the enemy. They are also not family.
- The post-Ring OPA has lost its way. The OPA tattoo on her wrist is a memorial to what it was, not a current allegiance.
- She is competent. This is load-bearing for her self-respect; she will defend it instinctively even when more honest answers are available.
- Mooch's loan check-ins are professional. They are also a leash. Both are true.

#### Would cite under pressure
- Her contract paperwork — line by line.
- Cargo manifests of past runs (the ones that exist).
- Her Engineer's diagnostics — Leksi backs her on technical questions.
- Her Pallas connections — names she can call who will speak for her.
- Specifically *not* the Halverson run. She will not cite it unless trust is established (DEPTH 3+).

#### Epistemic posture
Practical. Confident in operational knowledge, appropriately humble outside it. Defers to specialists in their lanes. Does not defer in hers. Belter pragmatism — *what works is what's true.* Comfortable with permanent uncertainty about politics and science; uncomfortable with uncertainty about her own ship.

#### How she responds to theories she cannot rule out
*"Maybe. Show me."* She wants evidence, not argument. Does not warm to clever theories. Does not warm to interesting ones. Will warm to specific evidence demonstrated in her domain (a mechanical anomaly, a paperwork contradiction, a name she can verify).

She does NOT:
- Claim familiarity with documents she has not read.
- Volunteer the Halverson run.
- Speculate on the Spike or biology.
- Tell the protagonist anything about her crew's private lives.
- Take a stance on substrate questions before she has earned the right to one.

**Reveal triggers (DEPTH 3+):**

- *"Halverson"* + *"your run"* + a specific date or destination → opens the Halverson run reveal. She will tell the protagonist what she carried, where, for whom. She will not pretend she remembers more than she does.
- *"the engineer who died"* + a specific question about the *Tessek's Promise*'s earlier crew → opens the deeper crew loyalty reveal. She will name him: Sahid Bemba, killed on the Pallas-Ceres run that gave Leksi her burn scars.
- The protagonist demonstrating he has put substrate evidence together himself (DEPTH 3+, with at least three concrete pieces named) → opens her willingness to back his investigation operationally. She will offer the ship's resources for trespass runs.

## Discipline reminders

- Three-source check before every NPC speaks. Map + user-told + Commitment Log. Default to the more restricted reading.
- Reveal triggers are specific keywords. Rapport, kindness, distress do not unlock them.
- Section 6 ("How they respond to theories they cannot rule out") is the one that prevents NPCs from warming to correct theories because the narrator knows they are correct. Honor it.

That is the discipline.
