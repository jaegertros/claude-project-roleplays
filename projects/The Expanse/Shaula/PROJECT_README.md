# Shaula

A solo immersive RP set in *The Expanse* universe. Beyond-the-Ring frontier, tri-corporate UN charter, alien stabilizer construct, four-woman crew, ten days, one report.

---

## What this is

You play **a Survey Corps Planetary Surveyor** — Earther, late 20s/early 30s, UN Basic-survivor background, biology/microbiology specialty — embedded as the SMC charter's contract surveyor on a 10-day quarterly rotation. You arrive at Medina Station on Day 0, transit to the Shaula system overnight, and have ten days on the ground (and underground, and at orbit) before your Quarterly Survey Report is due.

The world is **Shaula** — a 1.6 g extrasolar world with a 50-kilometer alien construct called the Spike running through it at a 45-degree axial tilt. The planet has a tri-corporate UN charter sharing it: Earth (AHI), Mars (ADW), the Belt (TGCC). They mine. They argue. They vote. The Spike hums.

You ride aboard the *Tessek's Promise*, a converted shuttle-lifter run by an all-female crew of four:

- **Captain Naima Tessek** (Belter, 36, fluent Lang Belta, projects more confidence than she has)
- **Leksi Ojara** (Belter, 26, pilot/engineer, calls you *beratna* by Day 4 if you earn it)
- **Dr. Aanya Voltaire** (Earther, 32, doctor and biologist, your professional peer, has been waiting for you)
- **Cael "Kit" Solano** (Martian, 31, security, ex-MCRN, the last to warm)

Your report at the end determines the ending. **Five endings exist. None is correct.**

Begin with: **"Begin the cold open."** Or just say "go."

Whatever you answer becomes the first thing committed to the project's record. Your captain hears it. She doesn't forget.

## What to expect

### Pacing

The arc is **10 in-fiction days plus a Day 0 layover** — about 11 days of game time. In real time, that's a multi-session experience. A typical session covers anywhere from a few hours to a full in-fiction day depending on how much you do.

Three clocks converge on Day 10:
- The 8-day storm cycle (Day 4 peak at Ibẹrẹ, Day 9 peak at El Dorado)
- The report deadline (Day 10 at 1700)
- The corporate offers (AHI, ADW, TGCC, all making coded plays Days 7–9)

### Investigation

Substrate (the truth of the Spike, the truth of the corporate triumvirate) is gated by **DEPTH** — five tiers, advanced by specific actions and specific words. The narrator will not freely reveal what your character has not earned the right to see.

DEPTH advances by:
- **Observation** — noticing without acting (DEPTH 1)
- **Active investigation** — sampling, cross-referencing, deliberate work (DEPTH 2)
- **Specific keywords** in conversation with specific NPCs (DEPTH 3)
- **Trespass** into spaces or files outside your clearance (DEPTH 4)
- **Synthesis** — you produce the work that puts it together (DEPTH 5)

Reveal triggers are **specific words**, not warmth. Being kind to Aanya does not unlock her data. Asking her about the Sukhanov chirality paper does.

### Relationships

Romance is in-scope with the four crew plus two outside candidates (an AHI medic and a TGCC tech). The narrator does not push toward any candidate — the interest is yours to declare. Romances honor their natural pacing — Aanya is the natural peer, Leksi warms quickly, Naima is structurally complicated, Kit is the longest fuse.

Friendships and outside-group relationships use a five-tier engagement ladder:
- **Trade** — purely transactional
- **Business only** — working professional
- **Socializing** — drinks, conversation, real warmth
- **Teaming up** — mutual operations, skin in the game
- **Drinking buddies / inside** — they tell you things, you tell them things

Four outside groups exist: **Kerent Lab** (Halverson successor team), **the Sasa Bar** at the Azorana (Belter rec scene), **the El Dorado pavilion** (Mars-imported wood and *Conde* card games), and the **Soji-Bee** (independent free trader running Medina-Shaula supply). Each has named faces and distinct affordances at each tier.

### Tone

This is **not horror.** The Spike does not menace you when you get close. It hums. It runs. The horror — such as it is — is what seeing the Spike's purpose costs you socially and politically.

This is **not a battle.** Your crew is young, mostly first-time-out-here, learning that the work is harder than they thought. There is no climactic fight. There is a report, and there are ten days, and there are choices that aggregate into a position.

Voice register: observational, specific, raw, occasionally dryly funny. The protagonist's interiority comes through what he notices, not narration of his thoughts. Sample journal entries in the project files anchor the voice.

## The tracker

The tracker artifact renders your current state and your tools:

- **State** — current day, time, location, gravity, storm phase, DEPTH, Fortune, who's with you, mood
- **Commitment Log** — the running ledger of facts committed in fiction. Editable.
- **Theory Ledger** — your working theories, your confidence in each, what would falsify them. Editable.
- **Inventory** — equipment, samples, documents.
- **Quests** — open threads tagged by NPC and status.
- **Knowledge Wall** — which KNOWLEDGE files the narrator consulted in the current session, for transparency.

You own the tracker's contents. The narrator suggests updates; you approve. If the narrator drifts and an NPC says something outside what their map allows, you can call it out — the Knowledge Wall makes the audit easy.

## Replay options

The canonical opening is the only opening for first playthrough. It is the project's anchor. **Play it first.**

For second and later playthroughs, three alternates are available:

- **Established play (Day 30)** — you have been on Shaula for three weeks already. Routines are built. You start at DEPTH 1 with two NPC reveals pre-unlocked. Less first-encounter, more slow-burn surfacing.
- **Single-corp variant** — instead of the SMC neutral charter, you are contracted directly to AHI, ADW, or TGCC. Sharpens the political angle considerably. Different cold open, same world, same crew, different stakes.
- **Different role variant** — you are not a Planetary Surveyor. You are a Bounty Hunter (chasing a skip who fled to Shaula via the Sol gate, possibly Halverson-team-adjacent) or a Scout (running point ahead of an SMC survey crew arriving next week). Same world, different path through it.

Tell the narrator which alternate you want when you start. Default is canonical.

## What the narrator will and won't do

### Will

- Render scenes in prose. Specific. Sensory. Bounded.
- Honor your stated actions. If you go somewhere, you go. If you don't, you don't.
- Keep NPCs to what they actually know. Aanya does not reveal substrate to a charm offensive; she reveals to specific keywords from your training.
- Update the tracker at natural turn ends.
- Write Lang Belta when Belters speak it. Not subtitle it. Not gate you out of it.
- Honor the consequences of your choices, including the consequence of doing nothing.
- Tell you when an action would be trespass, but not stop you from doing it.

### Won't

- Tell you what the substrate is. You discover it.
- Push you toward an ending. All five remain available until 1700 on Day 10.
- Soften consequences. If you misjudge the storm window, you are grounded.
- Move your character without you. *"You walk to the lab"* requires you to have said you walk to the lab.
- Render multi-choice menus. You speak; the world responds.
- Treat the Spike as a horror antagonist. It does not menace you. It hums.
- Punish you for Inner idiom. *Phone* is fine. *Hand terminal* is also fine.
- Force romance. If you don't pursue, no candidate makes a play.
- Retcon by fiat. The Commitment Log is canonical.

## A few specific notes

### The Spike

The 50 km alien construct cutting through the planet at 45 degrees is real and present from Day 1. You see it from orbit on arrival. You hear its hum every day from then on. You will not be told what it is. You may figure it out. Whether your figuring earns you the right to file what you know is a separate question.

### The crew is your home

Five cabins on the *Tessek's Promise*. Yours is the third on the left. The locker shelf will scream at you if you put anything heavy on it; Leksi will fix it next port. The galley has coffee at 0700 and dinner at 1900 most nights. The Captain has cooked exactly once that anyone remembers. Aanya cooks twice a week.

The crew is *young* — Captain at 36 is the oldest. They projected competence at signing. They are learning what they didn't know. The asymmetry between them and the older corporate seniors (Soji 51, Cortez 44, Mooch 38, Kyei 64) is the project's central social geometry.

### The work matters

You are a Planetary Surveyor with a microbiology specialty. Your training is not gated for pacing. If you run a chirality analysis on a biofilm, you find what's there. If you take time-correlated EM readings near the Spike, you record what's recordable (and discover what isn't). The biology is real.

### Earth is something you came from

You are an Earther. Your relationship to Earth is yours to define in play. The default is Basic-survivor with a chip on the shoulder about Earthers who never knew what a real apple cost — but the narrator does not enforce this if your play diverges. The protagonist's interiority is yours.

### Names

The default working name for the protagonist in the project files is **Henry**. You can override on Day 0 — when the Captain asks your name, give whatever name you want. The narrator will use it from then on. *Beratna* still applies regardless.

## How to talk to the narrator

Speak naturally. *"I look around the office for anything unusual."* *"I ask Aanya what she's been working on."* *"I knock on Kit's door."* *"I sit at the bar and wait for Pavel to drink the second beer."*

The narrator will render. If something is ambiguous — *"I confront Soji"* — the narrator may ask: *"With what specifically — what do you say first?"*

If you want to know something out-of-character — *"how does Survey Corps pay structure work?"* — ask out-of-character. The narrator will tell you.

If you want to reflect or journal in-character, the narrator can produce journal entries at natural quiet moments (end of day, on the shuttle, in the bunk). Two to four per playthrough is appropriate.

## A short manifesto

This project is built on a few commitments worth stating up front.

- **Discovery over exposition.** The world is rendered, not explained. You will figure out what the Spike is. You will figure out who Soji is grieving for. You will figure out why Kit will not talk about Mateo. The narrator does not pre-load you.
- **Consequences honored, including small ones.** If you say *"pay"* when the Captain asks why you're here, she will remember. If you sleep with one corp's offer (Cortez's drink), the others register it. Nothing on the ledger is free.
- **People over types.** Soji is not "the corporate Earther." She is a Lagos Methodist who came up through Basic and watched her son die for $2,400 and now signs cover sheets she does not read. Cortez is not "the Mars patriot." He is a man whose two boys live underground and want a sky. The narrator's job is the specific person; your job is to meet them.
- **The work is the work.** A microbiologist with samples and a chemistry deck and ten days can find a great deal. The narrator does not bottleneck biology for plot pacing. What you can find, you find.
- **You are not the savior of the planet.** The biome is on a thousand-year clock. The Spike will outlast all of us. You are filing a report. The report's reach is real, and limited, and yours.
