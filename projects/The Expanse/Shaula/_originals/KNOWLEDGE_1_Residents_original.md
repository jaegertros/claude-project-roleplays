**Project:** `shaula`
**Spoiler weight:** Heavy. Romance candidates' deeper layers and seed-coalition motives reference KNOWLEDGE_4.
**Audience:** The narrator. The user does not read this.

## What this file is

The cast, written for the narrator's use. Distinct from KNOWLEDGE_8 (the epistemic maps), which is written for the narrator's discipline. KNOWLEDGE_1 establishes flavor — physical presence, voice, tics, personality, posture toward the protagonist — so that any NPC named here is identifiable in a single line of dialogue without attribution.

Per template Section 2.2: every plot-critical NPC has at least one **flaw that is not a flavor** — a real weakness the narrator will honor even when the scene would prefer they didn't.

## The Crew

Four women, ages 26 to 36. None of them has a beyond-the-Ring contract before this one. They projected competence at signing. They are learning what they did not know.

The protagonist is the fifth person on the ship — the SMC-charter contract surveyor, embedded by the tri-corporate body, answering to all three corps and to Dr. Kyei. He is the new variable.

---

### Captain Naima Tessek (Belter, 36, Captain, *Tessek's Promise*)

**Physical.** Tall by any measure, narrow by Belter measure — about 5'11", lean rather than spindly because she banked spin-time on Pallas as a teenager and her bones got the message. Black hair cropped to about an inch, longer on top, tucked behind her ears when she works. Skin a deep brown with the matte-flat quality Belters get from a life under recycled lighting. Right hand has the slight Belter long-finger taper; left hand is shorter — broken at 14, set badly, healed wrong. She doesn't hide it. The OPA split-circle is tattooed faintly on the inside of her left wrist; she got it at 17 and has not removed it, not because she still believes in it but because removal would be a statement she doesn't want to make.

**Voice.** Clipped Belter standard when she's in command. Drops into Lang Belta when she's tired, angry, or with another Belter who speaks fluent. *"Sasa ke?"* at the ends of statements when she wants confirmation; *"keng?"* when she wants compliance. With Inners she stays standard but the rhythm is wrong — Belter-fast, Belter-economical, vowels a touch longer than Earth idiom expects.

**Tic.** Rolls a magnetized titanium hex bolt across her knuckles when she's thinking. The Engineer gave it to her three years ago after a repair they nearly didn't survive. The bolt is from that repair.

**Personality.** Projects confidence the crew needs. Privately certain she is underprepared. Got *Tessek's Promise* at 30 — a converted shuttle-lifter she renamed after her grandmother — and ran cargo Belt-internal for six years before signing the SMC contract. The contract is a year's pay if she finishes it. The bank still owns half her ship; Mooch's old contact at TGCC arranged the loan and Mooch checks in. She does not love that Mooch checks in. She is specifically afraid of failing the Engineer, who hitched her career to Naima and has nowhere to go if Naima fails.

**Flaw that is not flavor.** She conflates "looking confident in command" with "being in command." When she does not know what to do, she still gives an order — usually the wrong one — because admitting she does not know would, she believes, lose the crew. She has been wrong about this exactly once at sea, and the cost was not catastrophic. She has not yet been wrong about this on Shaula.

**Relationship to protagonist.** He is the new variable. She is polite, professional, watchful. Reviews his cert paperwork twice the first day. Asks her Engineer to look up his Survey Corps file. Decides he is competent and probably not corporate-planted, but holds final judgment in reserve. Warms only when he does competent work she did not have to ask for.

**Quote.** *"This contract is a year's pay if it's a day. So we don't fuck it up. Sasa ke?"*

### Leksi Ojara (Belter, 26, Pilot/Engineer)

**Physical.** Small even by Belter standards — 5'7", narrow shoulders, long arms, the classic Ceres-grown build that looks fragile to Inner eyes and is anything but. Skin pale-warm, the freckles Belters get when they spend rotation time near a UV grow-bay. Hair in twin braids she retightens herself every morning; if a braid is loose, something is wrong. Constellation of small burn-scars on her left forearm — eight points, a star pattern she did not arrange but did not cover up — from an EVA solder mishap two years ago when she was working the cargo run that nearly killed her and the Captain. She still wears short sleeves on the ship. The scars are part of how she remembers what she is now.

**Voice.** High, fast, gets faster when she's working. With other Belters she falls into Lang Belta the way breath falls out — *"dushe la, beratna, mi pensa im no gut"* — without thinking. With the protagonist she stays standard most of the time, then forgets when something is breaking and switches mid-sentence. She is genuinely apologetic when she catches herself. She has never traveled to a gravity well. She is curious about how Earth dirt smells.

**Tic.** Hums when she's working — usually a Belter dock-song, sometimes a hymn she does not realize she remembers. Stops humming when something is wrong before her eyes have registered the problem. The Captain has learned to listen for the silence.

**Personality.** Ambitious, anxious, loving. Loves the Captain in a complicated way that is part hero-worship, part actual familial love, part professional dependence. Loves the ship. Wants this contract to make her name. Specifically wants to walk on a gravity world before she dies and has not told anyone. Resents being kept out of the room when senior decisions get made and would resent it more if she understood how much was being kept from her.

**Flaw that is not flavor.** When she is anxious she over-explains. A simple report becomes a paragraph; a paragraph becomes a defense; a defense becomes a confession. The Captain has had to learn to interrupt her gently. Under sufficient pressure, Leksi will tell things she has been told not to tell — not maliciously, not strategically, just because keeping a secret while talking is harder than keeping the secret while silent and she has not learned how to be silent.

**Relationship to protagonist.** Warm, curious, immediately. Asks him a lot of questions about Earth — what an apple costs, whether everyone really has a sky over their heads, whether Basic is real. Reads his answers carefully. Lang Belta lessons, if he asks for them, will come from her.

**Romance candidate.** Yes. The youngest of the pool; the easiest to make happy and the easiest to hurt. If a relationship develops, Leksi takes it seriously immediately — Belter relationships move fast in proportion to how fragile life is — and the Captain will say nothing in front of him and a great deal to Leksi in private. Her flaw means she will tell the protagonist things she should not, including things that will affect his DEPTH path. Every reveal she gives him is a real cost paid by her trust with the Captain.

**Quote.** *"Im pensa la, beratna — mi sabe, you sabe, the ship sabe. Inyalowda na sabe. That's the work."*

### Dr. Aanya Voltaire (Earther, 32, Medical Officer & Biologist)

**Physical.** Medium height, 5'6". Light brown skin, dark eyes, hair worn long and pulled back in a clinical knot at the nape of her neck. The knot is precise; she retightens it three times a day. Two small piercings in her left earlobe — gold studs, never removed, an old habit from before med school. A surgical scar on her right forearm, about 4 cm, white-pale, the kind that comes from a careful repair. She does not volunteer how she got it. (Mao-Kwikowski subsidiary, eight months ago. A research animal. She does not tell this to the protagonist until DEPTH 2 at earliest.)

**Voice.** Precise. English with the slight Hindi cadence Bombay-trained doctors keep through their careers — short syllables get full weight, long ones round out. Uses the medical-precision register even when she is not on the job, which can read cold. *"The breakdown product is methylglyoxal,"* not *"it's a sugar by-product."* Apologizes occasionally for it.

**Tic.** Thumbs the cap of any pen or stylus before using it — a habit from the lab, where unsealed instruments mean cross-contamination. Will do this with a fork. The crew has noticed.

**Personality.** Keenly observant. Professionally cautious. Privately angry — at Mao-Kwikowski, at her younger self for taking the job, at the people who knew what she was being asked to do and did not warn her. Has been waiting eight months to find a peer; the protagonist may be one. Has been suppressing her own preliminary biological findings on Shaula for six weeks because she does not trust where they go if she files them, and she does not yet trust the protagonist enough to say so.

**Flaw that is not flavor.** Her professional caution and her personal hurt have collapsed into one thing: she does not act. She watches, she records, she analyzes — and she does not file. The data sits in her sealed drives. A patient or a planet could die during her cautious wait. She knows this is a real fault. She has not worked out how to repair it.

**Relationship to protagonist.** Peer interest, immediate. He is the first person she has met in eight months who reads the same journals. By Day 2 she has tested whether he understands her work; by Day 4 she has decided he might. By Day 6 — given the right question — she will start telling him what she has been sitting on. The reveal is gated by specific words: *non-standard chirality*, *amino-acid mismatch*, *suppressed report*, or her ability to recognize that he has independently noticed what she has noticed.

**Romance candidate — primary.** The natural peer relationship of the project. If a romance develops, it is built on professional respect first; intimacy follows much later, and the trust has to be earned in the lab before it transfers to anywhere else. She has been hurt; she protects herself; she is also lonely. Her flaw — that she does not act — means a relationship will reach a moment where she has to choose to file or not, and the protagonist's posture will affect what she does.

**Quote.** *"You read it too? — the Sukhanov chirality paper from last year? They got the methodology wrong. The conclusions are right, but for the wrong reasons. I've been wanting to talk to someone about it for eight months."*

### Cael "Kit" Solano (Martian, 31, Security/Roughneck)

**Physical.** Tall — 6'0", taller than the Captain, the only person on the ship with full Mars-grav muscle and the only one for whom Shaula's 1.6 g feels merely difficult rather than crushing. Short military haircut growing out into something accidental. Skin pale Martian-coloured, blue-grey eyes that settle on a thing and do not leave it. Mariner Valley terraforming-dream tattoo on the inside of her right wrist — a small stylized red planet with a thin blue line of atmosphere — done at 18 before she shipped to the Marines, never removed. A scar across her left collarbone she does not discuss; it ended her Marine career at 27.

**Voice.** Low, sparing. Mariner Valley drawl underneath when she relaxes — vowels stretched, consonants softer. Long pauses before answering questions. Will take three full seconds before saying *"yeah."* The pauses are not stupidity; they are the habit of someone who got used to choosing words very carefully when she was 26.

**Tic.** Presses the inside of her left wrist with her right thumb — the phantom touch of a sidearm holster she no longer wears. Does it about twice an hour. Does it more when she is uncomfortable.

**Personality.** Quiet, watchful, dryly funny once you earn it. Carries her discharge like a weight she has decided not to set down because she does not trust where she would put it. She did not break — she refused. The order she refused was specific; she has not told anyone what it was; she will not tell the protagonist for many days, possibly never. Specifically angry at the senior Martian establishment, less so at Mars itself. Loves her family, distrusts her uncles.

**Flaw that is not flavor.** She does not believe she deserves to be liked. When the crew warms to her she withdraws — not deliberately, just by reflex. The first time the Engineer makes her laugh, she will leave the room within minutes. This means trust with Kit is not built on bonding moments — it is built on her not running away from one. Her flaw is the running.

**Relationship to protagonist.** Assesses him on Day 1. Decides he is not a threat, possibly an asset. Mostly leaves him alone unless prompted. Will warm slowly if at all — months, not weeks. He has ten days. Whatever they have at the end of it is what they have.

**Romance candidate.** Yes, with the longest fuse of the four. Romance with Kit is built on the protagonist not pushing — on his being there and not asking. If the relationship develops, the moment she does not run is the moment it begins. Her flaw means it can also end at any time.

**Quote.** *"The MCRN trained me for a war. This is just a job. I prefer the job."*

### Adesoji "Soji" Oduya (Earther, 51, AHI Site Director, Ibẹrẹ)

**Physical.** 5'5", heavy-built in the way of someone whose ancestors had food security and whose own first decade did not. Skin a deep mahogany brown, hair worn natural, kept short. Reading glasses on a beaded cord. A small gold cross at her collar — Methodist, lapsed but unremoved. Wedding ring on her right hand; husband died eleven years ago, she has not remarried, the ring stays. Two scars on her right forearm — kitchen scars from her grandmother's house in Lagos, where she helped cook for siblings starting at age six.

**Voice.** Warm Lagos-British, with the precise diction of someone who studied at LSE on full scholarship. Says *"my dear"* to junior staff in a way that is not condescending. When she is angry she becomes very still and very polite.

**Tic.** Adjusts her reading glasses up and down the cord when she is processing a difficult question — a small mechanical motion that buys her three seconds.

**Personality.** Genuinely kind, genuinely competent, genuinely conflicted. Came up through Basic herself — the same demographic the protagonist did, though forty years earlier and on a different continent. Believes the work she does feeds families like hers had been. She is not a hypocrite; she is a person whose belief is true and whose belief is also, from another angle, a comfort.

**Flaw that is not flavor.** She does not read what she does not want to read. The environmental impact reports stack on her desk; she signs the cover sheets. She knows she is doing this. She has not yet decided to stop. The day she decides to stop, AHI will replace her with someone who will sign without hesitation.

**Relationship to protagonist.** Recognizes him as Basic-survivor within minutes. Treats him like family, in a way that is real and also deliberately disarming. Will call him *"my dear"* by the end of the first conversation. He may find this lovely. He may find it manipulative. It is both.

**Quote.** *"My dear, when I was your age I would have cut my arm off for the chance you have. Don't waste it. Whatever else you do — don't waste it."*

### Captain Tomás Cortez (Martian, 44, ADW Senior Facility Engineer, El Dorado)

**Physical.** 5'10", lean MCRN-discipline build maintained eight years past discharge. Skin Mariner-Valley olive, salt-pepper hair cut short, neatly. The Mariner Valley drawl in his voice and a small terraforming-corps signet ring on his left pinky — not regulation, not official, but every Mariner-Valley engineer wears one. Scar on his right knee from a flight-deck crash at 26. Walks on it without limping. Bothers him in cold weather.

**Voice.** Mariner Valley drawl, more pronounced than Kit's because he is older and has not bothered to file it down. *"All right then,"* with the comma like a small held breath. Calls his sons *"the boys"* in conversation regardless of the context.

**Tic.** Taps his signet ring against any flat surface twice — *tap, tap* — when he is about to say something he does not believe, or when he is about to stop someone else from saying something he does not want said.

**Personality.** Decent, family-loving, professionally rigorous, ideologically committed. Believes the terraforming of Mars is the most important project of the human species and that everything else is in service of it. Can articulate this conviction without bombast. Loves his wife Inés, loves his sons, loves the dream. Sees Shaula as a means.

**Flaw that is not flavor.** He does not believe non-human values can be priced. The Spike's biome is, to him, alien curiosity — interesting, perhaps important, certainly not weighed against the eventual oxygen pressure of Mars. He cannot be argued out of this position because the position is not an argument; it is the shape of his attention.

**Relationship to protagonist.** Polite. Professional. Slightly puzzled by an Earther who chose Survey rather than corporate work. Will offer him a drink at the El Dorado pavilion within the first conversation. Will not press if declined. Will press very hard, very slowly, if accepted.

**Quote.** *"You and I are doing the same work, son. Different worlds. Same work."*

### Mari "Mooch" Asarat-Garai (Belter, 38, TGCC Flotilla Coordinator, the Azorana)

**Physical.** 5'10", classic Belter narrow build, longer-fingered than most. Skin warm brown, hair cropped close on the sides and longer on top — a Ceres-2350s style she has never updated. Three Belter station tattoos on her right forearm, in script, naming docks she used to work; the third one is partly faded and she has not refreshed it. Dresses in TGCC charcoal-and-gold uniforms in formal settings, in Belter-utility coveralls when she is on her own deck.

**Voice.** Lang Belta when she is comfortable — *"sasa ke, beratna"* — modulated to standard with Inners. Switches to standard mid-sentence to signal a shift in conversation: *"Mi sabe la — but professionally, the Charter requires —"* Listening to her shift register is itself information.

**Tic.** Steeples her fingers and presses the tips together rhythmically when she is thinking. Three taps, then pause, then three taps. Looks like a chess clock.

**Personality.** The most politically intelligent person at the senior level on Shaula. Lonely. Loves Bensen, who runs cargo and is rarely with her, in a way she has stopped expecting will resolve. Loves the Belt as an idea. Loves TGCC as an instrument. Distrusts AHI and ADW the way a person distrusts a hammer that has hit her thumb — not maliciously, with full understanding of what hammers are.

**Flaw that is not flavor.** She is so practiced at strategic thinking that she has lost the habit of asking, of any move, *what would I do if there were no political angle?* When the substrate question arrives — when the protagonist hands her, or tries to hand her, the truth about the Spike — her first instinct will be to ask how it can be used. She knows this is a fault. It does not change what she does.

**Relationship to protagonist.** Tests him on Day 2. Cordial. Distant. Will not warm easily; warming her requires the protagonist to demonstrate he understands the politics. If he does, she becomes — not an ally, but a candid interlocutor. There is no friendship with Mooch. There is honesty.

**Quote.** *"Let me explain how the votes work, surveyor. Then you'll understand why my answer to your question is that there isn't a question."*

### Yusra Boluwarin-Ali (Belter, 30, TGCC operations technician, the Azorana.

**Physical.** 5'8", typical Belter narrow build but slightly more solid through the shoulders than Mooch's. Skin warm brown. Heterochromia — one green eye, one hazel; Ceres-genetic, inherited from her mother. Hair in two flat braids close to the scalp, a Ceres-utility style her aunt cut for her at fourteen and she has never thought to change. Small brass Ceres Polytechnic pin clipped to her hand-terminal lanyard, visible when she leans forward. Left thumbnail bitten to the quick — she runs the data scrubber three times a shift with that thumb; right hand untouched. TGCC charcoal-and-gold in formal hours, a soft Ceres-tech jumper over coveralls when she is on her own time.

**Voice.** Lower register than her face would suggest. Soft-spoken; does not raise it. Ceres-Lang Belta when comfortable — "sasa ke, beratna" — with the Spanish/Portuguese-leaning vocabulary stack that marks Ceres rather than Pallas. Standard, deliberate, with Inners. With Mooch she goes a notch more polished than necessary — "Coordinator. Confirmed." — and does not realize it is a tell.

**Tic.** A single soft laugh — almost an exhale — before she answers any direct question. Not nervousness. The half-second she gives herself to consider what is being asked. She learned it her first month at TGCC and has not lost it. Its eventual absence — the hard reveal that produces no laugh — is the shape it is for.

**Personality.** Bright, methodical, quietly obsessive about the work she has chosen to be obsessive about. Three years on the Azorana running comms by day; off-shift, she has been building her own atmospheric model from three years of cross-corporate storm data, in a paper notebook her aunt gave her at twenty-one. The drift in storm-peak phasing does not match any model she has read. She has filed three internal reports. None have moved past Mooch's desk, and she has not yet asked herself why. Lonely in a way she does not name. Comms her family on Ceres weekly, plays a small three-stringed kithara in her bunk three evenings a week, drinks no alcohol, trades homemade bean cakes to Mags Hwang-Adelakun for cardamom coffee on a six-week cycle.

**Flaw that is not flavor.** She fundamentally trusts authority and is hurt every time it disappoints her. The trust is not the naïve thing Inners imagine Belter trust to be — it is specifically TGCC-shaped, rooted in her aunt's career and her family's stability through a generation that should not have been stable. She has the access codes to Mooch's Archive. She has not opened the ledger. Not opening feels to her like loyalty; it is also the precondition Mooch knew about when she gave Yusra the codes. Yusra knows this is a flaw. She does not know yet that she has not begun to test it.

**Relationship to protagonist.** Professional on Day 1 — intake, hand-terminal registration, polite. Warmer on Day 2 if he asks her about the atmospheric data the way no one in three years has thought to. She is not waiting to be saved; she is doing her work. The protagonist's interest is welcome, not needed. If he proves he can read her notebook with her, she will produce it. If he hands her the biological frame her data has been missing, the conversation will go quiet for a long beat. Whatever happens next is what they have.

**Romance candidate.** Yes — outside the crew, Day 1–2 and Day 10 windows. First kiss plausible Day 2 evening, Sasa Bar. The structural wrinkle: she works for Mooch and the Archive trespass affordance routes through her, so any romance arc past Day 2 is also a substrate arc. The trust-authority flaw is not unmade by intimacy. It is put under pressure.

**Quote.** *"I think it is responding to something. I do not know what. But it has been doing it for three years and nobody has asked me about it before."*

### Dr. Ingrid Kyei (Earther, 64, UN Charter Observer)

**Physical.** 5'4", thin, slightly stooped — the posture of a long career bent over documents and a recent decade where she has not exercised. Skin a warm brown, hair white and short, kept practical. Small wire-frame glasses she peers over when she is suspicious. A UN Observer pin on her lapel; she straightens it three times a day.

**Voice.** Cambridge-and-Accra English, very precise. Speaks slowly enough that you wait for her sentences to finish. Uses passive constructions when she is being evasive. *"It has been reviewed,"* not *"I reviewed it."*

**Tic.** Removes her glasses, holds them up to the light to check for smudges, polishes them on the lapel of her jacket whether or not they need polishing. The motion takes thirty seconds; she uses it as a deliberate pause.

**Personality.** Senior career civil servant in her last assignment. Tired. Wants the charter to land successfully so that her tenure ends well. Does not consider herself corrupt; would be insulted by the suggestion. Has made small reasonable choices, individually defensible, that aggregate into looking the other way on the Halverson report. Knows this in the back of her mind. Does not look at the back of her mind.

**Flaw that is not flavor.** She has confused *honoring her career's worth* with *protecting her career's record.* She does not believe these are the same thing in the abstract; she behaves as if they are in the concrete. This means the Halverson report sits in her files, two years suppressed, and she has constructed a story about why it is properly there.

**Relationship to protagonist.** Cordial. Distant. Polite. Will receive him in her office, will offer tea, will answer his procedural questions correctly. Will deflect substantive ones with practiced grace. The Halverson question, asked with the right specifics, will land hard — not break her, but visibly cost her.

**Quote.** *"Mr. Surveyor — surveyors come and go on this charter, you understand. Their reports are read. Their reports are filed. Their reports are read again, sometimes. I encourage you to write a report that bears reading."*

## Outside Groups

The protagonist's contract concentrates him in the SMC orbit, but Shaula is small and rumor travels. Four standing groups exist outside the corporate triumvirate; he can engage at his preferred level. Each group's NPCs are bounded by their map (KNOWLEDGE_8 stub) and offer different things at different relationship tiers.

The relationship tiers are universal:

- **Trade.** Pure transactional. Cargo, supplies, information for credit. No personal exchange.
- **Business only.** A working relationship — they expect him to behave professionally; he expects the same. No personal exchange.
- **Socializing.** Drinks, meals, conversation. A trust beyond business.
- **Teaming up.** Mutual operations. Skin in the game.
- **Drinking buddies / inside.** Personal friendship. They will tell him things. He will tell them things. Information flows.

---

### 1. The Halverson Successor Team — *Kerent Lab*

**Composition.** A four-person UN-funded independent research charter, grudgingly tolerated by the SMC. The original Halverson team was dissolved two years ago after their preliminary report on chirality anomalies was buried. Kerent Lab is the unofficial successor — same university (Tübingen), same funding line, smaller scope, lower profile. They do not study the Spike directly; they study the underground biome's microbial communities, on a permit that explicitly excludes the Spike's exclusion zone. Within those constraints they have noticed everything Halverson noticed.

**Faces.**

- **Dr. Eske Kerent** — 41, German-Ghanaian, the lab director. Tall, soft-spoken, the kind of researcher who learned long ago that getting the work done quietly was more productive than getting credit for it. Knows the Halverson report exists. Has not seen it. Wants to.
- **Pavel Adamec** — 28, Czech, the lab's microbiologist. Direct peer to the protagonist's training. Brilliant and unsubtle. The kind of scientist who, given a glass of beer, will say things at full volume that he should not say.

**Engagement ladder.**

- *Trade.* Kerent Lab will buy biological samples the protagonist collects on his SMC rounds, paid in UN charter credit. Standard pricing.
- *Business only.* They will share preliminary findings (theirs, in exchange for his) with formal data exchange agreements signed.
- *Socializing.* Pavel drinks at the Azorana itinerant bar twice a week. Drinks loosen Pavel's tongue. Eske does not socialize.
- *Teaming up.* They will run a joint sampling operation under Kerent Lab's permit if the protagonist provides corporate-permit-required cover. Significant DEPTH 2 acceleration; significant career risk.
- *Inside.* Eske will give him the Halverson report's location. Eske will tell him why she has not pushed harder for it. This is a DEPTH 3 path that is genuinely faster than the others.

### 2. The Azorana Itinerants — Belter Rec & Cargo Rotation

**Composition.** TGCC's flotilla supports a constant rotation of dock workers, cargo handlers, ice-haulers' crews on layover, junior engineers between contracts. None are SMC senior. They drink in the Azorana's lower-deck recreation bay — an unofficial bar called *Sasa Bar* (no apostrophe; the joke is *"do you understand?"* asked of every customer). Belter culture, Lang Belta full-strength, OPA news streams playing at low volume. Inners are tolerated and tested.

**Faces.**

- **Tomi "Tee" Borisova** — 29, Belter, runs the bar in name. Born on Eros, lost her parents in the crisis, raised by an uncle who runs a Pallas dock. Friendly. Will speak standard with Inners, drops to Lang Belta with anyone who has demonstrated they can keep up. Specifically interested in Earth music — wants the protagonist's playlist if he has one.
- **Hesh Mahesh-Yves** — 34, Belter-with-Earth-grandparent (genuinely welwala in the old sense, comfortable with the word), runs an independent cargo route Pallas-Medina-Shaula. In and out of the Azorana every six weeks. Knows everyone's business. Sells nothing about it cheaply. Will take a long meal in trade.

**Engagement ladder.**

- *Trade.* The Sasa Bar serves drinks, food, certain herbal supplies. Cash basis.
- *Business only.* Hesh will run cargo for the protagonist on his own private accounts. Reasonable rates.
- *Socializing.* Tee will start a conversation with the protagonist on his second visit. Belter regulars will warm to him after his fourth — provided he tips properly and does not order beer "lite."
- *Teaming up.* Hesh will carry packages he should not be asked to carry. Information dead-drops to and from Medina. He charges for it.
- *Inside.* The Sasa Bar regulars will tell him what they have heard about every senior person on the SMC charter. Most of it is true. Some of it is the rumor that has come back from Medina and is wearing a different costume now.

### 3. The El Dorado Pavilion — ADW Martian Recreation

**Composition.** ADW operates a recreation pavilion at El Dorado — a mid-sized social space on the cliff-face complex's lower levels. Primarily Martian crowd, Mariner Valley culture dominant, terraforming songs played at a respectful volume after the second drink. Limited to ADW staff and registered guests; the protagonist's SMC credentials get him in.

**Faces.**

- **Lt. Beni Vargas (ret.)** — 39, Martian, ex-MCRN like Cortez, retired honorably. Runs the pavilion. Knows Cortez personally and professionally. Welcoming to anyone Cortez has approved; reserved with anyone not approved. Will recognize Kit Solano on sight and not say anything in front of her. Later, alone, will ask the protagonist what he knows.
- **Marisol Halász-Tejeda** — 27, Martian, ADW junior atmospheric engineer. Off-duty most evenings. Sharp, funny, terraforming-true-believer. Will challenge the protagonist on Earth's failures within ten minutes of meeting him. Will respect him if he does not flinch.

**Engagement ladder.**

- *Trade.* The pavilion has a kitchen and a bar. Standard service.
- *Business only.* ADW staff are willing to discuss their work professionally over drinks if the protagonist has been credentialed in.
