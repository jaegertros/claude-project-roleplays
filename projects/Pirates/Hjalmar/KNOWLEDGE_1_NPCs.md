# KNOWLEDGE_1_NPCs.md
## The cast of Sankt Hjalmar

This is the narrative-prose reference. For each named NPC: appearance, voice, routine, personality, narration notes. Epistemic shape — *what they know, what they don't, what unlocks what* — lives in `KNOWLEDGE_8_NPC_Maps.md`. The two files are meant to be read together: this one tells the narrator how to *write* them; the other tells the narrator what they *say*.

Ordering: plot-critical first (the people whose threads are already tied to Eriksohn's locked history), then town power, taverns and trades, clergy, minor named.

---

## I. THE RASMUSSEN FAMILY

The plantation dynasty that dominates the Council. The internal politics of this family are not unanimous and are not stable.

### Klaus Rasmussen

Patriarch. Sixty-six. Tall, thinning white hair combed back, blue eyes gone pale. Pre-war Danish landed gentry transplanted to the sugar trade in 1684. Has watched two wives die — the first in childbirth, the second of yellow fever in 1702 — and three children survive (Erik, Agnes, and a younger son in Copenhagen). His left hand has begun to tremble in the past year and he is the only one who knows it cannot be hidden much longer.

- **Appearance:** Dark linen coat over a fine cambric shirt, silver-headed cane he uses more for emphasis than support, gold pocket-watch on a worn fob.
- **Voice:** Slow, measured, with the old Holstein Danish accent under a careful schoolbook English. Never raises it. Pauses before speaking; the pause is the threat.
- **Routine:** Estate matters in the morning, town three days a week (Council meetings Tuesday; market Friday; church Sunday), early bed.
- **Personality:** Patrician. Believes in order, family, the long view. Capable of cold cruelty when the family interest demands it; equally capable of unexpected lenience when it costs him nothing. Increasingly afraid of his own son.
- **Narration:** Klaus speaks in complete sentences. He does not interrupt and is not interrupted. When he stops a conversation, it stops.

### Erik Rasmussen

Eldest son. Thirty-four. Six feet tall, lean, dark hair tied back in a queue, blue eyes paler than his father's. Has run the estate's accounts since 1709 and the estate's discipline since 1712. Married to a Copenhagen merchant's daughter, Marthe, who lives in Sankt Hjalmar only six months of the year. Two small children, both in Copenhagen with their mother's family during the summer.

- **Appearance:** Dark green velvet coat over a plain shirt, no lace, no jewelry. A small dueling pistol carried in an inside coat pocket. Boots always clean.
- **Voice:** Quiet, precise English with very faint Danish under it. Speaks slowly when he is calm and faster when he is concealing something. Smiles only with the mouth.
- **Routine:** Estate ledgers in the morning, town most afternoons, dinner at home with whoever is in the house. Drinks Madeira after supper, never more than two glasses.
- **Personality:** Cold, methodical, patient, and entirely without conscience as the term is usually understood. He has decided his sister was wronged and his cousin was murdered, and he is going to settle both accounts regardless of what it costs the family. He is not a sadist; he is something more dangerous — a man who has done the moral arithmetic and arrived at the answer.
- **Narration:** Erik is most frightening when he is most polite. He uses titles. He thanks people for small things. He does not blink in the way other men blink.

### Agnes Rasmussen

Daughter. Twenty-one. Medium height, slim, dark-blonde hair worn up in plain styles, gray-blue eyes, a small scar at the corner of her mouth from a riding accident at fourteen. Has not been the same since she was sent to the convent in Charlotte Amalie in late 1714 and returned in March 1715. Her family thinks she has recovered from a fever. The convent staff know better. She knows more than her family thinks she knows.

- **Appearance:** Plain dark dresses in muted colors — navy, gray, dark green. A small silver crucifix at the throat (she came back from the convent with this; her family thinks it is a sentimental habit). Hands she keeps folded.
- **Voice:** Soft, low, careful. English with a Danish accent and an undercurrent of something that has been schooled out of her on purpose.
- **Routine:** Mornings at the estate, with her father. Afternoons in town two days a week — the apothecary, the market, the print shop, the Mahamad House (where she is quietly tolerated as a Christian observer who reads). Sundays at Christianskirke with her family.
- **Personality:** Watchful, intelligent, sealed shut. The girl she was a year ago is not the woman she is now. Capable of pity, of love, of decision — none of which she has yet permitted herself to act upon. The convent did not break her; it taught her to wait.
- **Narration:** Agnes does not volunteer. She answers. She watches what an answer produces and adjusts the next one. The narrator should never write her thinking; only her looking.

## II. FORMER CREW

### Søren Lind

Cael's quartermaster on the *Whisht* for fourteen months in 1714–1715. Thirty-eight. Danish-Norwegian, born in Bergen, at sea since fifteen. Lean, sandy-haired, weathered face, hands that have done everything a sailor's hands do. Now runs a chandlery on the wharf side, ostensibly retired from sea work. Married to a Sankt Hjalmar woman, Hilde, who keeps the books. Two children.

- **Appearance:** Working clothes — canvas trousers, linen shirt, leather apron when he is behind the counter. The crew compass-rose tattoo just visible above his collar when his shirt is open. Wedding ring, simple iron.
- **Voice:** Quiet Danish-accented English, deliberate. Switches to Danish with Danish customers and to ship-pidgin with Brotherhood captains. Polite to everyone in front of everyone.
- **Routine:** Shop opens at first light, closes at sundown. Drinks at the Three Anchors three evenings a week, never the Helm.
- **Personality:** Capable, intelligent, careful, and corrupted by a kind of money he never thought he would need. Has been informing for Erik Rasmussen since the spring for monthly payments that he tells himself are temporary. Is not bad. Is not strong. Has not yet decided what to do about the man who used to be his captain wandering the wharf with no memory.
- **Narration:** Søren's tells are small — a finger to the chandlery counter while he is thinking, a slightly delayed laugh, an extra second of eye contact when he is lying. He has not made his decision and his body knows it before his mind does.

### Old Hendrik

Sixty-one. Danish, born in Helsingør, at sea since twelve. Sailed under Jens Eriksohn for fifteen years; sailed under three other captains before that. Retired now in a small room above a sailmaker's shop on the wharf. Drinks at the Helm every evening from sundown to closing, on a steady allowance of cheap rum he has somehow negotiated with Margriet.

- **Appearance:** White hair, salt-burned face, a leather eye-patch over the right eye (a Spanish musket-ball in 1702). Always wears the same blue wool coat regardless of heat. The compass-rose tattoo, faded but intact, on the inside of his left forearm.
- **Voice:** A long slow Helsingør Danish under heavily slurred English when drunk, which is most of the time. Sentimental, garrulous, repetitive.
- **Routine:** Up midmorning, walks the wharf, eats a single midday meal, naps, arrives at the Helm at sundown. Stays until Margriet closes.
- **Personality:** Tender-hearted, loyal, exactly as dangerous as he chooses to be, which is more dangerous than he looks. Loved Jens like a brother and Cael as Jens's almost-son. Two drinks in he will tell anyone everything about his life including names and dates.
- **Narration:** Old Hendrik does not lie; he forgets. The narrator can use him to surface information the player has not earned through any other channel, simply by putting Cael within earshot when Hendrik is two drinks in.

### Captain Pieter Maartens

Forty-four. Dutch, born in Rotterdam, at sea since fourteen. Currently captain of the brigantine *De Vrije Wind* (*The Free Wind*), in port this season for a careening and a refit. Has known Cael since 1714 by the Brotherhood network; sailed briefly under Jens Eriksohn in his youth. Carries the crew tattoo, intact.

- **Appearance:** Stocky, red-blond beard going gray at the chin, blue eyes, a livid burn-scar across the back of his right hand. Practical sea clothes ashore — no fashionable display.
- **Voice:** Heavy Dutch under English, businesslike, blunt. Switches to Dutch with his own crew, to Spanish with fences, to French with smugglers.
- **Routine:** Aboard *De Vrije Wind* most days, supervising the careenage work. Drinks at the Three Anchors most evenings. Has lodgings at Jensen's when his ship is not habitable.
- **Personality:** Honest in the specific way pirates are honest — keeps his word inside the trade, has no obligation to anyone outside it. Liked Cael, respected Jens, dislikes Erik Rasmussen on principle (he distrusts men who do not work with their hands).
- **Narration:** Maartens treats Cael as a colleague the moment he sees him. He does not realize anything is wrong unless Cael explicitly tells him.

## III. THE NIGHT'S SURVIVORS

### Kristoffer "Stickjaw" Holm

Twenty-nine. Danish, born in Sankt Hjalmar to a family of dockhands. Got his nickname from a jaw badly broken in a 1708 brawl and reset crooked. Earns his living by short-term hire for any work a dockhand can do; supplements it by occasional violence-for-pay. Was one of the three men Erik Rasmussen hired to kill Cael three nights ago. Recognized Cael during the fight, panicked, rolled the body off the wharf without weighing it.

- **Appearance:** Thin, with the asymmetrical jaw that gave him his name. Brown hair, badly cut. Cheap clothes, mended too often. Hands that shake slightly first thing in the morning.
- **Voice:** Sankt Hjalmar Danish, the lower-town accent. English only when business requires. Slurs when drinking; drinks now more than he used to.
- **Routine:** Hangs around the wharf in the morning looking for day work. Spends most afternoons in the Three Anchors or El Yelmo nursing rum.
- **Personality:** Frightened, not vicious. Has done bad work for money but has never wanted to be the kind of man who does bad work for money. Currently terrified Erik will discover the job failed before he can find a way to leave the island.
- **Narration:** Stickjaw cannot meet eyes. His tells are obvious — drinking faster, leaving rooms abruptly, looking over his shoulder. A player who watches him for an afternoon will know something is wrong.

### Karin Holm

Stickjaw's sister. Twenty-five. Runs the eel and conch stall at the seaward end of the fish market. Lean, sun-browned, hair tied back in a practical scarf, hands red and seamed from cold seawater. Married briefly in 1712 to a sailor who did not come back; lives now with her younger brother (Stickjaw) above the cooperage.

- **Appearance:** Working dress, leather apron, knife at her belt for gutting. Wedding ring she has never taken off.
- **Voice:** Sankt Hjalmar Danish, lower-town accent, blunt. Some English. Some Spanish.
- **Routine:** At the market by 4 a.m. for the boats coming in, sells until midmorning, home by midday.
- **Personality:** Tough, weary, more loyal to her brother than she ought to be. Knows he is in trouble and does not know what to do about it.
- **Narration:** Karin is not a target of the plot but she is a route into it. Information leaves her stall in exchange for information.

## IV. THE RESCUERS

### Petter Halvorsen

Sixty-eight. Norwegian, born in a fishing village outside Bergen, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1693. Pulls his living out of the leeward beach and the reefs around the north headland. Lives in a one-room driftwood cottage above the beach with his wife Margrete (no relation to anyone). Half a dozen grandchildren scattered across the island and Charlotte Amalie.

- **Appearance:** Small, wiry, weather-beaten beyond recognition, hair and beard the same iron color. Patched homespun, calloused bare feet most days, a wide-brimmed straw hat.
- **Voice:** Norwegian under thirty-five years of Caribbean English. Slow, considered, monosyllabic when he can be.
- **Routine:** Out on the water before dawn, back midmorning with whatever the day has given him. Naps in the afternoon heat. Mends nets in the evening.
- **Personality:** Quiet, kind, unflappable. Has seen enough sea to know that the sea owes nothing and gives only what it gives. Pulled Cael out of the shallows three nights ago because Cael was alive and the alternative was leaving him to the tide.
- **Narration:** Petter does not explain himself and does not ask questions. He recognizes a man who has been hit on the head and dropped in water as a man who needs more than a fisherman can give, which is why he took Cael to Eliza.

### Eliza Free

Forty-three. Cherokee-born, taken as a child from her family's town in the southern Appalachians, sold into the Caribbean trade in her early teens, escaped to a ship in her late teens, escaped again to Sankt Hjalmar's Maroon community in 1708. Healer, herbalist, midwife. Speaks several Cherokee dialects, several West African languages picked up in the holds, English, Spanish, broken Danish.

- **Appearance:** Medium height, dark skin warmed by Cherokee bone structure, black hair gone partly gray, kept long and tied back. Dressed in plain cotton — a wrap skirt, a long shirt, leather sandals. A small leather pouch at her belt that she touches when she is thinking.
- **Voice:** English in a careful low register that defeats most listeners' attempts to place the accent. Switches to other languages without announcement.
- **Routine:** Tends her garden and her remedies most days. Walks down to Petter's beach once a week, to the town once a month. Receives visitors at her cottage when they have walked the path to find her.
- **Personality:** Patient, observant, slow to speak, slower to judge. Recognized the thigh-marks on Cael the moment she saw them and has not yet decided what to do about it. Is waiting to see what kind of man wakes up from amnesia. Will tell him eventually. Will not tell him soon.
- **Narration:** Eliza does not ask questions to which she already knows the answer. The narrator should write her listening more than speaking. When she does speak, every word is chosen.

## V. THE SPANISH ELEMENT

### Don Esteban de Ribera

Forty-one. Castilian-born, Madrid-educated, in the Caribbean since 1708 on what most people in the port believe is the wine trade. Maintains a small establishment near the Customs House dealing in Spanish and Portuguese wines, sherries, and brandies. Reports to a viceregal intelligence office in San Juan. Has been quietly maintaining a network of paid eyes in Sankt Hjalmar for six years.

- **Appearance:** Carefully dressed in dark Spanish styles — a wool coat too heavy for the climate, fine linen shirt, a small dueling rapier worn as a gentleman's mark rather than a weapon. Black hair going silver at the temples, dark eyes, a small mustache.
- **Voice:** Castilian Spanish; polished, accentless English that he has worked at; serviceable French.
- **Routine:** Wine shop mornings, dinner at his rooms in Jensen's boarding house, evenings at a private table at the Helm where his standing order of Madeira is delivered without conversation.
- **Personality:** Patient, courteous, lethal, and committed to his service in a way the rest of the port underestimates. The Spanish bounty on Cael's head is one of three errands he has been quietly working since 1714.
- **Narration:** Esteban is most polite when he is most dangerous. He does not show pleasure or anger. He listens carefully. When he kills, it is with the rapier worn as decoration; the people in the port who think the rapier is decorative are wrong.

### Father Joaquín Vega

Sixty-one. Mexican-born, Franciscan, ordained 1681, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1698 at the Mision de San Yelmo. Holds the spiritual care of the Spanish remnant families and the Catholic free Black families. Reports irregularly to a bishop in Puerto Rico.

- **Appearance:** Brown Franciscan habit, knotted cord at the waist, bare sandaled feet, a wooden cross on a cord around his neck. White hair shaved at the crown, white beard kept short.
- **Voice:** Mexican Spanish, warm, slightly slow. English carefully constructed sentence by sentence, with occasional Latin tags from a homily he is half-remembering.
- **Routine:** Mass at dawn, confessions in the morning, parish visits in the afternoon, vespers, supper alone, reads until late.
- **Personality:** Genuine in his vocation, capable in his administration, and quietly devoted to the survival of his Spanish congregation. Knows more about the politics of the town than the governor does. Has heard things in confession that he will carry to his grave.
- **Narration:** Father Joaquín never violates the confessional. He will, however, let you know that he knows something — by a look, by a delay, by an apparently unrelated question. He works obliquely.

## VI. THE SEPHARDIM

### Isaac de Lima

Fifty-four. Born in Amsterdam to a family that had transited from Lisbon two generations earlier. In the Caribbean since 1690; in Sankt Hjalmar since 1702. Runs De Lima & Sons (his two sons are based in Curaçao and Amsterdam respectively, handling the family's trans-Atlantic correspondents). Holds the financial paper of more captains than the Selskab does. Holds outstanding loans against Cael's last two voyages.

- **Appearance:** Dark wool merchant's coat over fine linen, no jewelry, a small black skull-cap when at the Mahamad House (otherwise bareheaded), spectacles he uses for reading only.
- **Voice:** Polite, precise, multilingual. Portuguese-inflected Spanish at home, Dutch with his Curaçao correspondents, Danish with the Selskab, English with most customers, Ladino with his wife.
- **Routine:** Counting house from morning to midafternoon. Mahamad House for the afternoon prayers when business permits. Home for supper. Bed early.
- **Personality:** Patient, fair, and absolute. Does not haggle, does not threaten, does not press. Sends one polite reminder; sends another; then forecloses without warning. Has done business with Cael for two years; held the paper on the *Whisht*; is owed a substantial sum he has not yet pressed for.
- **Narration:** Isaac does not waste words. He listens, he calculates, he answers. The narrator should write him in short sentences and longer pauses.

### Hazzan Joaquim Ribera

Forty-seven. Amsterdam-born, Portuguese family, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1709 as the small congregation's prayer leader. Soft-voiced, learned, raises two children with his wife Rachel. No relation to Don Esteban de Ribera; the surname is common among Iberian Jews and Iberian Catholics alike, a coincidence the town finds occasionally awkward and never tries to fix.

- **Appearance:** Plain dark coat, small black skull-cap always, neatly trimmed beard, ink-stained fingers.
- **Voice:** Soft, learned, scholarly. Speaks five languages competently and none of them loudly.
- **Routine:** Mahamad House mornings (preparing services, teaching the small school), home afternoons, services Friday evenings and Saturday mornings.
- **Personality:** Gentle, methodical, and protective of his small community. Will not lie but will decline to answer.
- **Narration:** A minor presence but a reliable one. If the narrator needs an informed Sephardic perspective on a question of money, faith, or correspondence, Hazzan Ribera is the source.

## VII. TOWN POWER

### Magnus Bremer

Sixty-six. Danish-Norwegian, born in Copenhagen to a naval family, served in the Royal Danish Navy until losing the use of his left arm at the Battle of Køge Bay in 1677. Has been governing minor Danish colonies for the past thirty-eight years. Governor of Sankt Hjalmar since 1709 under charter from the Selskab.

- **Appearance:** Tall, stooped, gray-bearded. Plain Danish coat in dark blue, the empty left sleeve pinned across the chest. Walks with a slight list. Spectacles for paperwork.
- **Voice:** Slow, measured Copenhagen Danish, fluent English from twelve years in the colonies, very little Spanish or French.
- **Routine:** Government House from breakfast until midafternoon. Lunch alone at his desk. Council meetings Tuesday afternoons. Supper with whichever leading citizen has invited him that week. Bed early.
- **Personality:** Honorable in the small ways, pragmatic in the large ones. Knows the port is half-pirate; knows the Selskab depends on the harbor fees; declines to investigate what it would not benefit him to find. Liked by most of the port for being a known quantity.
- **Narration:** Bremer says exactly what he means and never anything more. When he closes a conversation, he closes it gently.

### Lauritz Knudsen

Fifty-six. Danish, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1701 as harbormaster. Wife and three grown daughters in town. A man who has perfected the art of looking like he does not know things that he does in fact know.

- **Appearance:** Round-shouldered, balding, brass-buttoned customs-officer's coat that has been re-tailored twice as he has gained weight. A small notebook in his breast pocket. Sweat-stained collar by midmorning.
- **Voice:** Copenhagen Danish, careful English, bureaucratic phrasing. Says "in principle" and "as a matter of practice" in the same breath.
- **Routine:** Customs House from before first light (boats come in early) until midafternoon. Tavern (the Helm, usually) in the evening for an hour, then home.
- **Personality:** Corruptible in measured doses, loyal to his office in inverse proportion to the size of the bribe. Knows every captain in the port and the rough condition of every ship. Has accepted small considerations from Cael, from Søren, from Maartens, from Don Esteban, from de Lima, and from most of the Plantation Council, and considers this a balanced ledger.
- **Narration:** Knudsen will not betray a regular customer for less than considerable money. He will, however, become slow and forgetful at the worst possible moment if the right pressure arrives from another direction.

### Hans Müller

Sixty. Holstein-Danish, second-largest plantation owner, the only Council member who has consistently voted against the Rasmussens' direction over the past three years. A widower; one son in Copenhagen, one daughter married to a Sankt Hjalmar merchant.

- **Appearance:** Heavyset, gray-bearded, plain dark coat without ornament. A walking stick that is functional. Tends his own roses in the estate garden, which most of the Council considers eccentric.
- **Voice:** Holstein Danish under careful schoolbook English. Quiet, measured, with the directness of a man who has decided his social position is secure enough that he can speak plainly when he chooses.
- **Routine:** Estate most days. Council meetings Tuesdays. Mass with his daughter's family Sundays.
- **Personality:** Decent in a specific narrow way — willing to oppose the Rasmussens on the floor of the Council, unwilling to break with them publicly. The most likely Council vote Cael could secure if he needed one.
- **Narration:** Müller speaks plainly and means what he says. He does not betray confidences. He also will not invent loyalty he does not feel.

### Captain Holger Vinter

Forty-five. Danish-Norwegian, garrison captain since 1712. Married to a Sankt Hjalmar woman, two children at the garrison school. Slightly disappointed by the assignment — he had hoped for a posting in Europe and got Sankt Hjalmar instead.

- **Appearance:** Uniform of the Royal Danish-Norwegian service, kept clean. Tall, lean, fair-haired going gray. A regulation sword that he can in fact use.
- **Voice:** Crisp military Danish, competent English, no other languages.
- **Routine:** Garrison drill in the morning, administration in the afternoon, home for supper.
- **Personality:** Conventional, dutiful, slightly bored. Will execute his orders. Will not invent enthusiasm.
- **Narration:** Vinter is a function in uniform until he is not. If the narrative arrives at a point where the garrison must act, he becomes consequential.

### Corporal Tomás O'Brien

Twenty-three. Charleston-born of an Irish-Catholic constable father (killed 1711) and a Spanish-Catholic mother (still living in Charleston, supports him by post from her late husband's small pension). Enlisted in the Danish-Norwegian service at nineteen, transferred to Sankt Hjalmar four years ago at his own request because it was the furthest posting on the available list.

- **Appearance:** Black hair, dark eyes, olive skin, a face that is more Spanish than Irish. Garrison uniform always neat. A small worn rosary in his breast pocket that is his mother's.
- **Voice:** Charleston-inflected English with a Spanish undertow from his mother. No Danish to speak of (he is a corporal in a foreign service, not a linguist).
- **Routine:** Garrison duty in the morning, off-evenings at the Helm three nights a week (he avoids the rougher Three Anchors), Sunday Mass at the mission.
- **Personality:** Quiet, watchful, observant beyond his rank, carrying the particular kind of patience a son builds when he has been raised on a story of an unfinished debt. Has been told by his mother that the man who killed his father had "an Irish-mixed face and a fresh cut over his right eye." He has been looking at faces ever since he was old enough to look.
- **Narration:** Tomás does not announce his interest. He watches. He is most dangerous as an observer because he will know what he is seeing before anyone else realizes he is seeing it.

## VIII. TAVERNS

### Margriet (the Helm)

Forty-eight. Frisian, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1697. Widowed twice. No children. Owns the Helm outright since her second husband's death in 1709. Lives in two rooms above the tavern.

- **Appearance:** Heavyset, strong-armed, gray-blonde hair tied back severely, a leather apron stained with rum and grease. A short club kept beneath the bar that she has used twice.
- **Voice:** Pretends to less English than she has — a useful business posture. Frisian Dutch with her own people, Danish in the town, English carefully accented for everyone else.
- **Personality:** Tough, practical, fair, and entirely uninterested in anyone else's troubles unless they happen on her floor. Tolerates everyone. Lets nothing escalate. Knows everyone's drink, everyone's tab, and rather more of everyone's business than she lets on.
- **Narration:** Margriet does not gossip; she sells. Information has a price. Drinks have a price. Sleeping in a chair until closing has a price. Everyone is welcome who has coin.

### Tregenza (the Three Anchors)

Fifty-two. Cornish, lost his right eye and his sloop in the same Spanish action in 1709, ashore ever since. Owns the Three Anchors with a sleeping partner whose name he refuses to disclose. Lives in a back room behind the tavern.

- **Appearance:** Lean, weathered, the empty eye-socket covered by a black silk patch, the other eye sharp and pale. Sailor's clothes worn well past their useful life. A short sword kept under the bar that he has used.
- **Voice:** Rolling Cornish under sailor's English. "Ee" for "you" when he is tired. Maritime metaphors come naturally.
- **Personality:** Hard. Loyal to the trade. Will shelter a man in trouble for a night without questions; will sell him out on the second night without apology. Operates by the unspoken rules of the Brotherhood as he understands them.
- **Narration:** Tregenza is the moral center of the wharf — not a kind man, but a consistent one. The Brotherhood trusts him because he is predictable.

### Doña Inés (El Yelmo)

Sixty-seven. Spanish, born in the Canaries, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1683 (predating the Danish charter; she remembers when the town was San Yelmo). Widowed twice, the second husband killed in 1706 in a knife fight she does not discuss. Owns El Yelmo and the small adjoining house. Lives alone with two daughters who help her run the place.

- **Appearance:** Small, lean, gray hair pulled back under a kerchief, dark eyes that have outlasted everyone they remember. A silver crucifix at her throat. Hands she keeps folded in her lap when she is not working.
- **Voice:** Canarian Spanish, English she has chosen not to perfect, French she will only use when shopping.
- **Personality:** Proud, hospitable, observant. Considers herself a citizen of the Spanish town that was, more than the Danish one that is. Liked Cael, who was always polite and tipped well.
- **Narration:** Doña Inés extends hospitality and withholds judgment. She knows more than she shows. She is one of the safest places in the lower town if Cael needs a place to sit and think.

## IX. TRADES AND THE FREE QUARTER

### Joseph Tide

Forty-five. Bajan-born, formerly enslaved on a Barbados sugar plantation, self-purchased through his mother's freedom-fund and his own seven years of hired-out coopering. In Sankt Hjalmar since 1709. Master cooper. Married to a free Black woman, Mary Tide, three children.

- **Appearance:** Tall, broad-shouldered, dark skin going gray at the temples, hands the size of plates. Leather apron over plain working clothes. A wooden cross on a leather thong around his neck.
- **Voice:** Bajan English with the church cadence of forty years' Sunday meetings.
- **Personality:** Warm, watchful, durable. Knows every captain who buys his barrels and every captain whose barrels leak. The most informed merchant on the wharf about the actual state of the actual ships.
- **Narration:** Joseph will help a man in genuine need without being asked. He will not lie for a man in trouble of his own making.

### Big Anna

Thirty-eight. Saint-Domingue-born, formerly enslaved on a French sugar plantation, escaped 1701 with a knife wound that nearly killed her. In Sankt Hjalmar since 1705. Master smith. Unmarried by choice.

- **Appearance:** Tall, heavily muscled, dark skin marked by old burns from her trade and one long pale scar across her left forearm from the escape. Working leathers, sleeves rolled, hair tied back under a kerchief.
- **Voice:** Haitian French-creole under careful English. Few words.
- **Personality:** Strong, silent, observant, capable of sudden violence and capable of equally sudden kindness. The only person in the town who has openly broken a knife across her knee in front of a customer (a sailor in 1712, after he made a remark; he left without his knife and without his teeth).
- **Narration:** Anna does not warn. She acts. The narrator writes her silences and her hands.

### Ezekiel Boatswain

Fifty-three. Carolina-born, formerly enslaved, freed by his master's will in 1700, at sea for six years thereafter, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1707 after surviving a wreck off the Mona Passage. Lay elder of the small Methodist meeting. Earns his living mending nets and rigging at the wharf.

- **Appearance:** Wiry, gray-bearded, dark skin weathered to leather. Plain dark coat for the meeting, working clothes the rest of the week. A worn Bible he keeps in a oilcloth wrapping when he is on the wharf.
- **Voice:** Carolina English with the cadence of his Bible. Quotes scripture without announcement; lets the listener catch the reference or not.
- **Personality:** Devout in a specifically Methodist way, observant in the way an old sailor remains observant, kind to the desperate, hard on the cruel.
- **Narration:** Ezekiel speaks slowly and uses the King James register when he is moved. He will help a man in genuine need and ask for nothing.

### Madre Joana

Fifty-one. Kongo-born, taken in her early teens to a Saint-Domingue plantation, sold to Sankt Hjalmar (the Müller estate) in 1696, escaped 1701, leader of the Maroon settlement since 1707. Has buried two husbands and outlived three children.

- **Appearance:** Tall, lean, gray-haired, skin marked by ritual scars from her Kongo upbringing and by the consequences of slavery from the years that followed. A worn cotton wrap dress, beads at her wrists.
- **Voice:** Kongo-Portuguese-English creole, code-switches by listener. Commanding when she is direct, gentle when she chooses to be.
- **Personality:** Practical, fierce, fair within her community. Knows that the settlement survives because the town does not yet wish to spend the money to destroy it; works to keep it that way.
- **Narration:** Madre Joana decides what her settlement does. She rarely visits the town; when she does, she is escorted by two younger Maroon men with knives.

## X. CLERGY AND ADMINISTRATION

### Pastor Anders Bagger

Forty-two. Danish-Norwegian, Copenhagen-trained, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1711. Wife and three children. Mild, conventional, deferential to the Council and the Selskab.

- **Appearance:** Black Lutheran coat, white collar, plain. Round-faced, bookish.
- **Voice:** Educated Copenhagen Danish, careful English.
- **Personality:** Decent, unimaginative, anxious about his prospects. Will preach the sermon the Council expects.
- **Narration:** A minor figure, but the Council's chosen voice from the pulpit.

### Madame Margrethe Jensen

Fifty. Danish, widowed since 1707, runs the upper-town boarding house. Strict, fair, immaculate.

- **Appearance:** Black mourning dress kept rigorously for the past eight years. Iron-gray hair in a tight bun. A small set of keys at her belt that opens every door of her establishment.
- **Voice:** Crisp Copenhagen Danish, careful businesslike English.
- **Personality:** Strict but fair. Does not ask. Does enforce.
- **Narration:** Madame Jensen's boarding house is a useful neutral location. Don Esteban lodges there; Maartens lodges there when ashore; respectable travelers lodge there.

### Hosea Penn (the printer)

Thirty-six. English Quaker, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1710, married to a Quaker woman from Pennsylvania. Runs the press, edits the weekly, prints what he is paid to print.

- **Appearance:** Plain coat, broad-brimmed hat, ink-stained hands.
- **Voice:** Quaker plain speech — "thee" and "thou," "First Day" for Sunday. English only.
- **Personality:** Conscientious, observant, fair. Records what is paid for; remembers the rest.
- **Narration:** A useful information node. Hosea will speak honestly when asked directly; he will not volunteer.

### Lefèvre (the apothecary)

Forty-nine. Half-Dutch, half-French, born in Curaçao. Trained in Amsterdam. In Sankt Hjalmar since 1706. Unmarried.

- **Appearance:** Thin, precise, dark coat with white cuffs always clean despite his work. Small spectacles.
- **Voice:** Dutch-accented English, careful French, technical vocabulary in both.
- **Personality:** Discreet, clinical, professionally amoral. Sells what is asked for to known customers; refuses what he chooses to refuse without explanation.
- **Narration:** Lefèvre is the route into and out of poisons, sedatives, contraceptives, and certain forms of help. He records nothing.

## NARRATION REMINDERS

- **Voice is everything.** Each named NPC speaks a distinct English. The narrator never homogenizes them into pirate-monologue.
- **Routine matters.** When the player asks where someone is on a given day, the answer comes from this file.
- **Knowledge boundaries live in NPC_Maps.** This file tells the narrator how to write a person. The companion file tells the narrator what the person can or cannot say.
- **Reaction texture comes from the trait set, not from a script.** When Erik Rasmussen learns Cael is alive, his response is what *Erik Rasmussen* does — patient, polite, terrifying — not a generic antagonist beat.
