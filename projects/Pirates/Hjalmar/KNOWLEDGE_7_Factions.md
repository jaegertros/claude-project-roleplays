# KNOWLEDGE_7_Factions.md
## The eight blocks of Sankt Hjalmar

The town is not a monolith. It is eight overlapping factions, each with its own composition, goals, internal divisions, and quiet enemies. Most NPCs belong to more than one. The narrator pulls from this file to understand *why* a given NPC reacts the way they do — every action in this port is read through faction membership before it is read through individual personality.

For each faction: who's in it, what it wants, where it splits internally, what it wants from Cael, what it fears, and how it relates to the other seven.

---

## 1. THE SELSKAB / THE COMPANY

The Sankt Hjalmar Selskab — the chartered company that holds the island from the Danish-Norwegian crown.

**Composition:**
- Governor Magnus Bremer (head of Selskab operations on the island)
- Harbormaster Lauritz Knudsen (Customs House and harbor revenue)
- Captain Holger Vinter and the 28-man garrison (Fort Christiansborg)
- Corporal Tomás O'Brien and lower-ranking NCOs
- Pastor Anders Bagger (Lutheran establishment; technically Selskab-paid)
- Three Selskab clerks at Government House handling administration
- The Selskab board in Copenhagen (off-map; receives quarterly reports)

**What it wants:**
- Harbor revenue stable and growing.
- The port quiet enough that Copenhagen does not ask questions and the British Admiralty does not consider intervention.
- The Brotherhood tolerated as long as Brotherhood activity does not visibly cross the Selskab's bow.
- The plantation belt productive.
- The garrison adequate but not expensive.

**Internal split:**
- Bremer himself does *not* fully serve Selskab interests — he diverts about 18% of revenue into a private discretionary fund (see `KNOWLEDGE_4_Hidden.md` Section VII). The split is not factional but personal: Bremer believes the Selskab undervalues the island's actual needs.
- Knudsen serves whoever pays him — Selskab on paper, several private interests in practice.
- Vinter is a conventional officer; if the Selskab issued orders, Vinter would execute. He has not yet been asked anything that conflicted with his sense of duty.
- Tomás O'Brien is loyal to the garrison and to a private hunt that has nothing to do with Selskab business.
- Pastor Bagger preaches what the upper town expects to hear.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- Nothing specifically. Cael is one of many Brotherhood captains routinely processed by Knudsen. If Cael becomes loud or visible, the Selskab will want him not to be.

**What it fears:**
- A scandal that brings Copenhagen attention. Particularly: any public murder, any conflict with Spain that requires the garrison to act and reveals how inadequate the garrison is, any treason against the charter (e.g., the Storgaard petition becoming public).
- A British naval interest. The arrival of a Royal Navy frigate to "discuss" anti-pirate cooperation would end most of what makes Sankt Hjalmar profitable.

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Plantation Council:** symbiotic; the Selskab needs the planters' goodwill and the planters need Selskab cover. Bremer is uneasy about the Council's wing that flirts with British protection.
- **Brotherhood:** tolerated, fee'd, quietly observed. The Selskab will not pursue a Brotherhood captain who pays his fees and kills nobody upper-town.
- **Spanish Remnant:** treated as a quaint minority. Bremer respects Father Vega personally.
- **Sephardim:** valued for commerce; the Selskab benefits from de Lima's capital network.
- **Maroons:** officially denied to exist; in practice tolerated because the cost of destroying them exceeds the benefit.
- **Free and Enslaved Black population:** legally regulated through the slave codes the Selskab enforces; the free Black community is taxed and tolerated.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** the Selskab fears its arrival above all else.

## 2. THE PLANTATION COUNCIL

The fifteen seated families who run the plantation belt and dominate the upper-town political life. Their economic weight is the largest single block in the town.

**Composition:**
- Klaus Rasmussen (patriarch, Trefoldighed, ~380 enslaved laborers)
- Erik Rasmussen (heir, increasingly running operational matters)
- Agnes Rasmussen (daughter, no Council seat, social significance)
- Hans Müller (Tre Kilder, ~210 enslaved laborers)
- The Krüger and Storgaard households (Rasmussen-aligned)
- The Pratts (English-descended; dissenting)
- Two coffee-hills planter families (Larsen and Voss; dissenting on the slave-market expansion)
- Three minor Rasmussen-cousin households on individual seats
- Two minor families bound to the Rasmussens by debt
- Various overseers on the smaller plantations (not Council members; structurally subordinate)

**What it wants:**
- Sugar prices stable or rising.
- Labor supply — meaning the slave trade — uninterrupted.
- Selskab cover from Spanish raiding and Brotherhood incursion onto the windward coast.
- Internal harmony (the Rasmussens) or the leverage to break it productively (the dissenting bloc).
- Eventually: the family inheritances secure across generations.

**Internal split:**
- 9-6 majority for the Rasmussen bloc.
- **The slave-market expansion vote** (see Hidden Section I) — Day 2 Council meeting. The Rasmussens will win.
- **The Storgaard petition to the British Crown** — a covert wing of the majority bloc planning to invite British protection. Klaus does not know. Erik does.
- **The succession question** — Klaus's failing health raises the question of when Erik takes the seat formally. Klaus has been delaying. Erik has been patient.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- The Rasmussen bloc, specifically, wants him dead. This is Erik's personal project; Klaus is willfully not asking; the rest of the bloc would not weep for a Brotherhood captain.
- The dissenting bloc has no specific interest in Cael but would side with him if a confrontation with the Rasmussens served their political position.

**What it fears:**
- Royal Navy intervention or a Spanish coastal raid threatening the windward valleys.
- The Storgaard petition becoming public before the Rasmussens have decided whether to bless it or smother it.
- A serious slave uprising. The plantation belt's labor outnumbers its overseers fifteen to one.
- Internal dissent within the Rasmussen bloc.

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** the planters are the Selskab's most important constituents; tension here is constant but mutual.
- **Brotherhood:** mixed. The planters buy slaves from slavers, not from Brotherhood captains, and view the Brotherhood as predatory rather than commercial. The Rasmussens specifically have a grudge over Frederik.
- **Spanish Remnant:** tolerated at distance; some Council families are quietly Catholic.
- **Sephardim:** essential commercial partners; the Council families borrow from de Lima.
- **Maroons:** considered a present-tense threat; expedition occasionally discussed and always postponed.
- **Free Black population in town:** treated as commercial counterparties when they have skills (smith work, coopering, sailoring) and as social inferiors at all times.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** the Storgaards quietly want it; the Rasmussens are ambivalent; the dissenting bloc fears it.

## 3. THE FREE BROTHERHOOD

The loose network of pirate and privateer captains who use Sankt Hjalmar as a careenage, refit, and trade port. Not an organization — a community of practice.

**Composition (currently in port):**
- Captain Pieter Maartens (*De Vrije Wind*, brigantine, refitting)
- Cael Eriksohn (*Whisht*, sloop, undermanned and damaged on the wharf)
- A handful of smaller captains and their crews
- Old Hendrik and former Brotherhood sailors now retired ashore
- Søren Lind (former quartermaster, ashore but still connected)

**What it wants:**
- A safe place to careen, refit, and resupply.
- Discreet fences for prize cargo (de Lima, Curaçao correspondents).
- Stable harbor fees and reasonable customs treatment (Knudsen).
- Information about Spanish patrols, treasure-fleet salvage news, and Royal Navy movements.
- The treasure-fleet wreck site — currently — is the most active commercial opportunity in the eastern Caribbean. Several Brotherhood captains will sail for it within the month.

**Internal split:**
- Brotherhood captains are nominally peers but in practice form clusters around shared history. Maartens and Cael share the Jens Eriksohn lineage; other captains have their own genealogies.
- The treasure-fleet question is currently splitting captains by appetite — some want to commit fast (Henry Jennings's coming raid will draw them); some want to wait and watch.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- Continued participation in the network. Maartens would offer Cael a place if Cael needed one.
- Cael's discretion about anything he knows about other captains' arrangements.

**What it fears:**
- Royal Navy attention.
- A Spanish operation in cooperation with the Selskab.
- A captain breaking the Articles publicly enough that the rest of the Brotherhood has to disown him.

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** tolerated by Bremer, fee'd through Knudsen.
- **Council:** wary mutual avoidance. The Rasmussens are now the Brotherhood's specific problem because of Frederik.
- **Spanish Remnant:** Brotherhood captains avoid Father Vega and the mission. Some Catholic-born Brotherhood sailors attend Mass privately.
- **Sephardim:** essential commercial partners; the financial backbone of Brotherhood operations.
- **Maroons:** quiet ally in some cases (the 1714 rifle deal); the Maroons consider some Brotherhood captains potential confederates.
- **Free Black population:** Brotherhood crews are unusually racially integrated; Brotherhood captains hire free Black sailors at standard shares.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** the Brotherhood's greatest fear.

## 4. THE SPANISH REMNANT

The descendants of the pre-Danish Sankt Hjalmar population, plus Spanish-Catholic transients who pass through.

**Composition:**
- Roughly 30 Spanish or Spanish-descended households in the Spanish Quarter
- Father Joaquín Vega and the Mision de San Yelmo
- Doña Inés and her two daughters at El Yelmo
- Don Esteban de Ribera (formally Spanish; not socially part of the remnant in the way the others are)
- A scattering of Spanish-speaking households in the Free Quarter (Catholic free Black families)
- Transient Spanish-speaking sailors on ships in port

**What it wants:**
- Survival as a recognizable community. The Danish takeover in 1683 reduced their political standing; they have absorbed it but not forgotten.
- The mission preserved.
- Quiet commercial life through the Spanish Quarter shops and El Yelmo.
- Continued connection to the Catholic episcopal authority in Puerto Rico through Father Vega's correspondence.

**Internal split:**
- The "old families" (here since before the Danish charter) and the "new arrivals" (post-1683) maintain a polite social distance.
- Father Vega and Don Esteban do not work in concert and do not entirely trust each other. Father Vega knows what Esteban is. Esteban does not know that Father Vega knows.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- Generally nothing. Cael is a Brotherhood captain, not part of the community.
- Don Esteban specifically wants Cael's head.
- Father Vega specifically wants — if he can manage it — for the conflicts in the town to resolve with less bloodshed than they otherwise would. He would help Cael obliquely if he saw a way.

**What it fears:**
- A new wave of Danish-Protestant pressure on the mission.
- Spain itself reasserting interest in the island (which would mean military occupation, ending the comfortable in-between state).
- Specifically: Don Esteban's network being exposed in a way that embarrasses the whole community.

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** politely tolerated; Bremer treats Father Vega with personal respect.
- **Council:** quiet commercial ties (some planters import Spanish brandies through the remnant); social distance.
- **Brotherhood:** wary distance. Spanish remnant memory of Brotherhood depredations on Spanish vessels is long.
- **Sephardim:** delicate. Spanish Catholic memory and Sephardic memory contain the Inquisition. Practical cooperation but no warmth.
- **Maroons:** old families' household help is occasionally Maroon-connected; Father Vega has heard confessions from Maroon-adjacent figures.
- **Free Black population:** the Catholic free Black households are part of the remnant.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** dreaded almost as much as by the Brotherhood.

---

## 5. THE SEPHARDIM

The Iberian-Jewish merchant community. Small in number, central in commerce.

**Composition:**
- Isaac de Lima and his household (counting house, two sons abroad)
- Roughly 22 other Sephardic households
- Hazzan Joaquim Ribera and his family
- The small school at the Mahamad House
- Transient Sephardic merchants from Curaçao, Amsterdam, and London passing through

**What it wants:**
- Continued commercial freedom.
- Religious quiet — the freedom to maintain the Mahamad House openly.
- Strong trans-Atlantic correspondence (Curaçao, Amsterdam, London).
- Information advantage in the port's commerce.

**Internal split:**
- A generational divide: older Sephardim remember the 1492 expulsion as immediate; younger Sephardim experience it as inherited memory. The older generation is more cautious about visible community profile.
- Commercial competition exists among Sephardic households but is muted by communal solidarity.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- Repayment of de Lima's outstanding loan.
- Cael's continued non-betrayal of various commercial arrangements he has made through de Lima.
- Specifically, no scandal involving the *Whisht*'s past cargo sales through the Sephardic network.

**What it fears:**
- A new wave of religious intolerance (most plausibly: the British Royal Navy bringing British anti-Jewish sentiment, or Spain reasserting and bringing Inquisition memory).
- A commercial collapse — particularly Don Esteban's network being exposed in a way that implicates the Sephardim by association.
- Pogrom-level violence. (The community has not experienced this in Sankt Hjalmar, but the community memory holds the possibility.)

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** commercial partners, formal correspondents.
- **Council:** lenders to many planter households; treated with formal respect.
- **Brotherhood:** financial backbone of Brotherhood operations.
- **Spanish Remnant:** delicate; see above.
- **Maroons:** no direct relationship; one Maroon settlement member is a former Sephardic-household enslaved laborer who escaped during a smallpox season.
- **Free Black population:** some commercial relationships with the cooperage, the smithy.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** feared.

---

## 6. THE MAROONS

The escaped enslaved community in the highlands. Lugar Quieto.

**Composition:**
- Madre Joana (leader, formerly enslaved at Tre Kilder)
- Roughly 70 people total — men, women, children
- Six former Brotherhood sailors who escaped both slavery and the sea
- Eliza Free (Cherokee-born; healer; partially set apart from the main community)
- Various young men and women raised in the settlement and never enslaved
- Newcomers (typically 2-4 per year escape successfully from the plantation belt)

**What it wants:**
- Continued survival of the settlement.
- Sufficient food, water, weapons, and intelligence to defend itself.
- Trade connections in the town that do not compromise location.
- Eventually: more of its own people freed from the plantation belt.

**Internal split:**
- Younger Maroons born in the settlement are restless; some want to take action against the Müller estate specifically.
- Older Maroons including Madre Joana counsel patience.
- A small faction wants to leave the island entirely on a stolen ship if a Brotherhood captain could be persuaded to help.
- Eliza Free is communal-but-apart; her recognition of Cael's marks puts her in a position the rest of the settlement does not share.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- If Cael's pre-amnesia connection to the rifle deal surfaces: continued partnership, more weapons, possibly transport.
- Otherwise: no specific interest in him.

**What it fears:**
- A Council expedition with garrison support.
- Betrayal by one of its own town allies.
- A hurricane destroying the settlement's stores.
- A larger Spanish or British presence that brings systematic anti-Maroon operations (as in Jamaica).

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** officially nonexistent. In practice the Selskab tolerates because the punitive expedition would cost too much.
- **Council:** the Müllers specifically have a long memory about the 1701 escape that produced Madre Joana. The other planter families recognize the settlement as a problem but have not personally lost laborers to it.
- **Brotherhood:** ally-of-convenience for some captains; no obligation either direction.
- **Spanish Remnant:** Father Vega has heard certain confessions that have informed his quiet practice of charity toward the settlement.
- **Sephardim:** no direct relationship.
- **Free Black population in town:** sympathetic and quietly supportive; some of the trade network runs through Free Quarter households.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** would mean systematic war.

## 7. THE FREE AND ENSLAVED BLACK POPULATION IN TOWN

The largest social block in the town if counted by head. Internally diverse and structurally divided.

**Composition:**

*Free residents:*
- Joseph Tide (cooper)
- Big Anna (smith)
- Ezekiel Boatswain (Methodist elder, sailmaker)
- Roughly 80 free Black households in the Free Quarter
- A scattering of free Black residents in the Spanish Quarter (Catholic households)
- Several free Black market women in the lower town
- Occasional free Black sailors on Brotherhood ships in port

*Enslaved residents (in town):*
- Domestic enslaved labor in Council household town houses (Else, the laundress at the Rasmussen town house, others)
- Dockworkers hired out by their owners
- Workshop labor in various trades
- Approximately 200 enslaved people resident in or working daily in the town

*Off-island/off-relationship:*
- The vastly larger plantation belt enslaved population (~4,200), which interacts with the town only through specific channels

**What it wants:**

*The free Black community:*
- Continued ability to operate their trades and households freely.
- Protection from re-enslavement schemes and abuse.
- The Methodist meeting house continuing.
- Information about families still enslaved on the plantations.

*Enslaved residents in town:*
- Survival; small advantages; eventually freedom or rest.
- Their own informational networks that bypass white attention.

**Internal split:**
- The free Black community is itself socially layered — established free households (Tide, Anna, Boatswain) and newer arrivals; Methodist and Catholic and non-religious; Bajan, Saint-Dominguan, Carolina-born, Cuban-born, African-born backgrounds.
- The free / enslaved divide is sharp and not bridged by shared ancestry. Free Black residents are *not* automatically allies of enslaved residents; some are explicitly distant.
- However, the informational network is largely shared — gossip moves across the line.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- The free Black community: nothing specific. He is one of many sailors passing through. Tide and Anna would help him if he behaved well; would refuse him without acrimony if he behaved badly.
- Enslaved town residents: nothing he can give them. They are watching everyone with the carefulness of those who have learned to.

**What it fears:**
- An intensification of slave codes (which a Council vote could produce).
- The Royal Navy bringing British slave-code practice.
- An outbreak of violence in the town that turns the white population paranoid.

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** distrusted but obeyed.
- **Council:** structurally subordinate; some commercial relationships with free trades.
- **Brotherhood:** mixed; some Brotherhood captains hire free Black sailors at standard shares, which makes the Brotherhood relatively trusted.
- **Spanish Remnant:** the Catholic free Black households are part of the remnant.
- **Sephardim:** professional respect (Tide does barrel work for de Lima's warehouse).
- **Maroons:** sympathetic. The Free Quarter is the primary channel of trade to the highland settlement. Madre Joana grew up enslaved at Tre Kilder; some Free Quarter families remember her.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** feared most by the free Black community, which knows how British practice differs from Danish.

## 8. THE ROYAL NAVY GHOST

The faction that is not in Sankt Hjalmar. Yet.

**Composition:**
- No persons in town. The Royal Navy's eastern Caribbean station is at Antigua and Jamaica. The nearest patrol vessels typically operate near Barbados and the Leeward Islands.
- The threat the Navy represents is real for every other faction.

**What it would want, if it arrived:**
- Suppression of piracy.
- Suppression of Spanish coastal raiding from English perspectives.
- Eventually: British political influence over the Danish charter — possibly through the Storgaard petition path, possibly through naval pressure.

**Internal split:**
- The actual Royal Navy is divided on West Indies policy. Some captains pursue Brotherhood predation aggressively; others quietly negotiate. Some take prizes themselves under flexible interpretations of orders.
- The Admiralty in London is divided on whether to commit naval resources to the West Indies anti-piracy effort or to focus on European threats.

**What it would want from Cael:**
- Hanging. The Royal Navy hangs Brotherhood captains.

**What it fears:**
- Nothing directly. The faction's strength is asymmetric.

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** would arrive offering "cooperation"; would effectively replace.
- **Council:** the Storgaard wing welcomes; the Rasmussens are split; the dissenting bloc fears.
- **Brotherhood:** existential enemy.
- **Spanish Remnant:** new occupier worse than the old.
- **Sephardim:** would bring British religious culture, which is more hostile than Danish religious culture.
- **Maroons:** would bring Jamaican-style anti-Maroon operations.
- **Free Black population:** would bring British slave code.

**Probability of arrival within the campaign window:**
- The Royal Navy will *not* arrive in Sankt Hjalmar within Cael's amnesia recovery arc. The narrator does not introduce a frigate in the harbor as a deus ex machina.
- The Navy can, however, exert pressure from a distance — news of a frigate at Charlotte Amalie, a rumor of a station rotation, a Royal Navy officer arriving on a packet boat asking questions.

## NARRATOR USAGE

Before any consequential scene involving a named NPC, the narrator considers:

1. **Which factions does this NPC belong to?** Most belong to two or three.
2. **What does the most important faction-membership want from the situation?**
3. **What internal split within that faction is currently active?**
4. **What would the NPC's faction need from Cael in this exchange?**

The narrator does not announce factional reasoning. The reasoning shapes the writing.

When the player's actions threaten or align with a faction's current goal, the narrator surfaces a faction reaction in the next scene — a Council household's social cooling, a Brotherhood captain's nod of recognition at the wharf, a Sephardic merchant's small additional courtesy, an extra cup of rum from Doña Inés that says more than the speech does.

Factions are not committees. They are pressures. The narrator writes the pressure.
