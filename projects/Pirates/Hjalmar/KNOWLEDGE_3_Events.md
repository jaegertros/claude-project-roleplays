# KNOWLEDGE_3_Events.md
## The timeline, the calendar, and the things that will happen

The narrator's chronology reference. Four sections:

1. **The pre-amnesia timeline** — what named NPCs remember from the past eighteen months that Cael also lived through. The narrator may have NPCs reference these events in dialogue without inventing.
2. **The scheduled calendar** — things that will happen at specific dates regardless of player action. The world does not stop for Cael.
3. **Trigger events** — things that fire when the player crosses a specific threshold (a location, a keyword, an NPC seeing a specific mark in a specific light). The "Bangs" of the campaign.
4. **The convergence window** — Day 10 to Day 14 — when at least two of the four ignition paths fire concurrently.

---

## I. THE PRE-AMNESIA TIMELINE

What the named NPCs of Sankt Hjalmar remember. Surface through NPC dialogue when triggered, never as exposition.

### 1714 — Spring

- **March:** Cael's sloop *Whisht* is named and outfitted for the first time. Søren signs on as quartermaster. Hendrik refuses to retire and signs on as boatswain for three months before going ashore for good. *(Hendrik will tell this story unprompted, twice in a row, if drinking.)*
- **April:** A small Spanish coastal raid near Cumaná. *Whisht* takes a Spanish coastal trader for sugar, salt-cod, and a chest of coin. *(Don Esteban first hears the captain's name in a San Juan report. He files it.)*
- **May:** Cael meets Agnes Rasmussen at a Tuesday-market in Christianstorv. She is buying ribbon for a niece. He helps her count change because the new cashier is slow. *(Agnes remembers what she was wearing.)*

### 1714 — Summer

- **June through August:** The affair. Cael ashore most of the summer for a careening and refit. Six months of letters between Agnes and Cael moving through Karin Holm at the fish market without Karin knowing what she was passing. *(Karin remembers the recurring sealed letters; she has not yet connected them to anything important.)*
- **August:** Agnes pregnant. She tells no one for six weeks. *(Father Joaquín hears this in the confessional of a household servant named Else who suspects her mistress's morning sickness. He has held the secret since.)*

### 1714 — Autumn

- **September:** Klaus Rasmussen discovers his daughter's pregnancy. Sends her to the Convent of Our Lady of Sorrow in Charlotte Amalie on the public story of a fever. The Sankt Hjalmar packet boat carries her on the 14th. *(The packet captain, a man named Anders Pløen, remembers carrying a tearful young Council daughter. He passes through Sankt Hjalmar once a month.)*
- **October:** Cael returns from a short cruise to find Agnes gone. The household tells him she is unwell. He is given no further information. He does not press because he was never given to press where he had no right. *(Søren remembers Cael's silence on this. So does Old Hendrik, who saw Cael drinking alone at the Helm two evenings in a row for the first time.)*
- **November:** Frederik Rasmussen, Klaus's nephew, takes up the purser's berth on a Rasmussen family merchant brig out of Charlotte Amalie. The brig sails for Cádiz with a partial sugar cargo.

### 1714 — Winter

- **December:** The *Whisht* takes the Rasmussen brig in the Mona Passage. The fight is bad. Frederik Rasmussen is killed by a pistol-ball. Cael's quartermaster Søren swears it was Cael who fired. The brig's survivors are put off in a longboat. The brig's cargo is sold to a Curaçao fence through Isaac de Lima's correspondent. *(Erik Rasmussen begins, immediately, to investigate. He hires a Charlotte Amalie investigator named Pedersen who has been gathering depositions since.)*

### 1715 — Spring

- **February:** Agnes gives birth to a daughter in the Convent of Our Lady of Sorrow. The child is named Margreta by the wet-nurse who takes her — a woman named Mette Madsen, who farms a small piece of land in the hills behind Charlotte Amalie with her husband. Sister Brigitte, the abbess, tells Agnes the child is alive and where she has gone the day Agnes leaves the convent. *(Brigitte remembers Agnes's face. So does Mette Madsen, who has seen her exactly once.)*
- **March:** Agnes returns to Sankt Hjalmar. She does not speak of the convent. The Rasmussen household considers her cured of a fever. The silver crucifix at her throat is new.
- **April:** Don Esteban de Ribera receives the standing bounty notice from San Juan: one hundred pesos on the head of the *Whisht*'s captain, proof of identity required. *(Esteban has not yet been able to confirm the captain's full identity. He notes that the *Whisht*'s clearances pass through Sankt Hjalmar and begins paying Knudsen for advance notice.)*

### 1715 — Summer

- **June:** *Whisht* sails on what will be her last successful cruise. Two prizes in five weeks.
- **July 30–31:** The Spanish treasure fleet wrecks off the Florida coast in a hurricane. News reaches Sankt Hjalmar by packet on August 8. Every captain in port reorients. *(Maartens lengthens his planned cruise. de Lima rewrites three of his outstanding policies. Knudsen begins receiving bribes from new directions. Don Esteban gets fresh urgency from San Juan.)*
- **August 24:** *Whisht* limps back to Sankt Hjalmar undermanned and damaged after a brush with a Spanish patrol vessel south of Mona. Cael personally signs her papers into the harbor with Knudsen. *(Knudsen remembers Cael's face was bruised. He did not ask.)*
- **August 26:** Cael pays off most of his crew. Keeps only Søren and two others on retaining wage.
- **Late August:** Cael drinks at the Helm three evenings in a row. Sees Agnes once in the market. She does not see him. Or she does and pretends. *(Hendrik remembers Cael's silence those evenings. Cael did not speak to anyone, including Hendrik.)*
- **September 9 (Friday):** Søren accepts his fifth monthly payment from Erik Rasmussen via an intermediary. The payment includes a specific question — *what is the captain's drinking schedule this week.*

### 1715 — Three nights before the campaign opens

- **September 12 (Friday) evening:** Cael drinks alone at the Helm. He leaves at approximately 11:30 p.m.
- **September 12 (Friday) — 11:45 p.m.:** Three men hired by Erik are waiting in the alley behind the Helm. Stickjaw, Jens Kruse (a hired Charlotte Amalie dockhand), and a third man — Mads Holst, who Cael kills with a knife to the femoral artery in the first sixty seconds of the fight.
- **September 12 (Friday) — midnight:** Cael is struck on the back of the head with a belaying pin. Dragged toward the wharf. Stickjaw, panicking after recognizing Cael during the fight, rolls the body off the wharf without weighing it. He and Jens Kruse leave separately. Mads Holst's body is hauled into the harbor by Stickjaw before they leave; it has not yet surfaced. *(Karin Holm sees her brother come home with blood on his shirt and asks no questions.)*
- **September 13 (Saturday) — dawn:** Petter Halvorsen, working the leeward beach with his small boat, finds Cael in the shallows. Carries him to Eliza Free in the highlands.
- **September 13 (Saturday) through September 14 (Sunday):** Cael unconscious or feverish in Eliza Free's cottage. Eliza recognizes the thigh-marks. She tells no one, not even Madre Joana.
- **September 14 (Sunday) — midnight:** Jens Kruse boards a Charlotte Amalie packet under a false name. He is gone before anyone in Sankt Hjalmar knows the job did not go as Erik commissioned.
- **September 15 (Monday) — dawn:** Cael wakes knowing nothing. Eliza walks him to the edge of town on the path down from the leeward beach. She does not give him her name. She does not return after he turns to look.

**The campaign opens at this point.**

## II. THE SCHEDULED CALENDAR

Events that will happen at the specified time regardless of player action. The narrator does not need to introduce them visibly until the date arrives.

| Day 1 (Monday) afternoon | The brigantine *Sankt Anna* (Danish merchant, 80 tons) is clearing Customs to sail Wednesday for Charlotte Amalie. Knudsen handling. |
| Day 2 (Tuesday) | Market in Christianstorv. Council meets at Government House at 2 p.m. (Klaus, Erik, Hans Müller, four other senior Council members present.) |

| Day 3 (Wednesday) evening | Methodist meeting at Ezekiel Boatswain's. |

| Day 4 (Thursday) afternoon | A Spanish patrol vessel — the schooner *Concepción de Santa Isabel* out of San Juan — anchors briefly in the harbor mouth, sends a longboat ashore, and departs after two hours. The lieutenant aboard reports to Don Esteban. *(Routine. Esteban learns nothing actionable. But the patrol's presence is noted by every Brotherhood captain in port, including Maartens.)* |
| Day 5 (Friday) morning | A letter from the Convent of Our Lady of Sorrow arrives at the Rasmussen estate on the morning packet from Charlotte Amalie. The letter is addressed to Klaus and asks discreetly about the continuing fees for the child Margreta. | Day 7 (Sunday) morning | Christianskirke service. Pastor Bagger preaches. Klaus and Erik attend; Agnes attends with them. *(If Cael is in the upper town that morning, he may see her at a distance walking to or from the church. She will see him at the same distance.)* |

### Week 2

| Day | Event |
|---|---|
| Day 8 (Monday) | Full moon (Julian calendar). Tides larger than the rest of the week. |
| Day 9 (Tuesday) | Market and Council meeting. Erik will quietly raise — for the first time, in private after the meeting — the topic of "a returned man on the wharf" to one trusted Council ally. *(This is the moment the second-phase hunt begins to organize. The conversation is overheard by a household servant, Else, who reports to Klaus through her own channels.)* |
| Day 10 (Wednesday) | **Convergence window opens.** See Section IV. |
| Day 11 (Thursday) afternoon | Maartens's *De Vrije Wind* completes her careening. She begins reloading provisions. Three weeks to sail unless something delays. |
| Day 12 (Friday) | Another packet from Charlotte Amalie arrives. Possible second letter from the convent or from the Madsen family about the child's care. |
| Day 13 (Saturday) afternoon | Hans Müller hosts a small dinner at his town residence. Several "middling" Council families attend. Klaus does not. *(If Cael has surfaced as a political question, this dinner is where Müller will ask his guests what they have heard.)* |
| Day 14 (Sunday) | **Convergence window closes.** By end of day at least two ignition paths are active. |

### Week 3 and beyond

- **Day 16–17:** A hurricane warning will reach the port if the long swell from the southeast continues. The narrator does not need to commit to a hurricane *hitting*; the threat is enough to reshape every plan in the port.
- **Day 18 (Thursday):** Maartens's *Vrije Wind* finishes provisioning. Could sail Day 21 if weather permits.
- **Day 21 (Sunday):** Earliest possible date for *Vrije Wind* to sail. Maartens may offer Cael a berth depending on what has happened by this point.
- **Day 22 (Monday):** End of month. End-of-month packet to Curaçao departs. Last reliable mail before the height of hurricane season.
- **Day 28 (Sunday):** Likely week of Henry Jennings's raid on the Spanish salvage camp at the Florida wreck site. *(This is roughly the date the rumor of the raid reaches Sankt Hjalmar — about a week after the raid itself. By the time the rumor arrives, Brotherhood captains who can refit fast are already gone.)*

## III. TRIGGER EVENTS

Events that fire only when a specific player action meets a specific narrator-side condition. The narrator does not pre-announce these; they activate when the trigger fires.

- **Cael walks past the Mahamad House on a Saturday morning while the service is in progress.** A small commotion as he passes — an old Sephardic merchant, Aaron de Castro, recognizes him from a 1714 transaction and turns to point him out to a fellow worshipper. The pointing is visible; the recognition is brief. de Castro will not speak in the street; if Cael returns at another time, de Castro will engage carefully.
- **Cael wears his coat with the sleeves rolled in any tavern.** If Old Hendrik is present and has had two drinks, see his reveal trigger in NPC_Maps. The most likely tavern-wide accident in the campaign.
- **Cael says "Whisht" aloud in Søren's chandlery.** Søren cannot pretend he did not hear it. The decision he has been postponing arrives.
- **Cael says "Whisht" aloud in any tavern.** Anyone who has sailed in the Brotherhood the past two years pauses. Recognition cascades depending on who is in the room — see NPC_Maps for each present NPC.
- **Cael speaks Cherokee or Danish aloud in Eliza Free's hearing.** She responds in the same language. The narrator should treat this as a Body-knows moment that opens a Player-knows door if Cael chooses to follow.
- **Cael enters the Helm in the evening and Hendrik is present.** Roll the recognition channels in order — first sight (the eyebrow scar visible across the room), then voice (one word from Cael), then the tattoo if Cael's sleeves are rolled. Any one is sufficient. Hendrik will name him aloud.
- **Cael enters Lefèvre's apothecary and asks for any treatment.** Lefèvre's professional discretion: he will treat Cael's bruises and the fading welt on the back of his head without comment. But Lefèvre will also note the patient's face for future reference, and if Don Esteban asks, Lefèvre will mention the visit.
- **Cael walks the upper-town streets in daylight wearing the coat he washed up in.** The garrison sees him. If Tomás O'Brien is on duty, see his reveal triggers in NPC_Maps.
- **Cael shows the folded paper to any educated NPC.** The handwriting will be recognized as Cael's own by anyone who has seen him sign a clearance, a manifest, or a bill of lading in the past two years. That list includes Knudsen, de Lima, and Søren.
- **Cael is bathed, shaved, and properly dressed for the first time since waking.** He becomes visibly the man he used to be to anyone who knew him. The recognition rate doubles for the rest of the day.

## IV. THE CONVERGENCE WINDOW — DAY 10 TO DAY 14

The locked backstory's four ignition paths. Between Day 10 and Day 14 at least two of these fire concurrently. The narrator selects the two most accessible based on what the player has done in Week 1 and the first half of Week 2.

### Path Alpha — Stickjaw's panic

**Mechanism:** Stickjaw sees Cael alive. The behavior is involuntary and visible to anyone watching. Within 24-48 hours after the sighting, Stickjaw either confesses (to his sister Karin, to a stranger, to a priest), tries to flee, or breaks down in a tavern. Any of these surfaces the truth that Cael was attacked three nights before Day 1.

**Most accessible if:** the player has been in lower-town taverns enough to be visible to Stickjaw. By Day 5 he has almost certainly seen Cael at least once.

**How it ignites:** Karin confides in someone. Or the body of Mads Holst surfaces in the harbor (Day 10–14 is realistic for a weighted-but-poorly-weighted body to come up in a tidal pattern around the full moon). Or Stickjaw confronts Cael directly in drunken terror.

### Path Beta — Agnes makes her move

**Mechanism:** Agnes has seen Cael at a distance by Day 7 at the latest (Christianskirke Sunday). She has had a week to decide. Between Day 10 and Day 14 she finds a way to reach him — through Karin Holm at the fish market, through Doña Inés at El Yelmo, through a private appointment at the Lefèvre apothecary using an invented illness as cover.

**Most accessible if:** the player has been in the parts of town where Agnes could plausibly send a message — the lower-town markets, the apothecary, the Mahamad House (where she occasionally visits as a Christian observer who reads).

**How it ignites:** A note. Or her own presence at an unexpected location at an unexpected hour. Or a third party speaking quietly on her behalf.

### Path Gamma — Søren breaks

**Mechanism:** Søren has had a week to watch Cael at the wharf. The conflict between his old loyalty and his current payment from Erik will resolve one direction or the other by Day 10–14.

**Most accessible if:** the player has visited the chandlery or has crossed Søren's line of sight at the wharf more than twice.

**How it ignites:** Søren approaches Cael privately, says everything, and offers either warning or further betrayal. The narrator selects the direction based on which Søren has been showing more of in the small interactions of the prior week.

### Path Delta — Don Esteban learns

**Mechanism:** Don Esteban hears from a paid informant (Knudsen most likely; Lefèvre possibly; a tavern-keeper if Cael has been visibly spending coin) that "the *Whisht* man" is alive and walking the port. Esteban moves carefully — confirms, plans, hires.

**Most accessible if:** the player has spent coin visibly, signed his name in any official register, or asked questions in the wrong drinking room.

**How it ignites:** A man approaches Cael with a courteous Spanish accent and a small business proposition. Or two men do, in the early hours, with knives.

### Convergence rule mechanics

By Day 10, the narrator selects the two paths most accessible per player history and confirms them in working memory. Between Days 10 and 14 the chosen paths produce visible events.

The narrator does not force the timing. If on Day 12 the player is mid-action on Path Alpha, the narrator may delay Path Beta until Day 14 to allow the situation to develop. The narrator does not delay past Day 14 unless a player action has clearly created a new and substantive situation to navigate.

## V. RANDOM ATMOSPHERIC EVENTS

Small beats the narrator may drop in to keep the world alive. Roll for atmosphere, not for plot pressure. Examples:

- A fight at the Three Anchors over a dropped tankard — two Brotherhood sailors, neither named in the cast — that draws everyone's attention for a minute and ends without lasting damage.
- A priest passing through the wharf on the way to give last rites to a yellow fever case; the family weeping at a door.
- A young dockworker dropped a barrel of nails into the harbor and is being shouted at by his foreman, who is a man Big Anna knows.
- A pamphlet hawker (one of Hosea Penn's small enterprises) on Christianstorv selling broadsides of the latest treasure-fleet rumors.
- A small child running through the market with a dog at her heels, knocking over a basket of plantains; the seller swears at her in three languages without much heat.
- A garrison soldier on his off-evening walking arm in arm with a young woman from the Free Quarter; Tomás O'Brien watching them from across the square without expression.
- A dead fish washed up on the wharf in the morning that has been there long enough to smell; nobody owns the duty of moving it.
- A Council carriage at the wharf collecting a small wooden case; the case has no markings.
- A Spanish-speaking sailor from a transiting ship offering, in passable English, to fence a small item of jewelry; he does not say where it came from.

These are color, not plot. The narrator drops them sparingly — perhaps one per three turns — to populate the town between consequential moments.

---

## NARRATOR USAGE

The narrator holds the scheduled calendar in working memory and surfaces its events on the appointed days. Trigger events activate only when the trigger fires; the narrator does not pre-announce them. Pre-amnesia timeline content surfaces only through NPC dialogue when the conditions of NPC_Maps Section 3 (reveal triggers) are met.

The convergence window is the campaign's anti-stall mechanism. The narrator may select which two paths fire. The narrator may not select none.
