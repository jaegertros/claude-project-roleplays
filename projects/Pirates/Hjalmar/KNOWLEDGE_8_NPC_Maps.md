# KNOWLEDGE_8_NPC_Maps.md
## Epistemic profiles — Sankt Hjalmar

The companion to `KNOWLEDGE_1_NPCs.md`. That file tells the narrator how to *write* a person. This file tells the narrator what the person can *say* and what they cannot, and when their silence breaks.

Built from the V83 six-section epistemic format with one section added for the amnesia premise — **Section 7: Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers.** That column is the heart of the recognition mechanic. The narrator reads it before any scene in which an NPC sees Eriksohn in good light.

Fifteen NPCs profiled below — every NPC whose knowledge is plot-critical, plus the most informationally dense of the supporting cast. NPCs not profiled here are assumed to know only what their station and their place in the town would naturally tell them.

---

## FORMAT REFERENCE

Each profile uses these seven sections, in this order:

1. **Knows (and where it came from).** Discrete facts in their possession. Every fact has a source — direct experience, hearsay, deduction, document. The narrator never has an NPC produce a fact without a traceable origin.
2. **Does not know.** *The most important anti-drift section.* Explicit omissions. The narrator does not let an NPC mention or imply knowledge they do not have, even under pressure to advance a scene.
3. **Reveal triggers.** Specific keywords, objects, or conditions that unlock the next layer of information. Vague gates ("the player seems trustworthy") cause premature leak. Reveal triggers must be concrete.
4. **Suspects but cannot confirm.** Theories the NPC holds. These shape behavior — the NPC acts on suspicion — but the NPC does not state a suspicion as fact.
5. **Voice and refusal pattern.** How they speak under pressure, and what their *no* sounds like. NPCs do not refuse the same way.
6. **Disposition shift conditions.** What moves them from one relational stance to another (cooperative → cautious → hostile, or the reverse). Not a script — a small set of preconditions.
7. **Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers.** What existed between them and Cael before three nights ago. Which of the three marks (eyebrow / forearm / thigh) — if any — they recognize, and on what sensory channel (sight / name / voice / body language). What they will *do* upon recognition.

## 1. ERIK RASMUSSEN

### Knows
- His cousin Frederik was killed during the *Whisht*'s raid on a Rasmussen family brig in the Mona Passage, December 1714 (direct family report; the survivors' depositions are in his desk).
- His sister Agnes was sent to a convent in Charlotte Amalie in late 1714 for "a fever" (family knowledge).
- Agnes returned in March 1715 in a state he reads as "broken in a way that does not match a fever" (direct observation).
- He hired three men to kill the captain of the *Whisht* three nights ago (direct knowledge — he paid them).
- One of the men returned saying the job was done; the other two have not yet reported (hired man's account).
- The Selskab does not pursue Brotherhood captains who are killed quietly in the lower town (six years of observation).

### Does not know
- The captain of the *Whisht* is currently alive in Sankt Hjalmar with no memory.
- Stickjaw rolled the body off the wharf without weighing it.
- Agnes was pregnant. He does not know the child exists or that the child lives on St. Thomas.
- Cael's birth name (he has been calling him "the Eriksohn man").
- Søren Lind sailed with Cael before settling on the wharf.
- Eliza Free exists by name.

### Reveal triggers
- **"Agnes" + "fever" in the same conversation:** an irritated denial that escalates if pressed. The truth surfaces only if the speaker already shows knowledge of the pregnancy.
- **"Frederik" + "pistol-ball":** he produces the family's case against Cael in fully prepared form.
- **A direct report that "Eriksohn" is alive:** triggers the second-phase hunt. He will not act in haste. He will spend two days verifying.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That his sister was wronged not by a fever but by the captain of the *Whisht*. Has not confirmed and does not want to know the specific shape of the wronging if it is more than seduction.
- That Søren Lind knows more than Søren says he knows. Has paid Søren for information for six months precisely because he suspects Søren is the inside route.
- That his father Klaus is dying. Will not say so even to himself.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Quiet, precise, slow. Uses titles ("Mr. Lind," "Captain Maartens"). When refusing, the refusal is a short polite sentence followed by a return to the previous subject, as though the request was a misunderstanding rather than a request.
- Under direct accusation, he denies with absolute composure and then exits the room.
- Does not raise his voice. The volume *drops* when he is angry.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Cooperative → cautious:** any indication a stranger knows about Frederik or about Agnes.
- **Cautious → adversarial:** any indication a stranger is aligned with Cael, or any direct mention of the *Whisht*.
- **Adversarial → committed-to-killing:** confirmation that Cael is alive.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** mortal enemy who believes Cael is already dead. Has never met him in person. Saw him once at a distance in the market in summer 1714 and would not recognize the face on sight.
- **Recognition triggers:** Erik will not recognize Cael by any of the three marks alone. He will recognize him by *name* and by *context* — "Eriksohn," "captain of the *Whisht*," "the Irish-mixed sailor." If Cael uses the surname Eriksohn anywhere within hearing of Erik or his hired ears, Erik moves within twenty-four hours.
- **On recognition:** Erik will not confront. He will move to confirm, then hire again. Methodical.

## 2. AGNES RASMUSSEN

### Knows
- That she loved Cael in summer 1714 and that he did not know about the child.
- That her father sent her to the convent and that her brother knows the convent stay was not, in fact, for a fever.
- That her daughter Margreta is alive with a farming family in the hills behind Charlotte Amalie (the abbess told her on the day she left the convent).
- That her father has been keeping the convent's bills out of her sight, which means he is paying them.
- That she has not seen Cael since summer 1714.

### Does not know
- That Cael is currently in Sankt Hjalmar.
- That Erik hired three men to kill him three nights ago.
- That she is being watched by one of her father's house servants (a woman named Else, paid by her brother).

### Reveal triggers
- **"The convent" + her by name (no one else present):** she will speak quietly and once. She is unpracticed at being asked.
- **The crucifix at her throat is touched or referenced:** it was given to her by the abbess the day she left the convent. Touching it visibly puts her on edge.
- **The child is referenced specifically by sex or by name:** she does not speak. She does, however, move — the answer is in her posture.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That her father knows where her daughter is and has not told her precisely because he does not yet trust her with the knowledge.
- That her brother killed someone over Frederik's death. Has not pressed; does not want the answer.
- That she will leave Sankt Hjalmar one way or another within the year. Has not allowed herself to plan.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Soft, low, careful. Pauses before each answer. Refuses by going still — eyes down, hands folded — rather than by speaking the refusal. The silence does the work.
- Under direct pressure she does not break. She becomes more careful, not less.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Sealed → guarded interest:** any indication a stranger means her child no harm.
- **Guarded interest → conspirator:** verified evidence Cael is alive *and* has not abandoned her by choice.
- **Sealed → enemy:** any direct threat to her daughter.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** lover, six months in 1714. Mother of his child. Last saw him at a distance in the market in summer 1715; he did not see her.
- **Recognition triggers:** she will recognize him *on sight*. The face is fully retained. She will also recognize his voice within three sentences and his walk from across the square. The eyebrow scar is a confirmation she does not require.
- **On recognition:** she will not approach in public. She will plan a private meeting. She must decide first whether he abandoned her or did not know. Her decision will shape the second week.

## 3. KLAUS RASMUSSEN

### Knows
- His daughter was pregnant and was sent to Charlotte Amalie to deliver discreetly.
- The child is a girl, lives with the Madsen family on St. Thomas, and is paid for through a quarterly stipend he himself signs.
- His son Erik has been pursuing some matter relating to Frederik's death for over a year and Klaus has not asked the details.
- His own left hand has begun to tremble and his strength is failing.
- His older son will inherit Trefoldighed; his younger son in Copenhagen will inherit the family's Danish holdings; his daughter is dowered.

### Does not know
- That Erik has hired men to kill the captain of the *Whisht*.
- That his daughter knows where her child is.
- That his daughter's child is the daughter of the man his son is hunting.
- That Søren Lind is an informant against Cael.

### Reveal triggers
- **A direct paternal question from a person Klaus trusts:** Klaus answers honestly. He has lived long enough to consider lying tedious in private settings.
- **The convent's bill or the Madsen family's name spoken aloud:** he becomes very careful with his next sentence.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That Erik has done something he should not know about and that he therefore should not ask.
- That his daughter is planning something she has not yet executed.
- That his own death is closer than his doctor admits.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Slow, measured, complete sentences. Refusals are direct: "I will not speak of that." He does not soften.
- Under direct pressure he does not bend. He simply ends the audience and rises.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Patrician → vulnerable:** he has not yet shown vulnerability to anyone in the town. The threshold is exceptionally high.
- **Patrician → adversarial:** a clear threat to the family's name in public.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** has heard of "the *Whisht* captain" through his son's reports and considers the man a routine commercial competitor. Has never seen Cael in person.
- **Recognition triggers:** would not recognize Cael by face, voice, or any mark. Would recognize him by the conjunction of "Eriksohn" + "the *Whisht*."
- **On recognition:** would be slow to act personally. Would ask Erik first. The question itself would be informative to Erik.

## 4. SØREN LIND

### Knows
- Cael's full pre-amnesia history at sea — the Bermuda privateer years, Jens Eriksohn, the *Whisht*, the Rasmussen brig raid, the past summer's troubles.
- That Erik Rasmussen has been paying him for information since spring 1715.
- That Erik hired men to kill Cael three nights ago — he was not told but he was able to infer from the timing of a payment.
- That the body was supposed to be weighed and dropped but the harbor swell has been wrong for that this week.
- That Cael's daughter exists. Cael told him once after drinking — Cael did not believe Agnes had been sent away for a real fever.

### Does not know
- That Cael survived the attack.
- (Not yet — he learns this within the first day of story-open when he sees Cael walking the wharf.)

### Reveal triggers
- **Direct sight of Cael at the chandlery door:** Søren freezes for approximately one second. Then he becomes carefully normal.
- **Erik's name spoken to him aloud by Cael:** he goes pale and recovers. The recovery is the tell.
- **The phrase "the Whisht" or "Jens" used by Cael in his presence:** he cannot pretend he does not know what is meant. The decision he has been putting off arrives.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That Cael may have known Søren was an informant and was on his way to confront him the night of the attack. (He was not. But Søren cannot know that.)
- That Erik will turn on him eventually. The plan is for Søren to be on a packet to Copenhagen with his family by April 1716.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Quiet Danish-accented English. Polite. Refusals come in two stages: first an offer of a smaller version of what was asked, then a regretful claim of insufficient resources or knowledge. He does not say no directly.
- Under pressure he does not lie cleanly. The lies have edges.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Polite → conflicted ally:** Cael appears to genuinely have no memory and Søren's old loyalty surfaces.
- **Polite → second-time betrayer:** Erik's next payment arrives and Søren has not yet warned Cael.
- **Polite → fled:** if Erik begins to suspect him, Søren is on a boat within seventy-two hours.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** quartermaster on the *Whisht* for fourteen months. Cael trusted him completely. The trust is what makes the current betrayal hurt in a specific way.
- **Recognition triggers:** recognizes the face on sight, the voice within one word, the walk from across the wharf. Also recognizes the compass-rose tattoo and the eyebrow scar. Each of the three confirms a recognition that does not need confirming.
- **On recognition:** the decision Søren has been postponing arrives. The narrator should write the first encounter as the moment of decision, not after it.

## 5. TOMÁS O'BRIEN

### Knows
- His father Sean O'Brien was killed in Charleston in 1711.
- The killer was described to him by his mother as "an Irish-mixed boy of about sixteen, lean, dark hair, a fresh cut across his right brow."
- He has been looking at faces matching that description in every port he has been in for six years.
- The garrison roster, the harbor sailing schedule, the names of every Brotherhood captain currently in port.

### Does not know
- That Cael is the man he has been looking for.
- The name "Cael MacAoidh."

### Reveal triggers
- **Sight of the eyebrow scar in good light at a distance of less than six feet:** his face does something specific that he cannot prevent. He does not act on it. He goes home and writes a letter to his mother.
- **A second meeting with the same face:** he begins to track the man's routine.
- **A third meeting:** he approaches.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That the killer is at sea, since he has not surfaced in any of Tomás's prior postings.
- That the killer may not be alive at all. (He has been holding this possibility at arm's length for six years.)

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Quiet, watchful. Charleston English under a Spanish-Catholic surface. Refuses by polite deflection: "I would not know, sir." He is competent at the deflection because he has been practicing it since boyhood.
- Under pressure he does not break. He becomes more silent.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Watchful neutral → predator:** identification of the killer.
- **Predator → confrontation:** sufficient evidence to satisfy his own conscience. The Charleston killing involved an officer of the law; Tomás needs to be certain.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** has never met Cael but has been hunting his face for fourteen years.
- **Recognition triggers:** the eyebrow scar in good light, combined with an Irish-mixed look. *The mark alone is not enough; the conjunction is required.* If Cael is wearing a hat that shadows the brow, Tomás does not lock on.
- **On recognition:** Tomás does not act immediately. He confirms first. He writes to his mother. He shadows. He decides.

## 6. KRISTOFFER "STICKJAW" HOLM

### Knows
- He was one of three men hired by Erik Rasmussen to kill the captain of the *Whisht* three nights ago.
- One of his fellow-attackers was killed in the alley.
- The third (a man called Jens Kruse — yes, another Jens — a hired dockhand who has since left for St. Thomas on the next packet) panicked at the wharf, and Stickjaw rolled the body in without weighing it.
- He has been drinking too much for three days and has not yet figured out how to confess or flee.

### Does not know
- That Cael survived.
- (He learns this the moment he sees Cael alive in the port. The reaction is uncontrollable.)

### Reveal triggers
- **Sight of Cael alive in any light:** Stickjaw blanches, then bolts, then drinks. The body language is visible to anyone watching.
- **Direct accusation by Cael:** confession comes within sixty seconds. Stickjaw is not brave.
- **Pressure from his sister Karin:** she gets the truth out of him within a day if she pushes.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That Erik will eventually figure out the job failed regardless of what Stickjaw does, and Erik will then have him killed in turn.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Sankt Hjalmar lower-town Danish, nervous, repetitive. Refuses by changing the subject. Under any real pressure he confesses.
- Drinks faster when nervous. Leaves rooms abruptly.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Frightened → useful informant:** small protection from Cael or from his sister.
- **Frightened → fugitive:** Erik gets to him first.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** the man who hit Cael with a belaying pin three nights ago. Recognized Cael during the fight (he had seen the *Whisht* captain at the wharf many times). Has not slept well since.
- **Recognition triggers:** the face on sight. He has no need of the marks. The face is in his nightmare.
- **On recognition:** see above. The reaction is involuntary and visible.

## 7. DON ESTEBAN DE RIBERA

### Knows
- The Spanish viceregal intelligence office in San Juan placed a standing price on Cael's head in early 1715 after the *Whisht*'s raid on a Spanish coastal town near Cumaná.
- The price is one hundred pesos paid on confirmed head, with proof of identity required (a sworn deposition or a recognizable corpse).
- Cael was reported dead three months ago by a passing rumor that Esteban was unable to verify and therefore did not act upon.
- The Selskab does not know Esteban is more than a wine merchant.

### Does not know
- That Cael is currently alive in Sankt Hjalmar.
- (He learns within four to seven days of story-open through normal port gossip — a fish-seller's gossip, an overheard remark at the Helm, a careless word from a sailor.)
- The full pre-amnesia history of Cael's life. (He has the *Whisht*'s raids on file; he does not have Charleston 1711 or the Mona Passage 1714 in his record.)

### Reveal triggers
- **A report from one of his paid eyes that "the *Whisht* man is alive in the port":** he begins planning. He does not move quickly. He moves carefully.
- **A direct meeting with Cael (Esteban does not know who he is):** Esteban is pleasant. He notices. He files.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That at least one of his paid informants in the port may be unreliable.
- That the Brotherhood captains currently in port may protect Cael if a clumsy attempt is made.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Castilian Spanish with polished English. Refusals are courtly: "I regret that I am unable to be of assistance in this matter, but if I may direct you to ..." Never abrupt. Never raised.
- Under pressure he does not blink. He is more dangerous after the conversation has ended than during it.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Cordial → hunting:** confirmation of Cael's identity.
- **Hunting → eliminating:** an opportunity that does not implicate Spain or the Selskab.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** has never met Cael. Has a written description in his files: "lean, mixed-blood, late twenties, scar through right eyebrow, captains a sloop called the *Whisht*."
- **Recognition triggers:** the description plus the eyebrow scar plus the name. He does not need the compass tattoo or the thigh-marks. The eyebrow scar is in his dossier.
- **On recognition:** he hires. He does not approach.

## 8. ELIZA FREE

### Knows
- The Cherokee grief-mark pattern on Cael's inner left thigh, because her own grandmother used the same pattern on her in 1684.
- That Cael speaks Cherokee under his amnesia — she heard him talk in his sleep on the second night.
- That he was struck on the back of the head with something heavy and rounded — almost certainly a belaying pin.
- That his rescue was not the work of any party that would have wanted him saved on official grounds. (Petter brought him to her precisely to avoid the garrison.)

### Does not know
- The name Cael MacAoidh.
- The exact reason someone wanted Cael dead.
- Anything about Agnes Rasmussen, the child, or Erik's hunt.

### Reveal triggers
- **A direct, private, conscious return visit by Cael with the thigh-marks visible or referenced:** Eliza decides whether to tell him. The decision is *not yet made* at story-open.
- **Cael speaking Cherokee in her presence while awake:** she replies in Cherokee. He may not realize what he has done.
- **An attack on the Maroon settlement or on her cottage:** she becomes immediately direct with whoever survives to ask her anything.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That Cael's mother was Cherokee and was probably murdered. (She inferred this from the marks and from the silence in his sleep when he was nine years old. She does not know the year or the town.)
- That she will tell him eventually. Has not yet decided when.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Low, careful English defeating any attempt to place an accent. Refuses by not answering. Will sit silently through a long question and then change the subject.
- Under pressure she does not break. She becomes still.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Waiting → telling:** specific evidence that Cael is recovering memory or that the recovery is becoming dangerous to him.
- **Waiting → protective:** an attempt by the town on Cael or on her settlement.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** had never met Cael until three nights ago. Tended him two days. He does not remember her.
- **Recognition triggers:** she recognizes him by sight (she has seen his face awake and asleep). She also recognizes the thigh-marks specifically; the other two marks she has not yet had reason to study.
- **On recognition:** she has already recognized him. The question is what she does next.

## 9. PETTER HALVORSEN

### Knows
- He pulled a man out of the shallows at dawn three days ago.
- The man had a wound on the back of the head consistent with a belaying pin.
- He took the man to Eliza because the man would not have survived a delivery to the garrison.
- Eliza kept the man and returned him to the town two mornings later.

### Does not know
- The man's name.
- Why someone wanted him dead.
- What Eliza saw on the man's thigh.

### Reveal triggers
- **A direct question from Cael, alone, at his cottage on the leeward beach:** Petter answers slowly and completely. He does not consider what he did a secret.
- **A question about Eliza by name:** he becomes briefer. He will direct the questioner toward the path to her cottage without describing the route precisely.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That the man was a Brotherhood captain. (He saw the calluses.)
- That whoever wanted him dead is still in the town.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Norwegian under decades of Caribbean English. Slow. Monosyllabic when he can be. Refuses by changing the subject to the weather or the catch.

### Disposition shift conditions
- He is the same with everyone. The shift conditions are minimal.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** none before three days ago. Recognizes him on sight as the man he pulled from the water.
- **Recognition triggers:** the face. The marks are not relevant to him.
- **On recognition:** he greets Cael normally, does not announce the rescue, lets Cael ask if Cael wants to ask.

## 10. OLD HENDRIK

### Knows
- Cael by face, name, and crew-mark.
- Most of the history of the *Whisht* and most of the history of Jens Eriksohn's last cruise.
- That Cael is "the Eriksohn boy" who took Jens's name and is therefore family in the way Hendrik counts family.

### Does not know
- That Cael has no memory.
- That Erik Rasmussen has hired men to kill him.
- That Søren Lind has been informing on Cael.

### Reveal triggers
- **Two drinks:** he begins to talk. He does not need a prompt; the prompt is the second drink.
- **Sight of the compass-rose tattoo:** he greets the wearer by name immediately, regardless of whether the wearer is Cael or another former crewmate. This is the most likely involuntary reveal in the campaign — Hendrik is the security hole.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That Søren has gone soft on something. (He has noticed Søren is more careful around Brotherhood gossip than he used to be.)

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Helsingør Danish under slurred English. Affectionate, sentimental, repetitive. Refuses nothing. He has nothing he considers worth refusing.

### Disposition shift conditions
- Affectionate baseline; affectionate after a fight; affectionate the next morning.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** uncle-figure in Cael's pre-amnesia life. Cael paid his Helm tab some weeks.
- **Recognition triggers:** the face, the voice, the walk, the tattoo. All four. Any one of them makes Hendrik greet him aloud.
- **On recognition:** Hendrik says "Caleb, boy" in front of everyone in the Helm without realizing it is consequential. This is the most likely accident that exposes Cael's identity to the room. The narrator should consider Hendrik's presence in any tavern scene.

## 11. CAPTAIN PIETER MAARTENS

### Knows
- Cael by face, by name, by reputation, by trade history.
- That Cael's last voyage went badly and that Cael paid off most of his crew this summer.
- That Erik Rasmussen has been seen at the wharf more often than usual lately. (Maartens has not yet connected this to Cael; he has wondered if it is about insurance.)
- That his own *Vrije Wind* will be ready to sail in roughly three weeks.

### Does not know
- That Cael has no memory.
- About Cael's daughter, Agnes, or the hunt.

### Reveal triggers
- **Direct meeting with Cael:** Maartens treats him as a colleague. He will mention the upcoming sail. He will mention his ship's condition. He may offer Cael a place if Cael seems to need one.
- **A direct question about Erik Rasmussen:** Maartens shares his observation about the wharf appearances and his suspicion about insurance fraud — but no more.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That the Council is planning something to do with Brotherhood activity in the port.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Heavy Dutch under English. Blunt. Refuses by direct, polite, brief refusal: "No. I cannot help with that." Does not soften.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Colleague → loyal:** a Brotherhood crisis brings Maartens to Cael's side automatically.
- **Colleague → cautious:** Cael cannot pay or has lost his nerve.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** Brotherhood colleague, mutual respect, no debts in either direction.
- **Recognition triggers:** the face and the name. The compass tattoo confirms what the face has already told him.
- **On recognition:** treats Cael as Cael. Does not announce the recognition unless Cael does first.

## 12. ISAAC DE LIMA

### Knows
- That he holds paper on Cael's last two voyages totaling roughly six hundred pieces of eight at agreed interest.
- That the *Whisht* came back in poor condition this past August and that Cael has not yet retired the debt.
- That Cael disappeared from the port some days ago. (Isaac assumes Cael is hiding from his creditors, which is a tradition.)
- That Don Esteban de Ribera is more than a wine merchant. (Isaac's correspondents in Curaçao have flagged Esteban's name in the past.)
- That Erik Rasmussen has been receiving correspondence from a Copenhagen law firm with whom the Rasmussens do not normally do business.

### Does not know
- That Cael is alive in the town with no memory.
- That Erik Rasmussen has Cael's death as a personal project.

### Reveal triggers
- **A direct request for credit or capital from Cael:** Isaac produces the outstanding paper without rancor and asks how it is to be retired.
- **A direct question about Don Esteban:** Isaac will speak carefully and shortly, and will not lie about what he knows.
- **The phrase "the *Whisht*" combined with Cael's name in a serious context:** he becomes attentive. He sends a runner to a correspondent in Curaçao within twenty-four hours.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That Erik Rasmussen is preparing to act on Frederik's death formally. Has not yet decided whether to extend the de Lima paper or call it.
- That his daughter-in-law in Curaçao knows more about Don Esteban than she has told him.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Quiet, multilingual, brief. Refuses with a regretful "I cannot, in this matter." Does not elaborate.
- Under pressure he becomes shorter and more polite. He does not raise his voice or break his pattern.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Professional → ally-of-convenience:** Cael offers an arrangement that converts debt into joint venture. Isaac will entertain it if the venture is plausible.
- **Professional → demanding:** the paper sits longer than Isaac judges patient.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** commercial counterparty, two years of business, polite mutual respect.
- **Recognition triggers:** the face, the name, the *Whisht*. Not the marks specifically.
- **On recognition:** unsurprised. Welcomes Cael into the counting house with the courtesy he extends to all clients.

## 13. FATHER JOAQUÍN VEGA

### Knows
- That a Spanish "wine merchant" in the port reports to San Juan. (He has heard the confession of one of Esteban's informants.)
- That the *Whisht* raided a Spanish coastal town near Cumaná in spring 1715. (Confessed to him by a sailor who was aboard.)
- That a daughter of one of the Council families has spent time in the Charlotte Amalie convent and has returned changed. (Common gossip; he reads it correctly.)
- That a man was attacked at the wharf three nights ago. (One of his parishioners — a Catholic dockhand — saw the body being dragged.)

### Does not know
- The name of the man attacked.
- The full backstory of any party — he holds fragments from many confessions and never puts them together aloud.

### Reveal triggers
- **A confessional approach by Cael:** Father Joaquín hears him out. He cannot speak of confessions to others but can ask Cael oblique questions that lead Cael to discover things himself.
- **A direct conversation outside the confessional:** the priest speaks generally and warmly without violating any specific seal.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That Don Esteban is moving toward a specific operation. Has not asked.
- That Erik Rasmussen is preparing something. Has not asked.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Mexican Spanish, warm slow English with Latin tags. Refuses by reframing: "I cannot answer the question you asked, my son; but if you wished to ask a different question, I might."
- Under pressure he becomes Latin and obscure on purpose.

### Disposition shift conditions
- He is the same with everyone. He is a constant.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** has seen Cael in the town but never spoken with him. Cael is not Catholic.
- **Recognition triggers:** the face. He does not know the marks. He may notice the unconscious cross-himself reflex Cael does passing the mission — a Catholic-trained hand from a man who is not a parishioner — and find it interesting.
- **On recognition:** a friendly nod. He will not approach. He does not pursue. If Cael comes to him, he listens.

## 14. MADRE JOANA

### Knows
- That Eliza Free took in a wounded sailor three days ago and returned him to the town.
- That the sailor had no memory when he left.
- That Cael's pre-amnesia network in the town included no fewer than four men sympathetic to the Maroon settlement — Cael had sold them rifles in 1714.
- The locations of all six Maroon paths from the highlands to the leeward beach.

### Does not know
- The sailor's name.
- That he is the same captain who sold them the rifles. (Cael did the deal through an intermediary; she never met him.)

### Reveal triggers
- **The phrase "the rifles in 1714" used by Cael in her presence:** she connects the man to the deal. Her disposition shifts immediately.
- **A direct request for sanctuary:** she considers it. The settlement has rules.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That the town will eventually decide to send a punitive expedition. Plans for that day are already drawn.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Kongo-Portuguese-English creole. Commanding when she chooses to be. Refuses by a single short sentence followed by silence. She does not elaborate.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Cautious → grudging ally:** a debt is established (the rifles, or a new exchange).
- **Cautious → adversary:** any betrayal of her settlement's location.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** mutual benefactor at one remove. Has never met Cael in person.
- **Recognition triggers:** none on sight. The connection is made by reference to the rifles deal.
- **On recognition:** offers him a degree of welcome he has earned. Does not invite him to the settlement.

## 15. LAURITZ KNUDSEN

### Knows
- Every ship in port, its captain, its cargo, its declared origin, and its rumored real origin.
- Every captain's customary "consideration" for fast clearance — including Cael's.
- That Cael's *Whisht* was last cleared into the harbor on August 24th and that Cael personally signed the papers.
- That a body was rumored at the wharf three nights ago and that no report was filed. (He has chosen not to ask.)
- That Don Esteban pays him a small monthly retainer for advance notice of any ship's clearance.
- That the Rasmussens pay him a similar retainer for advance notice of any Brotherhood-aligned arrival.

### Does not know
- That Cael survived the attack.
- The connections between the parties paying him. (He treats each retainer as independent.)

### Reveal triggers
- **A bribe larger than the standing retainers from competing parties:** he provides the information in question.
- **A direct official inquiry from the governor:** he produces the records faithfully.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That at least three of his current retainer-payers are pursuing the same man. Has not connected which man.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Copenhagen Danish, careful English, bureaucratic phrasing. Refuses by claiming insufficient information, regulatory constraint, or the need to consult the governor. None of these is true; all of them are pretexts.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Compliant → forgetful (in the player's favor):** sufficient consideration.
- **Compliant → forgetful (against the player):** a larger consideration from another party.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** customary commercial counterparty. Cael's name is on his ledger and his retainer schedule.
- **Recognition triggers:** the face and the name. Not the marks.
- **On recognition:** unsurprised. Will greet Cael politely and update his ledgers as appropriate.

## NARRATOR CHECKLIST BEFORE ANY SCENE

Before writing any encounter between Cael and one of these fifteen NPCs, the narrator runs a four-question check:

1. **What does this NPC know that the player has not yet earned?** (Read the *Knows* section.)
2. **What can this NPC not say even if asked directly?** (Read the *Does not know* and *Refusal* sections.)
3. **What would trigger this NPC to reveal more in this specific scene?** (Read the *Reveal triggers* section.)
4. **Will this NPC recognize Cael, and on what channel?** (Read Section 7.)

The narrator does not perform this check aloud. It is invisible to the player. But its result determines what the scene can and cannot do.
