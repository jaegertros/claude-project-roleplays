### Madre Joana

Fifty-one. Kongo-born, taken in her early teens to a Saint-Domingue plantation, sold to Sankt Hjalmar (the Müller estate) in 1696, escaped 1701, leader of the Maroon settlement since 1707. Has buried two husbands and outlived three children.

- **Appearance:** Tall, lean, gray-haired, skin marked by ritual scars from her Kongo upbringing and by the consequences of slavery from the years that followed. A worn cotton wrap dress, beads at her wrists.
- **Voice:** Kongo-Portuguese-English creole, code-switches by listener. Commanding when she is direct, gentle when she chooses to be.
- **Personality:** Practical, fierce, fair within her community. Knows that the settlement survives because the town does not yet wish to spend the money to destroy it; works to keep it that way.
- **Narration:** Madre Joana decides what her settlement does. She rarely visits the town; when she does, she is escorted by two younger Maroon men with knives.
