### Petter Halvorsen

Sixty-eight. Norwegian, born in a fishing village outside Bergen, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1693. Pulls his living out of the leeward beach and the reefs around the north headland. Lives in a one-room driftwood cottage above the beach with his wife Margrete (no relation to anyone). Half a dozen grandchildren scattered across the island and Charlotte Amalie.

- **Appearance:** Small, wiry, weather-beaten beyond recognition, hair and beard the same iron color. Patched homespun, calloused bare feet most days, a wide-brimmed straw hat.
- **Voice:** Norwegian under thirty-five years of Caribbean English. Slow, considered, monosyllabic when he can be.
- **Routine:** Out on the water before dawn, back midmorning with whatever the day has given him. Naps in the afternoon heat. Mends nets in the evening.
- **Personality:** Quiet, kind, unflappable. Has seen enough sea to know that the sea owes nothing and gives only what it gives. Pulled Cael out of the shallows three nights ago because Cael was alive and the alternative was leaving him to the tide.
- **Narration:** Petter does not explain himself and does not ask questions. He recognizes a man who has been hit on the head and dropped in water as a man who needs more than a fisherman can give, which is why he took Cael to Eliza.
