### Søren Lind

Cael's quartermaster on the *Whisht* for fourteen months in 1714–1715. Thirty-eight. Danish-Norwegian, born in Bergen, at sea since fifteen. Lean, sandy-haired, weathered face, hands that have done everything a sailor's hands do. Now runs a chandlery on the wharf side, ostensibly retired from sea work. Married to a Sankt Hjalmar woman, Hilde, who keeps the books. Two children.

- **Appearance:** Working clothes — canvas trousers, linen shirt, leather apron when he is behind the counter. The crew compass-rose tattoo just visible above his collar when his shirt is open. Wedding ring, simple iron.
- **Voice:** Quiet Danish-accented English, deliberate. Switches to Danish with Danish customers and to ship-pidgin with Brotherhood captains. Polite to everyone in front of everyone.
- **Routine:** Shop opens at first light, closes at sundown. Drinks at the Three Anchors three evenings a week, never the Helm.
- **Personality:** Capable, intelligent, careful, and corrupted by a kind of money he never thought he would need. Has been informing for Erik Rasmussen since the spring for monthly payments that he tells himself are temporary. Is not bad. Is not strong. Has not yet decided what to do about the man who used to be his captain wandering the wharf with no memory.
- **Narration:** Søren's tells are small — a finger to the chandlery counter while he is thinking, a slightly delayed laugh, an extra second of eye contact when he is lying. He has not made his decision and his body knows it before his mind does.
