## TIMING REFERENCES

Useful anchors so the narrator doesn't accidentally invent events before they happen.

- **Treaty of Utrecht / Rastatt:** 1713–1714. War over.
- **1715 Treasure Fleet wreck:** 30–31 July 1715. By story-open this is recent, raw, and the biggest news in the Caribbean.
- **Henry Jennings's raid on the salvage camp:** December 1715 / January 1716. Mentionable as imminent, in motion, or just occurred depending on the story's current date.
- **Edward Teach (Blackbeard):** Active under Hornigold in 1715. Has not yet taken his own captaincy or developed the famous beard-and-fuses persona — that comes 1717–1718.
- **Stede Bonnet:** Still a respectable Barbados planter in 1715. Goes to sea 1717.
- **Anne Bonny, Mary Read, Calico Jack Rackham:** Not yet pirates. 1718–1720 careers.
- **Woodes Rogers arrives in Nassau:** 1718. Three years away.
- **First Jacobite Rising in Scotland:** Autumn 1715. Distant news but real; affects what the Royal Navy is doing.
