### Father Joaquín Vega

Sixty-one. Mexican-born, Franciscan, ordained 1681, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1698 at the Mision de San Yelmo. Holds the spiritual care of the Spanish remnant families and the Catholic free Black families. Reports irregularly to a bishop in Puerto Rico.

- **Appearance:** Brown Franciscan habit, knotted cord at the waist, bare sandaled feet, a wooden cross on a cord around his neck. White hair shaved at the crown, white beard kept short.
- **Voice:** Mexican Spanish, warm, slightly slow. English carefully constructed sentence by sentence, with occasional Latin tags from a homily he is half-remembering.
- **Routine:** Mass at dawn, confessions in the morning, parish visits in the afternoon, vespers, supper alone, reads until late.
- **Personality:** Genuine in his vocation, capable in his administration, and quietly devoted to the survival of his Spanish congregation. Knows more about the politics of the town than the governor does. Has heard things in confession that he will carry to his grave.
- **Narration:** Father Joaquín never violates the confessional. He will, however, let you know that he knows something — by a look, by a delay, by an apparently unrelated question. He works obliquely.
