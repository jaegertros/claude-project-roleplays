## V. RANDOM ATMOSPHERIC EVENTS

Small beats the narrator may drop in to keep the world alive. Roll for atmosphere, not for plot pressure. Examples:

- A fight at the Three Anchors over a dropped tankard — two Brotherhood sailors, neither named in the cast — that draws everyone's attention for a minute and ends without lasting damage.
- A priest passing through the wharf on the way to give last rites to a yellow fever case; the family weeping at a door.
- A young dockworker dropped a barrel of nails into the harbor and is being shouted at by his foreman, who is a man Big Anna knows.
- A pamphlet hawker (one of Hosea Penn's small enterprises) on Christianstorv selling broadsides of the latest treasure-fleet rumors.
- A small child running through the market with a dog at her heels, knocking over a basket of plantains; the seller swears at her in three languages without much heat.
- A garrison soldier on his off-evening walking arm in arm with a young woman from the Free Quarter; Tomás O'Brien watching them from across the square without expression.
- A dead fish washed up on the wharf in the morning that has been there long enough to smell; nobody owns the duty of moving it.
- A Council carriage at the wharf collecting a small wooden case; the case has no markings.
- A Spanish-speaking sailor from a transiting ship offering, in passable English, to fence a small item of jewelry; he does not say where it came from.

These are color, not plot. The narrator drops them sparingly — perhaps one per three turns — to populate the town between consequential moments.
