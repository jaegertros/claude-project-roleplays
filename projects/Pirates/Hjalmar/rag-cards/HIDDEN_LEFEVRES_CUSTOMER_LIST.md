## V. LEFÈVRE'S CUSTOMER LIST

> ⚠ DO NOT VOLUNTEER. None of what follows is ever revealed by the narrator unprompted. Every revelation here has a DEPTH threshold; below that threshold, it does not exist as narrated reality. Read this section only when actively preparing to surface its content in scene.


The apothecary keeps no formal records — he is professional about that — but his memory is excellent and his categorization is precise.

**Recurring customers and their staple purchases:**

- **Agnes Rasmussen** — laudanum, in modest quantities, the past six months. She is not addicted; she sleeps poorly. (Lefèvre suspects what happened to her at the convent. He does not say.)
- **Klaus Rasmussen** — cinchona bark for an intermittent fever he has had since 1711, and a tincture for the trembling of his left hand which he now collects monthly.
- **Erik Rasmussen** — nothing personally. Sends a servant for the household supplies.
- **Don Esteban de Ribera** — sleeping draughts (occasional), a specific Andalusian-style preparation for joint stiffness, and — twice in the past two years — a quantity of arsenic for "rat control at the warehouse." Lefèvre believes the rat-control story exactly as much as one believes it ought to be believed.
- **Margriet (the Helm)** — herbal preparations for hangovers, for cuts, for the occasional wound she does not want a surgeon's name attached to.
- **Søren Lind's wife Hilde** — a recurrent stomach complaint that has worsened in the past three months. (Lefèvre suspects the stress of her husband's secret. He does not say.)
- **Father Joaquín Vega** — basic herbal preparations for the mission's poor, paid for in small donations.
- **Captain Maartens** — preparations for shipboard infections, gum disease, and one quiet purchase of mercurial preparation for an officer's case of pox.
- **The Madsen family wet-nurse on St. Thomas** — through a chain of intermediaries; small herbal preparations for an infant's colic. (Lefèvre does not know whose child. The order goes through a Sankt Hjalmar agent who pays in advance.)
- **An unknown buyer at the Three Anchors** — paid for laudanum and a small lock-picking set together in February 1715. Lefèvre remembers the face and the transaction; he does not know the name.

**What the player can learn and how:**

- Lefèvre does not volunteer customer information. He will, however, answer specific questions if the asker pays for the information (typical price: 1 piece of eight per question about a specific customer's habits).
- Lefèvre will refuse to answer questions about customers who are paying him for discretion (Agnes, Don Esteban, Hilde Lind).
- Lefèvre will answer questions about Klaus Rasmussen's health to anyone who pays — Klaus is not paying for discretion and Lefèvre treats him as a routine professional matter.
