<profile_heading>
## 10. OLD HENDRIK
</profile_heading>

<npc_knows>
### Knows
- Cael by face, name, and crew-mark.
- Most of the history of the *Whisht* and most of the history of Jens Eriksohn's last cruise.
- That Cael is "the Eriksohn boy" who took Jens's name and is therefore family in the way Hendrik counts family.
</npc_knows>

<npc_does_not_know>
### Does not know
- That Cael has no memory.
- That Erik Rasmussen has hired men to kill him.
- That Søren Lind has been informing on Cael.
</npc_does_not_know>

<npc_reveal_triggers>
### Reveal triggers
- **Two drinks:** he begins to talk. He does not need a prompt; the prompt is the second drink.
- **Sight of the compass-rose tattoo:** he greets the wearer by name immediately, regardless of whether the wearer is Cael or another former crewmate. This is the most likely involuntary reveal in the campaign — Hendrik is the security hole.
</npc_reveal_triggers>

<npc_suspicions>
### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That Søren has gone soft on something. (He has noticed Søren is more careful around Brotherhood gossip than he used to be.)
</npc_suspicions>

<npc_voice_refusal_pattern>
### Voice and refusal pattern
- Helsingør Danish under slurred English. Affectionate, sentimental, repetitive. Refuses nothing. He has nothing he considers worth refusing.
</npc_voice_refusal_pattern>

<npc_disposition_shift_conditions>
### Disposition shift conditions
- Affectionate baseline; affectionate after a fight; affectionate the next morning.
</npc_disposition_shift_conditions>

<npc_pre_amnesia_relationship_and_recognition_triggers>
### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** uncle-figure in Cael's pre-amnesia life. Cael paid his Helm tab some weeks.
- **Recognition triggers:** the face, the voice, the walk, the tattoo. All four. Any one of them makes Hendrik greet him aloud.
- **On recognition:** Hendrik says "Caleb, boy" in front of everyone in the Helm without realizing it is consequential. This is the most likely accident that exposes Cael's identity to the room. The narrator should consider Hendrik's presence in any tavern scene.
