## 2. THE PLANTATION COUNCIL

The fifteen seated families who run the plantation belt and dominate the upper-town political life. Their economic weight is the largest single block in the town.

**Composition:**
- Klaus Rasmussen (patriarch, Trefoldighed, ~380 enslaved laborers)
- Erik Rasmussen (heir, increasingly running operational matters)
- Agnes Rasmussen (daughter, no Council seat, social significance)
- Hans Müller (Tre Kilder, ~210 enslaved laborers)
- The Krüger and Storgaard households (Rasmussen-aligned)
- The Pratts (English-descended; dissenting)
- Two coffee-hills planter families (Larsen and Voss; dissenting on the slave-market expansion)
- Three minor Rasmussen-cousin households on individual seats
- Two minor families bound to the Rasmussens by debt
- Various overseers on the smaller plantations (not Council members; structurally subordinate)

**What it wants:**
- Sugar prices stable or rising.
- Labor supply — meaning the slave trade — uninterrupted.
- Selskab cover from Spanish raiding and Brotherhood incursion onto the windward coast.
- Internal harmony (the Rasmussens) or the leverage to break it productively (the dissenting bloc).
- Eventually: the family inheritances secure across generations.

**Internal split:**
- 9-6 majority for the Rasmussen bloc.
- **The slave-market expansion vote** (see Hidden Section I) — Day 2 Council meeting. The Rasmussens will win.
- **The Storgaard petition to the British Crown** — a covert wing of the majority bloc planning to invite British protection. Klaus does not know. Erik does.
- **The succession question** — Klaus's failing health raises the question of when Erik takes the seat formally. Klaus has been delaying. Erik has been patient.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- The Rasmussen bloc, specifically, wants him dead. This is Erik's personal project; Klaus is willfully not asking; the rest of the bloc would not weep for a Brotherhood captain.
- The dissenting bloc has no specific interest in Cael but would side with him if a confrontation with the Rasmussens served their political position.

**What it fears:**
- Royal Navy intervention or a Spanish coastal raid threatening the windward valleys.
- The Storgaard petition becoming public before the Rasmussens have decided whether to bless it or smother it.
- A serious slave uprising. The plantation belt's labor outnumbers its overseers fifteen to one.
- Internal dissent within the Rasmussen bloc.

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** the planters are the Selskab's most important constituents; tension here is constant but mutual.
- **Brotherhood:** mixed. The planters buy slaves from slavers, not from Brotherhood captains, and view the Brotherhood as predatory rather than commercial. The Rasmussens specifically have a grudge over Frederik.
- **Spanish Remnant:** tolerated at distance; some Council families are quietly Catholic.
- **Sephardim:** essential commercial partners; the Council families borrow from de Lima.
- **Maroons:** considered a present-tense threat; expedition occasionally discussed and always postponed.
- **Free Black population in town:** treated as commercial counterparties when they have skills (smith work, coopering, sailoring) and as social inferiors at all times.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** the Storgaards quietly want it; the Rasmussens are ambivalent; the dissenting bloc fears it.
