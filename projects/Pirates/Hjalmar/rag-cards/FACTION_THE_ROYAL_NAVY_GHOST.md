## 8. THE ROYAL NAVY GHOST

The faction that is not in Sankt Hjalmar. Yet.

**Composition:**
- No persons in town. The Royal Navy's eastern Caribbean station is at Antigua and Jamaica. The nearest patrol vessels typically operate near Barbados and the Leeward Islands.
- The threat the Navy represents is real for every other faction.

**What it would want, if it arrived:**
- Suppression of piracy.
- Suppression of Spanish coastal raiding from English perspectives.
- Eventually: British political influence over the Danish charter — possibly through the Storgaard petition path, possibly through naval pressure.

**Internal split:**
- The actual Royal Navy is divided on West Indies policy. Some captains pursue Brotherhood predation aggressively; others quietly negotiate. Some take prizes themselves under flexible interpretations of orders.
- The Admiralty in London is divided on whether to commit naval resources to the West Indies anti-piracy effort or to focus on European threats.

**What it would want from Cael:**
- Hanging. The Royal Navy hangs Brotherhood captains.

**What it fears:**
- Nothing directly. The faction's strength is asymmetric.

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** would arrive offering "cooperation"; would effectively replace.
- **Council:** the Storgaard wing welcomes; the Rasmussens are split; the dissenting bloc fears.
- **Brotherhood:** existential enemy.
- **Spanish Remnant:** new occupier worse than the old.
- **Sephardim:** would bring British religious culture, which is more hostile than Danish religious culture.
- **Maroons:** would bring Jamaican-style anti-Maroon operations.
- **Free Black population:** would bring British slave code.

**Probability of arrival within the campaign window:**
- The Royal Navy will *not* arrive in Sankt Hjalmar within Cael's amnesia recovery arc. The narrator does not introduce a frigate in the harbor as a deus ex machina.
- The Navy can, however, exert pressure from a distance — news of a frigate at Charlotte Amalie, a rumor of a station rotation, a Royal Navy officer arriving on a packet boat asking questions.
