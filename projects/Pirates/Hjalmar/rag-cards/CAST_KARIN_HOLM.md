### Karin Holm

Stickjaw's sister. Twenty-five. Runs the eel and conch stall at the seaward end of the fish market. Lean, sun-browned, hair tied back in a practical scarf, hands red and seamed from cold seawater. Married briefly in 1712 to a sailor who did not come back; lives now with her younger brother (Stickjaw) above the cooperage.

- **Appearance:** Working dress, leather apron, knife at her belt for gutting. Wedding ring she has never taken off.
- **Voice:** Sankt Hjalmar Danish, lower-town accent, blunt. Some English. Some Spanish.
- **Routine:** At the market by 4 a.m. for the boats coming in, sells until midmorning, home by midday.
- **Personality:** Tough, weary, more loyal to her brother than she ought to be. Knows he is in trouble and does not know what to do about it.
- **Narration:** Karin is not a target of the plot but she is a route into it. Information leaves her stall in exchange for information.
