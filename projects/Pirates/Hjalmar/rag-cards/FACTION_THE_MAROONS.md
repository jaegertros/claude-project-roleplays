## 6. THE MAROONS

The escaped enslaved community in the highlands. Lugar Quieto.

**Composition:**
- Madre Joana (leader, formerly enslaved at Tre Kilder)
- Roughly 70 people total — men, women, children
- Six former Brotherhood sailors who escaped both slavery and the sea
- Eliza Free (Cherokee-born; healer; partially set apart from the main community)
- Various young men and women raised in the settlement and never enslaved
- Newcomers (typically 2-4 per year escape successfully from the plantation belt)

**What it wants:**
- Continued survival of the settlement.
- Sufficient food, water, weapons, and intelligence to defend itself.
- Trade connections in the town that do not compromise location.
- Eventually: more of its own people freed from the plantation belt.

**Internal split:**
- Younger Maroons born in the settlement are restless; some want to take action against the Müller estate specifically.
- Older Maroons including Madre Joana counsel patience.
- A small faction wants to leave the island entirely on a stolen ship if a Brotherhood captain could be persuaded to help.
- Eliza Free is communal-but-apart; her recognition of Cael's marks puts her in a position the rest of the settlement does not share.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- If Cael's pre-amnesia connection to the rifle deal surfaces: continued partnership, more weapons, possibly transport.
- Otherwise: no specific interest in him.

**What it fears:**
- A Council expedition with garrison support.
- Betrayal by one of its own town allies.
- A hurricane destroying the settlement's stores.
- A larger Spanish or British presence that brings systematic anti-Maroon operations (as in Jamaica).

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** officially nonexistent. In practice the Selskab tolerates because the punitive expedition would cost too much.
- **Council:** the Müllers specifically have a long memory about the 1701 escape that produced Madre Joana. The other planter families recognize the settlement as a problem but have not personally lost laborers to it.
- **Brotherhood:** ally-of-convenience for some captains; no obligation either direction.
- **Spanish Remnant:** Father Vega has heard certain confessions that have informed his quiet practice of charity toward the settlement.
- **Sephardim:** no direct relationship.
- **Free Black population in town:** sympathetic and quietly supportive; some of the trade network runs through Free Quarter households.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** would mean systematic war.
