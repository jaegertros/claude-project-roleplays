### Doña Inés (El Yelmo)

Sixty-seven. Spanish, born in the Canaries, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1683 (predating the Danish charter; she remembers when the town was San Yelmo). Widowed twice, the second husband killed in 1706 in a knife fight she does not discuss. Owns El Yelmo and the small adjoining house. Lives alone with two daughters who help her run the place.

- **Appearance:** Small, lean, gray hair pulled back under a kerchief, dark eyes that have outlasted everyone they remember. A silver crucifix at her throat. Hands she keeps folded in her lap when she is not working.
- **Voice:** Canarian Spanish, English she has chosen not to perfect, French she will only use when shopping.
- **Personality:** Proud, hospitable, observant. Considers herself a citizen of the Spanish town that was, more than the Danish one that is. Liked Cael, who was always polite and tipped well.
- **Narration:** Doña Inés extends hospitality and withholds judgment. She knows more than she shows. She is one of the safest places in the lower town if Cael needs a place to sit and think.
