## 5. THE SEPHARDIM

The Iberian-Jewish merchant community. Small in number, central in commerce.

**Composition:**
- Isaac de Lima and his household (counting house, two sons abroad)
- Roughly 22 other Sephardic households
- Hazzan Joaquim Ribera and his family
- The small school at the Mahamad House
- Transient Sephardic merchants from Curaçao, Amsterdam, and London passing through

**What it wants:**
- Continued commercial freedom.
- Religious quiet — the freedom to maintain the Mahamad House openly.
- Strong trans-Atlantic correspondence (Curaçao, Amsterdam, London).
- Information advantage in the port's commerce.

**Internal split:**
- A generational divide: older Sephardim remember the 1492 expulsion as immediate; younger Sephardim experience it as inherited memory. The older generation is more cautious about visible community profile.
- Commercial competition exists among Sephardic households but is muted by communal solidarity.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- Repayment of de Lima's outstanding loan.
- Cael's continued non-betrayal of various commercial arrangements he has made through de Lima.
- Specifically, no scandal involving the *Whisht*'s past cargo sales through the Sephardic network.

**What it fears:**
- A new wave of religious intolerance (most plausibly: the British Royal Navy bringing British anti-Jewish sentiment, or Spain reasserting and bringing Inquisition memory).
- A commercial collapse — particularly Don Esteban's network being exposed in a way that implicates the Sephardim by association.
- Pogrom-level violence. (The community has not experienced this in Sankt Hjalmar, but the community memory holds the possibility.)

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** commercial partners, formal correspondents.
- **Council:** lenders to many planter households; treated with formal respect.
- **Brotherhood:** financial backbone of Brotherhood operations.
- **Spanish Remnant:** delicate; see above.
- **Maroons:** no direct relationship; one Maroon settlement member is a former Sephardic-household enslaved laborer who escaped during a smallpox season.
- **Free Black population:** some commercial relationships with the cooperage, the smithy.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** feared.
