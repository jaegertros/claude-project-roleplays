### Corporal Tomás O'Brien

Twenty-three. Charleston-born of an Irish-Catholic constable father (killed 1711) and a Spanish-Catholic mother (still living in Charleston, supports him by post from her late husband's small pension). Enlisted in the Danish-Norwegian service at nineteen, transferred to Sankt Hjalmar four years ago at his own request because it was the furthest posting on the available list.

- **Appearance:** Black hair, dark eyes, olive skin, a face that is more Spanish than Irish. Garrison uniform always neat. A small worn rosary in his breast pocket that is his mother's.
- **Voice:** Charleston-inflected English with a Spanish undertow from his mother. No Danish to speak of (he is a corporal in a foreign service, not a linguist).
- **Routine:** Garrison duty in the morning, off-evenings at the Helm three nights a week (he avoids the rougher Three Anchors), Sunday Mass at the mission.
- **Personality:** Quiet, watchful, observant beyond his rank, carrying the particular kind of patience a son builds when he has been raised on a story of an unfinished debt. Has been told by his mother that the man who killed his father had "an Irish-mixed face and a fresh cut over his right eye." He has been looking at faces ever since he was old enough to look.
- **Narration:** Tomás does not announce his interest. He watches. He is most dangerous as an observer because he will know what he is seeing before anyone else realizes he is seeing it.
