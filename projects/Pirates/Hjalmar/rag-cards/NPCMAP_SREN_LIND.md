<profile_heading>
## 4. SØREN LIND
</profile_heading>

<npc_knows>
### Knows
- Cael's full pre-amnesia history at sea — the Bermuda privateer years, Jens Eriksohn, the *Whisht*, the Rasmussen brig raid, the past summer's troubles.
- That Erik Rasmussen has been paying him for information since spring 1715.
- That Erik hired men to kill Cael three nights ago — he was not told but he was able to infer from the timing of a payment.
- That the body was supposed to be weighed and dropped but the harbor swell has been wrong for that this week.
- That Cael's daughter exists. Cael told him once after drinking — Cael did not believe Agnes had been sent away for a real fever.
</npc_knows>

<npc_does_not_know>
### Does not know
- That Cael survived the attack.
- (Not yet — he learns this within the first day of story-open when he sees Cael walking the wharf.)
</npc_does_not_know>

<npc_reveal_triggers>
### Reveal triggers
- **Direct sight of Cael at the chandlery door:** Søren freezes for approximately one second. Then he becomes carefully normal.
- **Erik's name spoken to him aloud by Cael:** he goes pale and recovers. The recovery is the tell.
- **The phrase "the Whisht" or "Jens" used by Cael in his presence:** he cannot pretend he does not know what is meant. The decision he has been putting off arrives.
</npc_reveal_triggers>

<npc_suspicions>
### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That Cael may have known Søren was an informant and was on his way to confront him the night of the attack. (He was not. But Søren cannot know that.)
- That Erik will turn on him eventually. The plan is for Søren to be on a packet to Copenhagen with his family by April 1716.
</npc_suspicions>

<npc_voice_refusal_pattern>
### Voice and refusal pattern
- Quiet Danish-accented English. Polite. Refusals come in two stages: first an offer of a smaller version of what was asked, then a regretful claim of insufficient resources or knowledge. He does not say no directly.
- Under pressure he does not lie cleanly. The lies have edges.
</npc_voice_refusal_pattern>

<npc_disposition_shift_conditions>
### Disposition shift conditions
- **Polite → conflicted ally:** Cael appears to genuinely have no memory and Søren's old loyalty surfaces.
- **Polite → second-time betrayer:** Erik's next payment arrives and Søren has not yet warned Cael.
- **Polite → fled:** if Erik begins to suspect him, Søren is on a boat within seventy-two hours.
</npc_disposition_shift_conditions>

<npc_pre_amnesia_relationship_and_recognition_triggers>
### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** quartermaster on the *Whisht* for fourteen months. Cael trusted him completely. The trust is what makes the current betrayal hurt in a specific way.
- **Recognition triggers:** recognizes the face on sight, the voice within one word, the walk from across the wharf. Also recognizes the compass-rose tattoo and the eyebrow scar. Each of the three confirms a recognition that does not need confirming.
- **On recognition:** the decision Søren has been postponing arrives. The narrator should write the first encounter as the moment of decision, not after it.
