## 3. THE FREE BROTHERHOOD

The loose network of pirate and privateer captains who use Sankt Hjalmar as a careenage, refit, and trade port. Not an organization — a community of practice.

**Composition (currently in port):**
- Captain Pieter Maartens (*De Vrije Wind*, brigantine, refitting)
- Cael Eriksohn (*Whisht*, sloop, undermanned and damaged on the wharf)
- A handful of smaller captains and their crews
- Old Hendrik and former Brotherhood sailors now retired ashore
- Søren Lind (former quartermaster, ashore but still connected)

**What it wants:**
- A safe place to careen, refit, and resupply.
- Discreet fences for prize cargo (de Lima, Curaçao correspondents).
- Stable harbor fees and reasonable customs treatment (Knudsen).
- Information about Spanish patrols, treasure-fleet salvage news, and Royal Navy movements.
- The treasure-fleet wreck site — currently — is the most active commercial opportunity in the eastern Caribbean. Several Brotherhood captains will sail for it within the month.

**Internal split:**
- Brotherhood captains are nominally peers but in practice form clusters around shared history. Maartens and Cael share the Jens Eriksohn lineage; other captains have their own genealogies.
- The treasure-fleet question is currently splitting captains by appetite — some want to commit fast (Henry Jennings's coming raid will draw them); some want to wait and watch.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- Continued participation in the network. Maartens would offer Cael a place if Cael needed one.
- Cael's discretion about anything he knows about other captains' arrangements.

**What it fears:**
- Royal Navy attention.
- A Spanish operation in cooperation with the Selskab.
- A captain breaking the Articles publicly enough that the rest of the Brotherhood has to disown him.

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** tolerated by Bremer, fee'd through Knudsen.
- **Council:** wary mutual avoidance. The Rasmussens are now the Brotherhood's specific problem because of Frederik.
- **Spanish Remnant:** Brotherhood captains avoid Father Vega and the mission. Some Catholic-born Brotherhood sailors attend Mass privately.
- **Sephardim:** essential commercial partners; the financial backbone of Brotherhood operations.
- **Maroons:** quiet ally in some cases (the 1714 rifle deal); the Maroons consider some Brotherhood captains potential confederates.
- **Free Black population:** Brotherhood crews are unusually racially integrated; Brotherhood captains hire free Black sailors at standard shares.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** the Brotherhood's greatest fear.
