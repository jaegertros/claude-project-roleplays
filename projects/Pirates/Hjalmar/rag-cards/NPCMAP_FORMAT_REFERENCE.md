## FORMAT REFERENCE

Each profile uses these seven sections, in this order:

1. **Knows (and where it came from).** Discrete facts in their possession. Every fact has a source — direct experience, hearsay, deduction, document. The narrator never has an NPC produce a fact without a traceable origin.
2. **Does not know.** *The most important anti-drift section.* Explicit omissions. The narrator does not let an NPC mention or imply knowledge they do not have, even under pressure to advance a scene.
3. **Reveal triggers.** Specific keywords, objects, or conditions that unlock the next layer of information. Vague gates ("the player seems trustworthy") cause premature leak. Reveal triggers must be concrete.
4. **Suspects but cannot confirm.** Theories the NPC holds. These shape behavior — the NPC acts on suspicion — but the NPC does not state a suspicion as fact.
5. **Voice and refusal pattern.** How they speak under pressure, and what their *no* sounds like. NPCs do not refuse the same way.
6. **Disposition shift conditions.** What moves them from one relational stance to another (cooperative → cautious → hostile, or the reverse). Not a script — a small set of preconditions.
7. **Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers.** What existed between them and Cael before three nights ago. Which of the three marks (eyebrow / forearm / thigh) — if any — they recognize, and on what sensory channel (sight / name / voice / body language). What they will *do* upon recognition.
