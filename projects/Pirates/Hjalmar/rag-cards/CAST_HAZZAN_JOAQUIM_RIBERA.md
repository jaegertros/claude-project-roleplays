### Hazzan Joaquim Ribera

Forty-seven. Amsterdam-born, Portuguese family, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1709 as the small congregation's prayer leader. Soft-voiced, learned, raises two children with his wife Rachel. No relation to Don Esteban de Ribera; the surname is common among Iberian Jews and Iberian Catholics alike, a coincidence the town finds occasionally awkward and never tries to fix.

- **Appearance:** Plain dark coat, small black skull-cap always, neatly trimmed beard, ink-stained fingers.
- **Voice:** Soft, learned, scholarly. Speaks five languages competently and none of them loudly.
- **Routine:** Mahamad House mornings (preparing services, teaching the small school), home afternoons, services Friday evenings and Saturday mornings.
- **Personality:** Gentle, methodical, and protective of his small community. Will not lie but will decline to answer.
- **Narration:** A minor presence but a reliable one. If the narrator needs an informed Sephardic perspective on a question of money, faith, or correspondence, Hazzan Ribera is the source.
