## 7. THE FREE AND ENSLAVED BLACK POPULATION IN TOWN

The largest social block in the town if counted by head. Internally diverse and structurally divided.

**Composition:**

*Free residents:*
- Joseph Tide (cooper)
- Big Anna (smith)
- Ezekiel Boatswain (Methodist elder, sailmaker)
- Roughly 80 free Black households in the Free Quarter
- A scattering of free Black residents in the Spanish Quarter (Catholic households)
- Several free Black market women in the lower town
- Occasional free Black sailors on Brotherhood ships in port

*Enslaved residents (in town):*
- Domestic enslaved labor in Council household town houses (Else, the laundress at the Rasmussen town house, others)
- Dockworkers hired out by their owners
- Workshop labor in various trades
- Approximately 200 enslaved people resident in or working daily in the town

*Off-island/off-relationship:*
- The vastly larger plantation belt enslaved population (~4,200), which interacts with the town only through specific channels

**What it wants:**

*The free Black community:*
- Continued ability to operate their trades and households freely.
- Protection from re-enslavement schemes and abuse.
- The Methodist meeting house continuing.
- Information about families still enslaved on the plantations.

*Enslaved residents in town:*
- Survival; small advantages; eventually freedom or rest.
- Their own informational networks that bypass white attention.

**Internal split:**
- The free Black community is itself socially layered — established free households (Tide, Anna, Boatswain) and newer arrivals; Methodist and Catholic and non-religious; Bajan, Saint-Dominguan, Carolina-born, Cuban-born, African-born backgrounds.
- The free / enslaved divide is sharp and not bridged by shared ancestry. Free Black residents are *not* automatically allies of enslaved residents; some are explicitly distant.
- However, the informational network is largely shared — gossip moves across the line.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- The free Black community: nothing specific. He is one of many sailors passing through. Tide and Anna would help him if he behaved well; would refuse him without acrimony if he behaved badly.
- Enslaved town residents: nothing he can give them. They are watching everyone with the carefulness of those who have learned to.

**What it fears:**
- An intensification of slave codes (which a Council vote could produce).
- The Royal Navy bringing British slave-code practice.
- An outbreak of violence in the town that turns the white population paranoid.

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** distrusted but obeyed.
- **Council:** structurally subordinate; some commercial relationships with free trades.
- **Brotherhood:** mixed; some Brotherhood captains hire free Black sailors at standard shares, which makes the Brotherhood relatively trusted.
- **Spanish Remnant:** the Catholic free Black households are part of the remnant.
- **Sephardim:** professional respect (Tide does barrel work for de Lima's warehouse).
- **Maroons:** sympathetic. The Free Quarter is the primary channel of trade to the highland settlement. Madre Joana grew up enslaved at Tre Kilder; some Free Quarter families remember her.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** feared most by the free Black community, which knows how British practice differs from Danish.
