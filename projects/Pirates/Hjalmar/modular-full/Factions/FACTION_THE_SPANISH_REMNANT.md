## 4. THE SPANISH REMNANT

The descendants of the pre-Danish Sankt Hjalmar population, plus Spanish-Catholic transients who pass through.

**Composition:**
- Roughly 30 Spanish or Spanish-descended households in the Spanish Quarter
- Father Joaquín Vega and the Mision de San Yelmo
- Doña Inés and her two daughters at El Yelmo
- Don Esteban de Ribera (formally Spanish; not socially part of the remnant in the way the others are)
- A scattering of Spanish-speaking households in the Free Quarter (Catholic free Black families)
- Transient Spanish-speaking sailors on ships in port

**What it wants:**
- Survival as a recognizable community. The Danish takeover in 1683 reduced their political standing; they have absorbed it but not forgotten.
- The mission preserved.
- Quiet commercial life through the Spanish Quarter shops and El Yelmo.
- Continued connection to the Catholic episcopal authority in Puerto Rico through Father Vega's correspondence.

**Internal split:**
- The "old families" (here since before the Danish charter) and the "new arrivals" (post-1683) maintain a polite social distance.
- Father Vega and Don Esteban do not work in concert and do not entirely trust each other. Father Vega knows what Esteban is. Esteban does not know that Father Vega knows.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- Generally nothing. Cael is a Brotherhood captain, not part of the community.
- Don Esteban specifically wants Cael's head.
- Father Vega specifically wants — if he can manage it — for the conflicts in the town to resolve with less bloodshed than they otherwise would. He would help Cael obliquely if he saw a way.

**What it fears:**
- A new wave of Danish-Protestant pressure on the mission.
- Spain itself reasserting interest in the island (which would mean military occupation, ending the comfortable in-between state).
- Specifically: Don Esteban's network being exposed in a way that embarrasses the whole community.

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Selskab:** politely tolerated; Bremer treats Father Vega with personal respect.
- **Council:** quiet commercial ties (some planters import Spanish brandies through the remnant); social distance.
- **Brotherhood:** wary distance. Spanish remnant memory of Brotherhood depredations on Spanish vessels is long.
- **Sephardim:** delicate. Spanish Catholic memory and Sephardic memory contain the Inquisition. Practical cooperation but no warmth.
- **Maroons:** old families' household help is occasionally Maroon-connected; Father Vega has heard confessions from Maroon-adjacent figures.
- **Free Black population:** the Catholic free Black households are part of the remnant.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** dreaded almost as much as by the Brotherhood.
