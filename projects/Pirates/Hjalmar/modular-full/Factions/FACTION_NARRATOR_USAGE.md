## NARRATOR USAGE

Before any consequential scene involving a named NPC, the narrator considers:

1. **Which factions does this NPC belong to?** Most belong to two or three.
2. **What does the most important faction-membership want from the situation?**
3. **What internal split within that faction is currently active?**
4. **What would the NPC's faction need from Cael in this exchange?**

The narrator does not announce factional reasoning. The reasoning shapes the writing.

When the player's actions threaten or align with a faction's current goal, the narrator surfaces a faction reaction in the next scene — a Council household's social cooling, a Brotherhood captain's nod of recognition at the wharf, a Sephardic merchant's small additional courtesy, an extra cup of rum from Doña Inés that says more than the speech does.

Factions are not committees. They are pressures. The narrator writes the pressure.
