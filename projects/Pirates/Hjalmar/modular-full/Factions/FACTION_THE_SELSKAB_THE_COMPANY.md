## 1. THE SELSKAB / THE COMPANY

The Sankt Hjalmar Selskab — the chartered company that holds the island from the Danish-Norwegian crown.

**Composition:**
- Governor Magnus Bremer (head of Selskab operations on the island)
- Harbormaster Lauritz Knudsen (Customs House and harbor revenue)
- Captain Holger Vinter and the 28-man garrison (Fort Christiansborg)
- Corporal Tomás O'Brien and lower-ranking NCOs
- Pastor Anders Bagger (Lutheran establishment; technically Selskab-paid)
- Three Selskab clerks at Government House handling administration
- The Selskab board in Copenhagen (off-map; receives quarterly reports)

**What it wants:**
- Harbor revenue stable and growing.
- The port quiet enough that Copenhagen does not ask questions and the British Admiralty does not consider intervention.
- The Brotherhood tolerated as long as Brotherhood activity does not visibly cross the Selskab's bow.
- The plantation belt productive.
- The garrison adequate but not expensive.

**Internal split:**
- Bremer himself does *not* fully serve Selskab interests — he diverts about 18% of revenue into a private discretionary fund (see `KNOWLEDGE_4_Hidden.md` Section VII). The split is not factional but personal: Bremer believes the Selskab undervalues the island's actual needs.
- Knudsen serves whoever pays him — Selskab on paper, several private interests in practice.
- Vinter is a conventional officer; if the Selskab issued orders, Vinter would execute. He has not yet been asked anything that conflicted with his sense of duty.
- Tomás O'Brien is loyal to the garrison and to a private hunt that has nothing to do with Selskab business.
- Pastor Bagger preaches what the upper town expects to hear.

**What it wants from Cael:**
- Nothing specifically. Cael is one of many Brotherhood captains routinely processed by Knudsen. If Cael becomes loud or visible, the Selskab will want him not to be.

**What it fears:**
- A scandal that brings Copenhagen attention. Particularly: any public murder, any conflict with Spain that requires the garrison to act and reveals how inadequate the garrison is, any treason against the charter (e.g., the Storgaard petition becoming public).
- A British naval interest. The arrival of a Royal Navy frigate to "discuss" anti-pirate cooperation would end most of what makes Sankt Hjalmar profitable.

**Relations with other factions:**
- **Plantation Council:** symbiotic; the Selskab needs the planters' goodwill and the planters need Selskab cover. Bremer is uneasy about the Council's wing that flirts with British protection.
- **Brotherhood:** tolerated, fee'd, quietly observed. The Selskab will not pursue a Brotherhood captain who pays his fees and kills nobody upper-town.
- **Spanish Remnant:** treated as a quaint minority. Bremer respects Father Vega personally.
- **Sephardim:** valued for commerce; the Selskab benefits from de Lima's capital network.
- **Maroons:** officially denied to exist; in practice tolerated because the cost of destroying them exceeds the benefit.
- **Free and Enslaved Black population:** legally regulated through the slave codes the Selskab enforces; the free Black community is taxed and tolerated.
- **Royal Navy ghost:** the Selskab fears its arrival above all else.
