## 8. ELIZA FREE

### Knows
- The Cherokee grief-mark pattern on Cael's inner left thigh, because her own grandmother used the same pattern on her in 1684.
- That Cael speaks Cherokee under his amnesia — she heard him talk in his sleep on the second night.
- That he was struck on the back of the head with something heavy and rounded — almost certainly a belaying pin.
- That his rescue was not the work of any party that would have wanted him saved on official grounds. (Petter brought him to her precisely to avoid the garrison.)

### Does not know
- The name Cael MacAoidh.
- The exact reason someone wanted Cael dead.
- Anything about Agnes Rasmussen, the child, or Erik's hunt.

### Reveal triggers
- **A direct, private, conscious return visit by Cael with the thigh-marks visible or referenced:** Eliza decides whether to tell him. The decision is *not yet made* at story-open.
- **Cael speaking Cherokee in her presence while awake:** she replies in Cherokee. He may not realize what he has done.
- **An attack on the Maroon settlement or on her cottage:** she becomes immediately direct with whoever survives to ask her anything.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That Cael's mother was Cherokee and was probably murdered. (She inferred this from the marks and from the silence in his sleep when he was nine years old. She does not know the year or the town.)
- That she will tell him eventually. Has not yet decided when.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Low, careful English defeating any attempt to place an accent. Refuses by not answering. Will sit silently through a long question and then change the subject.
- Under pressure she does not break. She becomes still.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Waiting → telling:** specific evidence that Cael is recovering memory or that the recovery is becoming dangerous to him.
- **Waiting → protective:** an attempt by the town on Cael or on her settlement.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** had never met Cael until three nights ago. Tended him two days. He does not remember her.
- **Recognition triggers:** she recognizes him by sight (she has seen his face awake and asleep). She also recognizes the thigh-marks specifically; the other two marks she has not yet had reason to study.
- **On recognition:** she has already recognized him. The question is what she does next.
