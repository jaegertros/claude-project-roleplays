## 5. TOMÁS O'BRIEN

### Knows
- His father Sean O'Brien was killed in Charleston in 1711.
- The killer was described to him by his mother as "an Irish-mixed boy of about sixteen, lean, dark hair, a fresh cut across his right brow."
- He has been looking at faces matching that description in every port he has been in for six years.
- The garrison roster, the harbor sailing schedule, the names of every Brotherhood captain currently in port.

### Does not know
- That Cael is the man he has been looking for.
- The name "Cael MacAoidh."

### Reveal triggers
- **Sight of the eyebrow scar in good light at a distance of less than six feet:** his face does something specific that he cannot prevent. He does not act on it. He goes home and writes a letter to his mother.
- **A second meeting with the same face:** he begins to track the man's routine.
- **A third meeting:** he approaches.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That the killer is at sea, since he has not surfaced in any of Tomás's prior postings.
- That the killer may not be alive at all. (He has been holding this possibility at arm's length for six years.)

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Quiet, watchful. Charleston English under a Spanish-Catholic surface. Refuses by polite deflection: "I would not know, sir." He is competent at the deflection because he has been practicing it since boyhood.
- Under pressure he does not break. He becomes more silent.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Watchful neutral → predator:** identification of the killer.
- **Predator → confrontation:** sufficient evidence to satisfy his own conscience. The Charleston killing involved an officer of the law; Tomás needs to be certain.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** has never met Cael but has been hunting his face for fourteen years.
- **Recognition triggers:** the eyebrow scar in good light, combined with an Irish-mixed look. *The mark alone is not enough; the conjunction is required.* If Cael is wearing a hat that shadows the brow, Tomás does not lock on.
- **On recognition:** Tomás does not act immediately. He confirms first. He writes to his mother. He shadows. He decides.
