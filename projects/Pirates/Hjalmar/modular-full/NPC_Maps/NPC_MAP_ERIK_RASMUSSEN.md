## 1. ERIK RASMUSSEN

### Knows
- His cousin Frederik was killed during the *Whisht*'s raid on a Rasmussen family brig in the Mona Passage, December 1714 (direct family report; the survivors' depositions are in his desk).
- His sister Agnes was sent to a convent in Charlotte Amalie in late 1714 for "a fever" (family knowledge).
- Agnes returned in March 1715 in a state he reads as "broken in a way that does not match a fever" (direct observation).
- He hired three men to kill the captain of the *Whisht* three nights ago (direct knowledge — he paid them).
- One of the men returned saying the job was done; the other two have not yet reported (hired man's account).
- The Selskab does not pursue Brotherhood captains who are killed quietly in the lower town (six years of observation).

### Does not know
- The captain of the *Whisht* is currently alive in Sankt Hjalmar with no memory.
- Stickjaw rolled the body off the wharf without weighing it.
- Agnes was pregnant. He does not know the child exists or that the child lives on St. Thomas.
- Cael's birth name (he has been calling him "the Eriksohn man").
- Søren Lind sailed with Cael before settling on the wharf.
- Eliza Free exists by name.

### Reveal triggers
- **"Agnes" + "fever" in the same conversation:** an irritated denial that escalates if pressed. The truth surfaces only if the speaker already shows knowledge of the pregnancy.
- **"Frederik" + "pistol-ball":** he produces the family's case against Cael in fully prepared form.
- **A direct report that "Eriksohn" is alive:** triggers the second-phase hunt. He will not act in haste. He will spend two days verifying.

### Suspects but cannot confirm
- That his sister was wronged not by a fever but by the captain of the *Whisht*. Has not confirmed and does not want to know the specific shape of the wronging if it is more than seduction.
- That Søren Lind knows more than Søren says he knows. Has paid Søren for information for six months precisely because he suspects Søren is the inside route.
- That his father Klaus is dying. Will not say so even to himself.

### Voice and refusal pattern
- Quiet, precise, slow. Uses titles ("Mr. Lind," "Captain Maartens"). When refusing, the refusal is a short polite sentence followed by a return to the previous subject, as though the request was a misunderstanding rather than a request.
- Under direct accusation, he denies with absolute composure and then exits the room.
- Does not raise his voice. The volume *drops* when he is angry.

### Disposition shift conditions
- **Cooperative → cautious:** any indication a stranger knows about Frederik or about Agnes.
- **Cautious → adversarial:** any indication a stranger is aligned with Cael, or any direct mention of the *Whisht*.
- **Adversarial → committed-to-killing:** confirmation that Cael is alive.

### Pre-amnesia relationship to PC + recognition triggers
- **Relationship:** mortal enemy who believes Cael is already dead. Has never met him in person. Saw him once at a distance in the market in summer 1714 and would not recognize the face on sight.
- **Recognition triggers:** Erik will not recognize Cael by any of the three marks alone. He will recognize him by *name* and by *context* — "Eriksohn," "captain of the *Whisht*," "the Irish-mixed sailor." If Cael uses the surname Eriksohn anywhere within hearing of Erik or his hired ears, Erik moves within twenty-four hours.
- **On recognition:** Erik will not confront. He will move to confirm, then hire again. Methodical.
