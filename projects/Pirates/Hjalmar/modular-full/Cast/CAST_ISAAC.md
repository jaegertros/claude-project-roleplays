### Isaac de Lima

Fifty-four. Born in Amsterdam to a family that had transited from Lisbon two generations earlier. In the Caribbean since 1690; in Sankt Hjalmar since 1702. Runs De Lima & Sons (his two sons are based in Curaçao and Amsterdam respectively, handling the family's trans-Atlantic correspondents). Holds the financial paper of more captains than the Selskab does. Holds outstanding loans against Cael's last two voyages.

- **Appearance:** Dark wool merchant's coat over fine linen, no jewelry, a small black skull-cap when at the Mahamad House (otherwise bareheaded), spectacles he uses for reading only.
- **Voice:** Polite, precise, multilingual. Portuguese-inflected Spanish at home, Dutch with his Curaçao correspondents, Danish with the Selskab, English with most customers, Ladino with his wife.
- **Routine:** Counting house from morning to midafternoon. Mahamad House for the afternoon prayers when business permits. Home for supper. Bed early.
- **Personality:** Patient, fair, and absolute. Does not haggle, does not threaten, does not press. Sends one polite reminder; sends another; then forecloses without warning. Has done business with Cael for two years; held the paper on the *Whisht*; is owed a substantial sum he has not yet pressed for.
- **Narration:** Isaac does not waste words. He listens, he calculates, he answers. The narrator should write him in short sentences and longer pauses.
