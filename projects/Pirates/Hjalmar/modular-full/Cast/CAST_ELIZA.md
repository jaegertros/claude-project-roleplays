### Eliza Free

Forty-three. Cherokee-born, taken as a child from her family's town in the southern Appalachians, sold into the Caribbean trade in her early teens, escaped to a ship in her late teens, escaped again to Sankt Hjalmar's Maroon community in 1708. Healer, herbalist, midwife. Speaks several Cherokee dialects, several West African languages picked up in the holds, English, Spanish, broken Danish.

- **Appearance:** Medium height, dark skin warmed by Cherokee bone structure, black hair gone partly gray, kept long and tied back. Dressed in plain cotton — a wrap skirt, a long shirt, leather sandals. A small leather pouch at her belt that she touches when she is thinking.
- **Voice:** English in a careful low register that defeats most listeners' attempts to place the accent. Switches to other languages without announcement.
- **Routine:** Tends her garden and her remedies most days. Walks down to Petter's beach once a week, to the town once a month. Receives visitors at her cottage when they have walked the path to find her.
- **Personality:** Patient, observant, slow to speak, slower to judge. Recognized the thigh-marks on Cael the moment she saw them and has not yet decided what to do about it. Is waiting to see what kind of man wakes up from amnesia. Will tell him eventually. Will not tell him soon.
- **Narration:** Eliza does not ask questions to which she already knows the answer. The narrator should write her listening more than speaking. When she does speak, every word is chosen.
