### Lauritz Knudsen

Fifty-six. Danish, in Sankt Hjalmar since 1701 as harbormaster. Wife and three grown daughters in town. A man who has perfected the art of looking like he does not know things that he does in fact know.

- **Appearance:** Round-shouldered, balding, brass-buttoned customs-officer's coat that has been re-tailored twice as he has gained weight. A small notebook in his breast pocket. Sweat-stained collar by midmorning.
- **Voice:** Copenhagen Danish, careful English, bureaucratic phrasing. Says "in principle" and "as a matter of practice" in the same breath.
- **Routine:** Customs House from before first light (boats come in early) until midafternoon. Tavern (the Helm, usually) in the evening for an hour, then home.
- **Personality:** Corruptible in measured doses, loyal to his office in inverse proportion to the size of the bribe. Knows every captain in the port and the rough condition of every ship. Has accepted small considerations from Cael, from Søren, from Maartens, from Don Esteban, from de Lima, and from most of the Plantation Council, and considers this a balanced ledger.
- **Narration:** Knudsen will not betray a regular customer for less than considerable money. He will, however, become slow and forgetful at the worst possible moment if the right pressure arrives from another direction.
