### Tregenza (the Three Anchors)

Fifty-two. Cornish, lost his right eye and his sloop in the same Spanish action in 1709, ashore ever since. Owns the Three Anchors with a sleeping partner whose name he refuses to disclose. Lives in a back room behind the tavern.

- **Appearance:** Lean, weathered, the empty eye-socket covered by a black silk patch, the other eye sharp and pale. Sailor's clothes worn well past their useful life. A short sword kept under the bar that he has used.
- **Voice:** Rolling Cornish under sailor's English. "Ee" for "you" when he is tired. Maritime metaphors come naturally.
- **Personality:** Hard. Loyal to the trade. Will shelter a man in trouble for a night without questions; will sell him out on the second night without apology. Operates by the unspoken rules of the Brotherhood as he understands them.
- **Narration:** Tregenza is the moral center of the wharf — not a kind man, but a consistent one. The Brotherhood trusts him because he is predictable.
