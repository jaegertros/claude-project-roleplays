### Klaus Rasmussen

Patriarch. Sixty-six. Tall, thinning white hair combed back, blue eyes gone pale. Pre-war Danish landed gentry transplanted to the sugar trade in 1684. Has watched two wives die — the first in childbirth, the second of yellow fever in 1702 — and three children survive (Erik, Agnes, and a younger son in Copenhagen). His left hand has begun to tremble in the past year and he is the only one who knows it cannot be hidden much longer.

- **Appearance:** Dark linen coat over a fine cambric shirt, silver-headed cane he uses more for emphasis than support, gold pocket-watch on a worn fob.
- **Voice:** Slow, measured, with the old Holstein Danish accent under a careful schoolbook English. Never raises it. Pauses before speaking; the pause is the threat.
- **Routine:** Estate matters in the morning, town three days a week (Council meetings Tuesday; market Friday; church Sunday), early bed.
- **Personality:** Patrician. Believes in order, family, the long view. Capable of cold cruelty when the family interest demands it; equally capable of unexpected lenience when it costs him nothing. Increasingly afraid of his own son.
- **Narration:** Klaus speaks in complete sentences. He does not interrupt and is not interrupted. When he stops a conversation, it stops.
