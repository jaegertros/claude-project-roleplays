### Don Esteban de Ribera

Forty-one. Castilian-born, Madrid-educated, in the Caribbean since 1708 on what most people in the port believe is the wine trade. Maintains a small establishment near the Customs House dealing in Spanish and Portuguese wines, sherries, and brandies. Reports to a viceregal intelligence office in San Juan. Has been quietly maintaining a network of paid eyes in Sankt Hjalmar for six years.

- **Appearance:** Carefully dressed in dark Spanish styles — a wool coat too heavy for the climate, fine linen shirt, a small dueling rapier worn as a gentleman's mark rather than a weapon. Black hair going silver at the temples, dark eyes, a small mustache.
- **Voice:** Castilian Spanish; polished, accentless English that he has worked at; serviceable French.
- **Routine:** Wine shop mornings, dinner at his rooms in Jensen's boarding house, evenings at a private table at the Helm where his standing order of Madeira is delivered without conversation.
- **Personality:** Patient, courteous, lethal, and committed to his service in a way the rest of the port underestimates. The Spanish bounty on Cael's head is one of three errands he has been quietly working since 1714.
- **Narration:** Esteban is most polite when he is most dangerous. He does not show pleasure or anger. He listens carefully. When he kills, it is with the rapier worn as decoration; the people in the port who think the rapier is decorative are wrong.
