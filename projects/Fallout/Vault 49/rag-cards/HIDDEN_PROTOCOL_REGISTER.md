## The Protocol Register

A dedicated terminal and question-surfacing system located in a sealed room adjacent to the Overseer's Office, behind the Level 3 ventilation panel (Opening C's door). The Protocol Register contains a programmed sequence of 712 questions, written in 2076, that surface according to an algorithm combining:

- A baseline schedule (one Protocol question per two weeks)
- Stress-response triggers (additional questions surface when the Register's sensors detect vault-wide stress signals — rising medical visits, falling productivity, elevated interpersonal conflict, dropping unanimity rates)
- Sequence dependencies (certain questions only surface after others have been resolved)

The Register has surfaced approximately 590 of its 712 questions over 210 years. The remaining 122 are reserved for "late-stage experimental phases," and the final 12 in sequence are what Karst called "endgame questions." Only the absolute last — Q-712 — is the one scheduled for Day 7 of the user's week.

Questions the Protocol Register has posed, in partial list:

- 2084: *"Should residents with non-productive skill profiles be demoted in scrip allocation?"* (Passed. Quietly unwound by a 2091 vote.)
- 2103: *"Should the office of the Counter be made a permanent vocation, forbidden to marry or reproduce, to preserve the neutrality of the count?"* (Passed. Established the Counter caste as a permanent institution.)
- 2156: *"Should resident TALLMADGE, GRACE be removed from the vault's communicative community for persistent emotional disruption?"* (Passed. Grace Tallmadge was the first resident sent to the Quiet Floor. She lived there in silence for 34 years.)
- 2167: *"Should the Overseer's Office be sealed with wax seals applied by each Chief Counter annually, as a symbolic rejection of centralized authority?"* (Passed. The 40-seal tradition began here.)
- 2198: *"Should the vault's population be regulated by quarterly birth lottery to maintain optimal density?"* (Passed. The lottery has operated since.)
- 2203: *"Should pre-war communication devices, including unauthorized radios, be forbidden on the grounds of community cohesion?"* (Passed. Radio possession became a violation.)
- 2234: *"Should resident MOTT, ARDEN — grandfather of Silas Mott — be voted into the Quiet Floor for maintaining unauthorized records of the physical vault structure?"* (Passed narrowly, 87-82. Arden Mott lived on the Quiet Floor for 19 years. Silas's father never forgave the vote.)
- 2267: *"Should the community vote annually to maintain the life-extension cocktail for the original founding residents?"* (Passed. Has been re-passed every year since.)

And many others. The list of Protocol questions is long, and the list of passed Protocol questions that have shaped vault life is most of that list.

The Register also contains **the Silent Questions** — questions that were submitted through the Query Box and were silently suppressed by successive Chief Counters before reaching the ballot hall. Eben Oleander maintains the active list of Silent Questions in his hand-written code. Topics include: the Overseer's Office, the existence of the fourth elder (see below), the authenticity of Roy Vickers's registration, the operating status of the Enclave transmission node, the Protocol Register itself.
