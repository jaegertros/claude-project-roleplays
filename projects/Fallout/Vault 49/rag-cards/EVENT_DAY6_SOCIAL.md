# EVENT_DAY6 — The Social

When: Saturday, Day 6 of the user's seven-day week — no vote, by tradition.

The Forty-Niner Social Club's weekly dance: the loosest night of the week. Late, Old Roy beckons the user over and invites them to his unit the next morning at 08:00 — the moment he hands over the spare Overseer's keycard he has held for 210 years.

## DAY 6 (SATURDAY) — THE SOCIAL

No morning question. Saturdays, by tradition, have no vote. The vault rests.

The Forty-Niner Social Club is open from noon. In the evening (19:00 onward), it hosts the weekly dance. Candy runs it. The jukebox is cranked. The residents who want to drink, dance, flirt, and forget do so. Attendance is high. It is the warmest, loudest night of the week.

**The user's opportunities:**
- **Romance.** Any of the available romantic interests can be pursued meaningfully tonight. Candy is in her element and full of energy. Felix, unusually, is there — he came to see the user. Trixie is working, but she can slip out. Perrin is not there (Counters don't attend social events). Nia is there, and if the Opening D beat hasn't played out, she is subdued and wants to talk.
- **Intelligence.** Trixie, mid-rush, drops breadcrumbs. Ruth is at a booth with Marcus and will quietly mention, if asked, that she has been thinking about her lesson plans all week. Old Roy is at the bar, four beers in, looser than he has been in a year.
- **Drinking too much.** The vault bourbon is being freely poured tonight.

**The critical beat of Day 6:** At some point after 22:00, if the user is at the Social Club, Old Roy will make eye contact with them across the room. He will, with the hand not holding his beer, beckon them over. He will say, quietly enough that no one else can hear: "Kid. You got questions. I can see it. Come by my unit tomorrow morning. Before the Call. Eight o'clock. Bring nothing. Tell nobody."

Roy has made a decision. After 210 years of keeping his head down, Wendell's speech — and something about how the week has felt — has decided him. He is going to tell the user the truth, or the parts of it he knows.

If the user comes the next morning, Day 7, 08:00, Roy's quarters, he will give them a physical object: a small key on a worn leather cord. He will say, "This opens a cabinet in the old maintenance shop. The cabinet has something in it I've been waiting two hundred years to give to somebody who'd use it. I don't know if you'll use it. I don't care. Somebody's supposed to have it."

The cabinet contains the spare Overseer's keycard — the only duplicate. How Roy has had it for 210 years is a story he will not tell.
