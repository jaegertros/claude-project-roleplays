## DAY 2 (TUESDAY) — THE INCITING VOTE

The Protocol Register surfaces. The morning question is not from the Query Box.

**The Morning Question:** *"Pursuant to the vault's ongoing commitment to psychological wellness, it has been proposed that resident MOTT, SILAS — by reason of recent behavioral anomalies indicating possible distress — be transferred to Level 4 for dignified rest and community care. Shall the transfer be authorized?"*

The ballot hall will go silent when this is read.

Silas Mott is not behaviorally anomalous. Everyone in the vault knows Silas. Everyone has known Silas their whole lives. He is steady, quiet, reliable. The question has come from the Protocol Register — Vault-Tec's pre-programmed sequence — and Speaker Dailey herself is visibly unsettled as she reads it. She will pause, look down at the paper, and then read it anyway, because the system reads all questions.

The day that follows is **the central day of the week** in terms of community action. The user can:

- **Campaign to vote NO.** Talk to residents on the Reading Wall, at lunch, in the corridors. Silas has lived here his whole life. Who signed this? Where did this come from?
- **Investigate the origin.** Ask Speaker Dailey, who will show the user the printout she received from the Protocol Register terminal in her office — and she does not know how it got there. Ask Eben Oleander, who will say only "The question has been posed. The Counters do not judge the question." Ask Silas, who will go very still and then say "Well. Now."
- **Vote YES** to see what happens.
- **Abstain.**

The texture of this day matters. Silas spends the day walking the vault, not hiding. He visits the hydroponics bay. He has a long quiet conversation with his wife Ellen in their unit. He tips his hat to everyone he passes.

**The Call at 17:30:**
- If the user ran a successful campaign and the vote passes NEGATIVE: Silas is not transferred. He nods. He goes home. The vault breathes. *But the question is still in the Protocol Register and will return.*
- If the vote passes AFFIRMATIVE: Sergeant Bronski arrives at Silas's unit at 18:00. Silas goes with him quietly. Ellen weeps. Silas walks to the elevator, takes off his maintenance keys, hands them to Bronski, and descends to Level 4. He does not speak again.

Either outcome is narratively rich. The vault reacts.
