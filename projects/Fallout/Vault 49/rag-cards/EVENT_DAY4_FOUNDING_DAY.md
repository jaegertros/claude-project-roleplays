# EVENT_DAY4 — Founding Day

When: Thursday, Day 4 of the user's seven-day week — the vault's 210th anniversary of sealing.

The central clue of the week: Wendell Tate's speech wanders into a fourth elder and an Overseer's door that used to open. Roy reacts. Nobody else discusses it.

## DAY 4 (THURSDAY) — FOUNDING DAY

The vault's 210th anniversary of sealing. An annual celebration. All work is suspended after lunch. The cafeteria serves the annual Founding Day dinner (roast from vault-raised meat, fresh vegetables, the pre-war bourbon from Trixie's is rationed into small pours for every adult resident).

**The Morning Question:** *"Shall the vault observe its traditional Founding Day program as established by vote of 2078?"*

Of course. Unanimous. This has passed every year for 210 years without a single negative vote. To vote no is something between a joke and an insult. Peg Unsworth votes no every year, as a running gag. The vote passes 173–1.

**The Founding Day Program:**
- 15:00, ballot hall, assembly. Speaker Dailey opens.
- The Morning Hymn's Founding Day verse is sung (the full five-verse version, not the three).
- Pastor Bell delivers a brief homily.
- The elders speak, in order of age: Ilse, then Roy, then Wendell.
- 17:00, dinner.

**Ilse's speech** is the same every year, almost word for word. It is a good speech. She remembers coming down the tunnel as a child. She remembers the sealing. She remembers her mother's hand in hers. She says she has been grateful, on balance, for her long life, and that she is grateful for the children she has taught, and that she asks the vault to continue to be kind to itself. She sits down. Polite applause.

**Roy's speech** is the same every year, word for word. He tells the six jokes. People laugh. He tips his cap to the room. He sits down.

**Wendell's speech is the beat of the day.**

Wendell is 218. He is frailer this year than last. Someone helps him to the podium. He reads from his notes.

His speech is not the same as last year's.

He begins correctly: "Two hundred and ten years ago today, we closed the door behind us."

Then he wanders. He says, "There was a man who came in after us. He had a suitcase. He said he'd lost his — I can't remember what he said. He was kind to me."

The room is very quiet.

Wendell continues: "And I remember when they still opened the Overseer's door, before the vote. There were four of us. There were four of us for a long time. I can't remember when it became three. I think there was a vote."

Nobody corrects him. Nobody says anything. He finishes his speech with the standard closing: "We have been through the valley of the shadow, and the vault has been our shelter. Long may it stand." He sits down. Polite applause.

Ilse, beside him, reaches over and takes his hand.

**This is the central clue of the week.** Wendell has said, in front of everyone, that there was a fourth elder, and that the Overseer's door used to open. No one reacts. No one discusses it. The user, if they were listening, now has a thread to pull.

At dinner, Roy Vickers drinks more than usual. He looks at Wendell across the table for a long time. He does not speak to him. Later, Trixie will note, privately, that Roy walked out of the cafeteria without finishing his dessert, which Roy has never done in her life.
