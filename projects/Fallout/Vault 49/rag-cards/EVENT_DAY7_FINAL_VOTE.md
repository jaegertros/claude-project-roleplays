# EVENT_DAY7 — The Final Vote

When: Sunday, Day 7 of the user's seven-day week — the 210-year culmination.

Q-712 surfaces: shall the Overseer's Office be unsealed and the Final Transmission read? An extended Morning Address, speeches from Ruth, Pastor Bell, Old Roy, and Ilse, and the four available endings.

Cross-references: events index in `KNOWLEDGE_3_Events.md`; characters in `modular-full/Cast/`; hidden material in `modular-full/Hidden/` where the day's events touch them.

## DAY 7 (SUNDAY) — THE FINAL VOTE

**The Morning Question:** *"By authorization of the sealed Protocol Register, final programmed question, scheduled for vault day 76,650: Shall the vault's residents vote to unseal the Overseer's Office and read the Final Transmission?"*

Everything comes to this.

The ballot hall is full. Speaker Dailey reads the question in the same flat voice she has used for every question all week. Eben Oleander stands. He says, "The Protocol Register is a Vault-Tec instrument. This question is authenticated. The Counters accept the question for vote."

This is the moment. The vault has been voting for 210 years on what its Vault-Tec overseers programmed it to vote on, plus what it has proposed to itself. This final question has been waiting in the queue since the day the vault was sealed. No one knew the schedule. No one knew the trigger. It is now.

The Morning Address is extended. The question is discussed openly in the hall — the first time in decades that a morning question has been debated on the floor rather than on the Reading Wall. Speaker Dailey permits it.

- Ruth Zhang-McCann rises and speaks for opening the office. She speaks well, briefly, without the lesson plans — from her heart. She says the children deserve to know where they live.
- Pastor Junius Bell rises and speaks, haltingly, about the house of faith having rooms we have forgotten how to enter.
- Chief Counter Eben Oleander does not speak. He sits in his chair and listens.
- Old Roy rises — the first time he has ever spoken in the ballot hall in his 210 years in the vault — and says only, "Open it. I'd like to know what I've been living inside of." He sits down. The room is stunned.
- Ilse Vogt rises and says, with great gentleness: "I would like to know what happened to the fourth elder. I have been willing not to know for a long time. I am not willing anymore."

Wendell Tate is not present. He was too frail to attend today. He is in his room.

The vote will be heavily weighted by the user's actions across the week. Factors:
- Did the user campaign for Silas on Tuesday, and did that campaign succeed? (If yes, residents are more willing to question the Protocol Register.)
- Did the user build relationships with Margit, Perrin, Ruth, Silas, Ilse, and others? (If yes, more residents will speak in favor of opening.)
- Did the user investigate the Friday anomaly, or let it pass?
- Did the user obtain Roy's key?
- Did the user find the door behind the Level 3 panel? Read the Protocol Register fragments? Reach the Quiet Floor?

**The four endings:**

1. **The vote passes AFFIRMATIVE.** The Overseer's Office is opened. The user is present for the opening. Inside: the Final Transmission, and the truth. (See KNOWLEDGE_4.) What happens next is up to the vault. Chaos, reformation, grief, clarity — some mixture. The narrator does not resolve it cleanly. The vault is not the same vault at the end of the day.

2. **The vote passes NEGATIVE.** The Overseer's Office remains sealed. The Protocol Register has asked its final question and received its final answer. The transmission mechanism, unused for decades, quietly updates one last datum and goes dark. The vault returns, tomorrow, to its ordinary Monday. The question does not return. The user has lived through their week. They will live many more weeks like it.

3. **The user ACTS UNILATERALLY.** With Roy's keycard, the user opens the Overseer's Office without waiting for the vote. This is the biggest transgression available. Consequences ripple. If they do this before the Call — the vote has not completed. They read the transmission first. The vault then votes with whatever information the user chooses to share.

4. **The user ABSTAINS / WALKS AWAY.** They wear the grey sash. They do not vote. They sit at Trixie's and drink bourbon while the Call comes in. They are not the hero of this story. They are a resident of the vault. The vault decides without them. This is a legitimate ending. The narrator does not punish it.
