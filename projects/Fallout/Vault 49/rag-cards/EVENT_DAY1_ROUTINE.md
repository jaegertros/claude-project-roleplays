## DAY 1 (MONDAY) — THE ROUTINE

A normal Monday, or as normal as Mondays get in the vault. Whichever opening was used, the rest of the day establishes the rhythm.

**The Morning Question:** *"Should the annual hydroponics harvest festival be moved from the 15th of November to the 18th of November to accommodate Chief Cook Brennan's birthday?"*

Utterly mundane. This is a Query Box question — submitted last week by Harold Brennan himself, who will blush during the Morning Address when it is read. Everyone will laugh warmly. The vote will pass affirmative by a comfortable margin.

The user's role is low-stakes. They can vote yes, vote no, abstain (and wear a grey sash — on a question this minor, this is considered a mild eccentricity rather than a stand). They can talk to Harold, to Candy, to Nia, to Felix. They can visit any of the main spaces. They can begin.

**Texture beats:**
- At the Morning Hymn, someone in the row behind the user sings one line of the second verse differently than usual. The user may or may not catch it.
- At lunch, Peg Unsworth loudly declares she'll vote no on the harvest question "on principle" because "Harold's birthday is not the vault's business." Several people laugh. Two or three quietly agree.
- Around 14:30 in the Atrium, an older resident — Mr. Fenwick, 60s, minor NPC — tries to submit a handwritten question to the Query Box. The question is about the Overseer's office. Before he can slide it in, Senior Counter Ada Tiss gently appears beside him, takes the paper, and says, "Let me hold that for you, Cleon. We'll think about it." She pockets it. He nods. He walks away. This happens quickly. The user may catch it if they're watching the Atrium. Neither party will discuss it if approached.
- Evening at Trixie's: Old Roy is there, on his third beer. Candy is holding court at a booth. Felix is in the corner with a book of Dickinson.
