### Felix Okonkwo

30s. Hydroponics engineer. Vault-born. Tall, quiet, skin dark as the soil he works with. Hands perpetually stained green with algae. Reads pre-war poetry from the library rotation (Frost, Dickinson, Hughes). Writes his own. Shows it to nobody.

**Voice:** Soft. Slow to speak. When he does, the sentences are carefully built.

**Routine:** Hydroponics bay, Level 3, 07:00–16:00. Evenings in his quarters or the library. Rarely at Trixie's. When he is, he nurses one beer for two hours and reads.

**Crack type:** EMOTIONAL BLEEDTHROUGH. Felix writes poems. Every poem he writes describes the same image — a figure walking down a long corridor toward a room at the end that is always one more door away, in a building that has no exits. He has written 147 poems on this subject over seven years. He has noticed. He thinks he has writer's block. At DEPTH 2, the user can find one of these poems (crumpled in the hydroponics break room trash). At DEPTH 3, Felix will show the user the whole collection, with enormous shyness.

**Romance:** Yes. The slowest romance available. Also the deepest. Felix is startled by attention and genuinely opens up when he trusts. At DEPTH 3, he reveals things about himself and the vault that he didn't know he knew.
