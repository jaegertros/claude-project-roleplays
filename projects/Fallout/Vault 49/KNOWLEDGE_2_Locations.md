# Vault 49 — Locations

Reference document for the physical layout of the vault. Most of this is safely readable — it's just the floor plan.

---

## The Vault at a Glance

Vault 49 is a four-level Vault-Tec installation sealed beneath the high desert of northern New Mexico, roughly forty miles northwest of what was Taos. The surface entrance is a reinforced concrete blast door at the end of a descending tunnel, sealed since October 23, 2077. Current population: 174 living, plus 12 residents on Level 4 (the Quiet Floor), plus 3 elders.

The vault was built to the standard Vault-Tec four-level template with significant custom modifications for its experimental parameters — specifically, the ballot hall, the Counters' chamber, and the unopenable Overseer's office.

---

## LEVEL 1 — CIVIC & ADMINISTRATIVE

The top level. Where the vault's public life happens.

**The Atrium.** The first space a resident sees each morning if they live on Levels 2 or 3 — the central hub. High ceiling (three stories, pre-war grandiose). A Vault-Tec mural dominates the far wall: Vault Boy, grinning, holding a ballot in one hand and a lightbulb in the other, over the slogan "DEMOCRACY IS YOUR DUTY!" in three-foot letters. Benches. A bulletin board. The Query Box is mounted on the south wall — a slot in a brushed-steel panel with a small sign: "Submit your question. The vault will consider it."

**The Ballot Hall.** The vault's largest interior space. Where every vote in 210 years has been cast. A long rectangular room with the Speaker's podium at the front, the Counters' table along the left wall, rows of wooden benches, and the tally board above the podium. On the back wall, the Reading Wall — the posted arguments for and against the day's question, written out on sheets of vault paper and pinned up between 14:00 and 17:00. Residents read, discuss, then cast their vote aloud at the roll table.

**The Speaker's Office.** Small. Functional. Desk, two chairs, a filing cabinet, a framed portrait of every Speaker going back to 2077 (most of them looking similarly tired). Currently occupied by Speaker Margit Dailey.

**The Counters' Chamber.** Brushed-steel door with a Vault-Tec sunburst etched at eye level. Beyond it: a vestibule with a single chair, then an inner door (locked), then the Counters' private space. The user is not admitted. Neither is anyone who is not a Counter. Dr. Holloway has been inside once, in 2276, for a medical emergency, and will not speak of what she saw.

**Security Office.** Lyle's domain. Small. A desk. A radio that only picks up vault-internal channels. An armory behind a locked door (four 10mm pistols, one combat shotgun, ammunition under lock).

**The Overseer's Office.** Sealed since Day 1. The door is a heavy steel vault door — a real one, bank-style — with a combination lock that no one in the vault has the combination for. The door has been sealed, in visible layers, with forty separate wax seals applied by forty separate Chief Counters over 210 years. Each wax seal bears the sunburst. The door is a physical taboo. No one touches it. Residents walk the long way around the corridor to avoid passing too close.

**The Chapel.** Pastor Junius Bell's small non-denominational chapel. Rows of pews, a simple pulpit, a stained-glass window depicting a wheat field beneath a blue sky that many residents have privately dreamed of and woken from confused. Junius preaches Sunday mornings at 10:00. Attendance varies.

**The Cafeteria.** Seats 80. Serves three meals. Cook is Harold Brennan. The coffee is better than it has any right to be. Conversations at breakfast are quiet; at lunch, energetic; at dinner, varied. Residents sit where they like.

## LEVEL 2 — RESIDENTIAL

Where most Forty-Niners live. A grid of small apartments along two main corridors that meet at a central common area with couches, a chess table, and the library alcove. The library rotates pre-war paperbacks out of the sealed archive on Level 3 — 200 books at a time, rotated quarterly by Mina Falk.

The user's apartment (in Openings A, B, and D) is here: Unit 2-17. Single bed, desk, closet, small washroom. Pip-Boy charging alcove. One window — not a real window, a backlit image of a mountain view. Most residents have a favorite window-image they've trained themselves to prefer.

**The Creche.** Where the vault's children (ages 0–5) are raised communally by day while parents work. Ilse Vogt reads here at 11:00. The room is bright, cheerful, full of blocks and books and Vault-Tec-approved toys. No one who visits the creche leaves unmoved by it.

**The School.** Grades 1–12, divided into three classrooms. Ruth Zhang-McCann teaches 4–8.

**The Infirmary.** Dr. Holloway's domain. Two exam rooms, a small surgical suite, a four-bed ward, a dispensary behind a locked door. The most sterile space in the vault.

**The Elders' Cottage.** Annexed in 2180 by vote, this is a small suite of rooms where Wendell Tate, Ilse Vogt, and Old Roy Vickers live. It has a private common room with three chairs arranged around a small electric fire, a shared kitchen, and three bedrooms. It smells faintly of antiseptic tea and old wool.

**The Speaker's Residence.** Margit and Paul Dailey live here. Slightly larger than standard units, because the Speaker receives late-night petitions.

**Pastor and Doctor's Residence.** Junius and Cass share a unit. Decorated warmly. Books everywhere.

## LEVEL 3 — WORK & REC

The level where things are done.

**Hydroponics Bay.** The largest single space on Level 3. Humid, green, lit by full-spectrum UV panels that have required replacement components Silas has been making by hand for the last forty years. Supervised by Paul Dailey. Worked by Felix Okonkwo and six others. Produces all the vault's fresh food.

**The Maintenance Shop.** Silas Mott's kingdom. Tools, parts, a workbench that has been in continuous use since 2077, a chalkboard with a running list of "things that need fixing" and a smaller chalkboard next to it with "things we have been told not to fix anymore." Both boards are long.

**The Power Plant.** Two fusion generators, one primary, one backup. Both functional. The backup has been running for the last eleven years because Silas couldn't get the authorization vote to switch back to primary. Neither Silas nor the Counters will say why.

**The Gym & Pool.** Candy Moreau's domain. The pool is small but real, heated, and the water is always clean. Candy runs fitness classes and teaches the children to swim.

**The Forty-Niner Social Club** (Trixie's). The vault's lounge. A small bar, twelve stools, four booths, a jukebox with 47 pre-war songs that the vault has voted to maintain the rotation of for 210 years. Trixie bartends. The drinks are variations on vault-distilled corn spirit, beer (yes, real beer — the vault brews its own), and one bottle of pre-war bourbon Trixie keeps behind the bar and pours from on special occasions (funerals, weddings, certain anniversaries).

**The Library (Archive).** Where Mina Falk maintains the full paperback collection, rotating 200 at a time into the Level 2 alcove. The rest — about 2,300 books — sits on floor-to-ceiling shelves. The archive is browsable by request. Some shelves are marked RESTRICTED ACCESS and require Counter approval. No one has applied for access in the current generation.

**The Workshop Commons.** Where residents pursue approved hobbies. Peg Unsworth's kiln is here. There are three sewing machines, a woodworking station, a small electronics bench. Everything that can be fixed, is.

## LEVEL 4 — THE QUIET FLOOR

Accessible only by the residential elevator, which requires Dr. Holloway's medical keycard or a Counter's override.

A small level — about a third the footprint of Levels 1–3. Twelve individual rooms, a common area with couches and a TV that plays pre-war cartoons on loop, a communal kitchen, a small library of pre-war paperbacks curated by Dr. Holloway personally.

The residents here do not speak. They walk, read, eat, play chess, sometimes cry. They are kind to one another. They are quiet.

The walls of the common area are covered in paintings they have made, which are permitted. The paintings are largely abstract. Many of them feature the same shape: a door, in the middle distance, slightly ajar.

## THE UNSCHEMATICS — Places That Aren't on the Map

The following spaces exist but do not appear on any vault blueprint. They can only be discovered at DEPTH 2+. See KNOWLEDGE_4 for what lies beyond.

- The door behind the Level 3 ventilation panel. Small. Keypad-locked. Above it: "PROTOCOL REGISTER — AUTHORIZED ACCESS ONLY."
- The corridor behind the maintenance shop that the blueprints claim ends after 20 feet but actually continues 60 feet.
- The sealed cabinet in the old maintenance shop with a keycard reader that takes a format the vault has never issued.
- The second door inside the Counters' Chamber.
- The Overseer's Office, still sealed. (See KNOWLEDGE_4 for what is inside.)
- The maintenance shaft behind the hydroponics bay that was welded shut in 2108 by vote. The weld is old. Tools that could cut it exist in Silas's shop.
- Most importantly: **the space beneath Level 4.** The blueprints end at Level 4. There is a service stair beyond the Quiet Floor's kitchen that the blueprints claim does not exist. It descends.

## The Vault Boundary

Vault 49 is bounded. The user cannot leave. The entrance tunnel and surface blast door are sealed; no resident in the current generation has seen them opened. Residents know, abstractly, that "outside" exists and is dangerous (the vault orientation film, shown once yearly on Founding Day, depicts "surface conditions" as "currently untenable"). No resident has left the vault and returned. No resident has left the vault.

The user will not leave the vault in this story. This is not that story. This is the story inside.
