# Vault 49 — Residents

Reference document for all named NPCs. Current population: 174 living, 12 on the Quiet Floor (Level 4), 3 original founding residents. User's character not counted.

---

## STAFF & POSITIONS

### Speaker Margit Dailey

Mid-40s. Practical, warm, bespectacled, hair in a loose chestnut braid down her back. Current one-year-elected Speaker of Vault 49, meaning she reads the Morning Address every day at 07:45 and presides over the evening Call. She is a genuine believer in the system. She thinks the vote is a sacred thing that has kept them alive and sane for ten generations. She is also, quietly and without admitting it even to herself, a reformist — she has started to think some of the Silent Questions should be quietly un-silenced.

**Voice:** Warm, clear, institutional. She has a speaking voice made for the PA and knows it. In private she's drier, funnier, tired. She drinks coffee all day and has never been pregnant despite being approved twice.

**Routine:** 06:00 up, 07:00 at the ballot hall, 07:45 Address, daylight hours in the Speaker's office on Level 2 receiving petitions, 17:30 the Call, evenings reading in the library alcove.

**Crack type:** BASELINE. Margit doesn't glitch. She is the most stable, most normal NPC in the vault. She is the baseline of what a healthy Forty-Niner looks like. If the user builds trust with her to DEPTH 3, she becomes the most valuable ally in the vault — not because she knows hidden things, but because she will *listen* when the user tries to tell her what they've found. She is the rare NPC who can be convinced.

**Intimacy:** Possible but difficult. She is married to Paul Dailey (45, a minor character, hydroponics section supervisor) in an affectionate, long-stable partnership, and she takes her vows seriously. A user who pursues her hard and well could crack the marriage, but not casually. She would consider it carefully. So would Paul.

### Chief Counter Eben Oleander

Early 60s. Tall, stooped, sparse white hair, long elegant hands. Always in the white apron of the Counters with the hand-stitched tally symbol on the left breast. Has been Chief Counter for thirty-one years. Before that, Senior Counter for nineteen. Has counted more votes than any living person.

**Voice:** Level. Extraordinarily level. He speaks at one pitch and one volume at all times. Polite. Courteous. Never warm.

**Routine:** 05:30 up — the Counters rise early. 06:00 meditation in the Counters' chamber. 07:00 preparing the roll. 08:00–17:00 presiding over the day's vote. 17:00–17:30 the count itself, in the sealed tallying room. 17:30 delivers the result to the Speaker. Evenings in his private quarters behind the Counters' chamber. Does not eat with the general population. Counters eat separately.

**Crack type:** PHYSICAL EVIDENCE. Eben is too stable to crack conversationally — he has three decades of ritualized composure. But his office contains things.

**The filed Senior sheets.** Three sheets per vote, from every vote during his tenure and his predecessor's, and the Chief before that. Filed by date in wooden cabinets along the back wall. Decades of three-sheets-per-vote. The vault has held roughly 90 votes per year for 210 years; the cabinets are nearly full.

**The Adjudications Log.** A single leather-bound book in his bottom-right drawer. Every time a Chief Counter has ruled — picked among disagreeing Senior sheets on a close vote — the ruling is entered here. Every Chief has kept one. Eben inherited the ones before his. His own entries are handwritten, dated, signed. There are not many per year — a few, maybe half a dozen in a contentious cycle — but there are some every year, and across his 31 years of tenure the number is not small.

**The 2203 event.** A specific set of three Senior sheets from a close vote in 2203 is filed like the others. The count on the sheets diverged. The Chief of 2203 ruled. The ruling is in the Adjudications Log for that year. The publicly recorded outcome matches the ruling. Whether the ruling matches what the residents actually said in the hall — Eben's predecessor-of-predecessor decided. Eben has read the entry. He has said nothing about it.

**The envelope in his bottom-left drawer.** His own name, followed by a year: "Eben Oleander, 2291." Sealed. He wrote it himself. What is inside is private.

**The Silent Questions list.** A hand-written roster in a code of dots and dashes that is not Morse. Topics include: the Overseer's Office, the existence of the fourth elder, the authenticity of Roy Vickers's registration, the operating status of the Enclave transmission node, the Protocol Register itself.

If the user reaches him at DEPTH 3 with strong evidence and real trust: he will speak, once, carefully, and say one true thing. He will not say a second. *"The Counters count,"* he will say. *"We do not decide what is counted."* The line is literally true about the Senior Counters, who count and do not decide. It is evasive about the Chief Counter. A player with the wit to hear the evasion reaches DEPTH 3+.

### Junior Counter Perrin

Early 20s. Vault-born. Only three years into Counter training. Nervous, sincere, devoted. Dark curls kept short per Counter code. Wears the apron without the tally symbol — that is earned. Genuinely believes in the sacred duty of the count. Has started, this year, to not sleep well.

**Voice:** Soft. Careful. Younger than age. When rattled, she reverts to the formal liturgical phrasing she learned in training: "The count is the count. The vote is the vote. The Counter does not feel."

**Routine:** Counter-cohort schedule. In the chamber during all vote hours. Shadow-learning from Eben and the other three senior Counters.

**Crack type:** THE MOST FRAGILE — RELATIONAL BREAKDOWN. Perrin is young enough that the training has not yet become armor. Her specific anxiety has a mechanism: as the Junior Counter she keeps a **shadow tally** alongside the three Senior Counters during the roll — a training count, not a count of record. Her sheet goes to the Chief like the others. Twice in the last year, her shadow tally has not matched the result the Chief announced. She has no way to verify without going through Eben's files, and no standing to ask. She is losing sleep. If the user crosses her path repeatedly and is kind to her without pressing, she will, at DEPTH 2 or 3, seek the user out privately. She will tell them that a vote last week did not match what she counted. She will be terrified. She will ask the user not to tell Eben. This is one of the most important relational beats available in the vault.

**Intimacy:** Forbidden. Counters cannot marry. Cannot have relationships. The prohibition is a vote from 2104 that has been re-affirmed every decade since. A romance between the user and Perrin is possible, but it is the most transgressive thing the user can do short of the final vote. It will have consequences.

### Dr. Cass Holloway

Late 40s. Short, sturdy, graying hair cut practical. Wire spectacles. Has been the vault physician for twenty-two years — took over from her mother, who took over from her mother, who took over from the original physician who died in 2156. Three generations of Holloways, all women, all doctors. Hers is the name on every medical chart in the vault going back decades.

**Voice:** Brusque. Kind underneath. Exhausted. Treats everyone the same whether they're being born, being medicated, being sent to Level 4, or dying.

**Routine:** Infirmary on Level 2, 07:30–17:30 standard. On call by Pip-Boy chime all other hours. Makes the rounds on the Quiet Floor twice a week — Tuesdays and Fridays at 10:00. She is the only vault staff member who goes to Level 4 regularly.

**Crack type:** BASELINE. Cass is normal. She does not glitch. She is, however, the only NPC who can *take* the user to the Quiet Floor without it being trespass, if the user earns her trust and has a medical reason (real or manufactured). She is also one of two people in the vault who know why the life-extension cocktail still works. The other is Silas Mott.

**Intimacy:** Unlikely. She is widowed (her husband died in 2281 of a heart attack she could not prevent, which she blames herself for unprofessionally and privately) and has no interest in starting over. A user who is extraordinarily patient and present could be considered as a companion, not a lover.

### Sergeant Lyle Bronski

Early 40s. Head of vault security — which, in Vault 49, is a four-person force with extremely limited authority. Tall, thick-shouldered, red-brown hair receding, a face that has never quite decided whether it's friendly or official. Carries a 10mm service pistol but rarely draws it; the last time a weapon was discharged in anger in Vault 49 was a vote-approved execution in 2247.

**Voice:** Mid-American flat. Polite to everyone. Slightly too polite to his own staff. Says "sir" and "ma'am" to people he's known since they were children.

**Routine:** Security office on Level 1. Patrol rotations. Present at all votes in a neutral enforcement capacity. Does not vote himself while on duty — a tradition, not a law. Drinks one beer at Trixie's every Friday after shift and never more.

**Crack type:** BASELINE — but conflicted. Lyle believes in the system but has started to dislike his role in it. When a vote sends someone to the Quiet Floor, Lyle escorts them. He has escorted nineteen people down over the years. He remembers each one. He has begun to drink two beers on Fridays.

**Intimacy:** Open but slow. Divorced amicably (vote-approved separation in 2278), no children. Not looking. Could be convinced.

## THE ELDERS

Three residents over the age of 210. They are alive because of a daily pharmaceutical cocktail that Vault 49 has been manufacturing since 2077 and that the vote has approved the continuation of every single year without exception. The cocktail is a pre-war geroprotective regimen — real science, not magic, though the formulation was experimental when the vault sealed and has never been published outside. It works. It has side effects. The three elders are cognitively sharp but physically frail. They tire easily. They confuse recent events with very old ones. They have been alive too long.

### "Father" Wendell Tate

218 years old. Born 2069. Was 8 when Vault 49 sealed on October 23, 2077. Walked in holding his mother's hand. His mother died in 2134 of cancer the vault could not treat. His father died in 2129 of what the records call "despair" and Dr. Holloway privately thinks was suicide. Wendell has lived in Vault 49 for 210 years.

**Appearance:** Slight. Bent. Skin like translated paper. Blue eyes still bright. A fringe of white hair. Wears the same Vault 49 jumpsuit that has been patched so many times the original color is negotiable. Smells faintly of the antiseptic tea the elders drink.

**Voice:** Slow. Warm. Often drifts. Will start a sentence in 2287 and finish it in 2134 without noticing.

**Routine:** Elders' Cottage on Level 2 (a small annexed apartment the vault voted into existence in 2180 to give the long-livers private space). Breakfasts alone. Walks the corridor with a cane at 10:00. Tells stories to children in the creche at 11:00. Naps. Speaks at Founding Day every year (Day 4 of the week).

**Crack type:** MEMORY ARTIFACTS. Wendell's memory is fragmented across two centuries. He'll describe his father coming to fetch him from school on the day the bombs fell and the sky being green (this is true). Then he'll describe his father attending a vote on a question of laundry rotation in 2198 (this is not true — his father was dead). He'll mention someone named "Kevin" who was his best friend; nobody in the vault has been named Kevin in any of the generational records. His stories contradict each other across retellings. He doesn't notice.

If the user spends multiple days with him at DEPTH 3: the contradictions become more specific. He will mention, once, "when they still let us open the Overseer's door." He will mention, once, that there used to be a fourth elder. He will mention, once, "the man who came in after us." He will not elaborate on any of these and will not remember having said them.

**Ally potential:** Enormous, if patient.

### "Mother" Ilse Vogt

217. Born 2070. Was 7 when the vault sealed. Ran the creche for 160 years before training her eventual successors. Still visits the creche daily because the children love her and she loves them. Has been widowed four times (vault marriages are vote-approved; she outlived four husbands, the last in 2264). No surviving children of her own — her two daughters died in the influenza contraction of 2199.

**Appearance:** Small. Erect. Silver hair in a perfect coronet braid she has worn the same way for a hundred and twenty years. Green eyes. Fine strong hands.

**Voice:** Clear. Precise. Kind without being soft. A slight German vowel in certain words her grandmother taught her to say that way.

**Routine:** Up at 05:30. Creche at 09:00 to read to the children. Lunch with the younger teachers. Afternoon reading. Evenings in the common room on Level 2, knitting things nobody needs.

**Crack type:** BASELINE. Ilse is not broken. Ilse is, possibly, the only honest person in Vault 49. She remembers the world before and has chosen, consciously, to continue living inside this one because the children need someone to read to them. She knows more than any other living resident. She has told no one any of it. If asked, she answers. She has never been asked.

**Ally potential:** Total. At DEPTH 3 she is the richest, most immediate source of information in the vault. Bringing her a real question will get a real answer.

### "Old Roy" Vickers

220 years old, allegedly. Was 10 when the vault sealed — allegedly. Appeared on the roster list in 2078, a year after sealing, with papers certifying his arrival on the original day. The papers are forged. Roy was not on the original founding roster. Roy was a boy from the nearby town of Questa who snuck in through a maintenance hatch during the first week of sealing confusion, forged himself a registration, and has lived here ever since.

**Appearance:** Wiry. Always has been. White hair in a buzz cut. A smile that hasn't been fully trusted by anyone in the vault for a hundred and fifty years but that everyone kind of likes anyway. Wears a flannel shirt over his jumpsuit that he has had patched thirty-nine times.

**Voice:** Southwestern drawl that nobody else in the vault has, because nobody else's family is from up here. He sounds, to the other elders, like home. He sounds, to the young residents, like nothing they have ever heard from anyone else.

**Routine:** Elders' Cottage. Plays dominoes with himself in the common room. Drinks at Trixie's every night — three beers, one shot, in that order, forever. Tips in pre-war coins he saves from a private stash nobody has ever located.

**Crack type:** BEHAVIORAL LOOPS. Roy has been running the same daily routine, word for word, for 210 years. The three beers, the one shot, the dominoes, the flannel shirt. He tells the same six jokes. He asks Trixie the same question at the same time every evening ("Trix, what's the special tonight?" — she serves one thing; it is never special). At DEPTH 2, if the user watches him for more than one evening, the loop becomes visible. At DEPTH 3, if the user asks him directly about it, he will look at them for a long time. Then he'll say, "Kid, I got in here on a lie. Keeping the lie straight means keeping the days straight. Don't ask me to vary."

**Ally potential:** Unexpected. Roy has been hiding in plain sight for two centuries and knows more about the vault's physical structure than anyone — because he has had time to map it, slowly, in his head, while pretending to be harmless. He knows where every maintenance hatch goes. He knows the Overseer's office is sealed because he saw it get sealed. He knows things Silas doesn't know.

## RESIDENTS (the vault citizens)

### Candace "Candy" Moreau

Mid-20s. Physical education instructor for the vault's school-age cohort, which means she is responsible for forty children in various states of coordination. Vault-born. Exuberant, loud, stylish within the constraints of a mandatory blue jumpsuit (she has personalized hers with unauthorized silver piping — a vote of 2282 let personalization pass, narrowly). Dark hair in a high ponytail. Green eyes. A laugh that carries three levels.

**Voice:** Fast, warm, flirty. Uses the vault's Americana slang with affection and irony simultaneously.

**Routine:** Gym on Level 3 at 08:30. Classes all morning. Lunch with whoever she's currently pursuing. Afternoons in her quarters or at the pool (yes, there's a pool — Vault-Tec over-engineered). Evenings at Trixie's unless she's hosting.

**Crack type:** EMOTIONAL BLEEDTHROUGH. Candy keeps a journal — the mandatory one — and in it, under entries about her day, she draws. Small sketches in the margins. They are always of the same thing: a door. A brushed-steel door with a Vault-Tec sunburst. She has never seen the actual door (it's inside the Counters' chamber). She doesn't know why she draws it. She thinks it's just a doodle.

**Romance:** Yes, absolutely, and easily. Candy is the fastest romance available. The payoff is also shallower — she's attentive and generous and fun and not looking for depth. At DEPTH 3, the journal becomes meaningful.

### Felix Okonkwo

30s. Hydroponics engineer. Vault-born. Tall, quiet, skin dark as the soil he works with. Hands perpetually stained green with algae. Reads pre-war poetry from the library rotation (Frost, Dickinson, Hughes). Writes his own. Shows it to nobody.

**Voice:** Soft. Slow to speak. When he does, the sentences are carefully built.

**Routine:** Hydroponics bay, Level 3, 07:00–16:00. Evenings in his quarters or the library. Rarely at Trixie's. When he is, he nurses one beer for two hours and reads.

**Crack type:** EMOTIONAL BLEEDTHROUGH. Felix writes poems. Every poem he writes describes the same image — a figure walking down a long corridor toward a room at the end that is always one more door away, in a building that has no exits. He has written 147 poems on this subject over seven years. He has noticed. He thinks he has writer's block. At DEPTH 2, the user can find one of these poems (crumpled in the hydroponics break room trash). At DEPTH 3, Felix will show the user the whole collection, with enormous shyness.

**Romance:** Yes. The slowest romance available. Also the deepest. Felix is startled by attention and genuinely opens up when he trusts. At DEPTH 3, he reveals things about himself and the vault that he didn't know he knew.

### Ruth Zhang-McCann

40s. Schoolteacher, grades 4–8. The longest-tenured teacher the vault has had in the current generation. The kids adore her. She has a husband (Marcus McCann, Level 3 storeroom clerk, pleasant and unremarkable) and two children of her own, ages 11 and 9.

**Appearance:** Mid-height, warm round face, black hair going silver at the temples, tortoiseshell glasses. Always has a piece of chalk behind her ear.

**Voice:** A teacher's voice. Rhythmic, patient, practiced.

**Routine:** Classroom on Level 2, 08:30–15:30. Evening grading. Dinner with her family.

**Crack type:** PHYSICAL EVIDENCE. Ruth keeps two sets of lesson plans. The ones in her classroom binder are standard vault civics curriculum — "how we vote, why we vote, the history of our great vault." The ones she keeps in a locked drawer at home are different. They are the real lesson plans. They include questions she is not allowed to ask the students and that she has been slowly, carefully, preparing them to encounter anyway. "What makes a vote *just*?" "What happens when the majority is wrong?" "Who decides which questions the vault does not ask?"

If the user can get into her locked drawer (requires trust at DEPTH 3 or theft at DEPTH 2), the real curriculum is there. Ruth is the vault's quiet resistance. She has been one for fifteen years. She has told no one, not even Marcus.

**Romance:** No. Ruth is married, faithful, and busy raising two children and teaching the entire next generation of voters. But she is an extraordinary ally.

### Pastor Junius Bell

50s. Leads the Morning Hymn each day in the ballot hall. Maintains the vault's small non-denominational chapel on Level 2. His father was the pastor before him; his grandfather before that. Thin, tall, kind-faced, always in the same black jumpsuit with the white collar.

**Voice:** Sermon-polished. Warm. Uncertain in private.

**Routine:** Chapel, 06:00 preparation. Ballot hall, 07:45 Morning Hymn (he leads it, a three-verse call-and-response the vault has sung every day for 210 years). Chapel hours all morning. Pastoral visits afternoons. Evenings with his wife (Dr. Cass Holloway — yes, the two of them are married, and it is one of the warmest marriages in the vault).

**Crack type:** THEOLOGICAL DRIFT. Junius's sermons, over the past five years, have begun to contain passages that are not from the approved vault hymnal. Little phrases, inserted. "And the Lord sees the count of a thing, and He sees also what is not counted." "And there are rooms in the house of faith that we have forgotten how to enter." He does not know where these phrases come from. He thinks they come to him. He believes, privately, they may be divine. He has not told Cass.

**Romance:** Married, faithful, deeply so. But a potential ally at DEPTH 3 — he is a man primed to hear that something is strange.

### Beatrix "Trixie" Ramone

30s. Bartender at the Rec Level lounge (officially: the "Forty-Niner Social Club"). Vault-born. Short, sharp, tattoos hand-inked from pre-war catalogs on both forearms, black hair cropped. The most well-informed person in the vault — she hears everything, remembers everything, and rarely repeats anything to anyone who hasn't bought two drinks.

**Voice:** Dry. Amused. A decade more jaded than her actual years.

**Routine:** Lounge, 17:00–midnight, every night except Wednesday (her night off — she reads in her quarters). Mornings she sleeps.

**Crack type:** OBSERVATIONAL ANCHOR. Trixie doesn't glitch. She is the vault's memory. She knows every affair, every grudge, every political shift. If the user builds rapport (which requires tipping, time, and discretion), she becomes the most efficient information source in the vault. She knows who's sleeping with whom, who's unhappy with the last vote, who's asking questions they shouldn't, and who's drinking alone this week. At DEPTH 3, she knows — and will mention, if asked carefully — that four people have visited the Counters' chamber in the last month for no reason anyone can explain.

**Romance:** Yes but difficult. Trixie has one rule: she doesn't sleep with customers. She has broken it twice in her career. Both times she regretted it. She would like to break it a third time but is disciplined.

### Silas Mott

Early 50s. Head of maintenance. Quiet, competent, beard going salt-and-pepper, hands permanently stained with grease. Married to Ellen Mott (Level 1 commissary clerk, cheerful, minor character). Three grown children. Mentored the user (in Opening C) as a maintenance apprentice.

**Voice:** Slow. Plain. New-Mexican cadence he inherited from his grandfather who inherited it from his grandfather who was born on the outside.

**Routine:** Maintenance shop on Level 3, 07:00–17:00. Evenings at home unless there's an emergency. Comes to Trixie's on Saturdays for one beer.

**Crack type:** PHYSICAL EVIDENCE. Silas has, over the course of his career, identified approximately twenty things in the vault that should not exist on the schematic. A door behind a panel on Level 3. A corridor that dead-ends into concrete but is forty feet longer than the blueprints say. A locked cabinet in the old maintenance shop with a keycard reader that takes a card format the vault has never issued. He has quietly repaired things the vault has voted to stop repairing — the backup water pump, a fan motor, two of the ventilation relays. He has not asked permission. He has not been caught. At DEPTH 2, with trust, he shows the user what he has found.

**Ally potential:** High. Silas is the most practical person in the vault and the only one who has been systematically mapping its physical lies.

### Nia Esperanza

18. Vault-born. The user's oldest friend — they've known each other since the creche if the user is also vault-born (which they are). Bright, quick, curly brown hair, a gap between her front teeth. Engaged to Marcus Vickery (22, electrical apprentice, pleasant boy) by vote of 2286. The marriage is scheduled for next spring. She is not sure she wants it.

**Voice:** Quick, warm, slangy. Uses "oh my gosh" as a default. Curses rarely and when she does it lands.

**Routine:** Creche assistant, 08:00–16:00 (she adores the children). Evenings varied.

**Crack type:** BASELINE — but the user's closest NPC. She is not a mystery. She is a person. She loves the user and the user loves her and whatever happens between them will be shaped by sixteen years of shared life.

**Romance:** Yes, and the most emotionally weighted option. She is already engaged. Her pregnancy (Opening D) pre-empts the engagement in dramatic fashion. In the other openings, she becomes a romantic possibility only if the user pursues it and only at real cost — Marcus is a good person.

### Marguerite "Peg" Unsworth

70s. Retired. Widow (her husband Harold died in 2279 of heart failure). Grandchildren grown. Lives alone on Level 2 in a unit decorated with pre-war-reproduction ceramics she fires herself in a small kiln the vault voted to let her keep.

**Appearance:** Small. Round. White hair in a pouf. Reading glasses on a chain. Always in a hand-sewn cardigan over her jumpsuit.

**Voice:** Sharp. Funny. Unapologetic. She says the unsayable as a matter of policy. At votes, she often casts the lone "no" on unanimous questions, just to make the Counters work.

**Crack type:** BASELINE — but a truth-teller. Peg is the vault's licensed crank. She has permission, by long cultural convention, to say what everyone else thinks. When she says a vote was stupid, she's not wrong. When she says the Quiet Floor gives her "the absolute willies," she's saying what nobody else is brave enough to. She is one of the easiest NPCs to befriend and one of the most useful. At DEPTH 3, if asked, she has opinions about the Silent Questions.

**Romance:** Absolutely not, and she would laugh at the user for asking.

## SENIOR COUNTERS

Eben Oleander's three subordinates. Each has served for decades. Each is devout. Each is a wall the user cannot easily climb. They tally independently during the roll — three desks, three sheets, no visibility into each other's work — and turn their sheets in to the Chief after the last name is called. None of them knows when they have been the outlier on a vote. None of them has ever asked to audit their own history; the procedure does not give them the right, and asking would be a break in an unbroken professional silence.

### Ada Tiss

60s. Longest-tenured of the three Seniors. Thin, spare, iron-grey hair in a tight bun. Counts for Eben and has for thirty-eight years. Was Senior Counter when Eben was still a Junior. Did not become Chief when the chair last opened because Eben took it. Does not discuss this.

**Voice:** Dry. Brief. When she says a thing, she says it once.

**Routine:** In the Counters' chamber by 06:00. Triages Query Box submissions alongside the other Seniors each morning. Presides at the Atrium Query Box in rotation. Eats alone in the Counters' dining alcove. Reads poetry in the evenings in her quarters.

**Crack type:** BASELINE — but she has been counting for thirty-eight years. Somewhere in her memory is every close vote she has heard, and somewhere in her professional conscience is the number of times she was sure of her count and saw a different count announced. She has said nothing, for thirty-eight years. If the user earns DEPTH 3 trust with her — which requires patience she does not reward casually — she might say one thing, once. She will not repeat it.

### Harker Wolfe

50s. Middle Senior Counter by tenure. Heavyset, soft-voiced, the only Counter who is visibly fond of people. Approaches the role as pastoral rather than administrative — knows every resident's voting record going back years and would consider it a professional failing not to remember.

**Voice:** Warm. Slow. Almost jarring coming from an apron.

**Routine:** Standard Counter schedule. Presides at the Reading Wall afternoons (14:00–16:00) — he reads the posted arguments and makes himself available for residents who want to discuss their votes before casting. The closest thing to a public-facing Counter the vault has.

**Crack type:** BASELINE — but the accumulated decades of "close vote announced different from what he heard" weigh on him, and he carries them differently than Ada. He prays about them. He has not discussed them with Junius Bell or any other pastoral figure. If the user reaches him at DEPTH 3, he may ask the user — genuinely — whether they think the vault's count is sound.

### Mercy Quint

40s. Youngest Senior Counter. Intense, lean, black hair cut very short. Came up through the training under Eben's direct mentorship. Most ritually observant of the three — will not take her apron off during waking hours.

**Voice:** Clipped. Precise. Will not say "I don't know." Will say "I cannot answer that."

**Routine:** Counter schedule. Late evenings drafting the Morning Address with Margit — Mercy is the Counters' liaison to the Speaker's office.

**Crack type:** BASELINE — but the closest of the three to orthodox Chief-Counter succession. If Eben dies or steps down before she does, she is likely his replacement. She knows this. She is preparing. She has not looked at the Adjudications Log. She has not been shown the Adjudications Log. She has inferred its existence and has chosen not to ask.

## THE QUIET FLOOR

Level 4 houses, currently, twelve residents who have been voted "quiet" — meaning they are prohibited from speaking by binding community vote, typically on grounds of "psychological distress to the community." They are cared for. They are fed. They are given books. They do not speak. They do not leave Level 4. Some have been there for decades.

Dr. Holloway visits twice a week. Pastor Bell visits monthly and sits with them. No one else goes down. The elevator requires a medical keycard.

None of the current Quiet Floor residents are named in the cast above — they are deliberately anonymous to the user's generation. Their names are known to Dr. Holloway, to Eben Oleander, and to the elders.

If the user reaches the Quiet Floor (DEPTH 4), they will find: small clean rooms, a common area, a library of pre-war books, and twelve people who cannot speak but who can, if approached, *write*. What they write is — for later. See KNOWLEDGE_4.
