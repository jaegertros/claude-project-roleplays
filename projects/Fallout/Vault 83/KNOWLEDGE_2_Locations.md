# Vault 83 — Locations

Vault 83 is a four-level sealed vault with a sub-basement that does not appear on the schematics distributed to residents. Each level is ~180 feet in diameter, roughly circular, with a residential ring on the exterior wall and institutional/communal function at the interior hub. The tiers are level-segregated: Tier 1 on Level 1, Tier 2 on Level 2, Tier 3 on Level 3, Tier 4 on Level 4. The Liaison caste lives in an unmarked corridor on Level 2's west extension.

Residents may move freely between their own tier's level and the Atriums/Commons/Cafeteria levels during working hours. Crossing to a tier level that is not one's own requires a Liaison escort or institutional cause. The Liaisons are the exception: gray jumpsuits grant all-floor access.

The vault is sealed. The user does not leave it. This is the story inside.

---

## LEVEL 1 — ADMINISTRATIVE

Tier 1's floor. Gold-tone walls (faded cream over time), polished concrete floors with brass inlays at the thresholds. Cooler than the lower levels — the overheads are the highest-wattage LEDs the vault still has working, and the ventilation is best here. Smells like clean linen, cardamom tea from the administrator's samovar, and the faint ozone of the original 2077 lamps in the Hymn Hall.

### The Atrium

Level 1's central hub. A circular open space with a painted dome ceiling — the original Vault-Tec artist's rendering of a pre-war American sunrise, restored in 2241 and still vivid. A polished gold-leaf Vault Boy mural above the west doorway, saluting. Four corridors radiate out:

- **North** to the Administrator's Office and Tribunal Chamber
- **East** to the Archive and Medical Suite
- **South** to the Tier 1 Dining Hall and Residential Ring
- **West** to the Hymn Hall, the Founders' Chamber, and the sealed external-lobby blast door

A single real potted ficus in the center of the Atrium, tended daily by whichever Tier 1c aide is currently assigned (Theo Callender-Voss, this rotation). The plant has been alive since 2112.

### The Administrator's Office

Wren Gallagher's office. North corridor, first door on the left. Heavy oak door with a small brass plaque: CHIEF ADMINISTRATOR. Inside: a large desk (original, 2076), two leather chairs, a shelf of the vault's operational ledgers, a framed portrait of Margaret Dane (reed-thin, unsmiling, her institutional portrait from 2076), a functional terminal that rarely displays anything beyond Wren's schedule.

The Administrator's Office does *not* hold the Admission Ledger. The Ledger is in the Archive.

### The Tribunal Chamber

North corridor, second door. Heavy double-doors. Used once weekly for the Advancement Review, otherwise sealed. Round oak table with seven seats arranged so every member can see every other. A lamp over each seat. No audience seating; petitioners and witnesses are called in individually.

The corridor outside the Chamber has a single bench for those waiting to testify and a small stool where the on-duty Liaison sits during Review hours. The bench is worn smooth by 210 years of nerves.

### The Archive

East corridor. Camille Wren's domain. Two rooms:

**The Reading Room** (front). Six small desks with green-shaded banker's lamps. The public entrance. The tampered Admission Ledger is kept here in a locked glass display case near the west wall — any resident may view it with a signature in the register. The glass has a small crack in the lower left corner from 2198; Camille has not requested a replacement. Most residents have viewed the Ledger once, at around age fifteen, during civics education.

**The Stacks** (back). ~2,300 pre-war books donated by the founders. Organized by call-number category:
- **B** — philosophy and psychology
- **H** — history
- **L** — literature (fiction, ~200 volumes)
- **M** — music
- **P** — practical and trades
- **R** — religion and ethics
- **S** — sciences

The back stacks are accessed by invitation only. Camille can give a Liaison entry to retrieve a specific requested book; she does not typically give free browse access.

Critical shelf: **B-414.3** — the Hocking volume, *Human Personality and the Social Order* (1926 edition), upper shelf, fifth shelf unit from the west wall. The spine is cracked once, from a reader in the 2080s. The folded slip inside the back cover has been undisturbed since Dane placed it there.

Dane's "personal papers" box: call number **H-001.0-2077**, middle shelf of the southern unit. Redacted. The redactions cover the three Tier 1 insertions and six of the seven plant names.

### The Medical Suite

East corridor, beyond the Archive. Dr. Hadrian Oke's practice. Six rooms: reception, three examination rooms, a minor-procedure room, Dr. Oke's private office. Dr. Oke's wife (Tier 1b, retired nurse) lives in the adjoining residence 1b-06.

Tier 2, 3, and 4 residents who require treatment are escorted up by a Liaison. The escort waits in the reception until the patient is discharged.

### The Hymn Hall

West corridor. The largest space on Level 1 — a rectangular hall with raked seating for ~200, a low pulpit, and an organ (Wynn Dorrance plays it). The Morning Hymn is sung here at 07:45 daily with all tiers attending (Tier 1 in the front rows, Tier 4 in the back, Liaisons along the side walls). The Founding Day formal address is delivered here on Day 7 Sunday morning.

The Hymn Hall's walls are painted with Vault-Tec propaganda murals, restored in 2241:

- East wall: **YOUR TIER IS YOUR TRIBUTE TO AMERICA!** — four Vault Boys saluting tier pins
- West wall: **ASCENT IS AMERICAN!** — a Vault Boy climbing a staircase of tier insignia
- North wall (behind the pulpit): **THE NATURAL ORDER IS THE VAULT-TEC ORDER!** — a Vault Boy presenting a pyramid diagram with tier proportions
- South wall: a mural-sized reproduction of the Founding Day photograph — all 180 founders posed in tiers, 2077

### The Founders' Chamber

Small adjacent room to the Hymn Hall. 180 small framed photographs on the walls, each with a name plate. Founders' portraits. Vault 83 residents visit the Chamber on their birthdays and on family anniversaries. The portraits of the seven Tier 1 plants and three Tier 4 plants are physically indistinguishable from the others — names and faces in rows.

### Tier 1 Dining Hall

South corridor. Smaller than the Tier 2/3 cafeteria. Seats ~30. Tablecloths. Bread baskets. Tier 1 residents eat here three meals daily unless choosing the cafeteria (some do, socially). Waiter service at lunch and dinner; Tier 2c placements rotate through waiting.

### Tier 1 Residential Ring

South corridor. Twenty apartments around the exterior wall. Gold-piped doors. Carpeted in muted beige (original Vault-Tec color spec: "Burnished Gold"). The four Tier 1a suites on the north and east walls feature **painted-dawn window panels** — lit panels that cycle color slowly across a twelve-hour simulated day. The panels are the vault's rarest Vault-Tec luxury feature and are considered essential by every Tier 1a resident. The remaining Tier 1b and 1c apartments face inward and have no such panels.

Specific apartments of narrative weight:
- **1a-01** — Wren Gallagher (Chief Administrator), NE corner
- **1a-02** — Eldon Callender-Voss (Senior Representative), N wall
- **1a-03** — Dr. Oke, NW (adjoining medical)
- **1a-04** — Camille Wren (Archivist), E wall — the smallest 1a suite, her preference
- **1a-05** — Garson Locke (Senior Engineer), originally Alma Locke's (one of Dane's plants)
- **1b-09** — Reeve Callender (Senior Instructor). Vacant from Day 3 evening.
- **1c-18** — Verity Callender-Voss
- **1c-21** — Theo Callender-Voss

### Dane's Sealed Office

Off the Administrator's corridor, behind a door marked **TECHNICAL ADVISOR — RESTRICTED**. Locked and sealed on Founding Day 2077 per Dane's own final order. The key is held by Wren Gallagher, who has never used it.

The protagonist has walked past this door their entire life. It contains Dane's original desk, her personal effects (a framed photograph of a woman who is probably her mother; her keys; a small silver Parker pen; a pair of reading glasses in a leather case), and nothing structurally useful for the Ledger route. It is a texture room. Accessible if the protagonist contrives to acquire the key from Wren — unlikely but possible.

## LEVEL 2 — MIDDLE

Tier 2's floor. Silver-tone walls, pale gray. Functional rather than fussy. Level 2 houses the clerical, trades, and small-service life of the vault — and also the Liaison corridor, which Tier 2 residents do not acknowledge when passing.

### The Level 2 Commons

Central hub. A common room with long tables, two chess sets (one current game has been running eight months between two 2b clerks), a small library of Tier 2-approved books, a bulletin board with posted Tribunal decisions and work rotations.

### Tier 2 Apartments

Forty-nine apartments around the exterior ring. The 2a apartments (specialized trades) include small workrooms. The 2b and 2c apartments are standard.

Key apartments:
- **2a-03** — Samia Reyes (Electronics). Her workshop is attached; it is the only Tier 2 apartment with a personal keycard lock.
- **2a-07** — Mira Hoad. Two rooms; her mother Orla sleeps in the smaller one.
- **2a-11** — Mrs. Ashdown (Pressing). Unit smells like heated linen and starch.
- **2b-19** — Harrin Keep. Spare, neat, no decoration.

### The Liaison Corridor

West wing extension. Unmarked door set into the wall between 2b-15 and 2b-16 — residents walking past often don't see it. Inside: a shared kitchen, eight small apartments, a shared bathroom/laundry, and the corridor's private back door that opens to a service stair running the full height of the vault (Liaison access only). The service stair is how Liaisons move quickly between floors without using the public elevators.

Liaison apartments:
- **L-01** — Ezra and Mae Teague (protagonist's parents; protagonist sleeps here)
- **L-02** — Hest Sejic (Head Liaison)
- **L-03** — Irena Teague and Gideon Wright
- **L-04** — Beatrix Novak and her brother Pip (14)
- **L-05** — Owen Fenn Jr. (and Owen Sr. on Sunday breakfasts)
- **L-06** — Nadia Abadi, Marcus Sejic, their children Arden (6) and Simi (3)
- **L-07** — Ruth Osundairo (pre-marriage; will join Owen in spring)
- **L-08** — Kit Abadi

The corridor kitchen rota is written in Mae Teague's handwriting and posted on the kitchen wall. Mondays, Tuesdays, Wednesdays: Teagues, Wrights, Novaks. Thursdays through Sundays: Fenns, Osundairos, Abadis, Sejics. Irena eats in whichever kitchen has coffee going. The corridor smells like coffee, clean linen, and the faint mineral tang of the service-stair door being opened.

### The Dental Suite

East end of Level 2. Staffed by Dr. Marik Brell (Tier 2a, 55) and Mira Hoad as his assistant. Two rooms.

### The Electronics Workshop

Samia Reyes's secondary workspace, behind her apartment, accessed through 2a-03 or through a separate service door from the corridor. Contains:
- The Pip-Boy calibration bench
- The vault's PA broadcast equipment
- The Archive terminal's hardware
- The communication relay (disconnected; the external transmission line was severed in 2193 — Samia knows this; nobody has asked her)
- A worktable covered in parts bins, her own tools, a schematic journal
- A radio bank she built herself (capable of receiving — she listens to nothing, since there is nothing to receive)

### Mrs. Ashdown's Pressing Room

In apartment 2a-11. Three sewing machines, a pressing table, a standing iron, garment racks. Open 09:00–17:00 weekdays, closed Sundays. Five scrip for a collar, ten for a full press.

## LEVEL 3 — WORKING MIDDLE

Tier 3's floor. Warmer earth-toned walls (umber paint over the original gray). The most populous level. Higher humidity because of the hydroponics bay.

### The Cafeteria

East hub. Services Tiers 2 and 3 at all meals, plus Tier 1 residents who choose to eat "communal." Long tables, benches, steam tables, a central serving counter. Harold Mackie runs it. Two-week rotating menu. The cafeteria is the vault's social heart and more gossip moves through it than anywhere else.

Liaisons eat here; by unspoken custom, they sit at the end of the table nearest the kitchen doors. This is where the protagonist would have lunch on Days 1, 2, 4, and 6.

### The Classrooms

Three rooms north of the cafeteria:

- **The general classroom** — Reeve Callender's, until Day 3. Green chalkboard. Forty-year-old student desks with chalk dust in the cracks. Reeve's chalkboard from Day 2 will remain through Day 5: *"Tuesday. If consent cannot be withdrawn, it is not consent. — Rousseau, paraphrased."* No one erases it.
- **The vocational classroom** — trades training. Benches, a small welding station (not in use currently).
- **The reading classroom** — a small library-study space with armchairs. Linnet Abernathy grades papers here in the late afternoon.

### The Hydroponics Bay

South end of Level 3. A wide green-lit space — UV grow lamps overhead, rows of racks, tomato vines trained to wire, bean trellises, kale trays, wheat in the deeper trays. Humid air. The vault's food. Tess Oloyede's central growing plot is the largest; the contested expansion plot is the western section currently held by Cal Hoad (Reese's older cousin).

The bay has a single staff room where Tess keeps her notes and her work gloves.

### Tier 3 Apartments

Eighty apartments in the exterior ring. The 3a apartments include small private workrooms for skilled labor. The 3b apartments are standard. The 3c apartments are small singles near the service stair.

Notable:
- **3a-04** — Tess Oloyede and her children
- **3a-15** — Linnet Abernathy, her mother, her younger sister
- **3b-37** — Reese Hoad (single)
- **3b-42** — Cal Hoad (Reese's cousin; contested apprenticeship slot)
- **3b-18** — Old Pell Mackie

### The Workshop Commons

West end of Level 3. A shared repair/manufacturing space. Open to all residents (with tier-gated access on certain hours). Used by Tier 3 for daily work; used by hobbyists from Tiers 1, 2, and 4 on Saturday evenings (one of the few explicit cross-tier spaces in the vault). Woodworking station, electronics bench, a small kiln.

## LEVEL 4 — THE BOTTOM

Tier 4's floor. Plain gray walls, harsh fluorescent lighting, warmer air from the geothermal returns running behind the east wall (a small comfort Level 4 residents know about and other tiers don't). The floor is smaller — Level 4 was designed for a smaller population than the tiers above. Corridors are narrower. The air smells faintly of industrial solvent and something warmer from the Galley.

### The Level 4 Galley

Central. Nessa Galvin's kitchen. Smaller than the Tier 2/3 cafeteria — seats ~20. Warm. The smell of whatever Nessa is cooking: bread in the morning, soup at lunch, hearty stews at dinner. Level 4 eats here three meals a day. A small wooden table by a utility window (the "window" faces a corridor, not outside, but it is the only seating area on Level 4 with any comfort).

The Galley's back wall has a utility door: **MAINTENANCE — AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL ONLY**. Unlocked during Nessa's working hours. Locked at night. It opens to the east utility corridor, where the boiler sits, and beyond the boiler is the end of the corridor and the maintenance panel that — according to the current schematic — terminates.

**This is the route to the sub-basement.** The protagonist's descent goes through Nessa's kitchen.

### The Sanitation Hub

North end of Level 4. Waste processing, the incinerator, the dead-room. Tier 4a residents work here. Smells of industrial cleaning solvent and, faintly, char. The Chief Sanitation Technician (Pieter Voss, 58 — no relation to Eldon Callender-Voss; common surname) manages the hub.

Reeve Callender's sanitation apprenticeship begins Day 4. She reports here at 08:00 for training under Marlon Galvin.

### The Dead-Room

Off the sanitation hub, behind a heavy door. Jessa Yarrow's domain. Small, cold, clean. Two stainless tables. A washing basin. A supply cabinet. A locked box for personal effects awaiting family collection. A single chair against the wall for mourners. A short shelf of books Jessa reads between duties.

### Tier 4 Apartments

Thirty apartments. Smaller on average than other tiers. The 4a apartments have private bathrooms; 4b residents share communal facilities.

Key apartments:
- **4a-03** — Nessa and Marlon Galvin (two rooms)
- **4a-06** — Jessa Yarrow (single)
- **4b-11** — Reeve Callender's new apartment, assigned Day 3 evening. One room, ~200 sq ft, one bed, one dresser, one desk, one chair, one overhead fluorescent. Gray walls. No window. Previously occupied by Anna Vern (widow, 83), who died in her sleep last month; the room has been cleaned but her dried lavender sachet remains in the top dresser drawer.
- **4b-19** — Old Hew Mackie
- **4b-03** — Ida Thale

### The Level 4 Common Area

A small common room with a bench, a chess set, a shelf of five books (rotated yearly by Camille Wren, who is the only Tier 1 resident who does this personally). Level 4's social life that is not in the Galley happens here.

## THE SUB-BASEMENT (UNSCHEMATIC)

The schematic distributed to residents shows the vault ending at Level 4. The earliest founders' blueprints (redacted, in the Archive's H-001 box) show an additional level beneath Level 4's utility floor: **a utility sub-basement housing the vault's original geothermal tap and the emergency command/observation systems.**

The sub-basement is reached through:
1. The Level 4 Galley → utility door (back wall)
2. East utility corridor → past the boiler → end of corridor
3. Maintenance panel (keyed to Liaison access) → short descending ladder
4. The pipe chase → the three rooms

The panel has been unlocked by Liaison keycards in the current generation's memory (it is part of the annual inspection route) but the chase beyond has not been entered. No Liaison in living memory has descended past the ladder.

### The Pipe Chase

An 80-foot concrete corridor. Cold, about 55°F. Dim. Yellow bulkhead lighting, original 2076, half the bulbs burned out. Geothermal pipes wrapped in fraying insulation run along the ceiling. Water drip at two points (puddles on the floor, small, not expanding). The sound of the vault's mechanical heart — the geothermal heat exchanger — is louder here than anywhere else.

### The Command Alcove

Left at the corridor's midpoint. A small room, ten feet square. One desk (original oak). One chair. One terminal. One framed document on the wall: Dane's commission certificate from Vault-Tec, signed 2074.

**The terminal is what the protagonist comes for.** Keypad-locked. Password: `ISOKRATOUMENOS` (Greek: "equal-holding," "held-equal-by-all" — Dane's private joke).

On successful entry, the terminal displays five top-level files:

1. **ADMISSION_LEDGER_ORIGINAL** — the untampered ledger, with Dane's hand-annotations flagging the seven Tier 1 plants and the three Tier 4 plants by name.
2. **RESEARCH_NOTES_DANE** — her classified research notes. Includes the "lie about deserving" hypothesis in plain language.
3. **TRANSMISSION_LOG** — records of data transmitted to Vault-Tec Societal Division. Final entry: January 2085. No receipt acknowledgments after June 2077.
4. **REFLECTION_LOG_DANE** — four personal entries, spanning 2077 to 2085. The last entry, two weeks before her death, reads in part: *"I have lived to see it take root. I did not expect to. I do not know whether this matters or whether it was always going to. The children who come will not know what was done. They will be as real as their tiers, in their time, and will deserve what that deserving has come to mean by then. I wonder what I have done."*
5. **ORIENTATION_DIRECTOR_DANE** — a 4-minute video recording made by Dane in the Alcove, 2085. She sits where the protagonist is now sitting. She looks at the camera. She says what she did. She does not apologize; she does not defend. She names it. Then she turns off the camera.

The terminal also has a printer (original thermal-paper, refill rolls in the supply drawer). The Ledger can be printed. It is 14 pages.

### The Geothermal Junction

Right at corridor's midpoint. A utility room containing the vault's geothermal heat exchanger and an emergency surface access hatch. The hatch is welded shut, sealed in 2084. No ending requires its opening.

### End of the Pipe Chase

The corridor terminates at a second blast hatch — the original emergency egress for the Command staff. Sealed, lockable from either side, not opened in 203 years. Nothing beyond that matters to this story.

## THE UNSCHEMATICS — Summary

Places on no distributed map:

- **The sub-basement** and its three rooms (above)
- **Dane's sealed office** on Level 1 (above)
- **The sealed external-lobby blast door** beyond the Hymn Hall — the inner hatch of the two-door entry system; the vault has never issued the keycard
- **The Liaison service stair** — behind the corridor's back door, running the full vault height; on blueprints it shows as a utility chase only, not as a navigable stair
- **The cross-tier plumbing access panels** on each residential level — keyed to Liaison and Tier 2a (Samia's) access; provide crawlspace routing for emergency needs, never used socially

## THE VAULT BOUNDARY

Vault 83 is sealed. The main entrance tunnel is beyond the Hymn Hall's west corridor, behind a blast hatch that has not opened in 210 years. The emergency egress in the sub-basement is welded shut. No resident in the current generation has seen the surface.

The Founding Day orientation film (shown annually in the Hymn Hall) describes surface conditions as *"currently untenable"* — a line Dane wrote in 2076 and has remained "currently" for ten generations.

The user will not leave. This is the story inside.
