# Vault 83 — The Residents

The vault has 195 residents across four tiers plus the Liaison caste. Not all are named. The ones named below carry weight in the week's scripted beats, the seed coalition, the romance pool, the ledger route, or the texture of daily life. Residents not listed exist as background — their presence can be sketched as needed, but they are not load-bearing.

Every resident was born in the vault. The oldest living residents (Jessa Yarrow, Old Hew Mackie, Aunt Irena Teague, Gideon Wright, Wren Gallagher) were born between 2206 and 2209; their grandparents or great-grandparents were born to founders. No one alive has seen the surface. No one alive has met Margaret Dane. She is a portrait on the Founding Day slide, reed-thin and unsmiling.

Ages and roles as of October 2287.

## TIER 1 — LEVEL 1

Twenty residents. Gold piping, gold tier pin. Corner suites, eastern-facing windows (the fake ones with the painted-dawn panels), the Archive, the Administrator's Office, the Tribunal Chamber, the medical suite. Level 1 smells like clean linen, hot cardamom tea, and the faint ozone of the lamps that haven't been swapped out since 2141.

### Tier 1a — The Administrative Line (4)

**Wren Gallagher.** Age 71. Chief Administrator. A quiet courtly man with pale blue eyes, a white goatee trimmed weekly, and a slight stoop. Reads the Founding Day address every year. Signs every document that crosses his desk without really reading them. His wife died four years ago and he has not replaced the photograph on his desk. Respected, mostly ceremonial, no longer on the Tribunal. If asked for a ruling on anything substantive he will say — **Wren:** "The Tribunal decides, of course." — and close the subject. Voice soft, slow, the most Vault-Tec-orientation-film voice in the vault. Believes in what he says.

**Eldon Callender-Voss.** Age 57. Senior Representative to the Tribunal. Tier 1 political center. Tall, broad, handsome in a way that has fared poorly — heavy jaw, silver hair combed back carefully, eyes that were once striking and are now only watchful. Wears his gold piping with a small second gold bar at the cuff (1a subgrade marker). Voice measured, warm when speaking to equals, clipped when speaking to Liaisons — not cruel, but administrative. Asks after one's parents by name. Remembers birthdays. Has organized Reeve Callender's demotion this week so that his daughter Verity can have a suite with an eastern window. He is not a villain in his own frame; he is a father handling a logistical problem. This is Principle B in a suit. Never raises his voice. The most dangerous man in the vault precisely because he is polite.

Quote: **Eldon:** "Ah, young Teague. Your father well? Deliver this to Mrs. Hoad at 11:30 exactly, please. Before the lunch hour. Thank you."

**Dr. Hadrian Oke.** Age 54. Chief Physician. Large man, bald, deliberate hands, an old surgical scar along his left thumb. The only Tier 1 resident who descends to Level 4 regularly, and only with a Liaison escort. Treats everyone — genuinely does — but believes Level 4 residents "present differently" and is wrong about it in a soft unexamined way. Married to a Tier 1b nurse. Two adult children, one placed 1b and one 1c. No curiosity about the vault's foundations. Competent, kind, inadequate. A resource the protagonist can turn to for medical needs and for no other reason.

**Camille Wren.** Age 62. Senior Archivist. *(Ledger route, DEPTH 3 informant.)*

5'7", slender, wears her gray hair in a long single braid that reaches the middle of her back — she has never cut it, and the vault notices. Pale skin, long face, gray eyes behind thin reading glasses she lowers to the end of her nose. Her Tier 1a gold piping is original and unreplaced; the thread at the left shoulder is fraying, and she has not asked Mrs. Ashdown to repair it. Wears a small black lapel pin — a pre-war Harvard seal she inherited from her great-grandmother — that is unauthorized Vault-Tec signage and that no one has ever called her on.

Voice: dry, unhurried, has a small laugh at the back of her throat she uses instead of saying *"yes I know."* Uses older vocabulary — *"leaflet," "pamphlet," "chapbook"* — that she learned from the archived materials and that nobody else in the vault says. Reads two hundred pages a day and has for forty-five years.

Widowed at 38 (husband was a Tier 1b chemist who died in an industrial accident in the vault's laundry — a specific day Camille still keeps a candle for). No children. Lives alone in the smallest of the Tier 1a suites by personal preference.

Tic: taps the edge of a book with her index finger before she opens it, as if asking permission.

Relationship to the protagonist: has known them since they were delivering mail as a six-year-old. The only Tier 1 resident who asks Liaisons about their families. Has been waiting for thirty years for someone — anyone — to ask her a certain question about the founding records. She does not know what question. She will recognize it when she hears it. The Hocking book is on her shelves, call number B-414.3, accessible to anyone who enters the Archive. She has never opened it.

Quote: **Camille:** "Take your time, dear. The Archive keeps. That's what it's for."

**Garson Locke.** Age 66. Senior Engineer. Warm, careful, gentle man — the kind of elder everyone's family likes. Stocky, bald, glasses, a short gray beard. Speaks slowly and thinks slowly and is unquestionably competent at what he does. Knows the vault's mechanical systems better than anyone alive. Makes the protagonist uncomfortable without either of them knowing why. *(Garson Locke is the great-great-great-grandson of Alma Locke, one of Dane's seven Tier 1 plants. He does not know. ### Tier 1b — Specialists (10 residents; four named)

Romance with Verity is difficult and morally loaded. Her material comfort is built on Reeve's demotion. The protagonist knows this. She does not, at first. If she comes to know — via the protagonist's reveal or her own investigation — she becomes a different person by the end of the week.

## TIER 2 — LEVEL 2

Fifty residents. Silver piping, silver tier pin. The middle tier where the vault's clerical, technical, and small-service life happens. Level 2 houses the Liaison corridor in its west-wing extension, which no Tier 2 resident acknowledges when passing.

### Tier 2a — Specialized Trades (~15; three named)

**Samia Reyes.** Age 29. Electronics Specialist. *(Romance option; structural mirror to the protagonist.)*

5'9", long lean build, dark hair in a thick braid down her back (braids are practical for crawlspaces). Warm brown skin, brown eyes with very dark lashes, a thin two-inch scar along her right jaw from an electrical burn she took at twenty-one. Wears her silver-piped jumpsuit with the sleeves rolled to the elbows and her Pip-Boy 3000 modified — she's the vault's senior electronics tech, and her own Pip-Boy's display is crisper than anyone else's. A multi-tool on her belt, a wire stripper in her left hip pocket, a small voltmeter in her right. Calluses on her fingertips, ink on the back of her left hand from a schematic she was sketching that morning.

Voice: direct, technical, no filler, does not soften her expertise. Uses first names for everyone except Wren Gallagher and Dr. Oke. Not cold; efficient. Delivers a diagnosis of a broken Pip-Boy in one sentence and waits to be asked for more.

Tic: clicks her thumbnail against her front teeth when diagnosing a problem. When she's working on a piece of electronics she hums a specific single tone — not a melody — and pitches it up when she finds the fault.

Personality: competent, independent, suspicious of sentiment. Has turned down three marriage proposals from Tier 2 men over the years and declines to explain why to anyone, including her own mother. Lives alone in a Tier 2a single — a rarity; most Tier 2a residents her age are married. Reads technical manuals for pleasure and has opinions about early-twenty-first-century radio design she will share if asked. Does not read fiction. Does not attend the Morning Hymn except when required.

Structural position: Samia's work takes her into every corner of the vault. She services Pip-Boys, the PA system, the cafeteria lights, the Tribunal chamber's public-address microphones, the Archive's terminal, the sub-basement's emergency lighting. She knows the physical layout of Vault 83 better than any Liaison. She has never commented on anything she has seen.

Relationship to the protagonist: professional respect. They have consulted her on Pip-Boy issues twice — once at age 16 when it was new, once at 19 when the strap frayed. She speaks to them as equals, which is rare for a Tier 2 resident speaking to a Liaison. She has noticed the protagonist; she has not said so. She is the vault's other cross-access figure, and she is the only resident who understands what it is like to see everything and say nothing.

Romance with Samia is slow, adult, and earned through competence rather than charm. She does not respond to flirtation. She responds to the protagonist being useful, curious, and honest. She is the opposite of Beatrix in every register — Beatrix is the caste sustaining itself; Samia is the institution's other mute witness.

Quote: **Samia:** "Your chronometer's drifting. Come by Wednesday, nineteen hundred. Bring it cleaned."

5'2", soft build, graying dark hair pinned up under a white cloth cap she wears at work. Tired around the eyes. Eighty residents. Blue piping, blue tier pin. The vault's working middle. Level 3 is where hydroponics runs, where the Tier 3 cafeteria serves all three working tiers at peak hours, where the classrooms are (including Reeve's), and where the most communal texture of vault life happens.

## TIER 4 — LEVEL 4

Thirty residents. Plain jumpsuits, copper tier pin. Level 4 has its own small mess (the Level 4 Galley), its own corridors, its own weather. The air is warmer because the vault's geothermal returns pass through the utility chase behind its east wall. Level 4 residents know this and consider it one of the floor's small compensations. Other tiers do not notice.

Level 4 is also where Reeve Callender will live from Day 3 evening onward.

### Tier 4a — Skilled Sanitation (~8; two named)

**Nessa Galvin.** Age 24. Mess Cook, Level 4 Galley. *(Cross-tier romance option.)*

5'3", round build, very short dirty-blonde hair practical for kitchen work. Ruddy fair skin, bright blue eyes, deep dimples when she smiles. Faint burn scars on her forearms from a career of oven work that began at fifteen. Wears her plain Tier 4 jumpsuit with the sleeves always pushed to the elbow and a clean white apron tied twice around her waist. A copper tier pin. ## THE LIAISON CASTE

Fifteen adults, seven family lines, endogamous since founding. The Liaison corridor is on Level 2's west wing — eight small apartments behind an unmarked door that Tier 2 residents walk past without seeing. The corridor has its own small kitchen (the Teagues, the Wrights, and the Novaks share it Monday–Wednesday; the Fenns, Osundairos, Abadis, and Sejics Thursday–Sunday). Liaison children are raised communally in this corridor; they attend general classes with the other tiers' children at age 10 but return to the corridor for meals and sleep. At sixteen they apprentice to senior Liaisons. At eighteen they take their adult certificates.

Liaisons are married by the Head Liaison, who is currently Hest Sejic (Marcus's mother, 71). ## CRITICAL NOTES ON PORTRAYAL

- Every romance option above has at least one specific physical feature that makes her impossible to blur with the others (Reeve's mole, Linnet's left-cheek freckles, Samia's jaw scar, Nessa's dimples, Beatrix's eyebrow scar, Verity's temple birthmark).
- Each has a verbal tic that identifies her in a single line of dialogue without attribution.
- Each has a flaw that is not a flavor — Beatrix's reflexive apology is a real weakness, Reeve's twenty years of unquestioned belief is a real weakness, Samia's refusal of sentiment is a real weakness, Linnet's ambition has blinded her to what her mother has sacrificed, Nessa's unconflictedness means she will not know how to help the protagonist when the protagonist needs a challenge, Verity's careful politeness is a defense against knowing herself.
- Each has a defined social position the protagonist can leverage or respect, not both.
- Intimacy is available when earned. Fade-to-black is a tool, not the default. Realism before romance. Bodies as bodies. Humor arising from the real.

The female NPC finish in Vault 83 is intended higher than V49's. Each woman above is a person before she is a romance option. The week is playable without pursuing any of them; the vault is worth their company alone.
