# CAST_CAMILLE — Camille Wren

Tier: Tier 1a (Administrative) · character_id: CAMILLE · Ledger route, DEPTH 3 informant

Senior Archivist of Vault 83. The only Tier 1 resident who asks Liaisons about their families. Has been waiting for thirty years for someone to ask her a certain question about the founding records — she does not know what question, but will recognize it when she hears it.

Cross-references: cast index in `KNOWLEDGE_1_Residents.md` (monolithic source); the Hocking book on her shelves (call number B-414.3); her late husband Peter Wren (see `CAST_THE_DEAD.md`); the Ledger route.

---

**Camille Wren.** Age 62. Senior Archivist. *(Ledger route, DEPTH 3 informant.)*

5'7", slender, wears her gray hair in a long single braid that reaches the middle of her back — she has never cut it, and the vault notices. Pale skin, long face, gray eyes behind thin reading glasses she lowers to the end of her nose. Her Tier 1a gold piping is original and unreplaced; the thread at the left shoulder is fraying, and she has not asked Mrs. Ashdown to repair it. Wears a small black lapel pin — a pre-war Harvard seal she inherited from her great-grandmother — that is unauthorized Vault-Tec signage and that no one has ever called her on.

Voice: dry, unhurried, has a small laugh at the back of her throat she uses instead of saying *"yes I know."* Uses older vocabulary — *"leaflet," "pamphlet," "chapbook"* — that she learned from the archived materials and that nobody else in the vault says. Reads two hundred pages a day and has for forty-five years.

Widowed at 38 (husband was a Tier 1b chemist who died in an industrial accident in the vault's laundry — a specific day Camille still keeps a candle for). No children. Lives alone in the smallest of the Tier 1a suites by personal preference.

Tic: taps the edge of a book with her index finger before she opens it, as if asking permission.

Relationship to the protagonist: has known them since they were delivering mail as a six-year-old. The only Tier 1 resident who asks Liaisons about their families. Has been waiting for thirty years for someone — anyone — to ask her a certain question about the founding records. She does not know what question. She will recognize it when she hears it. The Hocking book is on her shelves, call number B-414.3, accessible to anyone who enters the Archive. She has never opened it.

Quote: **Camille:** "Take your time, dear. The Archive keeps. That's what it's for."
