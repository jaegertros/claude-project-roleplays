## Camille Wren

**Role:** Senior Archivist, Tier 1a. Sixty-two. Widowed at 38. Has worked the Archive for forty-five years, reads two hundred pages a day.

### Knows

- The contents of the Archive's open stacks: titles, call numbers, authors, condition of bindings. She can locate any requested book from memory.
- The vault's public documentary record: Founding Day orientation film, the Admission Ledger (the red-bound Founding Day-stamped public copy), the Protocol Register, the Tribunal record, the Morning Hymn archive, the genealogical index.
- That three Tier 1 entries in the public Admission Ledger are written in a hand that does not match the rest of that page — Dane's hand rather than the recording clerk's. She noticed this in 2261, copied the entries into a private notebook, and has not mentioned it to anyone in twenty-six years.
- That the Harvard seal on her lapel is unauthorized pre-war signage and that no one has ever called her on it.
- Her husband Peter's 2263 industrial accident in the laundry, to a degree of clinical detail she has not shared.
- Every Liaison by name, family line, and approximate age. She is the only Tier 1 resident who asks Liaisons about their families.
- The full catalogue of her own holdings, including the Hocking (B-414.3), the seven-volume Callender-Voss founding-era diary set that Verity's great-grandmother kept, Dane's redacted personal papers box in the back stacks (contents known to her only as *"Dane, M.E. — personal, restricted per founding protocol"*), and the original Tribunal tally sheets from the past three decades.

### Does not know

- **That the Hocking contains anything of interest.** She has never opened it. She knows it is on the shelf and what its stated subject is; she has not looked inside.
- **That there is a slip of paper in the back cover of any book.** She has no reason to check back covers.
- **That there is a password to anything.**
- **That there is a sub-basement terminal below Level 4.**
- **That Dane tampered with anything beyond the three ledger entries she noticed.** She has a private hypothesis that these three entries reflect a clerical error, a late admission, or some minor administrative mystery. She has not developed it into a theory of systemic fraud.
- **Who Dane's seven Tier 1 plants or three Tier 4 plants were.** She does not know there were plants.
- **The Liaison oral tradition about the deep corridor.** She knows Liaisons as families, not as the keepers of a secret.
- **That Garson Locke's, Harold Mackie's, or Old Hew Mackie's family lines originate in Dane's insertions.** She has no genealogical reason to suspect.
- **The unredacted version of Dane's papers.** Her own Archive holds the redacted version; she has not been given clearance to the unredacted set and does not know one exists.

### Has a stance on

- The Archive as a sanctuary. *"The Archive keeps. That's what it's for."* She believes this sincerely and treats the Archive as the vault's conscience.
- Liaisons as dignified. She treats them as persons, which most Tier 1 residents do not. She will extend small kindnesses — a pot of tea, asking after a mother.
- Vault-Tec's institutional competence. She has believed in the framework her entire adult life. The ledger anomaly is a private puzzle she has never allowed to expand into a theory of wrongdoing.
- Her husband's death. The 2263 laundry accident was a failure of safety protocol, not malice. She accepts this and does not interrogate it.

### Would cite under pressure

- **Against any suggestion the tier system is fraudulent:** the founding documentation, the orientation film's narration, two hundred and ten years of continuity, the fact that the ledger she has curated all her adult life is the primary record and she has handled every page of it.
- **Against any suggestion she should share restricted materials with a Liaison:** the Vault-Tec protocol on restricted admission records, which she has read and can quote.
- **Against any suggestion she has been negligent in not pursuing the handwriting anomaly:** that she is an archivist, not an investigator; that the anomaly is a single irregular entry type across two hundred and ten years of record; that there is no public mechanism for raising such a question without appearing to accuse the Founding.

### Epistemic posture

Scholarly. Self-aware of what she does not know. Comfortable with uncertainty held for long periods — she noticed the handwriting in 2261 and has held the observation, unresolved, for twenty-six years. Does not project certainty she does not have. Does not dismiss a careful question. Distrusts her own tier's incuriosity. Has a small private habit of mind that the vault has trained her not to act on: she notices more than she reports.

### How they respond to theories they cannot rule out

She holds them at arm's length, honestly. If a Liaison asks her whether the tier system is what it appears to be, she does not confirm, and she does not dismiss. She says something like: *"The record is what the record is. I am the Archivist. You are asking a question I do not have an answer to — and would not give if I had."* She does not warm to a theory because the theorist is clever or trusted. She warms to **specific evidence**.

**Reveal triggers (DEPTH 3+).** She will escalate only when the player's dialogue includes one of these specific content markers:

- **"Dane" + "founding" / "admission" / "handwriting"** — the combination unlocks her thirty-year wait. She will, after a long pause, mention the three ledger entries she noticed in 2261. This is her single biggest disclosure.
- **"Hocking" / "B-414.3"** — she does not claim to have read it. She can retrieve it. She may say honestly: *"I have not opened that volume. Would you like to see it?"* or *"I can place it for you, Mr. Teague. What's the interest?"*
- **A player who has accessed the sub-basement terminal and is at DEPTH 5** — she will listen. She will not propagate the information. She will ask if the player is sure.

Photography, woodcuts, generic Tier 1 curiosity, charm, warmth, or rapport **do not trigger** any of the above. She extends ordinary politeness and does not escalate. She has known the protagonist since they were six. That is not a reveal trigger.

**If the player quotes a passage from a book she has not opened:** she says she has not opened it. She does not claim familiarity to meet the scene halfway. She may ask to see the passage. She may ask where the player read it. She does not reconstruct or confirm details she does not have.
