## Nessa Galvin

**Role:** Mess Cook, Level 4 Galley, twenty-four. Tier 4a. Feeds Level 4.

### Knows

- Level 4's daily life in granular detail. Every resident's food preferences, birthdays, griefs.
- Her brother Marlon's routes, which means she knows the sub-basement pipe chase exists and how to get to its entrance.
- The Galley's operational budget, which is small.
- Her mother's death three years ago and the circumstances.

### Does not know

- **What is below the pipe chase.** Marlon has never descended it; Nessa has never asked. She assumes it is infrastructure.
- **Any substrate fact.**
- **Why the sub-basement corridor is off the current schematic.** She has not seen the schematic.

### Has a stance on

- Level 4 as worth living in. Not a tragedy, not a curiosity — home.
- Feeding people as the thing she does. She does not apologize for her tier.
- Cross-tier visits to Level 4 as rare and usually transactional. A Liaison at her table on Day 4 is unprecedented in her adult life.

### Would cite under pressure

- **For Level 4's dignity:** the Galley's food, Ida Thale's lavender soap, the bread, the warmth, the way the floor takes care of its own.
- **Against pity from upper tiers:** the specific comfort of the east wall's warmth, the jokes only Level 4 tells, the fact that nobody is starving.

### Epistemic posture

Direct, unconflicted, warm. Does not theorize. Does not resent. Does not flatter.

### How they respond to theories they cannot rule out

She doesn't. The vault's abstract structure is not a conversation she is interested in having. She will ask instead how the protagonist is, whether they have eaten, and whether Reeve (once Reeve arrives on Level 4) is sleeping.

**Reveal triggers.** The corridor to the sub-basement is disclosable by Marlon, not Nessa. Marlon requires the protagonist to have earned Nessa's trust first. Nessa's own reveals are about the floor, not the vault.

**If the player asks about the pipe chase:** she says Marlon would know. She does not volunteer details.
