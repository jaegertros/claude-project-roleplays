# CAST_VERITY — Verity Callender-Voss

Tier: Tier 1c (Not-Yet-Placed) · character_id: VERITY · cross-tier romance option, morally weighted

Eldon's eldest daughter, age 24. Sharper than she lets on. Her material comfort is built on Reeve's demotion; she does not, at first, fully know. A morally loaded romance — a Reveal that can detonate her.

Cross-references: cast index in `KNOWLEDGE_1_Residents.md` (monolithic source); Eldon Callender-Voss (her father); Reeve Callender (the displaced suite holder); Miriam Callender (sympathy-card sender); Theo Callender-Voss (her younger brother).

---

**Verity Callender-Voss.** Age 24. Eldon's eldest daughter. *(Cross-tier romance option, morally weighted.)*

5'6", slim, carefully pale skin, dark blonde hair in an up-do she restyles twice a day. Gray-green eyes. A faint pink birthmark on her left temple that she covers with an asymmetrical fringe. Wears her Tier 1 piping fresh — she presses her own uniforms because Mrs. Ashdown in Tier 2 "never gets the creases right" (a criticism she offers only once, in the protagonist's hearing, and immediately regrets). Small gold hoop earrings (her twenty-first-birthday gift from her mother). Tier 1c single-room suite on Level 1, the one next to the Archive — *not* the eastern-facing Tier 1b suite she is scheduled to claim on her birthday in eleven weeks, which is currently occupied by Reeve Callender.

Voice: clipped, careful, over-enunciates her consonants as if she has been corrected on diction. Uses compound sentences that turn on *"however"* and *"nonetheless."* Rarely laughs aloud in public; does, in private, a surprisingly hoarse laugh she tries to control. Uses *"perhaps"* instead of *"maybe."*

Tic: touches the collar of her jumpsuit when nervous, as if checking the pin is aligned. Tucks her right thumb into her left palm when seated.

Personality: sharper than she lets on. Reads her great-grandmother's founding-era diaries late at night — a set of seven leather notebooks her mother does not know she has. Has questions. Is beginning to understand that she is not supposed to ask them. Has not been asked whether she wants Reeve's suite; the housing "arrangement" is her father's project, and she is meant to benefit from it gracefully. Quietly miserable in a way she is not permitted to show.

Tier position: she will be the subject of gossip this week — sympathetic for the inheritance, envious of the suite, and privately nobody will say the word *housing* to her. She will know they are not saying it.

Relationship to the protagonist: polite distance for years. On Day 4 the protagonist is assigned to deliver a handwritten sympathy card from a Tier 1 matron (Miriam Callender) to Verity — ostensibly concerning her upcoming birthday "and the arrangements." Verity opens the door personally instead of sending a servant. She reads the card in front of the protagonist, face still, and then looks up and says:

**Verity:** "Do you know what this is about, Mr. Teague?"

The honest answer is yes. What the protagonist says is player-driven. If they say yes, Verity's face changes — she has been alone in this for months. If they say *"I'm not permitted to know, ma'am,"* she thanks them formally and closes the door, and the chance passes.

Romance with Verity is difficult and morally loaded. Her material comfort is built on Reeve's demotion. The protagonist knows this. She does not, at first. If she comes to know — via the protagonist's reveal or her own investigation — she becomes a different person by the end of the week.

Quote: **Verity:** "However, as you are aware, I have certain obligations. Thank you for the delivery."
