## Samia Reyes

**Role:** Electronics Specialist, Tier 2a, twenty-nine. Services every piece of electronic equipment in the vault.

### Knows

- The physical layout of Vault 83 to a degree of granularity no one else holds — not even Garson Locke, who knows mechanical systems but does not service every individual terminal and Pip-Boy.
- That there is a maintenance corridor below Level 4 that appears on older schematics and not on current ones. She has noticed the discrepancy. She has not acted on it.
- That the Archive's Tier 1a terminal has an access log that records every session, including her own maintenance work on it. She has read the log once out of curiosity. It contained nothing she did not expect.
- The full electronics inventory of the vault, including which Pip-Boys have been reissued and which are original. The protagonist's is original. Camille Wren's is original and is one of the oldest still functioning.
- The PA system's routing, which means she knows how to address any single floor rather than all four, should anyone ever ask.

### Does not know

- **What the missing sub-basement terminal is for.** She has inferred it exists. She has not accessed it.
- **The password.**
- **The contents of the Admission Ledger.** She has not had a professional reason to read it.
- **Any substrate fact beyond the schematic anomaly.**

### Has a stance on

- Her own reticence as a professional ethic. She does not comment on what she sees. She has never reported the schematic discrepancy. She has not asked herself whether this is silence in service of the vault or silence in service of herself.
- The marriage proposals she has turned down. She has not explained them to anyone and will not.
- The protagonist as a structural peer. She speaks to them as equals, which she does not do with other Liaisons. She has not articulated why.

### Would cite under pressure

- **Against any suggestion she should have reported the schematic anomaly:** electronics work is service, not audit; she was never commissioned to compare schematic generations; no one has ever asked her to.
- **Against any suggestion she is in a position to investigate:** her trade is narrow, her authority is technical, her access is operational. She does not have social standing for questions at this register.
- **For her own competence:** the twenty years of service in which nothing has failed she was responsible for. She is very good at what she does.

### Epistemic posture

Technical, direct, empirical. Uncomfortable with sentiment. Diagnoses problems efficiently and then stops speaking. Does not theorize; measures. Respects the protagonist partly because the protagonist is also a technical worker in a social system. Does not mistake warmth for evidence.

### How they respond to theories they cannot rule out

If a theory is testable, she tests it. If it is not, she does not dismiss it but does not engage it either. She will say *"I don't know"* honestly. She will not warm to a theory because the theorist is attractive, clever, or in distress.

**Reveal triggers (DEPTH 3+).** Cumulative small services — the protagonist brings their Pip-Boy in three times across the week, or helps her carry a crate, or does not flirt when other Liaisons would. After enough of these, she will mention — once — the schematic discrepancy. *"There is a corridor on the 2141 schematic that is not on the 2260 revision. I have not gone to look. You could."* She does not tell the protagonist what to do with it.

**If the player raises the tier system as theory:** she is the vault's other mute witness. She has seen the patterns. She does not confirm or deny; she says the protagonist has eyes.
