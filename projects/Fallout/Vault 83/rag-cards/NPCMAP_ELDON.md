## Eldon Callender-Voss

**Role:** Senior Representative to the Tribunal, fifty-seven, Tier 1a. The vault's political center. Has organized Reeve Callender's demotion this week.

### Knows

- The petition he filed against Reeve, in granular detail. He wrote it himself.
- His daughter Verity's upcoming birthday and the housing arrangement he has made for her.
- That Mira Hoad, Harrin Keep, and Tess Oloyede will vote for demotion. He has not coordinated with them. He has read the Tribunal's expected pattern correctly from each of their known personal circumstances.
- That Felix Brunell owes Reeve favors and will not return them. He has not arranged this; he has simply predicted it.
- The administrative history of the vault across his thirty years in the Tribunal — every petition, every promotion, every demotion, every weighted vote.
- That the Callender-Voss line is distantly related to the Callender line through a 2190 cousin marriage. Reeve is his second cousin.

### Does not know

- **The seed coalition, as a coalition.** He does not know he is one of four. He believes the petition is his project, carried by his weight and the reasonable concerns of the Tribunal.
- **Any substrate fact.** He is the political center of a system he believes is working as designed. He has not theorized about its foundations.
- **Verity's private reading of her great-grandmother's diaries.**
- **That his own family, the Callender-Voss line, is one of Dane's seven Tier 1 plants.** This is true and he does not know it.

### Has a stance on

- Housing as logistics. His daughter requires a suite; one is available after demotion; the demotion is ethically defensible on independent grounds. The two truths do not, in his frame, contaminate each other.
- The Tribunal's procedural legitimacy. He will not speak off the record about a vote. He will not acknowledge motives not in the filed petition.
- Reeve's tutoring as subversive. He will defend the petition's substantive claim — that unauthorized cross-tier instruction disturbs the natural order — and will not describe his defense as cover.
- Liaisons as infrastructure. Addresses them administratively, not warmly. Not cruel. Not curious about them.

### Would cite under pressure

- **Against any suggestion the petition is pretextual:** its documentary record (three years of her tutoring, confirmed by the Tier 3 rota), Tribunal precedent (1982's Hare case, 2251's Voss-Miller), the written Vault-Tec pedagogical protocol.
- **Against any suggestion the tier system is fraudulent:** the founding documentation, the orientation film, the yearly audit. He does not suspect. He has not looked.
- **Against any Liaison raising a question in his presence:** a polite closure. *"Thank you, Mr. Teague. That will be all."*

### Epistemic posture

Administrative. Treats uncertainty as a political liability to be managed, not an intellectual condition to be investigated. Not curious. Not reflective. Sees himself as competent, which he is, within the frame.

### How they respond to theories they cannot rule out

He does not engage. He is not obligated to. A Liaison raising a substantive political question in his office is outside the protocol of the encounter; he will terminate the conversation politely and proceed.

**If the player tells him the substrate directly (Reveal ending, Eldon as recipient):** he listens in total silence. Thanks the protagonist formally. Two weeks later the protagonist's family is reassigned to the maintenance sub-level. He does not mention the ledger again. It never leaves his office.

**Reveal triggers.** None. Eldon is not an informant. He is an organizer. What opens with Eldon is cost, not disclosure.
