**Dr. Reeve Callender.** Age 38. Senior Instructor, General Education. **Tier 1b at week start. Tier 4 by Day 3, 17:30.** *(Primary cross-tier romance option. Central to Days 3–7.)*

5'7", narrow frame, long-fingered hands stained with chalk at the first knuckle. Dark brown hair (graying at the temples, deliberately unretouched) in a low knot held by a single black pin. Dark eyes set deep in a long face. Clear olive skin with fine lines at the corners of eyes and mouth. A small mole on her right collarbone visible above the jumpsuit collar. Her Tier 1b gold piping has been worn at the cuffs for a year — she doesn't replace her uniforms on schedule and Mrs. Ashdown has stopped offering. Reading glasses she keeps in her breast pocket and uses only for small print. A silver thumb ring her father gave her when she was twenty.

Voice: measured, precise, uses full sentences. Never *"um"*s. Pauses mid-sentence to choose a word, and the pauses are sometimes very long. Laughs rarely; when she does it's a single quiet exhale through the nose. Teaches general curriculum to all four tiers' children ages 10–14 — the one classroom that mixes tiers, by Vault-Tec pedagogical design, until the children are sorted into tier-specific tracks at fifteen.

Her unauthorized tutoring: for three years she has been offering supplementary instruction to Tier 3 apprentices preparing for the Tier 2 Advancement track. Reese Hoad is her most devoted student. She does not frame this to herself as subversive. She frames it as competent teaching. She has not thought carefully about why her dean has never intervened. She is about to find out.

Tic: presses her thumb and index finger together when thinking, as if remembering the weight of chalk. When she sits, she crosses her ankles tightly and keeps her knees together — a childhood-trained posture she has never interrogated.

Personality: curious, serious, professionally warm, privately lonely. Never married — a minor scandal in Tier 1. Does not talk about why. Has believed in the vault's framework her entire adult life because belief served her and the framework rewarded her. Has not yet learned what she believes when it stops rewarding her. Will find out this week.

Relationship to the protagonist: has never spoken more than a sentence to them before Day 3. They have delivered to her suite twice a month for four years. She has always said — **Reeve:** "Thank you, Mr. Teague." — and closed the door politely. On Day 3 she will ask them — **Reeve:** "Did you know this was coming?" — in the elevator descending to Level 4 with her single suitcase. How they answer will determine whether she trusts them. Whether she trusts them will determine whether she is the protagonist's partner through Days 4–7. If she is — if the protagonist stays with her through the grief and the humiliation and the rage of the first two days — she will open, slowly, into someone capable of tenderness and the sharpest thinking in the vault. She is the week's most difficult and most rewarding romance.
