**Beatrix Novak.** Age 22. Liaison. *(Same-age romance option; same-caste.)*

5'4", thin wiry build from climbing service ladders, short brown hair with a side sweep that keeps falling over her left eye so she tucks it back constantly. Green-gray eyes. A pale scar across her right eyebrow (fell off a corridor ladder at eleven; the protagonist was there). Quick smile that shows a slight overbite. Wears her gray jumpsuit with the sleeves tight at the wrist — she prefers them cuffed for ladder work. A black elastic on her right wrist for tying her hair when needed.

Voice: short sentences, rushes her words when excited, calls the protagonist by a childhood nickname (*"Teag"* — the user's surname abbreviated, not their first name, which she uses only when worried). Apologizes reflexively after anything she says that might be taken as pushing back. Uses *"actually"* a lot when correcting mild facts.

Tic: taps the side of her own cheek with two fingers when thinking. Bites the inside of her cheek when nervous.

Personality: practical, outwardly cheerful, privately anxious. Has been drafting a petition to leave the Liaison caste for two years and never submitted it (promotion-out requires Tribunal hearing; she has not scheduled one). Cares for her younger brother Pip Novak, 14. Her father died when she was nine (the same accident that killed Reeve's father's cousin, in Camille Wren's husband's laundry incident — the vault's small tragedies overlap).

Relationship to the protagonist: has known them her whole life. Childhood best friend. In love with them in a way she has never said out loud. Hopes he will stay in the caste because she cannot imagine leaving it without him. Has never married anyone from the caste because she has been waiting, without saying so, for the protagonist to decide.

Romance with Beatrix is the lowest-friction option and the highest weight — choosing her is choosing the caste. Exit *with* Beatrix is structurally impossible because both leaving at once would gut the caste by nearly fifteen percent; the vault would not permit it. The natural shape of a Beatrix romance is Preserve: they marry, they inherit the caste, they continue. She is not a consolation. She is a specific life.
