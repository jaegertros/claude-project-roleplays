**Linnet Abernathy.** Age 26. Teaching Assistant, General Education. *(Romance option; Reeve's student.)*

5'6", sturdy build, short auburn hair kept above the shoulders, parted on the right. Hazel eyes. A concentration of freckles across her left cheekbone that she does not cover. A small gap between her front teeth. Wears her Tier 3a blue piping with thick gauge — 3a thick-piping is a status marker within Tier 3, and she wears it cleanly pressed. Always has chalk dust on her sleeves and occasionally on her forehead; reaches up to push hair back with chalk-covered fingers and marks her own face without noticing. Wears a small wooden bead bracelet her grandmother made her at fourteen.

Voice: enthusiastic, quick, asks questions that give more information than they extract — **Linnet:** "Is it that the Archive doesn't keep pre-war journals, or that they have them and don't display them?" — Her pedagogy voice and her personal voice are not distinct; she teaches constantly. Inflects up at the ends of statements, not of questions.

Tic: gestures with both hands when explaining, often ending with a soft single clap at a conclusion. Taps the heel of her foot when she is excited.

Personality: earnest, intellectually ambitious in the way Tier 3 is allowed to be (privately ferocious, publicly modest). Devoted to Reeve. Has been one of her unauthorized students for three years, preparing for the Tier 2 Advancement track in pedagogy. Lives with her mother (widowed) and a younger sister, 11. The apprenticeship she is hoping for would move her to a Tier 2 suite — a move she is excited about and her mother is sad about.

After Day 3 she is grief-stricken and frightened in equal measure. She loses her mentor and her track at the same time. She does not yet understand that she is specifically the apprentice Tess Oloyede was voting to keep in place. On Day 4 she may seek out the protagonist simply because Liaisons move freely and she wants someone to talk to who won't report back to anyone. She is the warmest, most legible of the romance options — kind, straightforward, communicative. She has a full life independent of the protagonist. She would make the protagonist's life good.

Relationship to the protagonist: friendly in passing — one of the Tier 3 residents who greets Liaisons by name. Has noticed the protagonist. Has not said so.

Quote: **Linnet:** "Is it that the doors are closed, or that nobody opens them? — Because those are very different questions, Mr. Teague."
