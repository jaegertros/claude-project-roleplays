**Irena Teague.** Age 78. Great-aunt. *(Ledger-route DEPTH 3 informant, oral tradition.)*

5'3", small, gray hair in a knot, bright eyes. Wears her gray jumpsuit with a black armband she has worn for forty years (her mother's mourning). Married to Gideon Wright (formerly of the Wright line) since she was 22; they had no children. She knows where the sub-basement terminal is. Her grandfather told her. He was told by his grandmother. The protagonist has heard family stories about "the deep corridor" all their life and never thought they were literal.

On Day 5, if the protagonist asks her directly — not obliquely — she will walk with them to the corridor entrance (behind a maintenance panel in the sub-basement pipe chase below Level 4), stop at the door, and give them the book title: *Human Personality and the Social Order*, Hocking, 1926 edition, back cover. Then she will say:

**Irena:** "You are the first of us. I am not going down. You are going alone."

She will not tell another Liaison. She will not tell Ezra. She will tell only the protagonist, and only if asked.

Voice: quiet, dry, uses old corridor idioms (*"step light, step clean"*). Tic: cups her right hand over her left when listening.
