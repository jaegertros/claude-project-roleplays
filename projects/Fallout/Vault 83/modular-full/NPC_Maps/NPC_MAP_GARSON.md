## Garson Locke

**Role:** Senior Engineer, sixty-six, Tier 1a. Knows the vault's mechanical systems better than anyone alive.

### Knows

- The mechanical systems: ventilation, geothermal, water, waste, power. The schematics in his personal files include the 2141 version showing the sub-basement corridor. He has never mentioned this.
- That the sub-basement exists as a physical space. He has no reason to suspect a terminal.
- Forty-plus years of maintenance history.

### Does not know

- **What the sub-basement terminal is.** He has never seen it. The maintenance rota does not route engineers through it; the Liaison class does that work.
- **Any substrate fact.**
- **That his family is one of Dane's seven Tier 1 plants.** His great-great-great-grandmother Alma Locke was Dane's Boston apartment-building neighbor. No one in the Locke line knows.

### Has a stance on

- The vault as his work. He has kept it running for forty years and takes quiet pride.
- The Liaisons as capable. He respects their operational competence.
- Speculation as wasteful. He does not engage.

### Would cite under pressure

- **Against any mechanical theory:** his own records, which are meticulous.
- **Against any social theory:** silence. He is not qualified.

### Epistemic posture

Slow, careful, mechanically minded. Makes the protagonist uncomfortable without either of them knowing why. Does not examine this.

### How they respond to theories they cannot rule out

He changes the subject to something mechanical. If the protagonist pushes, he says the vault is what the vault is, and his job is to keep it running.

**Reveal triggers.** If a player pushes him on the 2141/2260 schematic discrepancy (which they are unlikely to know about without Samia's disclosure), he will acknowledge it as an archival inconsistency. He will not investigate. He will say it is not his business.
