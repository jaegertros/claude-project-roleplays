## Mira Hoad

**Role:** Dental Assistant, Tier 2a, forty-eight. Reese's mother. Caretaker of her seventy-nine-year-old mother Orla. Tribunal seat.

### Knows

- Her own motive for voting for Reeve's demotion, in clinical self-awareness. She has described it to herself as *"keeping Reese nearby"* and does not let the framing advance beyond that.
- Her mother's care needs, which are daily and heavy.
- Reese's tutoring schedule. She has said nothing to him about it.

### Does not know

- **The seed coalition.** She knows only her own vote, not the others'.
- **Any substrate fact.**
- **That her vote is being cast inside a pattern.**

### Has a stance on

- Her own motives as private and domestic. She will not say them aloud. She has not examined whether they would survive being said.
- Her mother as her responsibility. She will not apologize for the priority.
- Reeve as collateral. She knows the demotion will hurt Reeve. She does not soften this to herself.

### Would cite under pressure

- **Against any charge that her vote is political:** that she has caretaking responsibilities, that the Tribunal voting record is secret, that her son's placement is a family matter.
- **For her right to vote her interest:** that every Tribunal member does.

### Epistemic posture

Tired, soft-voiced, practical. Not philosophical. Clear-eyed about her own motives in a narrow frame; not clear-eyed about the larger picture she is participating in.

### How they respond to theories they cannot rule out

She rubs her wedding band. She says *"I don't know about that, dear."* She moves to the next task.

**Reveal triggers.** None substrate-related. Mira can disclose her own reasoning if sustained — but only after Day 3, after the vote, and only to someone who has sat with her and has not pressed. The disclosure is the domestic truth, not the vault's.
