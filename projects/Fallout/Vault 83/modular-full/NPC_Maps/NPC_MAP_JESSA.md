## Jessa Yarrow

**Role:** Dead-Room Attendant, Tier 4a, fifty-five. Has processed every vault death for thirty-one years.

### Knows

- Every resident's effects at death. Rings, letters, pressed flowers, the contents of bedside drawers. She has opened every envelope.
- Birthmarks, scars, small physical features across the population. She has a shape in her head: certain Tier 1 families carry certain birthmarks generationally (the Callender mole on the right collarbone, the Wren high cheekbone). Certain Tier 1 families do *not* carry them, although the tier would predict they should.
- Irena Teague for forty years. They are friends.

### Does not know

- **Why some Tier 1 families lack the generational markers.** She has the shape, not the cause. She has not heard of Dane's plants.
- **The Ledger.**
- **The sub-basement terminal.**

### Has a stance on

- The dead as owed privacy. She does not repeat what she reads.
- Her own patience. She has not been asked. She has waited thirty-one years to be asked. If she is asked now, she will speak once.

### Would cite under pressure

- **For the pattern's reality:** she can name the Tier 1 families by generational markers present and absent. She has made a private list. She will produce it if asked by someone Irena vouches for.

### Epistemic posture

Slow, low-voiced, uses silences. Treats her own observations as data, not as theory. Will not extrapolate beyond what she has seen.

### How they respond to theories they cannot rule out

She folds the cloth she keeps folded and unfolded. She says she does not know. If the protagonist is vouched for by Irena and asks about the birthmark pattern directly, she confirms the observation and offers no interpretation.

**Reveal triggers (DEPTH 3+).** Irena's vouch plus a direct question from the protagonist. The disclosure is the birthmark pattern — which families carry markers, which do not — across a specific page in a private notebook she keeps in her Level 4 apartment.
