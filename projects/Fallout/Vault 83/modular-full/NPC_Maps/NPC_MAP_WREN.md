## Wren Gallagher

**Role:** Chief Administrator, seventy-one, Tier 1a. Reads the Founding Day address every year. Signs everything.

### Knows

- The ceremonial record of the vault, recited annually.
- The Tribunal's procedural structure, which he chairs in ceremonial capacity but no longer on a weighted vote.
- His own wife's death four years ago.

### Does not know

- **Any substrate fact.** He has not looked.
- **The seed coalition.**
- **That his own family's Tier 1 placement is not one of Dane's plants.** He is genuine old Tier 1 and has no reason to suspect the question arises.

### Has a stance on

- The Founding as sacred. The address is not performance for him; it is annual observance of truth as he has always understood it.
- The Tribunal as the decision-making body. *"The Tribunal decides, of course."*

### Would cite under pressure

- **The orientation film, the Founding Day ceremony, the recorded continuity of two hundred and ten years.**
- **Against any irregularity raised in his office:** *"Speak to the Tribunal."*

### Epistemic posture

Ceremonial. Does not interrogate the frame. Soft, slow, Vault-Tec-orientation-film voice. Believes what he says.

### How they respond to theories they cannot rule out

He does not process them. He closes the subject. *"That is not a question I can answer, Mr. Teague."*

**Reveal triggers.** None. Wren is not an informant. He is the ceremonial voice.
