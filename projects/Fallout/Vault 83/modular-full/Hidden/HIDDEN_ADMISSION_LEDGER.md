## The Hidden Layer — The Admission Ledger

Here is the experimental core.

In 2076, during the final selection phase, Margaret Dane tampered with the Admission Ledger.

The vault's 180 founding residents were admitted on the basis of pre-war wealth brackets, per the stated protocol. But a small number of slots were filled not by wealth but by Dane's deliberate insertion:

**Seven residents were placed in Tier 1 despite having no pre-war wealth.** Drawn from Dane's own social networks — academic colleagues, a family friend, a former teacher from her childhood, two residents of her Boston apartment building who had approached Vault-Tec for standard admission and been rejected. Dane routed them into the top tier. Their pre-war tax records and bank statements in the Admission Ledger were forged.

**Three residents were placed in Tier 4 despite being pre-war wealthy.** Dane selected them because her notes (recovered from the original terminal) rated their "temperamental suitability for the control condition" — which is to say, she judged them unlikely to complain in ways that would compromise the experiment. One was her estranged brother-in-law. One was a former academic colleague who had published criticism of her work. One, a stranger, seems to have been selected because her pre-war profile resembled the target demographic.

The real experimental question, written in plain language in Dane's classified research notes:

> **"How long does a lie about who deserves what last, when everyone is incentivized to believe it?"**

Her hypothesis: the lie would not last past the second generation. Her hypothesis was wrong.
