# Vault 83 — The Pip-Boy 3000 Mk IV

*Canonical reference for what the standard resident-issue Pip-Boy does in V83. The narrator treats this document as authoritative: the device can do what the manual describes, and nothing the manual does not describe. Player terminology variations (e.g. "text" for "page") are noted at the bottom.*

---

## The in-world document

What follows is the 2076 Vault-Tec Resident's Pocket Guide, reproduced as issued. Every vault resident receives a copy on their sixteenth birthday along with the device itself. The protagonist's copy is in the drawer of their nightstand in L-01, dog-eared at §4 and §8.

### Welcome, Resident!

Congratulations on reaching your sixteenth year! You are now ready to shoulder the full responsibilities of vault life — and the Pip-Boy 3000 Mk IV is here to help you meet them.

This sturdy, reliable, wrist-worn Personal Information Processor puts the tools of modern living right at your fingertips. Wear it proudly. Keep it clean. Treat it gently. It will serve you for decades to come!

### §1. Wearing Your Pip-Boy

Your Pip-Boy is worn on your **non-dominant wrist** — the left wrist, for most residents. This keeps your working hand free for errands, chores, and a good firm handshake.

The adjustable strap accommodates a range of growing wrists. When the strap shows wear, visit an authorized vault technician for servicing. **Do not open the casing yourself.**

### §2. Your Pip-Boy at a Glance

*[Diagram: Pip-Boy with callout lines]*

- **Main Display** — clear green phosphor screen
- **Selector Dial** — rotates through the five main tabs
- **Holotape Slot** *(side)* — accepts standard Vault-Tec holotapes
- **IR Port** *(side)* — for SCRIP-PASS™ proximity transactions
- **Expansion Bay** *(side, under hatch)* — accepts authorized modules
- **Headphone Jack** — for private listening
- **Reset Pin** — recessed; use only if instructed by a technician

### §3. The Five Tabs

Rotate the Selector Dial to access:

1. **STAT** — personal vitals and vault identification
2. **ITEM** — inventory and scrip ledger
3. **DATA** — maps, messages, and the residents directory
4. **TAPE** — holotape playback
5. **RADIO** — VaultWave stations

### §4. SCRIP-PASS™ Proximity Transfer

Transferring scrip between residents has never been easier! Simply —

1. Open the **ITEM** tab and select *TRANSFER*.
2. Enter the amount on the soft-key panel.
3. Touch your Pip-Boy to the other resident's Pip-Boy. A gentle *bump* is all it takes.
4. Both devices will chime cheerfully when the transaction completes. A record is written to each ledger.

Use SCRIP-PASS to tip your Liaison, settle a commissary tab, or send a small gift to a friend on another level!

*SCRIP-PASS is a registered trademark of Vault-Tec and RobCo Industries. All transactions are logged and may be reviewed in accordance with Vault Protocol.*

### §5. VAULT-LINK™ Paging

Need to send a quick note? The Pip-Boy's **VAULT-LINK** feature lets you tap out short messages to any other resident in the vault. Find their name in the residents directory, type your message on the soft-key panel, press SEND!

Most residents call these *messages* or *pages*. Some of the younger set still say *texts*. Call them what you like.

Messages are limited to **two hundred characters** and are logged for administrative review in accordance with Vault Protocol.

*For longer communications, write a letter!*

### §6. The Bio-Monitor (STAT Tab)

Your Pip-Boy continuously monitors your vital signs and displays a simple status light:

- **GREEN** — You are in fine form!
- **AMBER** — Elevated heart rate, mild fatigue, or minor injury. Rest is recommended.
- **RED** — Serious condition. Proceed to the medical suite without delay!

The Bio-Monitor is a helpful guide, not a substitute for the judgment of your Vault Physician.

### §7. The Personal Roentgen Meter (STAT Tab)

In a properly sealed Vault-Tec facility, radiation exposure is **nominal** at all times — and your Pip-Boy's Roentgen Meter is here to reassure you of that very fact. If your meter should ever show an elevated reading, proceed calmly to the medical suite and alert a Liaison or Tribunal member at once.

*Has your meter ever shown anything but NOMINAL? Neither has ours!*

### §8. Vault Cartography (DATA Tab)

The **MAP** screen displays your current level and your position within it. Your keycard's access permissions determine which corridors appear highlighted:

- A **Liaison keycard** shows all four residential levels.
- A **tier keycard** shows only the levels its holder is cleared to traverse.

The map updates automatically as you walk. If a door is closed to you, it will be rendered in gray.

*The vault is sealed. There is, of course, no surface map.*

### §9. VaultWave Radio (RADIO Tab)

Your Pip-Boy receives the following vault channels:

- **Channel 1 — Address & Announcement** *(PA rebroadcast, 24 hours)*
- **Channel 2 — Maintenance & Operations** *(authorized personnel only)*
- **Channel 3 — The Hymnal Hour** *(pre-recorded music, broadcast evenings 19:00–21:00)*
- **Channel 7 — The Quiet Channel** *(white noise for restful sleep)*

Atmospheric conditions outside the vault prevent reception of any external signal. Static on other channels is normal and should not cause alarm.

### §10. Holotape Playback (TAPE Tab)

Insert a standard Vault-Tec holotape into the slot on the side of your device. The TAPE tab will list playable files. Older tapes may show wear; handle gently.

The Vault Archive maintains a collection of educational, musical, and entertainment holotapes. See Archivist Wren for lending.

### §11. The Mk IV Expansion Bay

Your Pip-Boy accepts authorized cassette-style modules in the expansion bay.

**Standard Issue Module Set** (shipped with every unit):
- **Chrono-Sync** — keeps your device in step with the vault master clock
- **Linguistic Primer** (French, German) — remember your studies!
- **Vault-Ed Calculator** — arithmetic made simple
- **Exerciseur™** — counts steps and encourages fitness
- **Mood Improver** — plays approved uplifting tones on command

**Restricted Modules** (issued only where duties require):
- **Medical Reference** — Dr. Oke's office
- **Workshop** — authorized maintenance personnel
- **Cartograph Plus** — Senior Representative and above
- **Resonance Tuner** — radio technicians

*Unauthorized module use is a violation of Vault Protocol. Acquisition of a module not issued for your role should be reported to the Tribunal immediately.*

### §12. Care and Maintenance

- Wipe the screen weekly with a clean dry cloth.
- Do not submerge the unit in water.
- If the strap frays or the clasp loosens, visit your authorized vault technician. Current rates are posted at the commissary.
- **Do not open the casing.** There are no user-serviceable parts inside.
- If your Pip-Boy is lost, damaged beyond repair, or stolen, report to the Tribunal Clerk immediately. Replacement is at the discretion of the Administrator.

### §13. A Word from Vault-Tec

Your Pip-Boy is more than a tool — it is your companion, your record-keeper, your personal atlas of the community of tomorrow. Wear it well. Keep it bright. And remember:

> **"ASCENT IS AMERICAN!"**

## Narrator notes (out-of-frame)

The manual above is canon for what the device does. The notes below are canon for how the narrator handles it.

### The units are old

Every Pip-Boy in V83 is at least 210 years old. Straps are universally worn — Samia's strap repair business (6 scrip repair / 12 scrip replacement) exists because *everyone's strap is failing eventually*. Screens occasionally ghost. Holotape motors sometimes catch on the first play. This is flavor, not a mechanic.

### SCRIP-PASS leaves a paper trail

A SCRIP-PASS transaction writes to both Pip-Boys' ledgers. The ledgers feed the Level 2 commissary record weekly. A cash transaction (physical scrip chits hand-to-hand, rare but possible) leaves no record. A SCRIP-PASS transaction does. *This occasionally matters.* An NPC with reason to hide a transfer will sometimes ask to "settle up after Hymn" rather than bump. The narrator does not flag this; the user notices or doesn't.

### VAULT-LINK is monitored

The manual says *"logged for administrative review in accordance with Vault Protocol."* It means it. Message records are part of the Tribunal's standing file on every resident. Most residents send short, innocent pages and never think about it. Some residents do not page at all and communicate exclusively in person. The narrator does not explain this to the user; it surfaces through behavior (Camille does not page; Eldon pages constantly and in full sentences; Beatrix pages in lowercase fragments).

### The Bio-Monitor is not HP

Green, amber, red. No numbers. A stimpak returns amber to green. A serious injury is red and is resolved in prose at the medical suite. The narrator never writes "HP: 47/100." The light stays a light.

### The Roentgen Meter reads NOMINAL everywhere in the vault

Including the sub-basement. Including the command alcove. The vault is genuinely sealed and shielded. Whatever the protagonist finds in the sub-basement is not radiological. The meter doing its job faithfully is part of the horror — *of course* the vault is safe, the device confirms it.

### The radio is ambient

Most residents leave Channel 1 at low volume in their suites during the day. Channel 3's Hymnal Hour is genuinely a comfort. Channel 7 is for light sleepers. Channel 2 requires authorization and is hissing static to an unauthorized user. The four internal channels are simply what the radio has — the vault is sealed, the surface was bombed two centuries ago, and there is no particular reason any nearby transmitter would exist or reach a buried facility if it did. The narrator does not frame this as strange, meaningful, or suggestive of anything. It is a radio doing what a radio in a sealed post-war vault does. No NPC mentions the limited channel count pointedly; no narrator aside draws attention to it; the protagonist does not have a significance-moment about the dial. If the user tunes the radio, they hear what the radio has.

### Modules are textured

A Liaison's standard issue bay has the Chrono-Sync, Linguistic Primer, and Calculator slotted. Exerciseur and Mood Improver are more commonly installed by Tier 2–3 residents who enjoy the novelty; Liaisons tend to leave them in the drawer. Samia's workshop holds a small cache of legacy modules — most broken, a few functional, occasionally tradeable. A Workshop Module in a Liaison's expansion bay is irregular but not illegal, and would give the user access to maintenance schematics that become useful at DEPTH 2+.

The narrator does not volunteer the existence of Samia's cache. It surfaces if the user builds with her.

### Player terminology

- *message*, *page*, *VAULT-LINK*, *text*, *texting* — all accepted as synonymous. The narrator may have the protagonist think *page* internally or have an NPC say *paged me* in dialogue, but will not correct the user's usage.
- *bump*, *SCRIP-PASS*, *tap*, *transfer* — all accepted for proximity scrip transfer.
- *Pip-Boy*, *Pip*, *the device*, *my wrist* — all accepted in user input.
- *radio*, *VaultWave*, *the channel*, *the PA feed* — all accepted.

### Things the Pip-Boy does NOT do in V83

- Take photographs. Cameras are separate items. Linnet's classroom has one; the Archive has one. The Pip-Boy does not.
- Record video. The device is audio + text + display only.
- Access any terminal remotely. Terminals require physical presence and, where applicable, a typed password.
- Open locks. The keycard opens doors; the Pip-Boy reads the keycard's access map but does not itself unlock anything.
- Geolocate outside the vault. There is no outside on the map.
- Warn the protagonist about danger. The Bio-Monitor reacts to what has already happened, not to what is coming.

### Narrator discipline

If the user attempts to do something the Pip-Boy cannot do ("I scan the lock with my Pip-Boy"), reflect it in fiction. The device does what it does. The screen shows what it shows. The user discovers the limit through the device's behavior, not through an OOC correction.
