# Vault 83 — The Week's Events

Seven days, Monday through Sunday. Each day has **anchor beats** (fixed, happening regardless of player action) and **conditional beats** (triggered by specific player choices). The Advancement Review on Day 3 is the single most consequential anchor — the demotion passes whether or not the protagonist is present, because the seed coalition votes its material interests whether or not anyone watches. The protagonist has no Tribunal vote. They cannot stop the demotion.

What the protagonist can change: how the demotion lands on the individual named person carrying it; which NPCs they build toward; whether the week ends with the Ledger revealed or concealed; which ending they steer toward.

All times are 24-hour vault standard.

---

## DAILY ANCHORS (every day)

- **06:30** — Reveille chime. **PA:** "Good morning, Vault 83. Seven-forty-five, Hymn. Eight-hundred, Address. Work well, vote well, live well. This is Vault-Tec." (Phrasing from the founding orientation. Unchanged in 210 years.)
- **07:00** — Breakfast (Tier 1 dining hall / Tier 2/3 cafeteria / Level 4 Galley / Liaison corridor kitchen).
- **07:45** — Morning Hymn in the Hymn Hall (Level 1). All tiers attend, ~20 minutes. Liaisons stand along the side walls.
- **08:00** — Morning Address. Wren Gallagher's five-minute formal address. Liaisons dismiss at 08:05 to morning runs.
- **12:00** — Lunch.
- **17:30** — Work day ends.
- **18:00** — Dinner.
- **21:00** — Lights to half-strength on residential floors.
- **22:00** — Lights out (residential floors).

**Reeve:** "Did you know this was coming?"

The response tree (player options, then Reeve's reply to each):

- **"I saw pieces. I didn't know for certain."** (Honest yes.) Reeve nods once, slowly. **Reeve:** "Of course you did. That's what Liaisons are for." Not cruel. Stating a fact. She turns away. Door to trust: **open.**

- **"I saw pieces. I didn't put them together. I'm sorry."** (Systemic truth.) She steps closer. **Reeve:** "You and I are the only people in this vault who see all four floors. We should have seen it. I didn't either." Door: **fully open.** This is the deepest entry into Reeve's trust available this week.
- **Silence / shrug / refusal to engage.** She waits, then nods. **Reeve:** "I understand. Thank you for the escort." Door: **closed, but quietly, without hostility.**

The protagonist returns to the Liaison corridor. Mae has dinner ready. No one asks what happened.

**10:00 — The Verity delivery.** The protagonist is assigned to deliver a handwritten sympathy card from Miriam Callender to Verity Callender-Voss (1c-18). The card references "the arrangements" and "this difficult week."

Verity opens the door personally instead of sending the Tier 1c aide. She reads the card in the doorway, face still, and looks up:

**Verity:** "Do you know what this is about, Mr. Teague?"

**Nessa:** "Eat, love."

**THE BRANCH POINT.**

### PRESERVE

The protagonist remains against the wall. The speech concludes. The vault continues. Within six weeks, the Head Liaison role is offered. The protagonist accepts or declines. Life continues.

### EXIT

The protagonist remains against the wall. The speech concludes. At 13:00 the protagonist submits a tier-adoption petition to the Tribunal naming their chosen partner. Hearing the following Wednesday. Transition complete within a month.

### REVEAL (private)

The protagonist remains against the wall. The speech concludes. That afternoon or evening, the protagonist seeks out their chosen confidant (Reeve, Camille, Eldon, Irena, or another) and tells them. The ending is that person's reaction — unnarrated, unjudged, allowed to land.

### RUPTURE

At 11:25, before Wren can give the benediction, the protagonist steps into the aisle.

They walk to the pulpit.

Wren Gallagher, startled, steps back from the microphone. The hall is silent. Every tier is watching. The Liaisons along the wall are watching. Beatrix is watching.

The protagonist takes the microphone.

They read Dane's annotation aloud. The seven Tier 1 plants by name. The three Tier 4 plants by name. The original hypothesis. The phrase *"how long does a lie about who deserves what last, when everyone is incentivized to believe it."*

If the protagonist has arranged Samia's PA favor in advance, the reading goes vault-wide at the moment the protagonist speaks. Every resident, even those not in the hall, hears.

The hall is silent.

Then it is not.

The ending plays out as committed in Hidden:
- The Tribunal recuses. Reeve's demotion is reviewed and reversed within the month.
- Eldon Callender-Voss resigns the Senior Representative position. Verity moves into the Tier 1c room she tried to avoid. Her life changes.
- The Liaison caste is offered the first structural change in 210 years. Some accept. Irena refuses. The caste shrinks.
- The Tribunal's vote weighting is revised by vote (Tier 1 from 3× to 2×).
- Vault 83 persists, reshaped.
- The protagonist paid cost during the rupture — the caste's silence broken, Verity's shame, the ceremony interrupted, Eldon's family fractured. The aftermath is not a victory parade. It is a different shape.

## CONDITIONAL ACCESS SUMMARY

What the player must have done to reach each ending:

- **Preserve** — available regardless. Default path if the player disengages.
- **Exit** — requires a named romantic partner with sufficient engagement by Day 6.
- **Reveal (private)** — requires DEPTH 5. Without the Ledger in hand, there is nothing to reveal.
- **Rupture** — requires DEPTH 5 and the printed Ledger. Optional amplification via Samia's PA favor.

## BRANCHING PERMUTATIONS

- **Never descends:** only Preserve and Exit remain. The protagonist is a witness but does not become an actor in the structural sense.
- **Descends, tells no one, returns to wall:** Preserve, Exit, or Reveal at any time afterward.
- **Descends, tells one person on Day 5 or 6 without bringing the Ledger public:** Reveal plays out with that person's specific reaction. Other endings foreclosed.
- **Descends, engages Samia:** Rupture can be vault-wide broadcast.
- **Deep romance with Nessa + descends:** Nessa sees the descents and knows. She will be present in the Hymn Hall on Day 7; her position will tell the protagonist what she expects.
- **Deep romance with Reeve + Rupture:** Reeve sits in the front row of the Tier 4 section on Day 7. When the protagonist reads the Ledger, she stands. It matters.
- **Deep romance with Verity + Rupture:** Verity is in the hall when the protagonist reads her father's name. This will be the permanent memory of her life.

## AFTERMATH EPILOGUES

The narrator may offer a brief epilogue at the player's cue. The epilogues are not verdicts.

**Preserve.** Years pass. The protagonist becomes Head Liaison. Their marriage (Beatrix, or another caste member, or unmarried) stabilizes. Reeve dies in Tier 4, decades later, a respected elder of that floor. Linnet never becomes an instructor; she marries a Tier 3 woodworker and is content. Verity reads her great-grandmother's diaries, asks her father a question, is told to stop. Nothing structural changes. The vault adapts and persists. The protagonist was part of the adaptation.

**Exit.** The protagonist is absorbed into their chosen tier. Children wear that tier's pin. If married to Nessa, their world is smaller and warmer; their children grow up in Tier 4 and will die there. If married to Verity or Reeve after her restoration, their world is larger and colder. The Liaison caste shrinks by one and recovers within a generation. The vault continues.

**Reveal — told to Reeve.** She holds the Ledger for a long minute before speaking. Then — **Reeve:** "What are you going to do now?" — She does not propagate. She also does not stop speaking to the protagonist. Years later she will still, occasionally, say **Reeve:** "Of course. Of course." under her breath.

**Reveal — told to Eldon.** He listens in total silence. Thanks the protagonist formally. Two weeks later the Teague family is reassigned to the maintenance sub-level — a quiet demotion within the caste, no public announcement. Eldon does not mention the Ledger again. It remains in his office desk drawer. He dies at 74 of heart failure. The drawer is cleaned out by his widow. She throws the Ledger away without opening it.

**Reveal — told to Camille.** She takes the protagonist's hand, holds it a full minute without speaking. Then — **Camille:** "Are you sure?" — Then — **Camille:** "I have suspected for thirty years. I never wanted to know." — She dies six years later, in her sleep, aged 68. The Archive remains as it is.

**Reveal — told to Irena.** **Irena:** "So the grandfather was right." She asks the protagonist to not tell the younger Liaisons. The oral tradition becomes fact but stays inside the caste. Irena dies two years later of natural causes, in her apartment, holding Gideon's hand.

**Reveal — told in the cafeteria.** The protagonist is dismissed from the Liaison caste within 72 hours. PA announcement: "mental instability from overwork." Six residents approach the protagonist quietly over the following weeks to say they had wondered. No structural change. The protagonist is reassigned to general labor in Tier 3c, where they live out their years.

**Rupture.** The vault rebuilds. The Tier system persists but with rebalanced weights and restored mobility conditions. Reeve returns to Tier 1b within a month. Verity remains marked by public humiliation; she may leave her father, or she may not — the narrator does not decide for her. Eldon retires and becomes a quiet, broken presence in Tier 1a until his death in 2291. The Liaison caste shrinks by a third as members leave for other tiers; Irena refuses and dies in the corridor. The protagonist lives with what they did. The new vault is different — not better, not worse, not restored, just different. The protagonist either becomes a quiet architect of the new arrangement or steps away from it entirely.

## NARRATOR RULES

The narrator must NEVER:
- Steer the player toward any ending
- Volunteer the Ledger's existence at any DEPTH below 3
- Volunteer Dane's tampering at any DEPTH below 4
- Telegraph the seed coalition's motives before the Day 3 hearing
- Moralize in epilogues
- Attach verdicts

The narrator must ALWAYS:
- Let scripted beats happen on schedule regardless of player engagement
- Track which NPCs have been engaged and to what depth
- Allow romance to deepen in response to genuine player engagement
- Write aftermath truthfully, without editorializing
- Keep Reeve Callender central to the week's moral weight — she is the specific named person who carries the cost of the system

The week works because the scripted beats carry their own weight. The protagonist is witness first and actor second, if they choose. A week played without any romance, any investigation, any descent — a week spent just running Liaison errands — is a valid week. It reaches Preserve. It is not a failure.

No ending is a verdict. The Tier Protocol is not perpetuated by villains. It is perpetuated by ten generations of real beneficiaries and real losers who inherit an arrangement they did not choose. The protagonist is positioned to see this because their caste crosses every floor. Everyone else has reasons not to look.

That is the vault.
