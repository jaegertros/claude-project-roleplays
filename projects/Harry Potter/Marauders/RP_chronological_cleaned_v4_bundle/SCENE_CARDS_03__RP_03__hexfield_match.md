# RP03-SC05 — Flag-capture rule is rewritten live

**In-story date:** Saturday 10 September 1977, 14:35–14:36  
**Location:** Hexfield pitch  
**Present characters:** Caleb, Liri, Mei, Cass, Match Three players, crowd  
**Status:** Canon; important rules update

## What happened
The thirty-second hold at the enemy flag pole broke down as a workable mechanic: point-blank wand standoffs and respawn-at-base defense made the rule unstable. Caleb called a live rule change. Each flag now has a ten-foot no-defender bubble. The enemy flag is taken immediately on touch, but recovering one's own flag after it has been dropped requires a thirty-second hold before it can be returned.

## Important canon established
- Hexfield rules evolve openly when play reveals a bad mechanic.
- Finalized flag mechanic v3: defender-exclusion bubble, instant enemy flag grab, thirty-second own-flag recovery.
- The Room can manifest rule changes visually and enforce them physically.

## Character / relationship beats
- Caleb values fair, playable mechanics more than preserving his first version.
- Liri functions as rules recorder/caller during live revision.

## Objects / materials / mechanics introduced
- Ten-foot shimmer-ring/bubble around flags.
- Hold-clock moved from enemy capture to own-flag recovery.

## Quotes worth preserving
> “You don't open with the miracle. You earn down to it.” *(Later Aisling's wording echoes the same design instinct.)*

## Open hooks
- Hexfield rules document must be updated to flag mechanic v3.

## Corrections / retcons resolved
- Discard any version where defenders stand point-blank at the flag pole for thirty seconds.
- Discard “respawn directly into contested base defense” as an intended mechanic.
