# RP01-SC00 — Campaign setup and initial canon

**In-story date:** Campaign setup before the opening  
**Location:** OOC/session setup  
**Present characters:** User/Caleb, narrator; generated/seeded NPCs  
**Status:** Canon setup, with later timeline retcons noted below

## What happened
The campaign was configured as a Hogwarts-student Marauders-era RP. Caleb Waddell was chosen as the PC: a half-blood, seventh-year-age student in the 1977–78 school year, with house to be settled through play. Mei Chang was generated as a Ravenclaw peer and older cousin/relative in Cho Chang’s family line. Caleb’s crush on Mei was established as private and unspoken for about two years.

## Important canon established
- PC: **Caleb Waddell**, half-blood, seventh-year age, entering Hogwarts in September 1977.
- Parent setup: one magical parent, one Muggle parent.
- Wand: **11¼ inches, cherry, unicorn hair, springy**.
- Mei Chang: Ravenclaw, same cohort, Chang family line, private two-year crush from Caleb.
- Era: Marauders’ upper years / final Hogwarts year overlap.

## Character / relationship beats
- Caleb’s longing for Mei is built into the campaign before the first scene.
- Mei is meant to be her own person, not a copy of Cho.

## Objects / mechanics introduced
- Caleb’s wand.
- Hogwarts-student vantage.

## Quotes worth preserving
> “Mei Chang. Cousin to Cho’s family branch. Crush unspoken. Sorted on opening.”

## Open hooks
- Sorting/house identity is initially play-discovered, though later play places Caleb as Ravenclaw.
- How/when Caleb speaks to Mei about the crush.

## Corrections / retcons resolved
- Early opening logistics had several wrong starts. The usable chronology becomes: **Wednesday 31 August 1977** is the day before Hogwarts Express, with Caleb travelling to London that evening and staying overnight before King’s Cross.

# RP01-SC01 — Retconned opening: Waddell home, father’s warning, and the pocket watch

**In-story date:** Wednesday 31 August 1977 morning, retconned from Thursday 1 September  
**Location:** Waddell family house and Waddell Clocks & Watch Repair, north Manchester  
**Present characters:** Caleb, Caleb’s mother, Caleb’s father, Mrs Crowther  
**Status:** Canon, but date corrected

## What happened
Caleb’s last morning at home played as a warm ordinary departure morning with an undertone of danger. He bought a Conway Stewart fountain pen and Quink ink for Mei from Mrs Crowther’s shop, with a note asking Mei on a date. He then went to his father’s clock/watch-repair workshop, where his father showed him a newspaper clipping about the Brackley fire in Salford. His father warned him that mixed magical/Muggle families may be targeted and asked Caleb not to volunteer his mother’s name at school.

## Important canon established
- Caleb’s father is magical and runs/works at **Waddell Clocks & Watch Repair**.
- Caleb’s mother is Muggle and close to Caleb.
- The family’s Muggle-side mail relay can plausibly route through the workshop.
- The Brackley fire/deaths are linked to the wider mixed-family danger.
- Caleb’s father believes Daniel Brackley is innocent/missing, not the murderer.
- Caleb receives his grandfather’s brass pocket watch. It is explicitly **not magical**.

## Character / relationship beats
- Caleb’s father treats him as old enough to hear the danger plainly.
- Caleb’s parents are loving but practical; they do not overdramatize farewell.
- The father’s dry humor becomes a strong family-voice marker.

## Objects / materials / mechanics introduced
- Conway Stewart fountain pen for Mei.
- Quink blue-black ink.
- Note to Mei: date request with Yes/No/Maybe.
- Grandfather’s brass pocket watch, hairline scratch, keeps good time, non-magical.

## Quotes worth preserving
> “There are people out there who think families like ours shouldn't be families, and they have started doing something about it.”

## Open hooks

- Whether Caleb’s mother becomes a vulnerability at school.

## Corrections / retcons resolved

# RP01-SC07 — First unlocking of Cuthbert’s herbal and the Path thread

**In-story date:** Wednesday 31 August 1977, late night  
**Location:** Mrs Penhale’s guesthouse room

## Important canon established

- Public contents: ordinary herbal recipes.
- Hidden quire: Aedith Wulfricsdohter’s visions and the Path sequence.
- Aedith: 11-year-old parish girl, seventeen involuntary visions, later silent for life, died AD 1332.
- Seventeen words: VIS, UMBRA, NOMEN, SANGUIS, CINIS, VOX, SIGILLUM, SOMNIUM, CARO, NUMERUS, NIHIL, STELLA, AURUM, OS, LIMEN, SILENTIUM, FINIS.

## Character / relationship beats
- Caleb’s “I want an actual puzzle” preference becomes important for how old magic threads should play.
- Caleb is not merely collecting lore; he wants to work it.

## Objects / materials / mechanics introduced
- The Path as a capitalized mystery thread.
- Cuthbert’s locks and hidden quire.

## Quotes worth preserving
> “Aedith daughter of Wulfric who being silent speaks for ever.”

## Open hooks
- Did Cuthbert walk the Path, or only record it?
- Are visions and Path separate phenomena?
- Who was the missing fourth petitioner, Iohannes Capellanus de Sleaforde?
- Does Margery the Little of Lacock’s commonplace book survive?
