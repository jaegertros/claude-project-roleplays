## 1. Silver water and `Waddellia rorilucens`

**Status:** Major live research field.

**Known observations**
- Silver night-flowers (`Waddellia rorilucens`) mark underground silver-water sources.
- Plucked flowers dull; rooted plants keep their glow.
- Source #1 can be opened into a proper well about ten feet down and does not visibly drop when drawn from.
- Other patches exist and can be opened later; the resource is repeatable, not scarce.
- Silver appears bound/carried in water; some spells, horn contact, and wandwoods interact with it.
- Cherry draws/interacts; walnut did not in the same way.
- Incarcerous amplified the draw/pull effect during well tests.

**Current protocol**
- Forest trips require escort/safety because of place/time, not because the water is rare.
- Do not boil/concentrate casually; ask Sage and use controls.
- Preserve Normal/Dense/Core silver-spot survey categories.

**Open questions**
- Is the silver-water behavior one law wearing many coats, as Maelor suggests?
- What exactly is the relation between flower, water, horn, Patronus, and wandwood?
- What does concentration/heat do?

## 6. Merdrake divergence protocol

**Status:** Live Herbology experiment.

**Core setup**
- Three glasses/roots:
  - Dark/root glass: tarped, no bloom, inward thickening/root development.
  - Fouled light glass: allowed to film/slime.
  - Clean light glass: light, film cleared regularly.

**Corrected principle**
- The experiment must show real divergence over time, not repeat the same maintenance beat.
- Light-on-leaves vs light-on-roots matters.

**Next actions**
