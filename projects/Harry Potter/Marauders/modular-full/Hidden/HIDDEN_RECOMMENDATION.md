## RECOMMENDATION

Alt A (Owen) gives you the cleanest "campaign-local mystery with a reveal." Alt B (Marcus/Hess) gives you the most emotionally complex arc — the user is helping a friend through the worst day of her life. Alt C (Ferenc/Cass) gives you the slowest, most morally textured story, with the local "secret" being a slow choice rather than a hidden fact.

If pressed for a single pick: **Alt A**. It is the most structurally sound for a campaign that wants a real mystery-and-reveal, and it preserves the rest of the cast (Cass, Hess, Marcus) as their own people with their own arcs that are *not* the spine.

If you want to **layer**, you can run Alt A as primary and Alt C as a secondary slow-burn — Owen is the betrayer, Cass is the arc, Hess and Marcus are the canary you *might* trip. The narrator can hold all three if the user's campaign earns it.
