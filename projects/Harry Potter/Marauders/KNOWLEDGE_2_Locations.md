# Marauders — Locations

Reference document for the physical world of the campaign. Most of this is safely readable in any session — it's just the geography. The narrator pulls by vantage: a Hogwarts student campaign uses mostly Hogwarts and Hogsmeade, with Diagon for the holidays. An Order campaign uses London, the Order safehouses, and operational sites. A Ministry campaign uses the Ministry itself plus the streets around it.

---

## HOGWARTS

A Scottish castle, eight centuries old, perched above a loch in the Highlands. Magical architecture — staircases that move, doors that vanish, corridors that lengthen on rainy days. Students reach it via the Hogwarts Express from King's Cross Station, Platform 9¾.

The castle has seven floors above ground plus dungeons and the Astronomy Tower above. The grounds include the lake, the Forbidden Forest, the Quidditch pitch, the greenhouses, Hagrid's hut, and the long lawn down to the gates.

### The Great Hall

The school's main eating and gathering space. Four long house tables run the length of the room; the staff table sits raised at the far end. Above is the famous enchanted ceiling, mirroring the sky. Breakfast 07:30–08:30. Lunch 12:00–13:30. Dinner 18:30–20:00. The *Daily Prophet* arrives by owl at breakfast — hundreds of owls, every morning, and the rustle of a hundred newspapers being unfolded simultaneously is one of the era's most consistent acoustic textures.

### The Houses

Each house has its own common room and dormitories, accessible only to its members.

**Gryffindor Tower.** Seventh floor, behind the portrait of the Fat Lady. Password rotates weekly. Common room is round, red-and-gold, with a fire that is always lit and the kind of armchairs you sink into and lose an evening in.

**Slytherin.** Dungeons, behind a stone wall that opens to the right phrase. Common room is long, low-ceilinged, lit greenish from the windows that look into the lake. Cold in winter. Atmospheric in any weather.

**Ravenclaw Tower.** West tower, behind a door with a bronze knocker shaped like an eagle. The knocker asks a riddle; you answer or wait for someone clever to arrive. Common room is round, blue-and-bronze, with a high domed ceiling painted with stars.

**Hufflepuff.** Basement, near the kitchens. Entered through a stack of barrels — tap the right barrel in the right rhythm. Common room is round, low, warm, full of plants. Smells like the kitchens. The portraits are of badgers.

### Classrooms

By subject:
- **Charms** — third floor, west wing. Professor Flitwick's domain. Student-friendly clutter, cushions on the floor for first-years.
- **Transfiguration** — first floor, near the Great Hall. Professor McGonagall's domain. Spotless, a row of writing slates, the desks arranged in a horseshoe.
- **Potions** — dungeons. Professor Slughorn through 1981 (he retires shortly before the canon present and is replaced by Snape). Fumes vent through enchanted ceiling slots. Cold and damp.
- **Defence Against the Dark Arts** — varies; the position is cursed, the room moves with the professor. In the late 70s the post turns over almost yearly.
- **Herbology** — greenhouses, on the grounds. Professor Sprout. Mud everywhere, dragon dung in pots, the kind of place that makes you sneeze and feel better afterward.
- **History of Magic** — second floor. Professor Binns. Ghost. Boring beyond endurance.
- **Astronomy** — Astronomy Tower, top of the castle. Classes at midnight. Bring a coat.
- **Care of Magical Creatures** — on the grounds, near the Forbidden Forest edge. Professor Kettleburn (the *real* Kettleburn — a different one from the Charms NPC; he's the Care of Magical Creatures professor in this era and he is missing increasing numbers of fingers).
- **Arithmancy** — sixth floor. Professor Vector if the campaign reaches that era; earlier, Professor Septima Bach (NPC, retired in 1979).
- **Ancient Runes** — sixth floor. Professor Hightower (see KNOWLEDGE_1).

### The Library

Madam Pince's domain, second floor, vast. The Restricted Section is at the back, gated, requiring a teacher's note. The library is where most of the campaign's research beats happen — looking up family histories in genealogies, old *Daily Prophet* archives bound by year, Ministry public records.

The library closes at 20:00 weeknights, 22:00 weekends. The librarian is implacable.

### The Hospital Wing

Fourth floor. Madam Pomfrey is the senior matron; Madam Pyne (KNOWLEDGE_1) handles overflow. Sixteen beds in the main ward; four private rooms behind a door. The hospital wing is one of the campaign's natural information-spread nodes — students go there for everything, gossip happens there, Pomfrey and Pyne hear what's happening in the school.

### The Headmaster's Office

Seventh floor, behind a stone gargoyle that requires a password (which Dumbledore changes regularly, often to confectionery). The office is a circular room with portraits of all previous headmasters and headmistresses on the walls — they sleep, they listen, they sometimes intervene. Dumbledore's desk is large, cluttered, the surface littered with silver instruments that emit faint smoke and small chimes. Fawkes the phoenix lives on a perch by the desk.

The user does not enter this office casually. Students summoned here are usually summoned for cause.

### The Forbidden Forest

A genuinely large forest, ancient, dense, dangerous. Centaurs live there — they have a rough territorial agreement with the school, but the forest is theirs, not Hogwarts's. Acromantulas have started colonizing the deeper sections (Aragog's nest is established by this era, descended from Hagrid's pet). Werewolves do not generally live there; werewolves in Britain are usually village-edge or urban, not forest-dwelling. (Remus's transformations during school years happen in the Shrieking Shack, accessed via the Whomping Willow tunnel.)

### The Whomping Willow & the Shrieking Shack

A Whomping Willow stands on the grounds, planted in 1971 specifically to guard the entrance to a tunnel that leads to the Shrieking Shack on the edge of Hogsmeade. The shack is where Remus Lupin transforms during full moons. The tunnel is accessed by pressing a knot on the trunk with a stick. The Marauders know this. The general student body does not.

If a non-Marauders campaign user discovers the tunnel — through curiosity, through being chased, through following someone — that is a significant DEPTH-2 event and must be handled with the war's gravity even if Remus's lycanthropy is not the user's concern.

### The Room of Requirement

Seventh floor, opposite the tapestry of Barnabas the Barmy. Appears to those who genuinely need it. Becomes whatever room they need. Few students know about it; the Marauders do; some seventh-years figure it out independently each generation.

### Quidditch Pitch

On the grounds, below the castle. Three goal hoops at each end. Stands seat the whole school for matches. The Quidditch season runs October to May; the four houses each play three matches, results determining the cup.

### The Astronomy Tower

The tallest tower in the castle. Astronomy classes meet at the top at midnight on Wednesdays. The view is, in this era, where students go to be alone or to be together. It is also, in canon, where Dumbledore will eventually die — but that is decades away from this campaign, and the tower has no shadow over it yet.

## HOGSMEADE

The only entirely wizarding village in Britain. A short walk from Hogwarts. Third-years and above visit on permitted weekends with a signed permission slip.

- **The Three Broomsticks** — the famous one. Madam Rosmerta runs it; she is, in this era, in her late 20s, sharp and friendly. Butterbeer, Firewhisky, hot pies, the place students go.
- **The Hog's Head** — Aberforth Dumbledore's pub. Goats live somewhere on the premises. The clientele is unsavory by design — Aberforth values privacy over reputation. The pub also functioned as the Order's meeting place when the Order was first formed (early 1971), and Aberforth is a discreet ally.
- **The Stoat & Sickle** — Doris Cubbins's pub (KNOWLEDGE_1). Quieter, smaller, the locals' pub. Few students go there; the ones who do tend to keep going.
- **Honeydukes** — the famous sweet shop. Run by Ambrosius Flume and his wife. Open every Hogsmeade weekend; mobbed.
- **Zonko's Joke Shop** — pranks and tricks. The Marauders are well-known regulars.
- **Madam Puddifoot's Tea Shop** — frilly, pink, embarrassing. Students go there on dates and immediately regret it.
- **Scrivenshaft's Quill Shop** — the Hogsmeade equivalent of Plumstead's in Diagon. More limited stock, similar function.
- **Gladrags Wizardwear** — a small clothier. Mostly socks and student staples.
- **The Post Office** — owls of every species, available for hire.
- **The Shrieking Shack** — boarded up, said to be haunted. The locals avoid it. (See above.)

## DIAGON ALLEY

The wizarding shopping district in London, accessed from the Leaky Cauldron via a brick wall. Most British wizards visit several times a year. Crowded, lively, slightly nervous in this era — the war means people glance over their shoulders.

- **Gringotts** — wizarding bank. Goblin-run. Marble pillars, sliding cart-rides down to the vaults. Vault security is famously ferocious.
- **Ollivanders** — wand shop. Garrick Ollivander, late 60s by this era, white-haired, alarming eyes. The shop is small, dusty, lined floor to ceiling with narrow boxes. Hade Ollivander-Brennan apprentices here.
- **Flourish and Blotts** — bookshop. Multi-storey, chaotic, the source of all Hogwarts textbooks.
- **Madam Malkin's Robes for All Occasions** — the school robe shop, plus formal wear, plus mourning wear (a brisk business in this era).
- **The Apothecary** — potion ingredients. Run by an older couple, the Throgmortons; Mrs Throgmorton has been heard to refuse to sell certain ingredients to certain customers.
- **Quality Quidditch Supplies** — brooms, gear, kits.
- **Eeylops Owl Emporium** — owls for sale and hire.
- **Fortescue's Ice Cream Parlour** — open in summer. Florean Fortescue is mid-30s in this era, just established.
- **Plumstead's Quills & Inks** — see KNOWLEDGE_1 (Florian Plumstead).
- **The Leaky Cauldron** — the pub at Diagon's London entrance. Tom is the landlord (already old in this era; he was old in the 70s and old in the 90s and there are private theories about why). Rooms upstairs for travelers.

### Knockturn Alley

A side-alley off Diagon. Dark Arts shops, dubious clientele, the place no respectable family takes children. Connor Lyle (KNOWLEDGE_1) works out of an office here. The Order operates here through Connor. Ministry Aurors patrol it sporadically.

- **Borgin and Burkes** — antiques and dark artefacts. A young employee named Tom Riddle worked here once. (The shop does not advertise this.)
- **The White Wyvern** — the most active pub in Knockturn. Death Eater associates drink here. Connor knows the bartender.
- Various unmarked shops dealing in cursed objects, banned potions, and information.

## LONDON

The wizarding world coexists with the Muggle one. Most British wizards are Londoners or have London business.

### The Ministry of Magic

Central London, accessed via a Muggle visitor entrance (a battered red telephone box on a side street, dialing the code 62442 — "MAGIC"), or for staff via Apparition into the Atrium, or via the Floo Network into the same Atrium.

The Ministry is underground, eight levels plus the Atrium. The Atrium is a vast hall with the Fountain of Magical Brethren at its center (a fountain depicting a wizard, a witch, a goblin, a centaur, and a house-elf, the latter three in worshipful poses around the wizard — a piece of unconscious propaganda no one has yet thought to remove).

The eight levels:
1. Minister's office and senior staff (top, just below the Atrium)
2. Department of Magical Law Enforcement (Aurors, Wizengamot Administration, Improper Use of Magic)
3. Department of Magical Accidents and Catastrophes
4. Department for the Regulation and Control of Magical Creatures
5. Department of International Magical Cooperation
6. Department of Magical Transportation (Floo Network Authority included)
7. Department of Magical Games and Sports
8. The Department of Mysteries (lowest, secretive, locked, the most heavily warded section of the Ministry)

The Wizengamot meets on Level 2 in a courtroom large enough to seat the full court.

### Order Safehouses (Order vantage)

The Order operates from a rotating set of safehouses. Order members do not all know all the safehouses — cell discipline. The user's cell knows two:

- **The Brixton Flat** — second-floor flat above a Muggle laundrette in south London. Tamsin Brae (KNOWLEDGE_1) keeps the keys. Used for cell meetings and short overnight stays. Wards include Muggle-Repelling, Anti-Apparition (can be lifted on signal), and a fidelius-lite that means the address slips out of memory for anyone not specifically told.
- **The Pembrokeshire Cottage** — a small stone cottage on the Welsh coast, three miles from the nearest village. Used for safe houses (Muggle-borns hiding their families, witnesses needing protection). Tended by Bree Tovey when she's in country. Smells like sea and wet wool.

There are other safehouses. The user's cell does not know them. This is by design.

### Other London locations

- **St Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries** — a large building disguised as an abandoned department store (Purge & Dowse, Ltd.) in central London. Five floors plus basements: artifact accidents, creature-induced injuries, magical bugs, potion and plant poisoning, spell damage (top — long-term curse cases), and the tea room. Tamsin Brae (KNOWLEDGE_1) works on Spell Damage.
- **Grimmauld Place, Number Twelve** — the Black family townhouse. In this era, occupied by Walburga and Orion Black; Sirius has run away from home (in 1975, per canon). Dark, oppressive, full of family heirlooms. Will become Order HQ in the second war but is *not* yet — in this era it is a private home of a Death Eater-aligned family.
- **The Burrow** — Arthur and Molly Weasley's home in Ottery St Catchpole, Devon. (Yes, in this era; they marry in 1969, and Bill is born 1970, so by the campaign present they have a small horde of red-haired children. The Burrow is a young family's home.) Not necessarily a campaign location, but a possible one.

## RURAL & REGIONAL

The campaign may visit:

- **Godric's Hollow** — small West Country village. Wizarding-Muggle mixed, with several wizarding families settled there. Bathilda Bagshot lives here. The Potters live here in the campaign's later years (post-prophecy, post-fidelius). Not yet famous in this era — it becomes so after Halloween 1981.
- **Ottery St Catchpole** — the village near the Burrow. Lovegood family lives nearby. Diggory family lives nearby.
- **Upper Flagley** — a small Yorkshire wizarding village, fictional but used for safehouses in this era.
- **The Highlands surrounding Hogwarts** — open country, lochs, hills, the long road to the Hogwarts Express. The user's family home, if they're from a remote family, may be here.

## CAMPAIGN STARTING LOADOUTS BY VANTAGE

### Hogwarts student
- Wand (issued at 11; rolled per KNOWLEDGE_5 if not specified)
- School robes, scarf in house colors, satchel
- Books for current year (per Hogwarts curriculum)
- 12 Galleons, 7 Sickles (term pocket money)
- Pewter cauldron, scales, telescope (standard kit)
- Whatever pet they brought (owl, cat, or toad — per Hogwarts policy)

### Order operative
- Wand (rolled or specified)
- Civilian clothes
- Whatever they had before joining (a job, a flat, a life)
- Pooled cell resources accessible by request
- A Patronus message-charm if they've learned the Patronus (intermediate skill — many but not all Order members can cast a corporeal one)
- A specific contact protocol with their cell coordinator

### Ministry employee
- Wand
- Office robes (departmental color trim)
- Ministry credential (standard issue)
- A salary in the appropriate Galleon range
- A flat or a room in the appropriate part of London
- Department-specific equipment per role

### Civilian (Healer, journalist, shopkeeper, etc.)
- Wand
- Profession-appropriate clothing and tools
- A workplace
- A residence
- An income matching the profession

The narrator should generate plausibly from the user's stated vantage, and adjust as the campaign moves. This is a starting point, not a fixed inventory.
