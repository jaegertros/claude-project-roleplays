# Wandcraft — Core Preservation & Binding (reference)

*Three methods of handling a core, plus Caleb's own experiment. Each traced to where it was taught.*

---

## Quick map

- **Curing** — *change the core to make it safe.* Dry it slow; it goes stable but a touch deadened. Orthodox, 300 years old. **Weakens.**
- **Keeping-fluid** — *refuse to let the core change.* Freezes it wet/alive/unchanged forever. Preserves fully; does **not** deaden.
- **Binding resin** — *seal the core into the wood.* The married build's sealant; bonds to the core permanently. (Fixing "always changes what it was.")
- **Wet-seal (Caleb's idea)** — *keep-alive + seat-stable, combined.* Untested hybrid.

---

## 1. Curing (the orthodox method)
*Taught by Jack Ablewhite — Sat 24 Sept, 10:36*

**Why:** a fresh core is *wet* — full of the creature's living volatiles. Wet things rot, or worse, stay lively and **shift in the wood after a wand's married** — and a wand that shifts is a wand that fails. Curing dries the core to stable/inert/safe-to-seat.

**Method:**
- **Slow heat, never quick** — a fire cooks a core to scrap.
- Hang it in a warm, dry, **dark** place (a curing-press, or Jack's cabinet by the flue).
- **Wait:** days for a hair, weeks for a heartstring.
- Draw the wet out **evenly**, a little at a time, so it doesn't **case-harden** (dry outside / wet within → cracks the moment you seat it).
- Blot as it gives up its oils, turn it, blot again.

**Doneness cue:** gone from **supple to firm** — *"bends but holds, like good leather"* — not a fresh twig, not a dry stick. A cured core sits in wood a hundred years and never shifts.

**The trade-off (the part nobody says plain):** curing **weakens** the core. The volatiles you cook off are some of the *life* of the thing — you trade a little of its fire for a lot of its reliability. Worth it for three centuries because a wild core in a sealed wand is no use to anyone.

## 2. Keeping-fluid (preservation without change)
*Taught by Emrys Bowen, apothecary — Fri 23 Sept, 17:40 (Sallow End). 5 galleons for the teaching; he threw in a crock of fixing-salt.*

**Why:** the **opposite** of curing. It doesn't dry the core or draw anything off — it **holds**, stops the clock. A core in it stays exactly as wet and alive as it went in, forever, and never rots, because nothing is allowed to happen to it at all.

**Recipe:**
- Water **distilled nine times, no fewer** — *"it's the patience that does it, not the cleverness."*
- **Fixing-salt folded in warm.**
- A **stilling-charm** worked over it as it cools, until the whole of it goes quiet and holds.
- Make it in a **big copper** and it keeps for years.

**Use:** store hairs, feathers — anything you want *held as it is.*

**Emrys's principle:** a material whose magic you want **left alone, you don't fix** — keep it as it came and use it before it turns. *Fixing is for dead things you've given up on.*

## 3. Binding resin (the married build's sealant)
*Taught by Jack Ablewhite — Sat 24 Sept, 11:05. "Three hundred years of wandmakers in fifteen lines."*

**Why:** seals/marries a core into the wood **permanently** — and the dragon-tooth gives it the magical bite to **bond to a core**, not just glue it.

**Recipe (order, heat, and look matter):**
- **Base:** fir and pine resin, melted and strained clear of bark and grit.
- **Cook:** long, low cook to drive the raw turpentine off, so it sets **hard** instead of staying tacky.
- **Body:** **beeswax** worked in, so it doesn't crack as the wand ages.
- **Magical bite:** a knock of powdered **dragon-tooth**, fine as flour — this is what lets it bond rather than glue.
- **Keeper:** a few drops of **murtlap essence** + a pinch of **ground bezoar**, so the resin never sours or turns on the core it seals.
- **Doneness by colour:** clear → golden → **the colour of dark honey.** Ready then, not a moment before.

**Warning:** cook it patient, mind the heat, **never let it boil** — or it's ruined gum and a week's stink in the kitchen.

**Reagents to source:** dragon-tooth (specialty), murtlap essence (standard healer stock — Imogen's wheelhouse), ground bezoar (Potions stores).

## Choosing per wand (design implication)

The four crafts line up as two roads:

- **Married & sealed** = cure the core (trade some fire for permanence) + bind it with the resin. Orthodox, durable, immovable. Fixing **changes what the material was** (Emrys's caution).
- **Kept & removable** = keeping-fluid (or the wet-seal, if it works), core not fixed hard, magic left intact. Caleb's house style.

So for any given wand the question is: do you want it *married* (cure + resin) or *kept* (keeping-fluid / wet-seal)? For a core whose magic you want untouched — a phoenix feather, say — the kept road preserves it; the married road trades some of its life for a wand that will never shift.
