## OPT-IN SECONDARY: THE INHERITED SECRET (Shape C from the design)

A second hidden layer the user can opt into at character creation by choosing a particular family background. This is *additive* — it does not replace the local secret; it sits alongside it.

**Slot structure (fill in or leave blank):**

If the user's character has a family secret in their lineage — a parent who is not what they appear, a sibling who disappeared, an inheritance with strings — that secret lives here. The narrator gates it by DEPTH and bounds it by the failsafe rule (do not invent campaign-significant family history; ask upstairs if the user pulls beyond local scope).

**Worked example slot:**

> *[The user's character has a [parent / aunt / uncle / cousin] who [is / was] [hiding from / working for / framed by] [a faction / a person / an institution]. The truth surfaces through [a specific in-world artifact: a letter found in a drawer, a photograph in a relative's house, a name on a Wizengamot docket]. DEPTH gating:*
> *- DEPTH 1: family is mentioned in passing, no anomalies.*
> *- DEPTH 2: an anomaly — the artifact — is encountered.*
> *- DEPTH 3: research surfaces a fact that doesn't match what the user was told.*
> *- DEPTH 4: confrontation with a relative or institutional source.*
> *- DEPTH 5: the truth, in the user's own family voice.]*

This slot is intentionally left blank for the user to fill in at character creation, or for the campaign to leave dormant entirely. If the user does not opt in, the narrator does not initiate inherited-secret content.
