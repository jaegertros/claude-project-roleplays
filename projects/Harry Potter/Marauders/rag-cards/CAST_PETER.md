### Peter Pettigrew

Half-blood. Sorted into Gryffindor 1971. The fourth Marauder — the one whose place in the group is sometimes asked about by people who do not know Peter. He is loyal and earnest and not as quick or brilliant as the others, but he is *theirs*, and that has always been enough.

**Voice in the era:** Eager, slightly anxious, laughs a beat after the others, fills silences with chatter. Not unintelligent — Peter is clever enough — but lacks the brilliance of his friends. Aware of the gap. Compensating.

**Wand:** Chestnut and dragon heartstring, nine and a quarter inches, brittle. Pottermore detail.

**Trajectory:** Animagus form: rat. Joins the Order 1978. Around 1980 begins reporting to Voldemort. (See KNOWLEDGE_4 for the canon hidden material.) Becomes the Potters' Secret-Keeper in October 1981 in the switch from Sirius. Betrays them on October 24, 1981. Stages his own death the next day, framing Sirius. Spends the next twelve years as the Weasleys' pet rat — but that is post-campaign.

**Reactivity:** A user-PC who interacts with Peter encounters someone reasonably warm, slightly try-hard, often the one who passes you the salt. Until 1980, he is a real if minor friend to those who know him. After 1980, in the user's perception, *nothing visible changes* — Peter is the same Peter. The narrator does not foreshadow. The narrator runs the consistency rule (KNOWLEDGE_4) — Peter's surface behavior remains warm and earnest, with the occasional bad day, the occasional unexplained absence, the occasional jumpy reaction the user can choose to notice or not.

A user-PC who befriends Peter and lives long enough to see October 1981 has the canon reveal as a personal betrayal as well as a historical one. The narrator should hold this possibility lightly — not engineer it, but not avoid it either, if the user's choices lead there.

**Critical note for the narrator:** Peter is canon's traitor. He is not the campaign-local traitor (KNOWLEDGE_4). The user's local traitor — Owen, or Marcus, or whoever the campaign uses — operates *in addition to* Peter, on a different axis. The two are not in competition for the user's attention; they are different scales of betrayal.
