# MARCUS BLETCHLEY — Revised Profile Fill Notes / Drift Flags

## Revision reason

The previous package was too rigid around the Marcus/Cass boundary. It over-weighted “Marcus must be addressed before anything proceeds” and under-weighted the later truth conversation.

The live state is now grayer:

> Marcus is owed a truthful end, but not necessarily full disclosure. Cass owns timing and scope.

## Strongest established anchors

- Marcus Bletchley is Slytherin 7th-year.
- He is Cass’s person / boyfriend / partner.
- They have been dating about nine months as of early September.
- He is pure-blood-correct, formal, distant, neutral.
- Not warm, not cruel.
- Capable wand-work in Defence.
- Played Slytherin reserve / pickup Quidditch against Lin several times.
- Bletchley family is old Slytherin pure-blood line, cautious-grey, not Vance-brothers shape.
- He lets the room forget he is in it.
- He absorbs Cass without making a thing of it.
- He does not flinch.
- Cass choosing him matters.
- Cass leaving him matters too.

## Key updated reading

### Willow/Shack beat

Marcus is:

- not nothing;
- not Lucian;
- safety/fine/old-life stability;
- not someone who would cleanse Cass or push her into overt harm;
- someone who might let her stay small and proper;
- not the person Cass ran to when she cracked.

Cass ran to Caleb and Mei because they are who she trusted to pull her out.

### Later truth conversation

The word “truth” becomes nuanced.

Marcus is owed honesty, but not necessarily everything.

Correct distinction:

- **Truth owed:** the relationship is ending / Cass cannot remain in the old shape / she is choosing another life.
- **Truth not necessarily owed:** names, Caleb/Mei details, trio dynamics, private intimate/emotional map, operational facts that could expose people to Slytherin/social danger.

Best summary:

> A clean breakup / true-enough ending with no operational detail is morally plausible.

## Main correction

Do **not** write:

> Cass must tell Marcus everything.

Also do **not** write:

> Cass can simply hide all of it forever.

Write:

> Cass owes Marcus an honest end; Cass owns timing and scope; full disclosure may be dangerous and unnecessary.

## Biggest continuity danger

There are two bad drift directions:

1. **Villain drift** — Marcus becomes Lucian-shaped or evil because the story wants Cass free.
2. **Confession morality drift** — the story treats full disclosure as the only honest option, even when details would create danger.

Both are wrong.

## House correction

Use Slytherin.

If any tracker/roster says Ravenclaw, treat as drift/typo unless explicitly recanonized.

## Voice guidance

Write Marcus as:

- quiet;
- formal;
- pure-blood-civil;
- not warm;
- not cruel;
- observant without advertising it;
- someone who uses fewer words than the scene wants.

Avoid:

- jealous melodrama;
- secret Death Eater reveal without evidence;
- comic cuckold framing;
- convenient full acceptance off-page;
- sudden chattiness;
- villainy by House association.

## Open threads

- What exact truthful ending Cass chooses.
- Whether Marcus already senses something has changed.
- Whether Cass tells him the relationship is over without names/details.
- Whether Marcus accepts it quietly, asks one dangerous question, or talks.
- Whether his tendency to talk makes operational disclosure unsafe.
- Whether stable-not-growth becomes a compassionate goodbye instead of a fight.
