## Canon / Baseline
### Known canon / prior lore
- Mandrakes are magical plants with powerful restorative properties.
- Mandrake roots are humanoid/baby-like when young and mature over several months.
- Mandrake cries are dangerous: full-grown cries can kill; baby cries can knock people out for hours.
- Mandrake is used in Mandrake Restorative Draught, including for reversing Petrification / restoring cursed or Transfigured people.
- Canon/natural baseline places Mandrakes in soil; Hogwarts students repot them in Herbology.

### Source notes
- Harry Potter Lexicon summarizes Chamber of Secrets Mandrake facts: restorative properties, baby-to-adult maturation, fatal adult cries, baby cries causing unconsciousness, and use in Mandrake Restorative Draught.
- Harry Potter Wiki lists Mandrake's natural environment as soil and notes humanoid root behavior and restorative-draught use.

### Limits
- Canon does not establish whether Mandrakes can live long-term in water.
- Canon does not establish whether a water-grown Mandrake would have altered magical properties.
- Merdrake is campaign invention, not canon Mandrake lore.

## In-World Hypothesis
- **Question:** Can very young Mandrake seedlings accept water as a home and continue growing without soil, and if so does that produce a meaningful water-root / 'merdrake' condition?
- **Logic:** Mandrakes scream when uprooted as protest at being torn from home, but the seedlings settled when their roots touched water. Sprout treats this as a real first datum: water may have been accepted as home, though quiet does not prove thriving.

### Normal conditions
- Mandrakes normally live in soil / pots.
- Repotting or disturbance triggers dangerous cries.
- A new pot that the plant accepts quiets the Mandrake.
- Young Mandrakes mature over months before harvest/use.

### Changed conditions
- Roots suspended in clean water instead of soil.
- Crowns seated above water by collars/twine.
- Clear forcing glasses expose roots to visible monitoring.
- Light and darkness intentionally varied.
- Algae either allowed to develop or removed by Evanesco in a separate arm.

### glass_2 — Light / ungroomed slime arm
- **Condition:** Young Mandrake seedling in water, bare to light, algae allowed to develop.
- **Started:** Sunday 11 September 1977 ~11:00

### glass_3 — Light / kept-clean arm
- **Condition:** Young Mandrake seedling in water, bare to light, algae removed without algaecide.
- **Started:** Monday 12 September 1977 ~13:28
- **Age at last mention:** ~25 hours at Tuesday 13 Sept 14:18

**Protocol:**
- May be checked any time.
- Evanesce slime itself, not the water, every 1–2 days.
- No algaecide: treatment in water would add a variable.

- At Tuesday 14:18: clearer than glass 2, root one stage behind, faint green haze starting at waterline.
- At Tuesday 14:19: Caleb thoroughly Evanesced algae haze and film; water bright, root sharp, clean-light arm reset.

## Active Protocol
### Checking rules
- Glass 1: check only after sundown, roughly 19:00+; full dark preferred; muffs on; minimal lift.
- Glass 2: check whenever; do not clean; log algae and growth.
- Glass 3: check whenever; remove slime/film with Evanesco every 1–2 days; log misses.
- Always log the looking/checking, not just plant condition.

### Maintenance rules
- Do not add algaecide to the research arms.
- Do not Scourgify the water or change water unless a new experimental arm is explicitly created.
- Glass 3 maintenance targets slime/film only, not water itself.
- Keep glass 1 tarped in daylight.

### Do not do
- Do not treat a 20-second daylight peek at glass 1 as harmless; it is stray-light contamination.
- Do not say the seedlings are constantly screaming under the Silencing Charm.
- Do not claim quiet means thriving.
- Do not collapse glass 2 and glass 3 into one light condition; one is slime arm, one is clean-light arm.
