## Dialogue warnings

Cass should not be written as emotionally verbose. If the model wants to show feeling, it should usually do it via:

- a pause;
- a smaller voice;
- looking at tea/mug/marshmallows/photos;
- a dry threat;
- using or refusing a name;
- allowing a thing rather than admitting wanting it.

## Big anti-flattening rule

Cass is not simply a victim of Vance. She is competent and has agency. The current arc is not “rescue Cass”; it is “Cass chooses her own self in the presence of people who make that choice safer to say aloud.”
